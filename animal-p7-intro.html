Introduction to Swine Production

Swine Production

Swine production is the branch of animal science that deals with the breeding, feeding, housing, care, and management of pigs for meat production and economic use.

It is one of the major livestock enterprises because pigs are efficient converters of feed into meat and can reproduce quickly.

  • Breed selection
  • Feeding
  • Housing
  • Reproduction
  • Health care
  • Marketing
  • Pork production
Swine production is the scientific and commercial raising of pigs for meat and profit.

Importance of Swine Production

  • Production of pork for human consumption
  • Source of affordable animal protein
  • Source of farm income
  • Efficient feed conversion
  • Fast turnover compared with large livestock
  • High reproductive rate
  • Source of manure and by-products
Swine production is important for food, income, and efficient meat production.

Swine

Swine is the general term used for domesticated pigs raised for agricultural and commercial purposes.

Swine are livestock animals commonly raised for pork production, breeding, manure, and processed meat products.

The word swine is the scientific livestock term, while pig is the common term.

Swine refers to domesticated pigs raised in production systems.

Pig

A pig is the common name for swine.

The term “pig” may refer to swine of any age, but in common farm usage it often refers to young or growing swine.

Pig is the common term for swine.

Origin of Swine

Domestic swine are believed to have originated from wild boars that were domesticated thousands of years ago.

Modern swine were later improved through selective breeding for growth, fertility, meat production, and adaptability.

Domestic pigs originated from wild boars and were improved through breeding.

Scientific Name of Swine

The scientific name of domestic swine is Sus scrofa domesticus.

  • Sus = genus
  • scrofa = species
  • domesticus = domesticated form
Domestic pig = Sus scrofa domesticus.

Characteristics of Swine

Swine are known for several important production characteristics:

  • Fast growth rate
  • High feed efficiency
  • Prolific reproduction
  • Short generation interval
  • High dressing percentage
  • Adaptability to different management systems
Swine are efficient, fast-growing, and highly productive livestock.

Fast Growth Rate

Fast growth rate means pigs gain body weight rapidly in a relatively short period.

Pigs grow rapidly and reach market weight early.

Feed Efficiency

Feed efficiency is the ability of pigs to convert feed into body weight.

Swine are efficient feed converters.

Prolific Reproduction

Prolific reproduction means pigs produce many offspring in one litter.

Pigs produce large litters.

Short Generation Interval

Short generation interval means pigs mature and reproduce relatively early in life.

Pigs reproduce early and multiply quickly.

High Dressing Percentage

High dressing percentage means a large proportion of the live pig becomes usable carcass after slaughter.

Pigs produce a high proportion of usable meat.

Adaptability

Adaptability is the ability of pigs to survive and perform under different environments and management systems.

Swine can adapt to different production conditions.

Classes of Swine

Swine are classified according to age, sex, reproductive status, and production purpose.

Swine are classified based on biological and production function.

Boar

A boar is a mature uncastrated male pig used for breeding.

Boar is the adult breeding male pig.

Sow

A sow is a mature female pig that has already farrowed or given birth.

Sow is the adult breeding female pig that has farrowed.

Gilt

A gilt is a young female pig that has not yet farrowed.

Gilt is a young female pig before first farrowing.

Barrow

A barrow is a castrated male pig raised mainly for meat production.

Castration improves meat quality and reduces aggressive behavior.

Barrow is a castrated male pig for meat production.

Stag

A stag is a male pig castrated later in life.

Stag is a late-castrated male pig.

Piglet

A piglet is a young pig from birth until weaning.

Piglet is a young pig before weaning.

Types of Swine Production

Swine production systems are classified according to the production stage managed by the farm.

  • Farrow-to-finish
  • Farrow-to-feeder
  • Feeder-to-finish
Swine systems differ by production stage handled.

Farrow-to-Finish

Farrow-to-finish is a complete production system where pigs are raised from birth until market weight.

This system includes breeding, farrowing, nursery, growing, and finishing.

Farrow-to-finish covers the complete swine production cycle.

Farrow-to-Feeder

Farrow-to-feeder is a system where pigs are raised from birth until feeder stage, then sold.

Farrow-to-feeder ends at feeder pig stage.

Feeder-to-Finish

Feeder-to-finish is a system where feeder pigs are purchased and raised until market weight.

Feeder-to-finish begins with feeder pigs and ends at market.

Major Uses of Swine

  • Pork production
  • Breeding stock
  • Manure
  • By-products
  • Processed meat
Swine are valuable for meat and multiple by-products.

Pork

Pork is the meat obtained from swine.

Pork is the meat of swine.

By-products

By-products are secondary products obtained from pigs aside from meat.

  • Lard
  • Skin
  • Blood
  • Internal organs
  • Bristles
Swine by-products have economic value.

Swine Industry Importance

  • Meat supply
  • Employment
  • Rural income
  • Food security
  • Livestock economy
The swine industry is important in agriculture and food production.

Board Exam Trap Terms

Gilt vs. Sow: A gilt has not yet farrowed, while a sow has already farrowed.

The dividing line between gilt and sow is farrowing.

Barrow vs. Stag: A barrow is castrated early, while a stag is castrated later in life.

The dividing line between barrow and stag is timing of castration.

Pig vs. Swine: Swine is the technical livestock term; pig is the common everyday term.

Swine = technical term. Pig = common term.

Swine vs. Pork: Swine refers to the live animal; pork refers to the meat product.

Swine = live animal. Pork = meat.

3-3-3 Rule: Swine gestation is remembered as 3 months, 3 weeks, and 3 days.

3-3-3 rule = 114 days.
