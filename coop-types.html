Types of Cooperatives

I. Based on Broad Sectors of the Economy

Agricultural Sector
• Marketing Cooperatives – supply inputs and market members’ products
• Supply Cooperatives – procure production inputs for members
• Credit Cooperatives – provide credit and financial assistance
• Production Cooperatives – engage in agricultural or industrial production
Non-Agricultural Sector
• Consumers Cooperatives – distribute goods to members and non-members
• Industrial Cooperatives – produce non-agricultural products
• Credit Union Cooperatives – organized under RA 2023

II. Based on Levels of Organization

• First Level (Primary) – members are natural persons
• Second Level (Secondary) – members are primary cooperatives
• Tertiary Level – members are secondary cooperatives

III. Based on Membership

• Federal Type – members are cooperatives in same line of business
• Union Type – cooperatives for non-business purposes
• Combination Type – federation and union combined

IV. Based on Capital Structure

• Stock Cooperative – issues shares of stock
• Non-Stock Cooperative – does not issue shares

V. Based on Services/Commodities

• Single-purpose – one commodity or service only
• Multipurpose – multiple commodities or services

VI. Based on Legal Personality

• Incorporated Cooperative – registered with government
• Unincorporated Cooperative – not formally registered

VII. Based on Geographical Coverage

• Local Association – local level
• Provincial/Regional – district or regional level
• National Association – nationwide
• International Association – global level

VIII. Based on Adherence to Principles

• Genuine Cooperative – fully follows principles
• Para/Pre-Cooperative – partially follows principles
• Pseudo-Cooperative – does not follow principles properly
