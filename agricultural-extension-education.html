Agricultural Extension Education

Definition 1

A system of educating and training rural people to develop skills and abilities in farming, homemaking, and youth building.

Definition 2

A well-planned program of bringing results of agricultural research and technology to rural families to help them solve problems in agricultural production, home, and community living.

Definition 3

A two-way process that brings agriculture and homemaking technologies from research agencies and/or universities to rural families in forms applicable to their own situations and, in turn, presents their problems in farming and homemaking for study by these institutions.

Definition 4

A process of getting useful information from people (communication dimension); teaching farmers management and decision-making skills as new technology inevitably places more demand on those abilities; and helping rural people develop leadership and organizational ability so that they can better organize, operate, and/or participate in cooperatives, credit unions, and other support services, and actively participate in making their local community conducive for living.
