💧 Part 5: Cropping Systems & Planning

Cropping System

  • A cropping system is the pattern of crops grown on a farm over time.
  • It includes crop sequence, management practices, and resource use.

Types of Cropping Systems

  • Monocropping – growing one crop repeatedly on the same land
  • Mixed Cropping – growing two or more crops together without a definite row pattern
  • Intercropping – growing two or more crops in a definite row arrangement
  • Crop Rotation – growing different crops in succession on the same land
  • Relay Cropping – planting a second crop before the first crop is harvested

Advantages of Crop Rotation

  • Improves soil fertility
  • Reduces pest and disease buildup
  • Improves soil structure
  • Enhances crop yield

Intercropping Advantages

  • Efficient use of land and resources
  • Reduces pest incidence
  • Improves productivity

Crop Planning

  • Crop planning involves deciding what crops to grow, when, and how.
  • It ensures efficient use of land, labor, and resources.

Factors in Crop Planning

  • Climate and season
  • Soil type and fertility
  • Water availability
  • Market demand
  • Labor and capital

Cropping Calendar

  • A schedule showing planting and harvesting periods.
  • Helps organize farm activities efficiently.

Sustainable Cropping Systems

  • Use of crop rotation and diversification
  • Efficient use of water and nutrients
  • Reduction of environmental impact
Sustainable cropping = long-term productivity + environmental protection
