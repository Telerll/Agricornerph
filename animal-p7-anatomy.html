External Parts and Anatomy of Swine

External parts and anatomy of swine refers to the visible body parts and structural regions of the pig used for identification, management, judging, breeding, and carcass evaluation.

The external anatomy of swine is important in breed identification, judging, health assessment, reproduction, and meat evaluation.

Swine external anatomy is important in management and production.

Anatomy

Anatomy is the study of body structure and body parts.

In swine, anatomy includes external anatomy, internal anatomy, and body systems.

Anatomy is the study of body structure.

External Anatomy

External anatomy refers to the visible body parts found outside the body.

These parts are used for identification, physical evaluation, judging, and production assessment.

External anatomy refers to visible body parts.

Importance of External Parts

  • Identifying breeds
  • Judging body conformation
  • Detecting defects
  • Evaluating health
  • Assessing meat-producing ability
External parts help evaluate the pig’s production value.

Snout

The snout is the projecting nose of the pig.

It is used for smelling, rooting, and exploring feed and surroundings.

Snout is used for smelling and rooting.

Mouth

The mouth is the opening used for feed intake and vocalization.

Mouth is used for eating and sound production.

Jowl

The jowl is the fleshy lower cheek region below the jaw.

Jowl is the cheek area below the jaw.

Ear

The ear is the external organ of hearing.

Ear type is important in breed identification.

Ears are important for hearing and breed recognition.

Eye

The eye is the organ of sight.

Eye is used for vision and health observation.

Neck

The neck is the body region connecting the head to the shoulders.

Neck supports the head and connects it to the body.

Shoulder

The shoulder is the front upper body region behind the neck.

It is important in body conformation, muscling, and carcass evaluation.

Shoulder is important in body structure and meat value.

Chest

The chest is the front body cavity region between the shoulders.

It indicates body capacity, strength, and constitution.

Chest reflects strength and body capacity.

Back

The back is the top body line extending from shoulder to loin.

Back is important in body length and topline evaluation.

Loin

The loin is the back region between the last rib and the hip.

It is one of the most valuable meat cuts.

Loin is a major meat-producing region.

Side

The side is the body region between the shoulder and ham on either side of the pig.

Side contributes to carcass length and body capacity.

Belly

The belly is the lower underside of the body.

It is important because it is the source of bacon.

Belly is the bacon-producing region.

Flank

The flank is the lower side region between the last rib and hind leg.

Flank is the lower side body region.

Rump

The rump is the top rear portion of the body near the tail.

Rump is the upper rear body region.

Ham

The ham is the muscular upper hind leg and rear quarter.

It is one of the most valuable meat regions.

Ham is a major hindquarter meat cut.

Tail

The tail is the rear appendage of the pig.

Tail is part of the rear external anatomy.

Hock

The hock is the rear leg joint below the ham.

Hock is the hind leg joint.

Leg

The leg is the limb used for standing and movement.

Legs support locomotion and body weight.

Pastern

The pastern is the lower part of the leg above the hoof.

Pastern supports weight and movement.

Hoof

The hoof is the hard protective structure covering the toes.

Hoof supports walking and weight-bearing.

Body Regions of Swine

The major body regions are:

  • Head region = snout, mouth, jowl, ears, eyes
  • Forequarter = neck, shoulder, chest, front legs
  • Middle region = back, loin, side, belly, flank
  • Hindquarter = rump, ham, tail, hock, rear legs
Swine body is divided into major structural regions.

Importance in Judging

In swine judging, external parts are evaluated for:

  • Balance
  • Muscling
  • Soundness
  • Structural correctness
  • Body capacity
External anatomy is essential in judging and selection.

Importance in Meat Evaluation

External anatomy helps estimate:

  • Muscling
  • Carcass quality
  • Meat yield
  • Cut value
Body parts indicate meat-producing ability.

Board Exam Trap Terms

Loin vs. Back:

  • Back = topline region
  • Loin = valuable meat cut behind ribs
Loin is more specific and more valuable.

Belly vs. Side:

  • Side = full lateral body region
  • Belly = lower underside and bacon source
Belly is part of the side, specifically the underside.

Ham vs. Hock:

  • Ham = muscular hindquarter
  • Hock = lower rear leg joint
Ham = meat, hock = joint.
