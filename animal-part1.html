🐄 Part 1: Introduction to Animal Science

Animal Science

Animal Science is the branch of agriculture that deals with the production, management, and care of farm animals.

It includes breeding, nutrition, health, and utilization of animals for human benefit.

Farm Animals

Farm animals are domesticated animals raised for food, work, and other agricultural purposes.

  • Cattle – for beef and milk
  • Carabao – for draft power and milk
  • Goat and Sheep – for meat, milk, and fiber
  • Swine – for pork
  • Poultry – for meat and eggs

Economic Importance of Animals

  • Food Source – meat, milk, eggs
  • Income – livestock business
  • Draft Power – plowing and transport
  • Raw Materials – leather, wool
  • Manure – organic fertilizer

General Terms

  • Livestock – farm animals raised for production
  • Domestication – process of taming animals for human use
  • Breed – group with similar inherited traits
  • Species – animals capable of interbreeding

Cattle / Carabao Terms

  • Bull – mature breeding male
  • Cow – mature female that has given birth
  • Heifer – young female not yet calved
  • Calf – young animal
  • Steer – castrated male

Swine Terms

  • Boar – male pig
  • Sow – female pig
  • Gilt – young female pig
  • Piglet – young pig
  • Barrow – castrated male

Poultry Terms

  • Chick – young bird
  • Hen – adult female
  • Rooster – adult male
  • Broiler – meat-type chicken
  • Layer – egg-type chicken

Horse Terms

  • Stallion – breeding male
  • Mare – female horse
  • Colt – young male
  • Filly – young female
  • Gelding – castrated male

Sheep and Goat Terms

  • Ram – male sheep
  • Ewe – female sheep
  • Buck – male goat
  • Doe – female goat
  • Kid – young goat
  • Lamb – young sheep

Importance of Animal Science

  • Improves animal production
  • Ensures food security
  • Supports farmer livelihood
  • Provides raw materials
  • Promotes sustainable agriculture
Animal Science = Production + Nutrition + Breeding + Management
