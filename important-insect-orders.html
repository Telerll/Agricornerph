🪳 Important Insect Orders

Entomology and Pest Management Reviewer

Definition

An insect order is a major taxonomic grouping used to classify insects based on their body structure, wings, mouthparts, metamorphosis, and other distinguishing characteristics.

Classification of insects into orders helps entomologists identify species, understand their biology, and develop proper pest management strategies.

Taxonomic Position of Insects

  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Phylum: Arthropoda
  • Class: Insecta
  • Order: Major grouping of insects
  • Family
  • Genus
  • Species

Importance of Studying Insect Orders

  • Helps identify insects correctly
  • Distinguishes pests from beneficial insects
  • Assists in pest management
  • Aids in biological control
  • Helps understand insect biology and ecology

I. Order Orthoptera

Common Members

  • Grasshoppers
  • Locusts
  • Crickets
  • Katydids

Characteristics

  • Chewing mouthparts
  • Hind legs adapted for jumping
  • Forewings modified into tegmina
  • Incomplete metamorphosis

Orthopterans are agriculturally important because grasshoppers and locusts can defoliate crops and cause serious yield losses.

II. Order Blattodea

Common Members

  • Cockroaches
  • Termites

Characteristics

  • Chewing mouthparts
  • Flattened body
  • Long antennae
  • Incomplete metamorphosis

Cockroaches are household pests and food contaminants, while termites damage wood and structures but also help decompose organic matter.

III. Order Mantodea

Common Members

  • Praying mantids

Characteristics

  • Triangular head
  • Large compound eyes
  • Raptorial forelegs
  • Predatory habit

Mantids are beneficial insects because they prey on many insect pests.

IV. Order Odonata

Common Members

  • Dragonflies
  • Damselflies

Characteristics

  • Two pairs of membranous wings
  • Large compound eyes
  • Predatory adults and naiads
  • Incomplete metamorphosis

Odonates are beneficial because they feed on mosquitoes and other small insects.

V. Order Hemiptera

Common Members

  • Aphids
  • Leafhoppers
  • Planthoppers
  • Stink bugs
  • Rice bugs

Characteristics

  • Piercing-sucking mouthparts
  • Hemelytra present in many species
  • Incomplete metamorphosis
  • Many species are plant sap feeders

Hemipterans are important crop pests because they suck plant sap and transmit plant diseases.

VI. Order Thysanoptera

Common Members

  • Thrips

Characteristics

  • Tiny insects
  • Fringed wings
  • Rasping-sucking mouthparts
  • May transmit plant viruses

Thrips cause silvering, scarring, curling, and deformation of leaves, flowers, and fruits.

VII. Order Homoptera

Homoptera is a traditional grouping formerly used for cicadas, aphids, whiteflies, mealybugs, and scale insects. Many modern classifications include these insects under Hemiptera.

Common Members

  • Cicadas
  • Whiteflies
  • Aphids
  • Mealybugs

Characteristics

  • Piercing-sucking mouthparts
  • Sap-feeding habit
  • Some have wings held roof-like over the body

VIII. Order Coleoptera

Common Members

  • Beetles
  • Weevils
  • Ladybird beetles

Characteristics

  • Forewings modified into elytra
  • Chewing mouthparts
  • Complete metamorphosis

Weevils attack grains and crops, while ladybird beetles are beneficial predators of aphids and other soft-bodied insects.

IX. Order Lepidoptera

Common Members

  • Butterflies
  • Moths

Characteristics

  • Wings covered with scales
  • Adults usually have siphoning mouthparts
  • Larvae are caterpillars with chewing mouthparts
  • Complete metamorphosis

Caterpillars are major crop pests because they feed on leaves, stems, fruits, and other plant parts. Adults may also serve as pollinators.

X. Order Diptera

Common Members

  • Flies
  • Mosquitoes
  • Fruit flies
  • Midges
  • Gnats

Characteristics

  • One pair of wings
  • Hind wings modified into halteres
  • Complete metamorphosis
  • Mouthparts may vary depending on feeding habit

Dipterans may transmit diseases, damage fruits, and serve as important pests in agriculture and public health.

XI. Order Hymenoptera

Common Members

  • Bees
  • Wasps
  • Ants

Characteristics

  • Two pairs of membranous wings
  • Narrow waist in many species
  • Complete metamorphosis
  • Many are social insects

Hymenopterans are important as pollinators, biological control agents, and sometimes household or agricultural pests.

XII. Order Trichoptera

Common Members

  • Caddisflies

Characteristics

  • Hairy wings
  • Aquatic larvae
  • Complete metamorphosis

Caddisflies are useful indicators of clean water and healthy aquatic ecosystems.

XIII. Order Neuroptera

Common Members

  • Lacewings
  • Antlions

Characteristics

  • Net-veined wings
  • Chewing mouthparts
  • Predatory larvae and adults
  • Complete metamorphosis

Neuropterans are beneficial biological control agents because they prey on aphids and other small insect pests.

XIV. Order Siphonaptera

Common Members

  • Fleas

Characteristics

  • Wingless
  • Jumping legs
  • Piercing-sucking mouthparts
  • Complete metamorphosis

Fleas are parasites of humans and animals and may transmit diseases.

XV. Order Phasmatodea

Common Members

  • Stick insects
  • Leaf insects

Characteristics

  • Camouflage appearance
  • Chewing mouthparts
  • Body resembles twigs or leaves

Some phasmids may become minor pests of forest trees and ornamental plants.

XVI. Order Isoptera

Isoptera is a traditional classification for termites. In many modern classifications, termites are placed under Blattodea.

Common Members

  • Termites

Characteristics

  • Social insects
  • Chewing mouthparts
  • Colony organization with castes
  • Important decomposers

Termites destroy wooden structures but also help recycle organic matter in natural ecosystems.

Quick ALE Reminder

The most commonly tested insect orders in the Agriculturist Licensure Examination are usually Orthoptera, Hemiptera, Thysanoptera, Coleoptera, Lepidoptera, Diptera, Hymenoptera, Odonata, Blattodea, and Neuroptera.
