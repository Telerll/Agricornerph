🌡️ Thermoregulation and Homeostasis

Thermoregulation

Thermoregulation is the process by which animals maintain a stable internal body temperature despite changes in environmental temperature.

It prevents:

  • Overheating
  • Chilling
  • Heat stress
  • Cold stress

Homeostasis

Homeostasis is the ability of the body to maintain a stable internal environment despite external changes.

It includes regulation of:

  • Body temperature
  • Water balance
  • pH
  • Blood sugar
  • Electrolytes

Importance in Farm Animals

Thermoregulation and homeostasis are essential for:

  • Survival
  • Health
  • Growth
  • Reproduction
  • Productivity
When balance is disrupted: feed intake decreases, stress increases, growth slows, and production drops.

Normal Body Temperature

  • Cattle – approximately 38.5°C
  • Swine – approximately 39.0°C
  • Sheep / Goat – approximately 39.0°C
  • Poultry – approximately 41–42°C

Heat Production

Metabolism

Metabolism is the normal body process that generates heat while using nutrients for energy.

Muscle Activity

Muscle activity, such as movement and shivering, produces heat.

Fermentation

Fermentation in the rumen produces additional internal heat in ruminants.

Heat Loss Mechanisms

Radiation

Radiation is heat loss from the body surface to cooler surroundings.

Conduction

Conduction is heat loss through direct contact with a cooler surface.

Convection

Convection is heat removal by moving air or water.

Evaporation

Evaporation is heat loss through sweating or panting.

Evaporation is very important during heat stress.

Thermoneutral Zone (TNZ)

Thermoneutral zone (TNZ) is the temperature range where animals maintain body temperature with minimal energy expenditure.

Within TNZ:

  • Normal feed intake
  • Efficient growth
  • Normal production

Lower Critical Temperature (LCT)

Lower Critical Temperature (LCT) is the lower limit of the thermoneutral zone.

Below LCT, the animal must increase heat production and may need to eat more just to stay warm.

Upper Critical Temperature (UCT)

Upper Critical Temperature (UCT) is the upper limit of the thermoneutral zone.

Above UCT, the animal reduces feed intake and increases panting or sweating.

Heat Stress

Heat stress occurs when animals cannot lose enough heat to maintain normal body temperature.

Causes:

  • High temperature
  • High humidity
  • Poor ventilation
  • Overcrowding

Effects:

  • Low feed intake
  • Poor growth
  • Poor fertility
  • Lower milk yield
  • Lower egg production

Temperature-Humidity Index (THI)

Temperature-Humidity Index (THI) measures the combined effect of heat and humidity on animals.

High humidity reduces evaporation. Even moderate heat can become dangerous when humidity is high.

Cold Stress

Cold stress occurs when heat loss exceeds heat production.

Effects:

  • Increased maintenance requirement
  • Reduced growth
  • Poor feed efficiency
  • Higher disease risk

Thermoregulation Mechanisms

Sweating

Sweating is evaporative heat loss through sweat.

It is effective in cattle but poor in swine.

Panting

Panting is rapid breathing used for evaporative cooling.

It is important in swine and poultry.

Vasodilation

Vasodilation is the widening of blood vessels to increase heat loss.

Vasoconstriction

Vasoconstriction is the narrowing of blood vessels to conserve heat.

Shivering

Shivering is muscle contraction that generates heat.

Brown Adipose Tissue (BAT)

Brown adipose tissue (BAT) is special fat in newborn animals used to produce heat rapidly.

Important in:

  • Calves
  • Lambs
BAT helps prevent neonatal hypothermia.

Homeostatic Regulation

Homeostasis is maintained by interaction of different body systems.

  • Nervous system
  • Endocrine system
  • Circulatory system
  • Excretory system

Water Balance

Water is essential for:

  • Temperature regulation
  • Nutrient transport
  • Waste removal
  • Metabolism
Dehydration rapidly disrupts homeostasis.

Electrolyte Balance

Important electrolytes include:

  • Sodium
  • Potassium
  • Chloride

Required for:

  • Nerve function
  • Muscle contraction
  • Fluid balance

Acid-Base Balance

Acid-base balance is the maintenance of normal pH for proper cell function.

Regulated by:

  • Lungs
  • Kidneys

Thermoregulation in Livestock Production

Poor thermoregulation causes:

  • Low feed efficiency
  • Poor growth
  • Low fertility
  • Lower milk production
  • Lower egg production
Thermoregulation directly affects profitability.

Poultry and Heat Stress

Poultry are highly heat-sensitive because:

  • They have a high metabolic rate
  • Feathers reduce heat loss
  • They have no sweat glands
Poultry rely mainly on panting.

Ruminants and Heat Stress

Ruminants produce additional internal heat through rumen fermentation.

Heat stress can be more severe in cattle, goats, and sheep because of fermentation heat.

Final Ultra Summary

Thermoregulation = temperature control
Homeostasis = internal balance

TNZ = comfort zone
LCT = eat more to stay warm
UCT = eat less + pant/sweat

Heat loss = radiation, conduction, convection, evaporation

THI = heat + humidity stress
BAT = newborn heat source

Panting = swine / poultry
Sweating = cattle

Homeostasis = water + pH + electrolytes
