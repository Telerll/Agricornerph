🌱 Crop Science

Part 1- Introduction & Fundamentals of Crop Science

I. Agriculture and Crop Science

A. Concept of Agriculture

  • Systematic raising of useful plants and livestock under human management
  • Purposeful use of natural resources to produce food and other needs
  • Broad industry involving production, processing, marketing, and distribution

B. World & Domestic Food Situation

  • World population projected to reach 9.1 billion by 2050
  • Majority of growth will come from developing countries
  • Increasing pressure on agricultural land
  • Arable land is only about 10% of total land area
  • By 2050, available arable land may decrease to around 0.1 ha/person

Implication: Food production must be increased efficiently.

C. Philippine Agriculture Situation

  • Population (2009): 92.23 million
  • Growth rate: about 2.04%
  • Agricultural land: about 32%
  • Agriculture GDP share: about 18%
  • Growth supported by:
    • Irrigation
    • Crop protection
    • Improved varieties
    • Better nutrition
    • Postharvest technology

D. State of Philippine Agriculture

  • RA 8435 – Agriculture and Fisheries Modernization Act (AFMA)
  • Signed in 1997
  • Main goal: Modernize the agriculture and fisheries sector

E. Crop Science

  • Study of economically cultivated crops
  • Based on observation and scientific principles
  • Main goal: maximize crop production and benefits

6. Basic Terms

Plant

  • Member of Kingdom Plantae
  • Has no locomotion
  • Photosynthetic organism

Crop

  • Cultivated plant grown for profit or use

7. Branches of Crop Science

Agronomy

  • Deals with field crops and soil management

Horticulture

  • Deals with garden crops
  • Pomology – fruits
  • Olericulture – vegetables
  • Floriculture – flowers
  • Nursery management
  • Landscape gardening

G. Crop Production as Science, Art, and Business

Science

  • Based on chemistry, physics, mathematics, plant physiology, meteorology, anatomy, and plant breeding

Art

  • Requires practical skill even without deep scientific training

Business

  • Main objective is profit

II. Classification of Crops

  • Crops are classified for order and organization
  • Identification:
    • Common name – varies by location
    • Scientific name – universal

A. Systems of Classification

  • Artificial – based on convenience
  • Natural – based on similarities
  • Phylogenetic – based on evolution

B. General Classification

Growth Habit

  • Herbs – soft stem
  • Vines – climbing
  • Shrubs – many branches
  • Trees – single trunk

Life Cycle

  • Annual – 1 year
  • Biennial – 2 years
  • Perennial – many years

Reproduction

  • Sexual – seeds
  • Asexual – vegetative

Special Types

  • Parasites- sucking nutrients
  • Epiphytes- grow on plants (not parasitic)
  • Saprophytes- grow on decaying matter

C. Classification Based on Purpose

  • Cereals – rice, corn
  • Legumes – mungbean, peanut
  • Root crops – cassava, ubi
  • Fiber crops – cotton
  • Oil crops – coconut
  • Sugar crops – sugarcane
  • Forage crops – napier
  • Beverage crops – coffee, cacao
  • Spices – flavoring crops
  • Latex/Resins – rubber
  • Medicinal crops – lagundi

D. Special Crop Groups

  • Green manure – improves soil
  • Cover crop – prevents erosion
  • Companion crop – planted together
  • Trap crop – attracts pests
  • Catch crop – short-season crop
  • Soilage – fresh-cut feed
  • Silage – fermented preserved feed
