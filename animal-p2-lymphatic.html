🛡️ Immune / Lymphatic System of Farm Animals

Immune System

Immune system is the body defense system that protects animals against pathogens and harmful substances.

Functions of the Immune System

Defense

Defense is the protection of the body from pathogens.

Recognition

Recognition is the ability to identify foreign substances.

Response

Response is the action of attacking and destroying pathogens.

Memory

Memory is the ability to remember pathogens for faster future response.

Pathogens

Pathogens are disease-causing organisms.

  • Bacteria
  • Viruses
  • Fungi
  • Parasites

Antigen vs Antibody

Antigen

Antigen is a foreign substance that triggers an immune response.

Antibody

Antibody is a protective protein produced by B-cells that binds to antigens.

Immunoglobulins

Immunoglobulins (Ig) are antibodies.

  • IgG
  • IgM
Antigen = foreign trigger / bad guy
Antibody = protective protein / good guy

Components of the Immune System

White Blood Cells (WBC)

White blood cells are defense cells of the immune system.

Lymph

Lymph is a clear fluid that carries immune cells.

Lymph Nodes

Lymph nodes filter lymph and trap pathogens.

Spleen

Spleen filters blood and removes pathogens.

Thymus

Thymus is the site of T-cell maturation.

Bone Marrow

Bone marrow produces blood cells and immune cells.

GALT in Livestock

GALT

Gut-Associated Lymphoid Tissue (GALT) is immune tissue located in the digestive tract.

Peyer’s Patches

Peyer’s patches are lymphoid tissues in the intestines that monitor intestinal pathogens.

GALT is important because livestock may ingest pathogens through feed, water, and grazing.

Types of Immunity

Innate Immunity

Innate immunity is the natural, immediate defense present at birth.

  • Skin
  • Mucus
  • Inflammation

Acquired / Adaptive Immunity

Acquired immunity develops after exposure to pathogens or vaccination.

  • Antibodies
  • Immune memory

Immune Cells

Neutrophils

Neutrophils are first responders that engulf pathogens.

Macrophages

Macrophages engulf pathogens and dead cells.

They also act as antigen-presenting cells.

Antigen-Presenting Cells (APCs)

APCs show pathogen fragments to T-cells to activate immune response.

Eosinophils

Eosinophils are white blood cells commonly associated with parasitic infections.

They are very important in grazing animals.

Lymphocytes

Lymphocytes are specialized immune cells.

  • B-cells
  • T-cells

B-cells and T-cells

B-cells

B-cells produce antibodies.

T-cells

T-cells destroy infected cells and coordinate immune response.

Inflammation

Inflammation is the local response to injury or infection.

Common Signs

  • Redness
  • Heat
  • Swelling
  • Pain

Vaccination

Vaccination introduces antigen to stimulate immunity.

Purpose

  • Disease prevention
  • Immune memory

Passive Immunity

Passive immunity is temporary immunity received from another source.

Colostrum

Colostrum is the first milk rich in antibodies, especially IgG.

Colostrum is critical for calves, piglets, and lambs.

Immune System in Farm Animals

  • Protects against disease
  • Improves survival
  • Reduces mortality
  • Supports growth and production

Immune System and Livestock Production

  • Healthier animals grow better
  • Less disease improves feed efficiency
  • Stronger immunity supports better production

Stress and Immunity

Stress can suppress the immune system.

Stress → Cortisol → Weak immunity

Effects

  • Higher disease risk
  • Poor growth
  • Lower production

Final Ultra Summary
