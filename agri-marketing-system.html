The Agricultural Marketing System

The agricultural marketing system refers to the organized system used for marketing agricultural products.

It is a complex structure where different subsystems interact with one another and with various marketing environments.

Components or Subsystems of the Marketing System

a. Producer Subsystem

This subsystem consists of producers or initiators of production such as small farmers and corporate farms.

b. Channel Subsystem

This subsystem includes intermediaries and market participants responsible for making products available to consumers at the proper place, time, and form.

c. Flow Subsystem

This subsystem facilitates the movement of products, finances, and market information.

d. Functional Subsystem

This subsystem consists of marketing functions and services related to the creation of place, time, and form utilities.

It includes activities such as:

  • Assembly
  • Concentration
  • Dispersion
  • Equalization

e. Consumer Subsystem

This subsystem refers to the final users or consumers of agricultural products.

f. Environmental Subsystem

This subsystem supports and influences market performance.

It includes factors affecting the marketing system such as:

  • Climatic or Physical Factors
  • Socio-cultural Factors
  • Economic and Technological Factors
  • Legal and Political Factors

Marketing Questions

1. What to produce and how to prepare it for sale?

Consumer preferences greatly influence production decisions.

Pre-sale services may also affect the value of products.

2. When and where to sell?

Product prices vary throughout the year, therefore storage and production practices may be adjusted to maximize returns.

Different market outlets also offer various advantages and disadvantages.

3. How much of the marketing work should farmers do individually or collectively?

Farmers may perform marketing activities individually or as members of organized groups.

4. What can be done to expand markets?

Understanding consumer behavior helps determine effective strategies for market expansion.

5. Which marketing arrangements are desirable?

Different marketing arrangements should be evaluated to determine the most beneficial option.

6. How can undesirable marketing practices be corrected?

Necessary changes and improvements should be implemented to improve marketing efficiency and fairness.
