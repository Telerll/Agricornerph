Cell, DNA, Gene, and Chromosomes

Cell

Cell is the basic structural and functional unit of life.

All living organisms, including animals, are made of cells.

Cell = basic unit of life.

Importance of the Cell

Cells are important because they:

  • Form tissues
  • Form organs
  • Perform body functions
  • Carry genetic information
Cells are the foundation of life and heredity.

Cell Structure

The animal cell has three major parts:

  • Cell membrane
  • Cytoplasm
  • Nucleus
The nucleus is the control center of the cell.

Cell Membrane

Cell membrane is the outer covering of the cell.

Functions:

  • Protects the cell
  • Controls movement of materials in and out
Cell membrane regulates transport.

Cytoplasm

Cytoplasm is the jelly-like material inside the cell.

Functions:

  • Holds organelles
  • Site of many metabolic activities
Cytoplasm is the working area of the cell.

Nucleus

Nucleus is the control center of the cell.

Functions:

  • Controls cell activities
  • Stores genetic material
Nucleus contains hereditary material.

DNA

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid) is the hereditary material found in the nucleus.

DNA contains instructions for:

  • Growth
  • Development
  • Reproduction
  • Inherited traits
DNA carries genetic information.

Structure of DNA

DNA is shaped like a double helix.

It is made of repeating units called nucleotides.

Each nucleotide contains:

  • Deoxyribose sugar
  • Phosphate
  • Nitrogen base
DNA is the molecular basis of heredity.

Nitrogen Bases

DNA contains four nitrogen bases:

  • Adenine (A)
  • Thymine (T)
  • Guanine (G)
  • Cytosine (C)

Base pairing rule:

  • A pairs with T
  • G pairs with C
A = T
G = C

Gene

Gene is the basic unit of heredity.

A gene is a segment of DNA that controls a specific trait.

Examples:

  • Coat color
  • Horned condition
  • Milk production potential
Genes determine traits.

Function of Genes

Genes carry instructions for making proteins.

These proteins influence:

  • Growth
  • Metabolism
  • Structure
  • Production traits
Genes control traits through proteins.

Chromosome

Chromosome is a thread-like structure in the nucleus made of DNA and protein.

Chromosomes carry genes.

Chromosomes are packages of genes.

Function of Chromosomes

Chromosomes:

  • Store DNA
  • Organize genes
  • Transmit hereditary information
Chromosomes carry and organize genetic information.

Chromosome Number

Each species has a specific chromosome number.

  • Cattle = 60
  • Carabao / River buffalo = 48
  • Swamp buffalo = 50
  • Horse = 64
  • Pig = 38
  • Goat = 60
  • Sheep = 54
  • Chicken = 78
Chromosome number is species-specific.

Homologous Chromosomes

Homologous chromosomes are chromosome pairs.

One chromosome comes from the sire, and the other comes from the dam.

Offspring inherit one chromosome from each parent.

Diploid Number

Diploid number (2n) is the total number of chromosomes in body cells.

Example:

  • Cattle = 2n = 60
Body cells are diploid.

Haploid Number

Haploid number (n) is half the chromosome number.

It is found in sex cells:

  • Sperm
  • Egg

These cells are formed through meiosis.

Example:

  • Cattle = n = 30
Gametes are haploid.

Gene Locus

Locus is the specific location of a gene on a chromosome.

Every gene occupies a specific position.

Alleles

Alleles are alternative forms of the same gene found at the same locus.

Example:

  • Black coat allele
  • Red coat allele

Homozygous

Homozygous means the alleles are the same, such as BB or bb.

Heterozygous

Heterozygous means the alleles are different, such as Bb.

Alleles create variation in traits.

Genome

Genome is the complete set of genetic material of an organism.

Genome = all genetic information of an animal.

Board Exam Master Concept

The flow of heredity is:

Cell → Nucleus → DNA → Gene → Protein → Trait

This is the biological pathway of inheritance.
