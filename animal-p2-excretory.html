🌡️ Excretory / Urinary System of Farm Animals

Excretory System

Excretory system is the body system responsible for removing metabolic wastes and maintaining fluid balance.

Functions of the Excretory System

Excretion

Excretion is the removal of metabolic waste products from the body.

Osmoregulation

Osmoregulation is the regulation of water and electrolyte balance.

Acid-Base Balance

Acid-base balance is the maintenance of normal pH of body fluids.

Homeostasis

Homeostasis is the maintenance of internal stability.

Main Organs of the Excretory System

Kidneys

Kidneys are the main organs that filter blood and produce urine.

Ureters

Ureters carry urine from the kidneys to the urinary bladder.

Urinary Bladder

Urinary bladder stores urine temporarily.

Urethra

Urethra carries urine out of the body.

Kidney Structure

Cortex

Cortex is the outer region of the kidney.

Medulla

Medulla is the inner region of the kidney.

Renal Pelvis

Renal pelvis is the funnel-like region that collects urine before it moves to the ureter.

Nephron

Nephron is the functional unit of the kidney.

Glomerulus

Glomerulus is a network of capillaries that filters blood.

Bowman’s Capsule

Bowman’s capsule collects the filtrate from the glomerulus.

Tubules

Tubules reabsorb useful substances and help modify the filtrate.

Loop of Henle

Loop of Henle is the part of the nephron responsible for concentrating urine.

It helps conserve water and maintain water balance.

Animals adapted to dry environments, such as goats, may have longer loops of Henle to conserve more water.

Urine Formation

Filtration

Filtration is the process where blood is filtered in the glomerulus.

Reabsorption

Reabsorption is the return of useful substances from the filtrate back to the blood.

Secretion

Secretion is the addition of wastes from the blood into the filtrate.

Excretion

Excretion is the removal of urine from the body.

Nitrogenous Waste

Urea

Urea is the main nitrogenous waste in mammals.

It is formed from protein metabolism.

Uric Acid

Uric acid is the main nitrogenous waste in birds.

It allows poultry to conserve water because it is excreted as semi-solid waste.

Water Balance

ADH (Antidiuretic Hormone)

ADH increases water reabsorption in the kidneys.

It reduces water loss from the body.

Aldosterone

Aldosterone increases sodium retention.

It helps maintain water and electrolyte balance.

ADH = water retention
Aldosterone = sodium retention

Renal Threshold

Renal threshold is the maximum amount of a substance that the kidney can reabsorb.

If the level of a substance exceeds the renal threshold, it may appear in the urine.

Example: Excess blood glucose may spill into urine, indicating metabolic imbalance.

Specific Gravity

Specific gravity measures urine concentration.

It is used to assess hydration status in animals.

  • High specific gravity – concentrated urine, possible dehydration
  • Low specific gravity – dilute urine, higher water content

Excretory System in Farm Animals

  • Removes waste products
  • Regulates body fluids
  • Supports metabolism
  • Maintains internal balance

Livestock Importance

  • Important in hydration
  • Important in feed efficiency
  • Important in heat stress management
  • Important in animal health monitoring

Poultry Difference

Birds do not excrete liquid urine like mammals.

They excrete uric acid as semi-solid waste.

Uric acid helps poultry conserve water.

Stress and Excretion

  • Dehydration affects kidney function
  • Heat stress increases water loss
  • Poor water balance can reduce animal performance
