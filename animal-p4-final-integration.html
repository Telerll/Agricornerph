Part 4 Final Integration

What Part 4 Covers

Part 4 focuses on Animal Genetics and Breeding.

It explains how traits are inherited, why animals differ genetically, and how breeders improve livestock through selection, mating systems, and biotechnology.

Part 4 explains how genetic improvement happens.

Core Foundation of Part 4

Animal breeding is built on five major foundations:

  • Genetics = study of heredity
  • Inheritance = passing of traits
  • Variation = differences among animals
  • Selection = choosing superior parents
  • Breeding systems = planned mating methods
These are the pillars of genetic improvement.

Genetics

Genetics is the study of heredity and variation.

It explains how traits are passed from parents to offspring.

Examples:

  • Coat color
  • Body size
  • Milk production
  • Growth rate
Genetics explains inheritance of traits.

Cell, DNA, Gene, and Chromosome

The genetic system follows this structure:

  • Cell = basic unit of life
  • Nucleus = control center of the cell
  • Chromosome = structure carrying DNA
  • DNA = hereditary material
  • Gene = unit of inheritance
Gene is the basic unit of heredity.

Mendelian Inheritance

Mendelian inheritance explains how traits are passed using genes and alleles.

Key laws:

  • Law of dominance = dominant trait masks recessive trait
  • Law of segregation = allele pairs separate during gamete formation
  • Law of independent assortment = genes assort independently
Mendel explains basic inheritance patterns.

Genetic Variation

Genetic variation is the difference in genes among animals.

It is the raw material for selection.

Sources:

  • Mutation = sudden DNA change
  • Recombination = reshuffling of genes
  • Segregation = separation of alleles
  • Fertilization = union of sperm and egg
No variation = no selection.

Heritability

Heritability (h²) is the proportion of a trait controlled by genes.

h² = Vg / Vp

Vg = genetic variance
Vp = phenotypic variance

It tells breeders how well selection will work.

High heritability = faster genetic progress.

Selection

Selection is choosing superior animals to become parents.

It is the foundation of animal improvement.

Selection may be based on:

  • Phenotype = observable traits
  • Pedigree = ancestry record
  • Performance = own production record
  • Progeny = offspring performance
  • Breeding value = genetic worth as parent
Selection identifies superior animals.

Breeding Value

Breeding value (BV) is the genetic worth of an animal as a parent.

It predicts what genes the animal can pass to offspring.

Related terms:

  • EBV = estimated breeding value
  • EPD = expected progeny difference
Breeding value predicts genetic usefulness.

Systems of Breeding

Breeding systems are planned mating methods used to achieve breeding goals.

Major systems:

  • Inbreeding = mating related animals
  • Linebreeding = mild inbreeding to preserve bloodline
  • Outcrossing = unrelated mating within breed
  • Crossbreeding = mating different breeds
  • Grading up = improving native stock with purebred sires
  • Purebreeding = mating within same breed
Breeding systems control uniformity, vigor, and progress.

Inbreeding vs Crossbreeding

These are the two major opposites in breeding systems.

  • Inbreeding = increases uniformity but increases risk
  • Crossbreeding = increases vigor but increases variation
Inbreeding fixes traits, crossbreeding improves performance.

Heterosis vs Inbreeding Depression

  • Heterosis or hybrid vigor = improved performance in crossbreds
  • Inbreeding depression = reduced performance in inbred animals
Heterosis improves fitness, while inbreeding depression reduces it.

Grading Up

Grading up improves local animals by repeated use of purebred sires.

It gradually upgrades native stock toward purebred quality.

Grading up improves native herds.

Biotechnology in Breeding

Biotechnology uses scientific tools to improve breeding faster and more accurately.

Major tools:

  • AI = artificial insemination
  • ET = embryo transfer
  • MOET = multiple ovulation embryo transfer
  • IVF = in vitro fertilization
  • Sexed semen = semen sorted by sex
  • Genomic selection = DNA-based prediction
  • CRISPR = precise gene editing
Biotechnology accelerates genetic gain.

Strategic Use of Biotechnology

Each biotechnology has a specific purpose:

  • AI = spread male genetics
  • ET / MOET = spread female genetics
  • Genomic selection = save time
  • Sexed semen = improve selection intensity
  • CRISPR = fix specific genes
Each biotechnology solves a specific breeding problem.

Formula Master Set

Important formulas in Part 4:

P = G + E → phenotype = genes + environment

h² = Vg / Vp → heritability

R = h²S → response to selection

EPD = ½ EBV → expected offspring merit
These are the core formulas in genetics and breeding.
