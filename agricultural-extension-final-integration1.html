Agricultural Extension and Communication

Definition

Agricultural Extension and Communication is an educational process that helps farmers, homemakers, rural youth, and communities improve their knowledge, attitudes, skills, productivity, and quality of life through communication, participation, and technology transfer.

1. History of Extension

Agricultural extension started during the 19th century in England and later spread throughout Europe and the United States.

Purpose of Early Extension

  • Spread scientific agricultural knowledge
  • Improve farming practices
  • Educate rural people
  • Increase agricultural productivity

Birth of Modern Agricultural Extension

Modern agricultural extension became an organized educational service connecting research institutions with farmers and rural communities.

2. Extension in the Philippine Setting

Spanish Regime

Agricultural instruction was limited and mainly influenced by colonial and religious systems.

American Regime

Organized agricultural education and extension services were introduced.

After Japanese Occupation

Rehabilitation programs focused on restoring food production and agriculture.

Recent Development

Extension work expanded through DA agencies, SUCs, NGOs, LGUs, cooperatives, and private organizations.

3. Philippine Agriculture

Philippine agriculture developed under different administrations, each implementing policies related to:

  • Food production
  • Farmer welfare
  • Agrarian reform
  • Modernization
  • Rural development

4. Meaning of Extension in Other Countries

Extension generally means extending useful knowledge and services to people so they can improve their lives and practices.

  • Teaching farmers
  • Helping communities solve problems
  • Applying scientific knowledge to daily life

5. Extension Terminologies

Extension Teaching

The process of educating farmers and rural people.

Learning Experience

Activities that create learning and behavioral change.

Extension Model

A framework used in delivering extension services.

Extension Strategy

A planned approach used to achieve extension objectives.

Extension Method

Specific teaching or communication techniques used by extension workers.

6. Agricultural Extension Education

Agricultural Extension Education is an applied science involving:

  • Education
  • Communication
  • Leadership
  • Community development
  • Behavioral change

It aims to improve the knowledge, attitudes, skills, and practices of farmers and rural communities.

7. Objectives of Agricultural Extension

Hierarchy of Extension Objectives

Objectives arranged from broad goals to specific outcomes.

Core Objectives

  • Increase productivity
  • Improve livelihood
  • Promote rural development

Direct Objectives

Immediate changes in knowledge, attitudes, and practices.

Result Objectives

Long-term improvements in income, productivity, and quality of life.

8. Four Major Paradigms of Agricultural Extension

1. Technology Transfer

Transfer of innovations and scientific knowledge from experts to farmers.

2. Advisory Services

Extension workers provide technical advice and consultation.

3. Non-Formal Education (NFE)

Educational activities conducted outside formal classroom settings.

4. Facilitation Extension

Extension workers help people analyze problems and make decisions themselves.

9. Nature or Dimensions of Extension

  • Altruistic Dimension – Service-oriented and people-centered
  • Educational Dimension – Develops knowledge and skills
  • Communication Dimension – Sharing of information and meaning
  • Behavioral Dimension – Influences practices and attitudes
  • Technology Dimension – Promotes innovations
  • Research Dimension – Connects research to farmers
  • Input Dimension – Links farmers to resources
  • Income Dimension – Improves livelihood and earnings
  • Management Dimension – Enhances planning and management

10. Methods for Influencing Human Behavior

  1. Advice – Giving recommendations
  2. Compulsion or Coercion – Forcing compliance
  3. Exchange – Giving rewards or incentives
  4. Influencing Knowledge and Attitude – Education and persuasion
  5. Manipulation – Influencing indirectly
  6. Providing Means – Giving resources and support
  7. Providing Service – Offering technical services
  8. Changing Social/Economic Structure – Reforming systems and institutions

11. Philosophy of Extension Education

  • Extension starts where the people are.
  • Programs are based on people’s needs.
  • People learn by doing.
  • Education may involve groups or individuals.
  • Extension works with and through people.
  • Self-help is essential in democratic living.

12. Principles of Extension

  1. Communication and Education
  2. Works with Rural People
  3. Accountability to Clientele
  4. Two-way Process Linkage
  5. Cooperation with Other Agencies
  6. Work with Different Target Groups
