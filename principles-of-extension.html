Agricultural Extension and Communication Reviewer

Principles of Extension

1. Communication and Education

  • The extension agent serves as both communicator and educator.
  • As communicator, the extension agent passes useful information or technology to the clientele.
  • As educator, the extension agent helps rural people acquire the skills, knowledge, and attitudes needed to effectively use information and technology.

2. Works with Rural People

  • For impact and sustainability, extension should work with, not work for the people.
  • People should participate and make decisions that benefit them.
  • The extension agent assists by providing information and possible solutions to clientele problems.

3. Accountability to Clientele

  • The extension agent must justify actions to the organization and remain accountable to the clientele regarding advice and information given.
  • The clientele judge the success or failure of extension programs.

4. Two-way Process Linkage

  • Extension disseminates information and technology while also receiving feedback from clientele.
  • Extension workers learn from the experiences of the clientele.

5. Cooperate with Other Agencies

  • Extension is only one aspect of broader economic, social, cultural, and political activities aimed at improving rural communities.
  • Extension workers should cooperate and collaborate with both government organizations and non-government organizations.
  • Extension cannot be effective alone because its activities are interconnected with other development activities.

6. Work with Different Target Groups

  • Extension clientele consist of groups with different needs, social status, cultural backgrounds, and economic conditions.
  • Extension cannot offer a single package of technology for all clientele because of heterogeneity.
  • Extension programs and technology packages should be targeted according to specific groups.

Purpose of Extension

1. Informative Extension

Helps people make well-considered choices among alternatives provided by extension to achieve goals.

2. Emancipatory Extension

  • An instrument to uplift the poor, achieve societal goals, and correct structural problems.
  • Paulo Freire referred to this as the Pedagogy of the Oppressed.

3. Formative Extension or HRD

Aims to develop or enhance the capabilities of individuals in decision-making, learning, management, communication, and organization.

4. Persuasive Extension

  • Acts as a policy instrument that induces preventive behavior regarding societal concerns.
  • Examples include environmental pollution, health hazards, vandalism, drug addiction, rape, and similar issues.
  • Such preventive behavior protects both present and future generations.
