Swine Housing and Management

Swine Housing and Management

Swine housing and management refers to the proper sheltering, care, handling, and environmental control of pigs to promote health, comfort, productivity, and efficient production.

It includes housing systems, pen design, sanitation, waste disposal, ventilation, temperature control, biosecurity, and routine management.

Swine housing and management ensure comfort, health, and efficient production.

Swine Housing

Swine housing is the provision of shelter for pigs to protect them from environmental stress, disease, and injury.

  • Heat
  • Rain
  • Cold
  • Wind
  • Disease exposure
Swine housing provides protection and comfort.

Importance of Swine Housing

  • Protects pigs from weather
  • Reduces stress
  • Improves health
  • Improves growth and reproduction
  • Supports sanitation
  • Improves labor efficiency
Proper housing improves performance and welfare.

Swine Management

Swine management is the daily care and supervision of pigs to maintain health, growth, and productivity.

  • Feeding
  • Cleaning
  • Health care
  • Breeding
  • Record keeping
  • Handling
Swine management is the daily operation of swine production.

Objectives of Swine Housing and Management

  • Provide comfort
  • Prevent disease
  • Reduce stress
  • Improve growth
  • Improve reproduction
  • Increase efficiency
Good housing and management improve overall production.

Housing System

Housing system is the method or arrangement used to keep pigs in a production facility.

Housing system refers to the type of swine shelter arrangement.

Types of Swine Housing Systems

  • Intensive system
  • Semi-intensive system
  • Extensive system
Swine housing systems vary by level of confinement.

Intensive System

Intensive system is a housing system where pigs are fully confined in pens and provided with controlled feeding and management.

  • Complete confinement
  • High management control
  • Efficient feeding
  • High productivity
Intensive system is the most common commercial swine system.

Semi-Intensive System

Semi-intensive system combines confinement with limited outdoor access.

Semi-intensive combines housing and limited grazing.

Extensive System

Extensive system allows pigs to roam freely in open areas.

Extensive system uses open-range production.

Pen

Pen is an enclosed space where pigs are housed.

Pen is the basic housing unit for pigs.

Pen Design

Pen design refers to the layout and structure of the swine enclosure.

  • Space
  • Drainage
  • Ventilation
  • Easy cleaning
  • Safety
Pen design affects comfort and efficiency.

Flooring

Flooring is the surface where pigs stand and rest.

  • Solid floor
  • Slatted floor
  • Concrete floor
Flooring affects hygiene and comfort.

Solid Floor

Solid floor is a continuous floor surface, usually concrete.

Solid floors are easy to construct but need frequent cleaning.

Slatted Floor

Slatted floor is a floor with openings that allow manure to pass through.

Slatted floors improve waste drainage and sanitation.

Ventilation

Ventilation is the movement of fresh air into and out of the housing facility.

  • Heat removal
  • Moisture removal
  • Gas removal
  • Odor control
Ventilation maintains air quality and comfort.

Temperature Control

Temperature control is the regulation of environmental temperature inside the housing unit.

Temperature control prevents stress and improves performance.

Heat Stress

Heat stress occurs when pigs are exposed to excessive heat and cannot cool themselves effectively.

  • Panting
  • Reduced feed intake
  • Poor growth
Pigs are highly sensitive to heat stress.

Sanitation

Sanitation is the maintenance of cleanliness in the swine facility.

  • Cleaning pens
  • Removing manure
  • Washing equipment
  • Controlling odors
Sanitation reduces disease risk.

Waste Disposal

Waste disposal is the proper handling and removal of manure and wastewater.

Proper waste disposal improves hygiene and environmental safety.

Manure

Manure is the fecal and urinary waste produced by pigs.

Manure is the main waste product in swine housing.

Drainage

Drainage is the removal of excess water and waste from the housing area.

Drainage prevents wet and dirty housing conditions.

Biosecurity

Biosecurity is the set of practices used to prevent disease entry and spread in the swine farm.

Biosecurity protects pigs from disease.

Biosecurity Practices

  • Limiting visitors
  • Disinfection
  • Footbaths
  • Quarantine
  • Rodent control
Biosecurity reduces disease introduction.

Quarantine

Quarantine is the isolation of newly arrived or exposed pigs to prevent disease entry.

Quarantine prevents disease transmission.

Isolation

Isolation is the separation of sick pigs from healthy pigs.

Isolation protects healthy pigs.

Swine Handling

Swine handling is the proper movement and control of pigs to reduce stress and injury.

Proper handling reduces stress and improves safety.

Record Keeping

Record keeping is the documentation of production and management information.

  • Growth records
  • Breeding records
  • Health records
  • Feed records
Record keeping improves decision-making.

Board Exam Trap Terms

Intensive vs. Extensive:

  • Intensive = confined
  • Extensive = free range
Key difference = level of confinement.

Solid Floor vs. Slatted Floor:

  • Solid = no openings
  • Slatted = manure passes through openings
Slatted floor improves sanitation.

Quarantine vs. Isolation:

  • Quarantine = new / exposed animals
  • Isolation = sick animals
Quarantine = prevent entry, Isolation = prevent spread.

Board Exam Master Concept

Board questions commonly test housing systems, floor types, sanitation, ventilation, and biosecurity.

Swine housing questions often focus on management efficiency and disease prevention.
