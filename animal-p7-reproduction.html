Swine Reproduction

Swine reproduction is the biological process by which pigs produce offspring.

It includes reproductive organs, puberty, estrus cycle, mating, fertilization, gestation, farrowing, lactation, and weaning.

Swine reproduction is the process of producing piglets.

Importance of Swine Reproduction

  • Determines herd productivity
  • Ensures piglet production
  • Provides replacement stock
  • Supports genetic progress
  • Improves profitability
Reproduction determines herd productivity and profit.

Male Reproductive System

Male reproductive system is responsible for producing and delivering sperm.

  • Testes = produce sperm and testosterone
  • Epididymis = stores and matures sperm
  • Vas deferens = transports sperm
  • Penis = delivers semen
  • Accessory glands = produce seminal fluid
Male system produces and delivers sperm.

Boar Penis

The boar penis is the male copulatory organ used to deliver semen.

In swine, the penis is corkscrew-shaped, which matches the structure of the sow cervix during mating.

Boar penis = corkscrew-shaped.

Accessory Glands

Accessory glands produce seminal fluid that nourishes and transports sperm.

  • Seminal vesicles
  • Prostate gland
  • Bulbourethral glands
Accessory glands produce seminal fluid.

Female Reproductive System

Female reproductive system is responsible for producing ova, supporting fertilization, gestation, and birth.

  • Ovaries = produce ova and reproductive hormones
  • Oviducts = transport ova and site of fertilization
  • Uterus = supports embryo development
  • Cervix = muscular passage between uterus and vagina
  • Vagina = receives semen and serves as birth canal
  • Vulva = external reproductive opening
Female system produces ova and supports pregnancy.

Bicornuate Uterus

Swine have a bicornuate uterus.

A bicornuate uterus has one uterine body and two long uterine horns.

This allows swine to carry multiple fetuses in one pregnancy.

Swine uterus = bicornuate; it supports litter-bearing.

Sow Cervix

The sow cervix has interlocking pads or folds.

These match the corkscrew-shaped penis of the boar and help create a natural lock during mating.

Sow cervix = interlocking pads.

Puberty

Puberty is the age when pigs become sexually mature and capable of reproduction.

  • Boar puberty = about 5–8 months
  • Gilt puberty = about 5–7 months
Puberty = sexual maturity.

Estrus

Estrus is the heat period when the female is sexually receptive and can be bred.

Estrus = heat period.

Estrus Cycle

Estrus cycle is the repeating reproductive cycle in the female.

In swine, the estrus cycle averages 21 days.

Swine estrus cycle = 21 days.

Signs of Estrus

  • Swollen vulva
  • Reddened vulva
  • Mucus discharge
  • Restlessness
  • Mounting behavior
  • Standing reflex
Standing reflex is the best sign of estrus.

Back-Pressure Test

Back-pressure test is used to check standing heat.

Pressure is applied on the sow’s back. If she stands still, she is in standing heat.

Back-pressure test checks standing reflex.

Standing Heat

Standing heat is the period when the sow stands still and accepts mating pressure.

Standing heat = best breeding indicator.

Ovulation

Ovulation is the release of ova from the ovary.

In swine, ovulation usually occurs about 36–42 hours after the onset of estrus.

Swine ovulation = 36–42 hours after heat begins.

Mating

Mating is the act of breeding the boar and sow.

During natural mating, the corkscrew-shaped penis of the boar locks into the sow’s cervix.

Natural mating = direct breeding with lock mechanism.

Artificial Insemination

Artificial insemination (AI) is the deposition of semen into the female reproductive tract without natural mating.

AI = semen deposited without natural mating.

AI Catheter Lock

In swine AI, the catheter is rotated counter-clockwise to lock into the cervix.

AI catheter = counter-clockwise lock.

Advantages of AI

  • Wider use of superior boars
  • Lower disease spread
  • Reduced boar maintenance
  • Improved breeding efficiency
  • Better genetic improvement
AI improves genetics and breeding efficiency.

Best Breeding Time

Because ovulation occurs later in estrus, swine are commonly bred twice:

  • First breeding = 12 hours after heat detection
  • Second breeding = 12 hours later
Best breeding = breed twice at 12-hour interval.

Fertilization

Fertilization is the union of sperm and egg.

In swine, fertilization occurs in the oviduct.

Fertilization occurs in the oviduct.

Gestation

Gestation is the period of pregnancy.

Swine gestation lasts about 114 days.

Swine gestation = 3 months, 3 weeks, and 3 days.

Farrowing

Farrowing is the act of giving birth in swine.

Farrowing = giving birth.

Signs of Farrowing

  • Enlarged udder
  • Milk letdown
  • Nesting behavior
  • Restlessness
Udder enlargement and nesting are signs of farrowing.

Lactation

Lactation is milk production after farrowing.

Lactation = milk production.

Colostrum

Colostrum is the first milk produced after farrowing.

It is rich in antibodies and nutrients.

Colostrum provides immunity and nutrients.

Weaning

Weaning is the separation of piglets from the sow and milk feeding.

Weaning = milk separation.

Litter Size

Litter size is the number of piglets born per farrowing.

Litter size = reproductive output.

Conception Rate

Conception rate is the percentage of bred females that become pregnant.

Conception rate = breeding success.
