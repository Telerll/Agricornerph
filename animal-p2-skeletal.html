🦴 Skeletal System of Farm Animals

Skeletal System

Skeletal system is the framework of bones and cartilage that supports the body, protects organs, and enables movement in animals.

Functions of the Skeletal System

Support

Support is the function of giving shape and structural framework to the animal body.

Protection

Protection is the function of shielding vital organs such as the brain, heart, and lungs.

Movement

Movement is the function of allowing the body to move through interaction with muscles and joints.

Blood Cell Formation

Blood cell formation (hematopoiesis) is the production of blood cells in the bone marrow.

Mineral Storage

Mineral storage is the storage of calcium and phosphorus in bones.

Divisions of the Skeleton

Axial Skeleton

Axial skeleton is the part of the skeleton that forms the central axis of the body.

Components of the Axial Skeleton

Skull

Skull is the bony structure that protects the brain and supports the face.

Vertebral Column

Vertebral column is the series of bones forming the spine that supports the body.

Ribs

Ribs are curved bones that protect the heart and lungs.

Sternum

Sternum is the breastbone where ribs are attached.

Appendicular Skeleton

Appendicular skeleton is the part of the skeleton that includes the limbs and girdles.

Components of the Appendicular Skeleton

Forelimbs

Forelimbs are the front legs used for movement and support.

Hindlimbs

Hindlimbs are the rear legs used for propulsion and movement.

Shoulder Girdle

Shoulder girdle connects the forelimbs to the body.

Pelvic Girdle

Pelvic girdle connects the hindlimbs to the body and supports weight.

Types of Bones

Long Bones

Long bones are bones longer than they are wide and are used mainly for movement.

Short Bones

Short bones are cube-shaped bones that provide stability and support.

Flat Bones

Flat bones are thin bones that protect internal organs.

Irregular Bones

Irregular bones are bones with complex shapes such as vertebrae.

Sesamoid Bones

Sesamoid bones are small bones embedded in tendons that help reduce friction.

Structure of a Long Bone

Diaphysis

Diaphysis is the shaft or central portion of a long bone.

Epiphysis

Epiphysis is the end part of a long bone where joints are formed.

Medullary Cavity

Medullary cavity is the hollow space inside the bone that contains marrow.

Periosteum

Periosteum is the outer covering of the bone that contains nerves and blood vessels.

Endosteum

Endosteum is the inner lining of the bone.

Bone Tissue

Compact Bone

Compact bone is dense bone tissue that provides strength and support.

Spongy Bone

Spongy bone is porous bone tissue that contains bone marrow.

Bone Marrow

Red Bone Marrow

Red bone marrow is the tissue that produces blood cells such as red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets.

Yellow Bone Marrow

Yellow bone marrow is the tissue that stores fat.

Joints

Joint

Joint is the point where two or more bones meet.

Types of Joints

Fibrous Joint

Fibrous joint is a joint that does not allow movement.

Cartilaginous Joint

Cartilaginous joint is a joint that allows slight movement.

Synovial Joint

Synovial joint is a freely movable joint.

Types of Synovial Joints

Hinge Joint

Hinge joint allows movement in one direction.

Ball and Socket Joint

Ball and socket joint allows movement in several directions.

Directional Terms in Animal Anatomy

Dorsal

Dorsal refers to the upper or back side of the animal.

Ventral

Ventral refers to the lower or belly side of the animal.

Cranial

Cranial refers to the direction toward the head.

Caudal

Caudal refers to the direction toward the tail.

Medial

Medial refers to a position toward the midline of the body.

Lateral

Lateral refers to a position away from the midline.

Proximal

Proximal refers to a part closer to the body.

Distal

Distal refers to a part farther from the body.

Movement in Animals

Lever System

Lever system is the mechanical action in which bones act as levers to produce movement.

Locomotion

Locomotion is the ability of the animal to move from one place to another.

Importance of the Skeletal System in Animal Science

  • Helps understand the structure and posture of animals
  • Important for locomotion and animal handling
  • Useful in veterinary practice
  • Important in meat production and animal performance
