Topic 07

Marketing Margins

Detailed discussion on price spreads, cost of marketing, and distribution of income.

Marketing Margins

Marketing margins refer to the difference between the price paid by consumers and the price received by producers (farmers).

It represents the total cost added by all marketing activities such as transportation, storage, processing, and the profit of intermediaries.

In simple terms, it shows how much value is added to a product as it moves through the marketing channel.

Price Spread

Price spread is the difference between the retail price (consumer price) and the farm gate price.

A wide price spread usually means higher marketing costs or more intermediaries in the marketing channel.

A narrow price spread usually indicates a more efficient marketing system.

Types of Marketing Margins

1. Total Marketing Margin

This is the total difference between what the consumer pays and what the farmer receives.

2. Farm-to-Retail Margin

This refers to the total price difference from the farm level directly up to the retail level.

3. Wholesale Margin

This is the difference between the price paid by wholesalers and the price they sell to retailers.

Components of Marketing Margins

Marketing Costs

Includes transportation, storage, handling, processing, packaging, and other operational expenses.

Intermediary Profit

Profit earned by traders, wholesalers, and retailers for providing marketing services.

Importance of Marketing Margins

Helps measure efficiency of the marketing system.

Shows how the final price is distributed among participants.

Helps identify if farmers are receiving fair share of consumer price.

Used to analyze whether marketing costs are reasonable or excessive.
