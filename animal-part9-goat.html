Goat Breeds

Goat

Goat is a small ruminant livestock animal commonly raised for meat, milk, skin, manure, and livelihood purposes.

The scientific name of the domestic goat is Capra hircus.

Goats are hardy animals and are well adapted to different environments, especially in backyard and smallholder farming systems.

Goat = Capra hircus.

Goat as a Biological Herbicide

Goats are sometimes called biological herbicides because they help control weeds and unwanted vegetation through browsing.

They can eat leaves, shrubs, grasses, and other plant materials that may not be readily consumed by other livestock.

This makes goats useful in clearing brushy areas and controlling vegetation naturally.

Goat = biological herbicide because of its browsing ability.

Browsing Behavior of Goats

Browsing is the feeding behavior of animals that prefer leaves, shrubs, twigs, vines, and young shoots rather than mainly grazing on grass.

Goats are natural browsers. They are very selective in choosing feed and often prefer young shoots, leaves, and shrubs.

Because of this, goats are different from sheep, which are more commonly known as grazers.

Goats are browsers; sheep are grazers.

General Feeding Characteristics of Goats

Goats are highly adaptable feeders. They can consume different kinds of forage and plant materials.

  • They can eat grasses, shrubs, and herbs.
  • They can nibble bark of trees.
  • They have mobile lips that help them select feed.
  • They can walk long distances in search of food.
  • They are selective feeders and prefer young shoot first.
Goats are selective browsers with mobile lips.

Basic Difference Between Goat and Sheep

Goats and sheep are both small ruminants, but they differ in feeding behavior, tail carriage, hair covering, horns, and growth habit.

  • Goat scientific name: Capra hircus
  • Sheep scientific name: Ovis aries
  • Goat feeding behavior: browser
  • Sheep feeding behavior: grazer
  • Goat tail: usually upward
  • Sheep tail: usually downward
  • Goat covering: mostly hair
  • Sheep covering: mostly wool
Board trap: goat = browser; sheep = grazer.

Goat Breeds

Goat breeds are groups of goats with common characteristics, origin, appearance, and productive purpose.

Goat breeds may be classified based on purpose such as meat production, milk production, or dual-purpose use.

In the Philippines, goats may include native goats, upgraded goats, meat-type breeds, and dairy-type breeds.

Goat breeds are classified based on origin, traits, and purpose.

Anglo-Nubian

Anglo-Nubian is a tropical goat breed successfully adapted in many areas.

It is known for its pendulous ears and Roman nose.

This breed is commonly used for meat and milk production and is valued for its adaptability under tropical conditions.

  • Adapted to tropical conditions
  • Has long pendulous ears
  • Has a Roman nose
  • Used for meat and milk
Anglo-Nubian = Roman nose and pendulous ears.

Boer Goat

Boer goat is a meat-type goat breed.

It grows faster than many other goat breeds and is commonly recognized as an important breed for meat production.

Boer goats usually have a light-colored body and a reddish or brownish head.

  • Meat-type breed
  • Fast-growing
  • Good body conformation
  • Commonly used for chevon production
Boer goat = meat-type goat breed.

Dairy Goat Breeds

Dairy goat breeds are breeds mainly selected for milk production.

These breeds are important in dairy goat farming because they produce milk that may be used for drinking, processing, and value-added products.

The common dairy breeds mentioned include Saanen, Toggenburg, Alpine, and La Mancha.

Dairy goat breeds = breeds mainly used for milk production.

Saanen

Saanen is a dairy goat breed that originated in Switzerland.

It is commonly white to off-white in color and is known as one of the important milk-producing goat breeds.

  • Dairy breed
  • Origin: Switzerland
  • Usually white to off-white
  • Known for milk production
Saanen = white dairy goat from Switzerland.

Toggenburg

Toggenburg is a dairy goat breed from Switzerland.

It is one of the recognized dairy breeds and is raised primarily for milk production.

Toggenburg goats are usually known for their distinct color pattern and dairy ability.

Toggenburg = Swiss dairy goat breed.

Alpine

Alpine is a dairy goat breed that originated from the Alpine region.

It is valued for milk production and adaptability.

Alpine goats may appear in different colors and patterns.

Alpine = dairy goat breed known for adaptability and milk production.

La Mancha

La Mancha is a dairy goat breed known for its very small external ears.

It is sometimes described as having “elf ears” or very short ears.

This breed is used mainly for milk production.

La Mancha = dairy goat with very small ears.

Purpose-Based Classification of Goat Breeds

Goat breeds may be grouped according to their main purpose.

  • Meat-type goats are raised mainly for chevon production.
  • Dairy-type goats are raised mainly for milk production.
  • Dual-purpose goats may be used for both meat and milk.
  • Native goats are usually hardy and adapted to local conditions.
Purpose classification: meat, dairy, dual-purpose, native/adapted.

Chevon

Chevon is the meat obtained from goats.

Goat breeds used for meat production are selected for growth rate, body conformation, adaptability, and dressing percentage.

Chevon = goat meat.
