Topic 11

Price & Pricing Strategies

Expanded discussion on price determination and pricing approaches in agricultural marketing.

Price

Price refers to the amount of money that a buyer gives in exchange for a product or service.

It represents the value assigned to a product in the market and is the only element of the marketing mix that generates revenue.

Price Determination / Price Discovery

Price discovery refers to the process of determining the price of goods based on interaction between buyers and sellers in the market.

Price Fluctuations

Prices of agricultural products do not remain constant and may change due to supply, demand, seasonality, and market conditions.

Types of price movements include:

1. Seasonal Variation

Price changes depending on production seasons.

2. Annual Variation

Price differences observed from year to year.

3. Trend Movement

Long-term upward or downward movement of prices.

4. Cyclical Variation

Price changes due to business cycles over several years.

5. Irregular Movement

Unpredictable price changes caused by unexpected events.

Pricing Strategies

1. Premium Pricing

Setting high prices for unique or high-quality products, often used for luxury goods.

2. Penetration Pricing

Setting low prices initially to attract customers and gain market share.

3. Economy Pricing

Using low-cost strategies to offer affordable products with minimal marketing expenses.

4. Price Skimming

Setting high initial prices then lowering them over time as competition increases.

5. Psychological Pricing

Pricing designed to influence consumer perception, such as setting prices at 9.99 instead of 10.
