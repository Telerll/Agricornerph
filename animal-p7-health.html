Swine Health and Disease Management

Swine health and disease management is the prevention, control, diagnosis, and treatment of diseases affecting pigs to maintain health, productivity, and profitability.

It includes health management, disease prevention, sanitation, vaccination, biosecurity, diagnosis, treatment, and parasite control.

Swine health management keeps pigs healthy and productive.

Health and Disease

Health is the normal physical and physiological condition of the pig.

Disease is any abnormal condition that impairs normal body function.

Health = normal body condition. Disease = abnormal condition.

Importance of Health Management

  • Prevents disease
  • Reduces mortality
  • Improves growth
  • Improves reproduction
  • Lowers treatment cost
  • Increases profit
Health management protects productivity and profit.

Signs of Healthy Swine

  • Active and alert
  • Bright eyes
  • Good appetite
  • Smooth hair coat
  • Normal breathing
  • Normal feces
Healthy pigs are active, alert, and eating well.

Signs of Sick Swine

  • Poor appetite
  • Weakness
  • Rough hair coat
  • Coughing
  • Diarrhea
  • Fever
  • Nasal discharge
  • Lameness
Poor appetite is one of the earliest signs of illness.

Disease Prevention

Disease prevention is preventing disease before it occurs.

  • Sanitation = maintaining cleanliness
  • Disinfection = using chemicals to kill pathogens
  • Vaccination = stimulating immunity against disease
  • Biosecurity = preventing disease entry and spread
  • Quarantine = separating new animals before mixing
  • Isolation = separating sick animals from healthy animals
Prevention is better than treatment.

Leptospirosis

  • Type: Bacterial
  • Cause: Leptospira
  • Signs: abortion, stillbirth, infertility
  • Transmission: urine and contaminated water
  • Prevention: sanitation, rodent control, vaccination
  • Treatment: antibiotics
Leptospirosis = abortion disease.

Colibacillosis

  • Type: Bacterial
  • Cause: E. coli
  • Signs: diarrhea, dehydration, weakness
  • Transmission: fecal contamination
  • Prevention: sanitation and good colostrum intake
  • Treatment: antibiotics and electrolytes
Colibacillosis = piglet diarrhea.

Salmonellosis

  • Type: Bacterial
  • Cause: Salmonella
  • Signs: diarrhea, fever, septicemia
  • Transmission: contaminated feed or water
  • Prevention: sanitation and biosecurity
  • Treatment: antibiotics and supportive care
Salmonellosis = diarrhea with septicemia.

Erysipelas

  • Type: Bacterial
  • Cause: Erysipelothrix rhusiopathiae
  • Signs: fever, lameness, diamond skin lesions
  • Transmission: contaminated environment
  • Prevention: vaccination and sanitation
  • Treatment: penicillin
Erysipelas = diamond skin disease.

Brucellosis

  • Type: Bacterial
  • Cause: Brucella suis
  • Signs: abortion, infertility, orchitis
  • Transmission: breeding and contaminated discharge
  • Prevention: test-and-cull and biosecurity
  • Treatment: no practical treatment
Brucellosis = zoonotic reproductive disease.

Atrophic Rhinitis

  • Type: Bacterial
  • Cause: Bordetella / Pasteurella
  • Signs: sneezing, nasal discharge, twisted snout
  • Transmission: direct contact
  • Prevention: sanitation and good ventilation
  • Treatment: antibiotics
Atrophic rhinitis = twisted snout.

Hog Cholera / Classical Swine Fever

  • Type: Viral
  • Cause: Classical swine fever virus
  • Signs: fever, hemorrhage, weakness, high mortality
  • Transmission: direct contact and contaminated materials
  • Prevention: vaccination and biosecurity
  • Treatment: no specific treatment; supportive only
Hog cholera = hemorrhagic viral disease.

African Swine Fever

  • Type: Viral
  • Cause: ASF virus
  • Signs: high fever, skin redness, weakness, sudden death
  • Transmission: direct contact, contaminated pork, contaminated materials
  • Prevention: strict biosecurity
  • Treatment: none
ASF = sudden death viral disease.

Foot-and-Mouth Disease

  • Type: Viral
  • Cause: FMD virus
  • Signs: mouth lesions, foot lesions, salivation, lameness
  • Transmission: highly contagious direct and indirect contact
  • Prevention: quarantine, vaccination, movement control
  • Treatment: supportive care
FMD = mouth and foot lesions.

PRRS

  • Type: Viral
  • Cause: PRRS virus
  • Signs: abortion, respiratory distress, weak piglets
  • Transmission: direct contact and semen
  • Prevention: biosecurity and herd control
  • Treatment: supportive care
PRRS = reproductive and respiratory disease.

Swine Influenza

  • Type: Viral
  • Cause: Influenza virus
  • Signs: cough, fever, nasal discharge
  • Transmission: aerosol and close contact
  • Prevention: ventilation and biosecurity
  • Treatment: supportive care
Swine influenza = sudden respiratory outbreak.

Ascariasis

  • Type: Parasitic
  • Cause: Ascaris suum
  • Signs: poor growth, pot belly, rough coat
  • Transmission: ingestion of parasite eggs
  • Prevention: sanitation and deworming
  • Treatment: anthelmintics
Ascariasis = roundworm infestation.

Coccidiosis

  • Type: Parasitic / protozoal
  • Cause: coccidia
  • Signs: diarrhea, poor growth, weakness
  • Transmission: fecal contamination
  • Prevention: sanitation
  • Treatment: anticoccidials
Coccidiosis = protozoal diarrhea.

Mange

  • Type: Parasitic
  • Cause: mites
  • Signs: itching, crusting, skin thickening
  • Transmission: direct contact
  • Prevention: sanitation and parasite control
  • Treatment: acaricides
Mange = mite infestation.

Lice Infestation

  • Type: Parasitic
  • Cause: lice
  • Signs: itching, irritation, poor growth
  • Transmission: direct contact
  • Prevention: sanitation and parasite control
  • Treatment: insecticides
Lice = external blood-sucking parasite.

Iron Deficiency Anemia

  • Type: Nutritional disorder
  • Cause: low iron intake in piglets
  • Signs: pale skin, weakness, poor growth
  • Prevention: iron injection
  • Treatment: iron supplementation
Iron deficiency anemia = pale piglets.

Rickets

  • Type: Nutritional disorder
  • Cause: Vitamin D, calcium, or phosphorus deficiency
  • Signs: weak bones, lameness, poor growth
  • Prevention: proper mineral and vitamin supplementation
  • Treatment: supplementation
Rickets = weak bones.

Heat Stress

  • Type: Management / environmental disorder
  • Cause: high environmental temperature
  • Signs: panting, reduced feed intake, poor growth
  • Prevention: cooling, shade, ventilation, water access
  • Treatment: cooling and water support
Heat stress = panting pigs.

Tail Biting

  • Type: Management / behavioral disorder
  • Cause: overcrowding, stress, boredom, poor environment
  • Signs: tail wounds, bleeding, infection
  • Prevention: reduce stress, improve space, provide enrichment
  • Treatment: isolate affected pigs and treat wounds
Tail biting = stress-related behavioral vice.
