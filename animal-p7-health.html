Vitamins in Swine Nutrition

Vitamins are organic micronutrients required in small amounts for normal growth, metabolism, reproduction, health, and body regulation.

Although needed only in small quantities, vitamins are essential because they regulate many biological processes necessary for life and production.

Vitamins are organic micronutrients that regulate body processes.

Importance of Vitamins

  • Growth
  • Metabolism
  • Appetite
  • Reproduction
  • Immunity
  • Bone development
  • Nerve function
  • Blood formation
  • Tissue repair
Vitamins regulate growth, metabolism, and health.

Classification of Vitamins

  • Fat-soluble vitamins
  • Water-soluble vitamins
Vitamins are classified based on solubility.

Fat-Soluble Vitamins

Fat-soluble vitamins dissolve in fat and are stored in body tissues, especially the liver.

  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin D
  • Vitamin E
  • Vitamin K
Fat-soluble vitamins are stored in the body.

Characteristics of Fat-Soluble Vitamins

  • Dissolve in fat
  • Stored in the body
  • Stored mainly in the liver
  • Deficiency develops more slowly
  • Excess may become toxic
Fat-soluble vitamins are stored longer and may become toxic in excess.

Water-Soluble Vitamins

Water-soluble vitamins dissolve in water and are not stored in large amounts in the body.

  • Vitamin B-complex
  • Vitamin C
Water-soluble vitamins are not stored much and must be supplied regularly.

Characteristics of Water-Soluble Vitamins

  • Dissolve in water
  • Poorly stored
  • Deficiency develops faster
  • Excess is usually excreted in urine
  • Toxicity is less common
Water-soluble vitamins are lost faster and deficiency appears sooner.

Fat-Soluble Vitamins and Their Functions

  • Vitamin A = vision, growth, reproduction, epithelial health, immunity
  • Vitamin D = calcium absorption, phosphorus use, bone formation
  • Vitamin E = antioxidant, muscle health, reproduction, immunity
  • Vitamin K = blood clotting, wound healing
A = vision, D = bones, E = antioxidant, K = clotting.

Water-Soluble Vitamins and Their Functions

  • B1 / Thiamine = energy metabolism, appetite, nerves
  • B2 / Riboflavin = growth, metabolism, skin
  • B3 / Niacin = metabolism, skin, digestion
  • B5 / Pantothenic Acid = energy and fatty acid metabolism
  • B6 / Pyridoxine = protein metabolism, amino acid use, nerves
  • B7 / Biotin = hoof, skin, fat metabolism
  • B9 / Folic Acid = blood formation, growth, reproduction
  • B12 / Cobalamin = blood formation, nerves, growth
  • Choline = liver function, fat metabolism, nerve transmission
  • Vitamin C = immunity, healing, antioxidant, stress support
B-complex supports metabolism, nerves, growth, and blood formation.

Vitamin Deficiency Signs

  • Vitamin A deficiency = night blindness, poor growth, rough hair coat, reproductive failure
  • Vitamin D deficiency = rickets, weak bones, poor growth, lameness
  • Vitamin E deficiency = muscle weakness, poor immunity, reproductive problems
  • Vitamin K deficiency = hemorrhage, prolonged bleeding, poor clotting
  • B-complex deficiency = poor appetite, slow growth, weakness, nervous signs
  • Biotin deficiency = skin lesions, poor hoof quality, rough skin
  • Folic acid deficiency = anemia, poor growth, reproductive failure
  • B12 deficiency = anemia, weakness, poor growth
  • Choline deficiency = fatty liver, poor growth
Board exams often give the symptom first, then ask the missing vitamin.

Vitamin Precursors and Converted Forms

  • Beta-carotene → Vitamin A
  • Sunlight / sterol activation → Vitamin D activation
  • Tryptophan → Niacin
  • Methionine partially spares choline requirement
  • Vitamin K may be produced by intestinal microorganisms
  • Vitamin D is activated through liver and kidney conversion
Board traps often test vitamin precursors and converted forms.

Vitamin C in Swine

Vitamin C is water-soluble and supports immunity, tissue repair, wound healing, antioxidant protection, and stress resistance.

In swine, Vitamin C is usually not essential in normal diets because pigs can synthesize it in the body.

However, supplementation may help during stress, illness, or heat stress.

Vitamin C in swine = usually non-essential, but useful during stress.

Board Exam Trap Rules

  • ADEK = fat-soluble vitamins
  • B-complex + C = water-soluble vitamins
  • Fat-soluble = stored in the body
  • Water-soluble = deficiency appears faster
  • Beta-carotene = Vitamin A precursor
  • Tryptophan = Niacin precursor
  • Vitamin K = can be produced by gut microbes
  • Vitamin C = usually non-essential in swine
Most board questions test vitamin class, function, deficiency, precursor, and exception.
