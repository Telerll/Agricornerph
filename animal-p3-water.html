Water and Its Importance

Water is the most essential nutrient in animal nutrition.

It is required in greater quantity than any other nutrient and is more immediately necessary for life than feed.

Water is the most important nutrient.

Importance of Water

Water is essential for:

  • Survival
  • Metabolism
  • Digestion
  • Nutrient transport
  • Waste removal
  • Temperature regulation
  • Production
No water = no life, no growth, no production.

Water in the Animal Body

Water makes up a large part of the animal body.

  • Newborn animals = very high body water
  • Adult animals = about 50–70% of body weight
Water is the major component of body tissues.

Functions of Water

Water functions in:

  • Digestion
  • Absorption
  • Circulation
  • Temperature regulation
  • Lubrication
  • Excretion
  • Metabolism
Water supports nearly every body process.

Water and Digestion

Water is needed for:

  • Feed softening
  • Saliva production
  • Enzymatic digestion
  • Rumen fermentation
Digestion depends heavily on water.

Water and Nutrient Transport

Water carries:

  • Nutrients
  • Hormones
  • Enzymes
  • Waste products

Water transports these through:

  • Blood
  • Lymph
  • Body fluids
Water is the transport medium of the body.

Water and Temperature Regulation

Water helps regulate body temperature through:

  • Sweating
  • Panting
  • Evaporation
Water is essential for cooling.

Water and Waste Removal

Water is needed to remove waste through:

  • Urine
  • Feces
  • Sweat
Water helps eliminate waste products.

Water and Production

Water is directly related to:

  • Feed intake
  • Milk production
  • Egg production
  • Growth rate
Low water intake quickly reduces production.

Water Sources

Animals obtain water from:

  • Drinking water
  • Feed moisture or preformed water
  • Metabolic water
Drinking water is the main source.

Drinking Water

Drinking water is the primary source of water for animals.

It must be:

  • Clean
  • Fresh
  • Safe
  • Available at all times
Water access must never be limited.

Feed Moisture / Preformed Water

Feed moisture or preformed water is water naturally present in feed.

Examples:

  • Fresh forage contains high moisture
  • Dry feeds contain low moisture
Moist feeds provide some water, but not enough alone.

Metabolic Water

Metabolic water is water produced during metabolism of nutrients.

It is formed when these nutrients are oxidized in the body:

  • Carbohydrates
  • Fats
  • Proteins
Fats produce the most metabolic water per gram compared with carbohydrates and proteins.

Factors Affecting Water Requirement

Water needs vary depending on:

  • Species
  • Age
  • Feed type
  • Temperature
  • Production level
  • Activity
Water requirement is not constant.

Species Differences

Water requirement differs among species.

Examples:

  • Dairy cattle need large amounts of water
  • Poultry need constant access to water
  • Swine are highly sensitive to water restriction
Species affects water demand.

Temperature Effect

Hot weather increases water requirement because:

  • More water is lost through evaporation
  • Heat stress increases the need for cooling
Hotter environment = greater water need.

Feed Effect

Dry feeds increase water requirement because animals need more water to digest dry matter.

Dry feed = higher water intake.

Production Effect

Higher production increases water need.

Examples:

  • Lactating animals need more water
  • Laying hens need more water
  • Fast-growing animals drink more
Production raises water demand.

Water Deprivation

Water deprivation means lack of adequate water intake.

It causes:

  • Reduced feed intake
  • Dehydration
  • Poor growth
  • Low production
  • Death in severe cases
Animals die faster without water than without feed.

Dehydration

Dehydration is excessive loss of body water.

Signs:

  • Sunken eyes
  • Dry mouth
  • Weakness
  • Poor appetite
  • Skin tenting
Dehydration quickly reduces performance.

Water Quality

Poor water quality reduces intake and performance.

Common problems include:

  • Contamination
  • Excess salts
  • Pathogens
  • Bad odor
  • Toxins
  • High TDS or Total Dissolved Solids
Poor water quality reduces water intake.

Water and Feed Intake

Water intake and feed intake are closely related.

If water intake decreases:

  • Feed intake decreases
  • Growth decreases
  • Production decreases
Less water = less feed intake.

Board Exam Master Concept

Water is the most critical nutrient because animals can survive longer without feed than without water.

Water directly controls:

  • Intake
  • Digestion
  • Metabolism
  • Production
  • Survival
Water drives life and production.

Final Ultra Master Summary

Water = most important nutrient

Functions: digestion, transport, cooling, waste removal, metabolism

Sources: drinking water, feed moisture / preformed water, metabolic water

Hot weather = more water
Dry feed = more water
High production = more water

Less water = less feed
Less feed = less growth
Less growth = less production

No water = no life
