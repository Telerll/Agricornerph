Forms of Business Organizations

Individually Owned (Proprietorship)

A business enterprise owned, operated, managed, and controlled by one individual who also receives all earnings. It is the oldest and most common form of business.

Partnership

A business owned and controlled by two or more people who agree to share responsibilities, profits, and losses. Common in family and farming businesses.

Corporation (Investor-Oriented)

A state-chartered business that operates as a separate legal entity and focuses on generating profit for its investors or shareholders.

Corporation (Cooperative Type)

A user-owned and user-controlled organization where benefits are distributed according to the level of use. Common in agricultural marketing and services.

Limited Liability Company (LLC)

A hybrid business structure combining features of partnership and corporation. It provides limited liability protection to owners called members.
