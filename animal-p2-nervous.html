🧠 Nervous System of Farm Animals

Nervous System

Nervous system is the body system responsible for controlling and coordinating body activities through electrical signals.

Functions of the Nervous System

Sensory Function

Sensory function is the ability to detect internal and external stimuli.

Integration

Integration is the interpretation and processing of sensory information.

Motor Function

Motor function is the response of muscles or glands to stimuli.

Divisions of the Nervous System

Central Nervous System (CNS)

Central nervous system (CNS) consists of the brain and spinal cord.

Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)

Peripheral nervous system (PNS) consists of nerves outside the CNS.

Parts of the Nervous System

Brain

Brain is the control center of the body.

Spinal Cord

Spinal cord transmits signals between the brain and the body.

Nerves

Nerves carry electrical signals throughout the body.

Parts of the Brain

Cerebrum

Cerebrum controls thinking, memory, learning, behavior, and voluntary movement.

Cerebellum

Cerebellum controls balance, posture, and coordination of movement.

Brainstem

Brainstem controls vital involuntary body functions.

Medulla Oblongata

Medulla oblongata is the part of the brainstem that controls breathing, heart rate, and circulation.

Medulla oblongata = vital center

Neuron

Neuron is the basic structural and functional unit of the nervous system.

Dendrites

Dendrites receive nerve signals from other cells.

Cell Body

Cell body contains the nucleus and processes nerve signals.

Axon

Axon carries nerve impulses away from the cell body.

Myelin Sheath

Myelin sheath is the fatty covering around the axon that acts as insulation.

It speeds up nerve impulse transmission.

Types of Neurons

Sensory Neurons

Sensory neurons carry signals from receptors to the central nervous system.

Motor Neurons

Motor neurons carry signals from the central nervous system to muscles or glands.

Interneurons

Interneurons connect sensory neurons and motor neurons inside the central nervous system.

Reflex Action

Reflex action is a rapid, automatic response to a stimulus.

Reflex Arc

Reflex arc is the pathway followed during a reflex action.

Stimulus → Sensory Neuron → CNS → Motor Neuron → Response

Autonomic Nervous System

Autonomic nervous system controls involuntary activities such as heart rate, digestion, and gland activity.

Sympathetic Division

Sympathetic division prepares the animal for stress or emergency response.

It is also called the fight-or-flight response.

  • Increases heart rate
  • Increases alertness
  • Reduces digestion
  • Prepares the body for action

Parasympathetic Division

Parasympathetic division promotes rest, recovery, and normal digestion.

It is also called the rest-and-digest response.

  • Supports digestion
  • Slows heart rate
  • Promotes recovery
  • Maintains relaxed body state

Nervous System in Farm Animals

  • Controls behavior
  • Coordinates movement
  • Regulates body functions
  • Helps animals respond to environmental changes
  • Affects feeding, handling, and production performance

Nervous System and Animal Production

  • Influences feeding behavior
  • Affects growth and reproduction
  • Important in stress response
  • Impacts animal welfare
  • Affects productivity under heat, transport, crowding, and handling stress

Stress and the Nervous System

Stress

Stress is the body’s response to environmental or physical challenges.

Cortisol

Cortisol is a stress hormone released during prolonged stress.

Effects of Stress in Livestock

  • Suppresses immune function
  • Reduces growth
  • Decreases feed efficiency
  • Reduces weight gain
  • May affect reproduction and production performance
Stress → Sympathetic Nervous System → Adrenaline + Cortisol → Lower production

Stress Checklist for Livestock

Stimulus: heat, transport, rough handling, overcrowding
System: sympathetic nervous system
Hormones: adrenaline and cortisol
Result: increased heart rate, reduced digestion, lower feed efficiency, reduced weight gain

Final Ultra Summary

Nervous system = control system

CNS = brain + spinal cord
PNS = nerves

Neuron = basic unit
Myelin sheath = faster signals

Reflex = automatic response

Sympathetic = fight or flight
Parasympathetic = rest and digest

Stress = cortisol → lower production
