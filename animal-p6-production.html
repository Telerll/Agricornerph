Poultry Reproduction and Incubation

Poultry Reproduction and Incubation

Poultry reproduction and incubation is the branch of poultry science that deals with how birds produce fertile eggs and how fertile eggs develop into chicks.

It includes reproductive organs, egg formation, fertilization, mating, incubation, embryonic development, hatching, and hatchability.

Poultry reproduction produces fertile eggs, while incubation develops them into chicks.

Reproduction

Reproduction is the biological process by which birds produce offspring.

Reproduction is the process of producing chicks.

Incubation

Incubation is the process of maintaining fertile eggs under proper environmental conditions so embryos can develop and hatch.

Incubation is controlled embryo development inside the egg.

Male Reproductive System

Male reproductive system is responsible for producing and delivering sperm.

  • Testes = produce sperm and testosterone
  • Vas deferens = carries sperm from testes to cloaca
  • Cloaca = common outlet for digestive, urinary, and reproductive systems
Male system = testes → vas deferens → cloaca.

Female Reproductive System

Female reproductive system is responsible for producing ova and forming eggs.

  • Ovary = produces ova or yolks and female hormones
  • Oviduct = tube where egg formation occurs

In hens, only the left ovary is functional; the right ovary is vestigial.

Only the left ovary is functional in most hens.

Ovum and Follicle

Ovum is the female reproductive cell. In poultry, the ovum is the yolk.

Follicle is a developing ovum surrounded by supportive tissue in the ovary.

Follicles are developing yolks in the ovary.

Oviduct Parts and Functions

  • Infundibulum = receives yolk and is the site of fertilization; about 15–30 minutes
  • Magnum = forms albumen or egg white; about 3 hours
  • Isthmus = forms shell membranes; about 1–1.5 hours
  • Uterus / Shell Gland = forms eggshell and shell pigment; about 20–21 hours
  • Vagina = final passage of the egg during laying
Uterus / shell gland is where the egg stays the longest.

Egg Parts Formed in the Oviduct

  • Albumen = egg white that surrounds and protects the yolk
  • Shell membranes = inner and outer protective membranes beneath the shell
  • Eggshell = hard outer covering that protects the embryo
Magnum = albumen, isthmus = membranes, uterus = shell.

Utero-Vaginal Junction

Utero-vaginal junction is the area between the uterus and vagina where sperm storage glands are located.

Utero-vaginal junction is the sperm storage site in hens.

Ovulation and Egg Formation

Ovulation is the release of a mature yolk from the ovary.

Egg formation is the process by which the ovum becomes a complete egg inside the oviduct.

Ovulation releases the yolk; egg formation occurs in the oviduct.

Fertilization and Zygote

Fertilization is the union of sperm and ovum to form a zygote.

Zygote is the fertilized egg cell and the first stage of embryo development.

In poultry, fertilization occurs in the infundibulum.

Site of fertilization = infundibulum.

Mating and Cloacal Kiss

Mating is the transfer of sperm from male to female.

Cloacal kiss is the brief cloacal contact during mating through which sperm is transferred.

Cloacal kiss transfers sperm.

Fertile and Infertile Egg

  • Fertile egg = contains a fertilized ovum and can develop into a chick
  • Infertile egg = lacks fertilization and cannot hatch
  • Fertility = ability of eggs to be fertilized and begin embryo development
Fertile eggs can hatch; infertile eggs cannot hatch.

Embryo and Embryonic Development

Embryo is the developing chick inside the egg.

Embryonic development is the growth of the embryo inside the egg.

Embryonic development is chick growth inside the egg.

Types of Incubation

  • Natural incubation = uses a broody hen to incubate eggs
  • Artificial incubation = uses an incubator machine
  • Incubator = machine that provides proper temperature, humidity, and ventilation
Natural incubation uses the hen; artificial incubation uses a machine.

Incubation Requirements

  • Temperature = degree of heat; standard is 37.5°C or 99.5°F
  • Humidity = moisture level in the incubator air; controls egg moisture loss
  • Ventilation = fresh air supply; provides oxygen and removes carbon dioxide
  • Egg turning = regular turning of eggs to prevent embryo sticking
Temperature, humidity, ventilation, and turning are the four incubation pillars.

Candling

Candling is the use of light to examine the egg interior.

It is used to check fertility, embryo development, and dead embryos.

Candling checks fertility and embryo development.

Hatchability

Hatchability is the percentage of fertile eggs that hatch successfully.

Hatchability = (Chicks Hatched ÷ Fertile Eggs) × 100

Hatching, Pipping, and Oviposition

  • Hatching = emergence of the chick from the egg
  • Pipping = first breaking of the shell by the chick
  • Oviposition = act of laying the egg
Pipping is the first shell crack before hatching.

Broody Hen and Hatchery

  • Broody hen = hen that naturally sits on eggs for incubation
  • Hatchery = facility where fertile eggs are incubated and hatched
Hatchery is the commercial incubation facility.

Incubation Period: Golden Board Numbers

  • Chicken = 21 days
  • Duck / Peking = 28 days
  • Muscovy duck = 35 days
  • Turkey = 28 days
  • Goose = 28–30 days
  • Quail = 16–18 days
Muscovy duck = 35 days is a common board exam outlier.

Board Exam Master Concept

  • Site of fertilization = infundibulum
  • Sperm storage site = utero-vaginal junction
  • Functional ovary = left ovary
  • Longest stay in oviduct = uterus / shell gland
  • Chicken incubation = 21 days
Fertile egg production + proper incubation = chick production.
