Pork Production, By-Products, and Marketing

Pork production, by-products, and marketing cover the final economic stage of swine production where pigs are processed for meat, converted into useful products, and sold for profit.

It includes pork production, slaughter, carcass evaluation, meat cuts, by-products, processing, and marketing.

This is the economic end-point of swine production.

Pork Production

Pork production is the raising and finishing of pigs for meat.

Pork production = swine raised for meat.

Importance of Pork Production

  • Supplies animal protein
  • Provides income
  • Supports food industry
  • Creates employment
  • Supports livestock economy
Pork production supports food and income.

Market Hog and Market Weight

Market hog is a pig ready for slaughter and sale.

Market weight is the ideal live weight at which pigs are sold for slaughter.

Market hog = slaughter-ready pig.

Slaughter

Slaughter is the process of killing pigs for meat production.

Slaughter = killing for meat.

Slaughtering Process

  • Fasting
  • Stunning
  • Bleeding
  • Scalding
  • Dehairing
  • Evisceration
  • Splitting
  • Chilling
Slaughter follows a standard meat processing sequence.

Pre-Slaughter Stress

Pre-slaughter stress is the physical and physiological stress experienced by pigs before slaughter.

Causes include rough handling, transport stress, overcrowding, fighting, heat stress, and prolonged fasting.

Stress before slaughter reduces meat quality.

Stress Hormones

When pigs are stressed before slaughter, they release:

  • Adrenaline (Epinephrine)
  • Noradrenaline (Norepinephrine)
  • Cortisol
Stress hormones affect glycogen, pH, and meat color.

Adrenaline and Noradrenaline

Adrenaline and noradrenaline are acute stress hormones released during fear, excitement, and rough handling.

They cause rapid glycogen breakdown, increased muscle activity, and rapid post-mortem pH drop.

Acute stress hormones = adrenaline and noradrenaline.

Cortisol

Cortisol is the major chronic stress hormone released during prolonged stress.

It causes long-term glycogen depletion, metabolic exhaustion, and poor meat quality.

Cortisol = chronic stress hormone.

Why Stress Affects Meat Color

Stress hormones affect muscle glycogen. After slaughter, glycogen is converted into lactic acid, which lowers muscle pH.

This post-mortem pH affects meat color, tenderness, texture, and water-holding capacity.

Stress hormones affect glycogen → pH → meat color.

PSE Meat

PSE means Pale, Soft, Exudative.

It is caused by acute stress before slaughter and high adrenaline release.

  • Pale color
  • Soft texture
  • Watery meat
Acute stress = PSE.

DFD Meat

DFD means Dark, Firm, Dry.

It is caused by chronic stress before slaughter and prolonged cortisol release.

  • Dark color
  • Firm texture
  • Dry surface
Chronic stress = DFD.

Good Pre-Slaughter Handling

  • Handle pigs gently
  • Avoid beating
  • Reduce transport stress
  • Avoid overcrowding
  • Allow rest before slaughter
  • Avoid excessive fasting
  • Minimize heat stress
Gentle handling improves meat quality.

Slaughter Operations

  • Stunning = unconscious before slaughter
  • Bleeding = removal of blood
  • Scalding = loosen hair with hot water
  • Dehairing = remove hair
  • Evisceration = remove internal organs
  • Chilling = preserve meat quality
Slaughter operations affect hygiene and meat quality.

Carcass

Carcass is the dressed body of the pig after slaughter.

Carcass = dressed body after slaughter.

Dressing Percentage

Dressing percentage is the proportion of carcass weight to live weight.

Formula: Dressing % = (Carcass Weight ÷ Live Weight) × 100

Dressing percentage measures carcass yield.

Carcass Evaluation

Carcass evaluation is judging the carcass based on backfat thickness, loin eye area, muscling, leanness, and carcass quality.

Carcass evaluation determines meat value.

Prime Cuts

  • Ham = hind leg
  • Loin = back
  • Shoulder = front upper portion
  • Belly = underside / bacon cut
Prime cuts = highest-value meat cuts.

By-Products

By-products are useful materials obtained from slaughter aside from the main meat cuts.

By-products are secondary products from slaughter.

Edible By-Products

  • Liver
  • Heart
  • Lungs
  • Kidneys
  • Intestines
  • Blood
Edible by-products are consumed as food.

Inedible By-Products

  • Bristles
  • Bones
  • Hooves
  • Skin scraps
  • Glands
Inedible by-products are used industrially.

Uses of By-Products

  • Food
  • Sausage casing
  • Gelatin
  • Animal feed
  • Fertilizer
  • Pharmaceuticals
  • Leather
By-products add economic value.

Common By-Product Uses

  • Blood = blood meal / food ingredient
  • Intestines = sausage casing
  • Skin = chicharon / leather
  • Bones = bone meal / gelatin
By-products can be converted into food, feed, and industrial materials.

Marketing

Marketing is the process of selling pigs, pork, and by-products for profit.

Marketing = selling for profit.

Marketing Channels

  • Farm gate
  • Traders
  • Slaughterhouses
  • Wet markets
  • Supermarkets
  • Processors
Marketing channels move pork to consumers.

Types of Selling

  • Farm gate = direct farm sale
  • Wholesale = bulk selling
  • Retail = direct consumer sale
Marketing method affects income and profit.

Factors Affecting Market Price

  • Weight
  • Carcass quality
  • Demand
  • Supply
  • Health status
  • Season
Market price depends on quality and demand.

Value Addition

Value addition is increasing product value through processing.

  • Tocino
  • Longganisa
  • Bacon
  • Ham
Value addition increases profit.
