Parts of Dairy Cattle and Udder

Parts of Dairy Cattle and Udder

Knowledge of the body parts of dairy cattle is important in animal evaluation, breed selection, dairy management, judging, and milk production assessment.

Identification of body parts is important in dairy production.

External Body Parts of Dairy Cattle

The external body parts of dairy cattle include:

  • Head
  • Neck
  • Body
  • Udder
  • Legs
  • Tail
External body parts are important in cattle evaluation.

Head

The head contains important sensory organs and feeding structures.

The head contains sensory organs.

Muzzle

The muzzle is the nose and mouth region of cattle.

Muzzle is important in feeding behavior.

Mouth

The mouth is used for prehension, chewing, and feed intake.

Mouth functions in feed consumption.

Nostrils

Nostrils allow passage of air during respiration.

Nostrils are important in respiration.

Eyes

The eyes function in vision and environmental awareness.

Eyes are organs of vision.

Ears

The ears function in hearing and temperature regulation.

Ears assist in hearing.

Horns

Horns are bony projections found in some cattle breeds.

Horns may be present or absent depending on breed.

Poll

The poll is the top portion of the head between the ears.

Poll is located between the ears.

Neck

The neck connects the head to the body.

Neck supports the head.

Dewlap

The dewlap is the loose fold of skin under the neck.

Dewlap is prominent in some breeds.

Withers

The withers are located at the highest point of the shoulders.

Withers are used as reference point for body height.

Shoulder

The shoulder connects the forelimbs to the body.

Shoulder supports body movement.

Brisket

The brisket is the lower chest region between the front legs.

Brisket is part of the chest area.

Chest

The chest contains important organs such as the heart and lungs.

Chest capacity affects respiratory efficiency.

Back

The back extends from the withers to the loin.

Strong back supports body structure.

Loin

The loin is located between the back and rump.

Loin contributes to body strength.

Barrel

The barrel refers to the body cavity containing digestive organs.

Large barrel supports feed intake.

Ribs

Ribs protect internal organs and support body capacity.

Open ribs indicate good dairy character.

Flank

The flank is the side area between the last rib and hind leg.

Flank is important in body evaluation.

Rump

The rump is the rear body portion near the tail head.

Rump affects calving ease and udder attachment.

Hooks

Hooks are the hip bones located at the sides of the rump.

Hooks are important in body conformation.

Pins

Pins are located below the hooks near the tail head.

Pins are important in calving evaluation.

Tail Head

The tail head is the point where the tail attaches to the body.

Tail head is located at the rear body.

Tail

The tail functions in insect control and communication.

Tail helps repel insects.

Forelegs

Forelegs support the front portion of the body.

Strong forelegs improve mobility.

Hind Legs

Hind legs support the rear body and udder.

Strong hind legs support dairy function.

Hooves

Hooves protect the feet and support locomotion.

Healthy hooves are important for mobility.

Udder Parts and Milk Production Structures

Udder

The udder is the external milk-producing structure of dairy cattle. It functions in milk production and milk storage.

Udder is the primary milk-producing structure.

Mammary Gland

The mammary gland is the internal milk-producing organ.

Mammary gland secretes milk.

Quarters

The udder is divided into four quarters:

  • Left front
  • Right front
  • Left rear
  • Right rear

Each quarter functions independently.

Dairy udder has four separate quarters.

Teats

Teats are projections through which milk exits.

Milk is removed through teats.

Teat Canal

The teat canal is the passageway where milk exits from the teat.

Teat canal controls milk flow.

Milk Veins

Milk veins transport blood to and from the udder. Prominent milk veins indicate good circulation and active milk production.

Milk veins are associated with milk production.

Udder Attachment

Strong udder attachment supports udder health, longevity, and milking efficiency.

Proper udder attachment improves productivity.

Fore Udder

The fore udder is the front portion of the udder.

Fore udder should blend smoothly into the body.

Rear Udder

The rear udder is the back portion of the udder.

High wide rear udders are desirable.

Median Suspensory Ligament

The median suspensory ligament supports the udder and separates left and right halves.

Strong ligament supports udder structure.

Udder Texture

Good udders should be soft, pliable, and elastic.

Soft udders are desirable in dairy cattle.

Teat Placement

Proper teat placement improves milking efficiency and udder health.

Proper teat placement is important.

Teat Size

Teats should be medium sized and uniform.

Medium-sized teats improve milking.

Dairy Character and Body Evaluation

Dairy Character

Body parts associated with dairy character include:

  • Angular body
  • Open ribs
  • Prominent milk veins
  • Wedge-shaped body
Dairy character indicates milk-producing ability.

Body Capacity

Body capacity refers to the size and depth of body cavity.

Large body capacity supports high feed intake.

Structural Soundness

Structural soundness refers to proper body conformation and leg structure.

Structural soundness improves longevity.

Importance of Strong Feet and Legs

Strong feet and legs support movement, feeding, and milking activities.

Strong feet and legs improve productivity.

Importance of Udder Health

Healthy udders improve milk yield, milk quality, and milking efficiency.

Udder health is essential in dairy production.

Importance of Body Conformation

Good body conformation improves productivity, reproduction, and longevity.

Good conformation improves performance.

Board Exam Trap Terms

  • Muzzle = nose and mouth region
  • Poll = top of head between ears
  • Dewlap = loose skin under neck
  • Withers = highest point of shoulders
  • Barrel = body cavity
  • Hooks = hip bones
  • Pins = bones near tail head
  • Udder = external milk-producing structure
  • Mammary gland = milk-producing organ
  • Quarter = one-fourth of udder
  • Teat canal = milk passageway
  • Median suspensory ligament = udder support structure
These are common board recall terms in dairy cattle anatomy.
