🔬 Cell & Cell Division

Cell

Cell is the basic structural and functional unit of life. It is the smallest part of the body capable of performing life processes.

Parts of the Cell

Cell Membrane

Controls movement of substances in and out of the cell.

Cytoplasm

Jelly-like substance where organelles are suspended.

Nucleus

Control center of the cell; contains DNA.

Cell Organelles

  • Mitochondria – produces energy (ATP)
  • Ribosomes – protein synthesis
  • Rough ER – protein production
  • Smooth ER – lipid production
  • Golgi Apparatus – modifies and packages proteins
  • Lysosomes – digests waste materials
  • Vacuoles – storage of water and nutrients
  • Centrioles – involved in cell division

Chromosomes, DNA, and Genes

Chromosomes carry genetic information.

DNA is the hereditary material that controls cell activities.

Genes are units of heredity that determine traits.

Cell Division

Cell division is the process by which a cell divides to form new cells for growth, repair, and reproduction.

Mitosis

Produces two identical cells used for growth and repair.

Phases of Mitosis

  • Interphase – cell prepares and duplicates DNA
  • Prophase – chromosomes become visible
  • Metaphase – chromosomes align at center
  • Anaphase – chromatids separate
  • Telophase – nuclei reform
  • Cytokinesis – cell splits into two

Meiosis

Produces four non-identical cells used for reproduction.

Meiosis I

  • Prophase I – crossing over
  • Metaphase I – pairs align
  • Anaphase I – homologous chromosomes separate
  • Telophase I – two cells formed

Meiosis II

  • Prophase II
  • Metaphase II
  • Anaphase II
  • Telophase II – four cells formed

Summary

Cell = basic unit of life
Mitochondria = energy
Ribosomes = protein

Mitosis = growth (2 identical cells)
Meiosis = reproduction (4 different cells)
