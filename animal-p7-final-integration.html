Swine Production

Swine production is the branch of animal science concerned with the breeding, feeding, management, health, and marketing of pigs for meat and profit.

It covers the complete production cycle from pig selection to pork marketing.

Swine production = raising pigs for meat and income.

Importance of Swine Production

  • Supplies animal protein
  • Provides income
  • Supports food security
  • Creates employment
  • Supports livestock economy
Swine production supports food and livelihood.

Swine Production Flow

The swine production cycle follows this sequence:

Breed Selection → Breeding → Gestation → Farrowing → Nursing → Weaning → Growing → Finishing → Slaughter → Marketing

Breed Selection

Breed selection is choosing pigs based on desirable production traits.

  • Growth rate
  • Fertility
  • Feed efficiency
  • Adaptability
  • Carcass quality
Breed selection affects productivity and profit.

Breeds of Swine

  • Large White / Yorkshire = Mother Breed
  • Landrace = Bacon Type
  • Duroc = growth and hardiness
  • Hampshire = lean carcass
  • Berkshire = pork quality
  • Pietrain = meat type and PSS trap
Breed affects growth, reproduction, and meat quality.

External Anatomy

  • Snout = smelling and rooting
  • Jowl = cheek area
  • Shoulder = front muscling
  • Back = topline
  • Loin = valuable meat cut
  • Belly = bacon source
  • Ham = hindquarter meat
External parts are used for identification, judging, and carcass evaluation.

Nutrition

Nutrition is the science of supplying nutrients for maintenance, growth, reproduction, and production.

  • Water = most important nutrient
  • Carbohydrates = energy
  • Protein = growth
  • Fats = concentrated energy
  • Vitamins = regulation
  • Minerals = bones and metabolism
Nutrition supports health, growth, reproduction, and productivity.

Vitamin Board Traps

  • ADEK = fat-soluble vitamins
  • B-complex + C = water-soluble vitamins
  • Beta-carotene → Vitamin A
  • Tryptophan → Niacin
  • Vitamin K = may be produced by gut microbes
  • Vitamin C = usually non-essential in swine
Vitamin questions often test classification, function, deficiency, precursor, and exception.

Feeding Systems and Management

  • Ration = daily feed amount
  • Balanced ration = complete nutrition
  • Ad libitum = free access feeding
  • Restricted feeding = controlled feed intake
  • Creep feeding = piglets while nursing
  • Starter = post-weaning
  • Grower = growth stage
  • Finisher = market stage
Feeding determines growth efficiency and production cost.

Feed Efficiency

Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) measures the amount of feed needed to produce one unit of weight gain.

FCR = Feed Consumed ÷ Weight Gain

Lower FCR = better efficiency.

Reproduction

  • Testes = sperm production
  • Ovaries = egg production
  • Boar penis = corkscrew-shaped
  • Sow cervix = interlocking pads
  • Uterus = bicornuate
Reproductive structures are common board traps.

Estrus and Breeding

  • Estrus = heat period
  • Estrus cycle = 21 days
  • Standing heat = best breeding sign
  • Back-pressure test = checks standing reflex
  • Ovulation = 36–42 hours after estrus onset
  • Best breeding = 12 hours after heat detection, then 12 hours later
Breeding timing determines reproductive success.

Gestation and Farrowing

  • Gestation = pregnancy
  • Gestation length = 114 days
  • 3-3-3 rule = 3 months, 3 weeks, 3 days
  • Farrowing = giving birth
  • Colostrum = first milk
  • Weaning = milk separation
Gestation = 114 days is one of the highest-yield swine numbers.

Health Management

  • Sanitation = cleanliness
  • Disinfection = kills pathogens
  • Vaccination = stimulates immunity
  • Biosecurity = prevents disease entry
  • Quarantine = separates new animals
  • Isolation = separates sick animals
Prevention is better than treatment.

Disease Board Traps

  • Leptospirosis = abortion
  • Colibacillosis = piglet diarrhea
  • Erysipelas = diamond skin lesions
  • Atrophic rhinitis = twisted snout
  • ASF = sudden death
  • FMD = mouth and foot lesions
  • PRRS = reproductive and respiratory signs
  • Mange = mites
  • Iron deficiency = pale piglets
Disease questions commonly test disease name + classic sign.

Slaughter and Processing

  • Stunning = unconscious before slaughter
  • Bleeding = removal of blood
  • Scalding = loosens hair
  • Dehairing = removes hair
  • Evisceration = removes internal organs
  • Chilling = preserves meat quality
Slaughter process affects hygiene, meat safety, and meat quality.

Pre-Slaughter Stress and Meat Quality

  • Adrenaline / Noradrenaline = acute stress hormones
  • Cortisol = chronic stress hormone
  • PSE = pale, soft, exudative meat from acute stress
  • DFD = dark, firm, dry meat from chronic stress
Stress hormones affect glycogen → pH → meat color.

Carcass and Meat Cuts

  • Carcass = dressed body after slaughter
  • Dressing percentage = carcass yield
  • Backfat = fatness indicator
  • Loin eye area = muscling indicator
  • Ham = hind leg
  • Loin = back
  • Belly = bacon cut
Carcass traits determine meat value.

By-Products and Marketing

  • By-products = secondary products from slaughter
  • Intestines = sausage casing
  • Skin = chicharon / leather
  • Bones = bone meal / gelatin
  • Farm gate = direct farm sale
  • Wholesale = bulk selling
  • Retail = direct consumer sale
By-products and marketing increase final profit.
