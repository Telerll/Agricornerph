Topic 05

Market Structure

Types of market organization based on buyers, sellers, and competition.

Market Structure

Market structure refers to how a market is organized based on the relationship between buyers and sellers.

It determines how prices are set and how competition operates in the market.

Types of Market Structure

1. Pure Competition

A market with many buyers and sellers where no single participant can influence the price.

Products are homogeneous and identical in nature.

2. Monopoly

A market structure where only one seller exists, controlling the supply and price of the product.

3. Monopolistic Competition

A market with many sellers offering similar but differentiated products.

Competition is based on branding, quality, and product differences.

4. Oligopoly

A market dominated by a few large sellers who influence prices and supply.

5. Monopsony

A market structure where there is only one buyer purchasing goods from many sellers.

Market Conduct

Market conduct refers to the behavior and strategies of firms in setting prices, output, and competition methods.

Market Performance

Market performance evaluates how well the market achieves efficiency, fairness, and economic goals.
