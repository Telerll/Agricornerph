Nature or Dimensions of Extension

1. Altruistic Dimension

  • Extension is aimed at helping farmers.
  • A basic premise of extension that must be observed by practitioners.

2. Educational Dimension

  • Extension is a non-formal method of adult education.
  • Extension professionals must be knowledgeable about how adults learn.

3. Communication Dimension

  • Extension is a communication intervention.
  • Knowledge of the basic communication process and its dynamics must be adequately understood by extension professionals.

4. Behavioral Dimension

  • Extension is aimed at inducing behavioral changes among farmers.
  • Behavioral change is considered the end goal of extension.
  • If the farmer has not changed his behavior for the betterment, then extension has not been done.

5. Technology Dimension

  • Extension helps in the transfer of technology.
  • Extension workers should identify technologies appropriate to clientele.
  • Alternative approaches and methods for diagnosing technical problems must be recognized.

6. Research Dimension

  • Extension aims at linking research with farmers.
  • Ways to foster closer links between farmers and researchers require further understanding.

7. Input Dimension

  • Provision of technical inputs plays an important linking function in technology transfer.
  • Extension professionals must be knowledgeable about technical inputs.

8. Income Dimension

  • Extension is aimed at increasing farmers’ income through improved production and productivity.

9. Management Dimension

  • Extension functions according to sound management principles.
  • Extension professionals are expected to be good managers.
