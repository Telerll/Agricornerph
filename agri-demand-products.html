Topic 16

Demand for Agricultural Products

Expanded discussion on demand concepts and consumer behavior in agricultural markets.

Demand

Demand refers to the quantity of agricultural products that consumers are willing and able to buy at different prices within a given period.

It represents the relationship between price and quantity demanded.

Effective Demand

Effective demand refers to the desire to buy a product combined with the ability to pay for it.

It is not enough to want a product; consumers must also have purchasing power.

Law of Demand

The law of demand states that there is an inverse relationship between price and quantity demanded, assuming all other factors are constant.

When price increases, quantity demanded decreases; when price decreases, quantity demanded increases.

Giffen’s Paradox

Giffen’s Paradox is a situation where demand increases even when price increases, which contradicts the normal law of demand.

Market Demand

Market demand refers to the total quantity of a product demanded by all consumers in a market at different price levels.

It is the combined demand of all individual consumers in a specific market area.
