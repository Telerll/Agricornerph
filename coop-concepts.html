The Main Concepts of Cooperation

A cooperative must succeed as a business institution; it cannot prosper long on any other basis.

Four Main Concepts of Cooperatives

1. Social – Cooperation is a means of social reform and community improvement.
2. Sociopolitical – Cooperative organizations improve social life through collective political and social participation.
3. Legalistic – A cooperative is a legally recognized organization; its structure is defined by law.
4. Economic – Cooperatives aim for business efficiency while improving members’ economic condition.
