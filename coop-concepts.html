Concepts of Cooperation

Understanding the Foundations of Cooperatives
Main Idea
A cooperative must succeed as a business institution; it cannot survive long without economic efficiency and proper management.
1. Social Concept
Cooperation is a tool for social reform that improves community welfare and promotes unity among members.
2. Sociopolitical Concept
Cooperatives strengthen participation in social and political life, helping members improve their social conditions through collective action.
3. Legalistic Concept
A cooperative is a legally recognized organization formed under laws and regulations that define its structure and operations.
4. Economic Concept
Cooperatives focus on improving economic efficiency, increasing income, and supporting the financial well-being of members.
