Extension in the Philippine Setting

Beginnings of Agricultural Extension in the Philippines

Spanish Regime

Agricultural extension may have started during the Spanish regime through the Granja Modelos or model farms.

Granja Modelos

  • Experimental or demonstration centers for farmers during the 19th century.
  • Initiated by the first Spanish missionaries.
  • Intended to educate rice, corn, and tobacco farmers in large encomiendas.

American Regime

April 30, 1902

The Bureau of Agriculture was established under the Department of Interior.

July 1910

  • The Demonstration and Extension Division was created under the Bureau of Agriculture.
  • It became the first formally organized government department implementing research and extension programs.
  • The Bureau of Agriculture expanded extension services through its Administrative Division.

July 1919

The Demonstration and Extension Division expanded its work to include:

  • Farmers’ cooperatives
  • Rural credit
  • Marketing
  • Animal insurance

1923

  • The Demonstration and Extension Division was renamed Agricultural Extension Services.
  • Home Extension Work was started in the Division of Organic Chemistry of the Bureau of Soils mainly on food preservation.
  • Maria Y. Orosa founded the home extension service.

1929

  • The Bureau of Agriculture was divided into the Bureau of Plant Industry (BPI) and the Bureau of Animal Industry (BAI).
  • The agricultural extension division was placed under BPI.

1932

The Agricultural Extension Division became the official name.

1936

Commonwealth Act No. 85 widened extension service coverage by authorizing local government units to support extension services.

Undated

Commonwealth Act 649 increased appropriations for extension work.

1942–1945 Japanese Occupation

Agricultural extension and home economics work suffered setbacks.

After Japanese Occupation

1947

The Home Extension Unit under BPI was fused with the Agricultural Extension Unit of the Bureau of Agriculture.

1950

The Philippine government requested the USA to conduct an economic survey mission.

Bell Survey Mission Recommendation

The recommendation was to consolidate scattered extension organizations into one bureau that could adequately extend information to farm families.

July 16, 1952

The Bureau of Agricultural Extension (BAEx) was created by virtue of R.A. 680.

BAEx consolidated all extension services conducted by different offices.

1963

BAEx was changed into Agriculture Productivity Commission (APC) through R.A. 3844 or the Land Reform Code.

1967

Republic Act No. 188 decentralized BAEx functions and granted powers to local government units.

1969

The National Food and Agricultural Council (NFAC) was created through EO 183.

NFAC supervised food production programs and managed agricultural funds from USAID.

1972

Presidential Decree No. 1 and Presidential Letter of Implementation No. 9 reverted APC to BAEx under the Department of Agriculture and Natural Resources.

July 1, 1973

BAEx was transferred back to the Department of Agriculture.

1977

The World Bank Mission appraised the Philippine Agricultural Extension Service.

Result

The Philippines adopted the Training and Visit System (T & V) to improve the quality of life of farm families.

March 27, 1979

The National Extension Project (NEP) became operational through a World Bank loan.

1978

  • PD No. 1579 and LOI No. 595 created ministry-wide regional offices in the Ministry of Agriculture.
  • EO No. 967 renamed the Ministry of Agriculture into the Ministry of Agriculture and Food (MAF).

1982

  • EO No. 803 designated provinces as political units for managing agricultural development.
  • RA 5185, also known as the Decentralization Act, empowered provincial governments to operate their own extension services.

Organizational Structure

Minister of Agriculture → Regional Director → Provincial Agriculture Officer → Municipal Agriculture Officer → Agricultural Food Technologists (AFTs)

1987

EO No. 116 merged BAEx, Agricultural Training Council, and the Philippine Training Center for Rural Development into the Agricultural Training Institute (ATI).

The birth of ATI marked the dissolution of BAEx.

Recent Developments in the Agricultural Extension System

1991

The Local Government Code (RA 7160) decentralized agricultural extension services to Local Government Units (LGUs).

1997

RA 8435, also known as the Agriculture and Fisheries Modernization Act (AFMA), was enacted.

Major Concerns of AFMA

  1. Food Security
  2. Poverty Alleviation and Social Equity
  3. Income Enhancement and Profitability
  4. Global Competitiveness
  5. Sustainability
