🌸 Crop Science

Part 3 - Plant Physiology

I. Plant Life Processes

A. Photosynthesis
  • Plants make food using light, water, and CO₂
  • Occurs in chloroplast
  • CO₂ enters via stomata
  • Water transported by xylem
  • Produces glucose and oxygen
  • Light Reaction → ATP, NADPH, O₂
  • Dark Reaction → glucose formation
  • C3, C4, CAM pathways

B. Respiration

  • Breakdown of glucose to release energy
  • Glycolysis
  • Krebs Cycle
  • ETS (ATP production)

C. Transpiration

  • Loss of water vapor
  • Stomatal (main)
  • Cuticular & Lenticular
  • Maintains turgor

D. Translocation

  • Xylem → water and minerals
  • Phloem → sugars
  • Source → producer
  • Sink → user/storage

E. Assimilation

  • Conversion of nutrients into tissues
  • Depends on sink strength
  • Affected by growth stage and proximity

II. Growth and Development

  • Growth → increase in size
  • Development → change in form
  • Differentiation → specialized cells
  • Organization → tissues and organs

Plant Movements

  • Tropic → directional (light, gravity)
  • Nastic → non-directional (touch, water)
  • Phototropism → response to light
  • Gravitropism → response to gravity

III. Plant Hormones

  • Auxin → elongation
  • Gibberellin → stem growth
  • Cytokinin → cell division
  • Ethylene → ripening
  • ABA → stress/dormancy

IV. Concepts Related to Growth

  • Liebig’s Law → limited by scarcest factor
  • Blackman’s Theory → rate depends on limiting factor
  • Mitscherlich Law → diminishing returns

V. Water Relations

  • Diffusion → movement from high to low
  • Osmosis → water movement across membrane
  • Imbibition → absorption by dry materials
  • Water potential: Ψw = Ψs + Ψp
  • Ψs → solute (negative)
  • Ψp → pressure (turgor)
  • Water moves from high Ψ → low Ψ
  • Turgor pressure → firmness
  • Plasmolysis → shrinking
  • Flaccid → partially wilted

VI. Mineral Nutrition

🌿 Macronutrients

  • N (Nitrogen) → leaf growth, chlorophyll
    Deficiency: yellowing (chlorosis)
  • P (Phosphorus) → roots & ATP
    Deficiency: purple leaves
  • K (Potassium) → enzyme activation, water balance
    Deficiency: leaf edge burn
  • Ca (Calcium) → cell wall formation
    Deficiency: tip burn
  • Mg (Magnesium) → part of chlorophyll
    Deficiency: interveinal chlorosis
  • S (Sulfur) → protein formation
    Deficiency: yellowing (young leaves)

🌱 Micronutrients

  • Fe (Iron) → chlorophyll formation
    Deficiency: yellowing of young leaves
  • Zn (Zinc) → enzyme activity
    Deficiency: stunted growth
  • B (Boron) → flower & fruit development
    Deficiency: poor fruiting
  • Mo (Molybdenum) → nitrogen metabolism
    Deficiency: yellowing of older leaves & stunted growth
  • Cl (Chlorine) → stomatal regulation & osmosis
    Deficiency: wilting even when soil is moist
  • Ni (Nickel) → urease enzyme function
    Deficiency: dark green leaves with brown tips

VII. Photoperiodism & Vernalization

  • Short-day → rice, chrysanthemum
  • Long-day → wheat, spinach
  • Day-neutral → tomato
  • Photoperiod → day/night length
  • Critical day length → flowering trigger
  • Vernalization → cold-induced flowering

VIII. Enzymes

  • Protein catalysts
  • Specific to substrate
  • Not consumed
  • RuBisCO → CO₂ fixation
  • PEP Carboxylase → C4/CAM
  • ATP synthase → ATP
  • NADP reductase → NADPH
  • Amylase → starch → sugar
  • Invertase → sucrose breakdown
  • Hexokinase → glycolysis
  • Cytochrome oxidase → ETC
  • Nitrate reductase → NO₃ → NO₂
  • Nitrogenase → N₂ → NH₃
  • Protease, Lipase, Cellulase
  • Catalase → detox
  • Polyphenol oxidase → browning
  • Factors: temperature, pH, substrate, inhibitors
