I. Plant Life Processes

A. Photosynthesis

Definition: Process by which green plants make food using light, water, and carbon dioxide.

Equation: 6CO₂ + 12H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ + 6H₂O

  • Stomata → pores where CO₂ enters
  • Xylem → carries water to leaves
  • Leaf → main organ for photosynthesis
  • Chloroplast → site of photosynthesis

Leaf Adaptations

  • Broad surface for light absorption
  • Good angle for light capture
  • Efficient vascular system
  • Contains pigments

Chloroplast

Double-membraned organelle with grana/thylakoids and stroma.

Chlorophyll

Green pigment that absorbs light; chlorophyll a is main, b is accessory.

Significance of Photosynthesis

  • Produces food
  • Releases oxygen
  • Stores light energy as chemical energy

Component Reactions

1. Light Reaction – occurs in thylakoids; produces ATP and NADPH and releases O₂.

  • Non-cyclic photophosphorylation → makes ATP, NADPH, and O₂
  • Cyclic photophosphorylation → makes ATP only

2. Dark Reaction / Calvin Cycle – occurs in stroma; uses ATP and NADPH to convert CO₂ into sugars.

Pathways

  • C3 Pathway → most common pathway; first product is 3-PGA
  • C4 Pathway → efficient in hot areas; first product is OAA
  • CAM Pathway → adapted to dry areas; fixes CO₂ at night

C3, C4, and CAM Plants

  • C3 → common plants; lower water-use efficiency
  • C4 → hot-climate plants; high efficiency
  • CAM → desert/succulent plants; stomata open at night

Factors Affecting Photosynthesis

Internal: enzymes, genes, leaf age, water, sink demand

External: light, CO₂, water, temperature, wind

B. Respiration

Definition: Breakdown of glucose to release energy.

Equation: C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6H₂O + 6CO₂ + E

Stages of Respiration

  • Glycolysis → glucose split into pyruvate
  • Krebs Cycle → releases CO₂ and energy carriers
  • ETS → produces most ATP

Factors Affecting Respiration

  • Age and tissue type
  • Temperature
  • Oxygen
  • CO₂
  • Plant condition
  • Moisture content

C. Transpiration

Definition: Loss of water vapor from plants.

Importance

  • Keeps cells hydrated
  • Maintains turgor
  • Helps cool the plant

Types of Transpiration

  • Cuticular → through cuticle
  • Lenticular → through lenticels
  • Stomatal → through stomata; main type

Factors Affecting Transpiration

  • Humidity
  • Temperature
  • Wind
  • CO₂ concentration
  • Light
  • Leaf/stomatal structure

D. Translocation

Definition: Movement of water, minerals, and food within the plant.

  • Xylem → transports water and minerals
  • Phloem → transports sugars
  • Source → food-producing organ
  • Sink → food-using/storage organ

Factors Affecting Translocation

  • Temperature
  • Light
  • Metabolic inhibitors
  • Concentration gradient
  • Mineral supply
  • Hormones

E. Assimilation

Definition: Use of absorbed nutrients and food to build plant tissues.

Assimilate Partitioning Factors

  • Sink strength → ability to attract assimilates
  • Proximity → nearer sinks get more
  • Stage of development → flowers/fruits become strong sinks
  • Vascular connection → affects movement of assimilates

II. Growth and Development

Development: Overall change in size, form, and structure.

Growth: Irreversible increase in size or mass.

Differentiation: Cells become specialized in structure and function.

Organization: Arrangement of cells into tissues and organs.

Correlation: Influence of one plant part on another.

Endogenous Rhythm: Internal biological rhythm.

  • Annual → yearly
  • Lunar → monthly
  • Circadian → daily

Plant Movements

Response of plant organs to stimuli.

Steps in Plant Movement

  • Perception
  • Transduction
  • Response

Tropic Movements

  • Phototropism → response to light
  • Gravitropism → response to gravity
  • Solar tracking → leaf follows sun

Nastic Movements

  • Hyponasty → upward bending
  • Epinasty → downward bending
  • Nyctinasty → leaf folding at night
  • Hydronasty → rolling due to water condition
  • Thigmonasty → response to touch
  • Seismonasty → response to shaking

Crop Adaptation

Adjustment of plants to the environment.

  • Morphological → structural changes
  • Physiological → functional changes
  • Biochemical → chemical adjustments

III. Plant Growth Regulation

Phytohormones: Natural plant chemicals that regulate growth and development.

  • Auxin → cell elongation, root formation, apical dominance
  • Gibberellic Acid → stem elongation, germination, fruit growth
  • Cytokinin → cell division, shoot growth, delays senescence
  • Ethylene → ripening, abscission, senescence
  • Abscisic Acid → dormancy, stomatal closure, stress response
