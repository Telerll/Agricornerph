⚡ Part 7: Chemical Properties of Soil

Soil Chemical Properties

  • These are properties related to the chemical composition and reactions occurring in the soil.
  • They influence nutrient availability, soil fertility, and plant growth.

Soil Colloids

  • Very small soil particles with large surface area.
  • Highly reactive and important in nutrient retention.
  • Main types:
    • Clay colloids
    • Organic colloids (humus)

Ion Exchange

  • Process where ions are exchanged between soil particles and soil solution.
  • Important for nutrient availability to plants.

Cation Exchange Capacity (CEC)

  • CEC is the ability of soil to hold and exchange positively charged ions (cations).
  • Examples of cations: Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, K⁺, Na⁺
  • Soils with high clay and organic matter have higher CEC.
High CEC = better nutrient holding capacity

Base Saturation

  • Percentage of exchange sites occupied by basic cations (Ca, Mg, K, Na).
  • High base saturation indicates fertile soil.

Soil pH

  • Measure of soil acidity or alkalinity.
  • Scale ranges from 0 to 14:
    • <7 = Acidic
    • 7 = Neutral
    • >7 = Alkaline
  • Most crops grow best at pH 6 to 7.

Importance of Soil pH

  • Affects nutrient availability.
  • Influences microbial activity.
  • Controls solubility of minerals.
Extreme pH can cause nutrient deficiency or toxicity

Soil Acidity

  • Occurs when there is an excess of hydrogen (H⁺) ions.
  • Common in regions with high rainfall.
  • May reduce availability of essential nutrients.

Soil Alkalinity

  • Occurs when there is an excess of bases like calcium and sodium.
  • Common in dry regions.
  • Can limit nutrient availability.

Buffering Capacity

  • Ability of soil to resist changes in pH.
  • Soils with high clay and organic matter have higher buffering capacity.

Soil Salinity

  • Refers to the presence of soluble salts in soil.
  • High salinity can affect plant growth and water uptake.

Good Chemical Condition of Soil

  • Balanced nutrient content.
  • Proper soil pH.
  • Good cation exchange capacity.
  • Low toxic elements and salinity.
