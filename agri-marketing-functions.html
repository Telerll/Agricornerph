Marketing Functions

Activities performed to move agricultural products from producers to consumers.

Marketing Functions

Marketing functions refer to the activities performed by intermediaries to ensure smooth movement of products from production to consumption.

Types of Marketing Functions

1. Exchange Functions

Activities involved in buying and selling products.

2. Physical Functions

Activities that involve handling, storage, transportation, and processing of goods.

3. Facilitating Functions

Activities that support exchange and physical functions such as financing, risk bearing, and market information.

Utility in Marketing

Utility refers to the ability of a product to satisfy human wants.

Types of Utility

1. Form Utility

Created when raw materials are transformed into useful products.

2. Place Utility

Created when products are available where consumers need them.

3. Time Utility

Created when products are available at the right time.

4. Possession Utility

Created when ownership of goods is transferred to consumers.
