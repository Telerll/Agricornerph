🌾 Nature of Insect Damage

Entomology and Pest Management Reviewer

Introduction

Insects damage crops in many different ways. The type of injury produced depends on the feeding habit, mouthparts, life stage, and behavior of the insect.

Understanding the nature of insect damage is important because proper diagnosis helps farmers identify the pest involved and select the appropriate management strategy.

Insect damage may affect leaves, stems, roots, flowers, fruits, seeds, and stored products.

Definition of Insect Damage

Insect damage refers to the injury caused by insects to plants, plant products, animals, or stored commodities through feeding, oviposition, boring, sucking, contamination, or disease transmission.

Types of Insect Damage

1. Direct Damage

Direct damage occurs when insects feed directly on plant tissues.

  • Caterpillars eating leaves
  • Stem borers tunneling inside stems
  • Fruit flies damaging fruits
  • Rice bugs sucking developing grains

Characteristics of Direct Damage

  • Immediate visible injury
  • Direct reduction in yield
  • Direct reduction in quality
  • Physical destruction of plant parts

2. Indirect Damage

Indirect damage occurs when insects transmit pathogens or create conditions favorable for disease development.

  • Aphids transmitting viruses
  • Leafhoppers transmitting Rice Tungro Virus
  • Whiteflies transmitting plant viruses

Characteristics of Indirect Damage

  • Damage may not be immediately visible
  • Often associated with disease symptoms
  • May affect large areas rapidly

Nature of Damage Based on Feeding Habit

A. Chewing Damage

Chewing damage is produced by insects with chewing mouthparts.

  • Grasshoppers
  • Beetles
  • Caterpillars
  • Armyworms
  • Cutworms

Symptoms

  • Holes in leaves
  • Ragged leaf margins
  • Missing plant parts
  • Defoliation

Economic Effects

  • Reduced photosynthesis
  • Stunted growth
  • Lower yield

B. Piercing-Sucking Damage

Piercing-sucking damage is produced by insects with piercing-sucking mouthparts.

  • Aphids
  • Leafhoppers
  • Planthoppers
  • Whiteflies
  • Scale insects

Symptoms

  • Yellowing
  • Leaf curling
  • Wilting
  • Stunted growth
  • Honeydew deposits

Economic Effects

  • Loss of plant sap
  • Reduced plant vigor
  • Disease transmission

C. Boring Damage

Boring damage is produced by insects that tunnel into plant tissues.

  • Rice stem borers
  • Corn borers
  • Coffee berry borers

Dead Heart

Dead heart occurs when the central shoot dries and dies during the vegetative stage. It is commonly associated with rice stem borer attack.

White Head

White head occurs when the panicle becomes white and empty during the reproductive stage. It is also commonly associated with rice stem borer attack.

Stem Tunneling

Stem tunneling occurs when internal feeding destroys vascular tissues.

Economic Effects

  • Reduced nutrient transport
  • Lodging
  • Yield loss

D. Mining Damage

Mining damage is produced by insects feeding between the upper and lower leaf surfaces.

  • Leaf miners

Symptoms

  • Serpentine mines
  • Blotch mines
  • Transparent patches

Economic Effects

  • Reduced photosynthesis
  • Reduced plant vigor

E. Root Feeding Damage

Root feeding damage is produced by insects feeding below the soil surface.

  • White grubs
  • Root weevils
  • Mole crickets

Symptoms

  • Wilting
  • Yellowing
  • Stunted growth
  • Plant death

Economic Effects

  • Reduced nutrient uptake
  • Reduced water absorption

F. Gall Formation

Gall formation occurs when insects stimulate abnormal plant growth.

  • Gall wasps
  • Gall midges

Symptoms

  • Swellings
  • Knobs
  • Tumor-like structures

Economic Effects

  • Distorted growth
  • Reduced plant productivity

G. Fruit Damage

Fruit damage is produced by insects attacking fruits.

  • Fruit flies
  • Fruit borers

Symptoms

  • Holes in fruits
  • Fruit rot
  • Premature fruit drop

Economic Effects

  • Reduced market value
  • Rejected produce

H. Seed Damage

Seed damage is produced by insects feeding on seeds.

  • Rice weevil
  • Bean weevil

Symptoms

  • Hollow seeds
  • Reduced seed weight
  • Reduced germination

Economic Effects

  • Lower planting quality
  • Reduced marketability

I. Flower Damage

Flower damage is produced by insects feeding on floral structures.

  • Thrips
  • Flower beetles

Symptoms

  • Flower abortion
  • Deformed flowers
  • Poor pollination

Economic Effects

  • Reduced fruit set
  • Reduced yield

Specific Forms of Insect Damage

Defoliation

Defoliation refers to the removal of leaf tissues by insect feeding.

  • Armyworms
  • Grasshoppers
  • Caterpillars

Defoliation reduces photosynthesis, delays growth, and lowers yield.

Skeletonization

Skeletonization occurs when insects consume leaf tissues between veins, leaving the veins intact.

  • Leaf beetles
  • Some caterpillars

Skeletonized leaves appear skeleton-like because the leaf veins remain.

Leaf Rolling

Leaf rolling occurs when leaves roll due to insect feeding or shelter-building activities.

  • Rice leaf folder

Leaf rolling reduces photosynthetic area and weakens plant vigor.

Leaf Folding

Leaf folding occurs when insects fold leaves and feed within the folded area.

  • Leaf folders

Sap Removal Damage

Continuous sap extraction weakens plants.

  • Chlorosis
  • Wilting
  • Reduced growth
  • Reduced yield

Honeydew Production

Certain sucking insects excrete sugary substances called honeydew.

  • Aphids
  • Whiteflies
  • Mealybugs

Honeydew attracts ants and encourages the development of sooty mold.

Sooty Mold Development

Sooty mold is black fungal growth that develops on honeydew deposits.

It reduces photosynthesis and causes poor plant appearance.

Oviposition Damage

Oviposition damage is caused by egg-laying activities.

  • Rice bugs
  • Certain stem borers

Common symptoms include puncture wounds and tissue injury.

Insect Damage in Stored Products

Storage insects damage grains and stored commodities.

  • Rice weevil
  • Lesser grain borer
  • Angoumois grain moth

Symptoms

  • Holes in grains
  • Powdery residues
  • Weight loss
  • Contamination

Economic Effects

  • Reduced grain quality
  • Reduced market value
  • Loss of seed viability

Economic Importance of Insect Damage

  • Yield reduction
  • Quality reduction
  • Increased production costs
  • Postharvest losses
  • Food shortages
  • Reduced farmer income

Diagnostic Importance

Recognizing the type of damage helps identify the pest involved.

Damage Type Likely Pest
Holes in leaves Chewing insects
Yellowing and curling Piercing-sucking insects
Dead heart Stem borers
White head Stem borers
Mines on leaves Leaf miners
Galls Gall insects
Hollow seeds Seed feeders

ALE Quick Facts

  • Direct damage = Actual feeding injury
  • Indirect damage = Disease transmission
  • Dead heart = Stem borer attack during vegetative stage
  • White head = Stem borer attack during reproductive stage
  • Defoliation = Removal of leaves
  • Skeletonization = Leaf tissue removed, veins remain
  • Leaf miner = Feeds between leaf surfaces
  • Gall = Abnormal plant growth
  • Honeydew = Sugary excretion of sucking insects
  • Sooty mold develops on honeydew

ALE High-Yield Exam Tips

Board Trap #1

Dead heart occurs during the vegetative stage, while white head occurs during the reproductive stage.

Board Trap #2

Chewing insects remove plant tissues, while piercing-sucking insects remove plant sap.

Board Trap #3

Leaf miners feed between the upper and lower leaf surfaces.

Board Trap #4

Honeydew is produced by aphids, whiteflies, and mealybugs, not caterpillars.

Board Trap #5

Black fungal growth on leaves caused by honeydew is called sooty mold.

Summary

The nature of insect damage depends on the feeding habit, mouthparts, and behavior of the insect. Damage may be direct or indirect and may affect leaves, stems, roots, flowers, fruits, seeds, and stored products. Common types include chewing damage, piercing-sucking damage, boring damage, mining damage, gall formation, root feeding, fruit damage, and seed damage. Understanding insect damage is essential for pest diagnosis, economic assessment, and effective pest management.
