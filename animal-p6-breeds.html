Breeds and Classes of Poultry

Breeds and Classes of Poultry

Breeds and classes of poultry refer to the classification of domesticated birds according to species, origin, body type, production purpose, and economic use.

This classification helps identify birds based on:

  • Physical characteristics
  • Productivity
  • Utility
Poultry are classified based on type, origin, and use.

Poultry Classification

Poultry may be classified according to:

  • Species
  • Class
  • Breed
  • Variety
  • Strain
Poultry classification follows a standard hierarchy.

Species

Species is the broadest biological classification in poultry.

Examples:

  • Chicken
  • Duck
  • Turkey
  • Quail
  • Goose
Species is the broadest poultry grouping.

Class

Class refers to a group of breeds developed in the same geographic region or sharing common body characteristics.

In chickens, classes are often based on geographic origin.

Examples:

  • American class
  • English class
  • Mediterranean class
  • Asiatic class
Class groups breeds by origin and body type.

Breed

Breed is a group of birds within a class having similar body shape, size, and production traits.

Examples:

  • Leghorn
  • Rhode Island Red
  • Plymouth Rock
  • Cornish
Breed is a specific recognized poultry group.

Variety

Variety refers to subdivisions within a breed based on plumage color, comb type, or feather pattern.

Examples:

  • White Leghorn
  • Brown Leghorn
Variety is based on visible physical differences.

Strain

Strain is a subgroup within a breed developed by selective breeding for specific traits.

Strains are usually developed for:

  • High egg production
  • Fast growth
  • Better feed efficiency
Strain is a selected line within a breed.

Hybrid

Hybrid is the offspring produced by crossing two or more selected strains or breeds.

Hybrids are developed for:

  • Better performance
  • Improved growth
  • High egg production
  • Better efficiency
Hybrids are produced for commercial performance.

Importance of Poultry Classification

Classification is important because it helps in:

  • Bird identification
  • Breed selection
  • Production planning
  • Management decisions
  • Breeding programs
Classification helps match birds to production purpose.

Classes of Chickens

The major chicken classes are:

  • American
  • English
  • Mediterranean
  • Asiatic
Chicken breeds are grouped into major classes.

American Class

American class includes dual-purpose birds developed in the United States.

Characteristics:

  • Moderate body size
  • Good meat production
  • Good egg production
  • Yellow skin
  • Red ear lobes
  • Brown egg layers

Examples:

  • Rhode Island Red
  • Plymouth Rock
  • New Hampshire
American class = dual-purpose birds with yellow skin.

English Class

English class includes large-bodied birds developed in England.

Characteristics:

  • Large body size
  • Good meat production
  • White skin
  • Red ear lobes
  • Brown egg layers

Examples:

  • Sussex
  • Orpington
  • Dorking
  • Australorp
English class = large dual-purpose birds with white skin.

Mediterranean Class

Mediterranean class includes light-bodied birds developed in Mediterranean regions.

Characteristics:

  • Light body
  • Active
  • Excellent egg production
  • White egg layers
  • White ear lobes
  • Efficient feed use

Examples:

  • Leghorn
  • Minorca
  • Ancona

Leghorns are highly efficient because their small body size requires less maintenance feed, allowing more nutrients to go into egg production.

Mediterranean class = efficient egg-type birds.

Asiatic Class

Asiatic class includes large heavy birds developed in Asia.

Characteristics:

  • Very large body
  • Feathered shanks
  • Slow maturity
  • Red ear lobes
  • Brown egg layers

Examples:

  • Brahma
  • Cochin
  • Langshan

Feathered shanks are undesirable in modern commercial systems because they:

  • Trap dirt and moisture
  • Increase sanitation problems
  • Favor external parasites
  • Worsen litter contamination
Asiatic class = heavy birds with feathered shanks.

Utility Classes

Poultry may also be classified by economic use:

  • Egg type
  • Meat type
  • Dual purpose
  • Ornamental
Utility class groups birds by production use.

Egg-Type Birds

Egg-type birds are specialized for egg production.

Characteristics:

  • Light body
  • Early maturity
  • High egg production
  • Efficient feed use

Example:

  • Leghorn
Egg-type birds are specialized layers.

Meat-Type Birds

Meat-type birds are specialized for meat production.

Characteristics:

  • Rapid growth
  • Heavy muscling
  • Broad breast
  • Good feed efficiency

Examples:

  • Cornish
  • Broiler hybrids

Modern broilers are commonly based on the Cornish Cross, primarily developed from:

  • White Cornish
  • White Plymouth Rock
Meat-type birds are specialized for rapid growth and meat yield.

Dual-Purpose Birds

Dual-purpose birds are suitable for both meat and egg production.

Characteristics:

  • Moderate egg production
  • Moderate meat production

Examples:

  • Rhode Island Red
  • Plymouth Rock
Dual-purpose birds provide meat and eggs.

Ornamental Birds

Ornamental birds are kept mainly for exhibition or aesthetic value.

They are selected for:

  • Plumage
  • Body shape
  • Feather pattern
  • Rarity
Ornamental birds are kept for appearance, not production.

Native and Commercial Poultry

Poultry may also be classified as:

  • Native
  • Commercial
Poultry may be traditional or commercial.

Native Poultry

Native poultry are locally adapted birds.

Characteristics:

  • Hardy
  • Disease resistant
  • Low production
  • Slower growth
Native poultry are hardy but less productive.

Commercial Poultry

Commercial poultry are improved birds bred for high production.

Characteristics:

  • Fast growth
  • High egg yield
  • Efficient feed use
Commercial poultry are specialized for high productivity.

Ear Lobe and Egg Color Rule

In poultry, ear lobe color often relates to egg shell color:

  • White ear lobes usually indicate white eggs
  • Red ear lobes usually indicate brown eggs

Common exception:

  • Araucana = blue or green eggs
Ear lobe color often predicts egg shell color.
