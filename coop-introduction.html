Definition of a Cooperative

The International Co-operative Alliance (ICA), established in 1895, is the oldest and largest worldwide federation of cooperative organizations. It has coverage in around 90 countries, 8 international organizations, 207 national organizations, and more than 750 million members globally. Its headquarters is located in Geneva, Switzerland.

ICA Definition of a Cooperative

“A cooperative is an autonomous association of persons united voluntarily to meet their common economic, social, and cultural needs and aspirations through a jointly-owned and democratically-controlled enterprise.”

Alternative Definition

A cooperative is an organization formed by people with common problems and shared interests. Its main goal is to improve the standard of living and quality of life of its members through collective effort, operating as a voluntary and autonomous business based on universally accepted cooperative principles.

Core Values of Cooperatives

  • Self-help
  • Self-responsibility
  • Democracy
  • Equality
  • Equity
  • Solidarity

Although cooperatives operate as businesses, their main purpose is not individual profit. Instead, they aim to improve the economic and social condition of all members through joint action and cooperation.
