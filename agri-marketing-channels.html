Topic 07

Marketing Channels

Expanded discussion on distribution pathways and intermediaries in agricultural marketing.

Marketing Channels

Marketing channels refer to the sequence of intermediaries and pathways that agricultural products pass through from producers until they reach consumers.

These channels ensure that goods are delivered in the right form, at the right place, and at the right time.

Importance of Marketing Channels

They help move products efficiently from farms to markets, reducing delays and losses.

They bridge the gap between producers and consumers, especially in large geographic markets.

They add value by making products available in usable form, location, and timing.

Intermediaries or Resellers

Intermediaries are individuals or organizations that assist in moving products along the marketing channel.

They play an important role in storage, transportation, pricing, and distribution of goods.

Distribution Flow

Distribution flow refers to the movement of goods, ownership, and information from producers to final consumers.

It includes physical flow (product movement), financial flow (payment), and information flow (market data and communication).

Functions of Marketing Channels

1. Product Movement

Ensures goods are transported from production areas to consumption centers.

2. Storage Function

Maintains supply availability by storing products until needed.

3. Price Formation

Helps in determining prices through supply and demand interactions.

4. Risk Reduction

Minimizes losses due to spoilage, delay, or market uncertainty.
