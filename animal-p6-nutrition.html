Poultry Nutrition and Feeding

Poultry Nutrition and Feeding

Poultry nutrition and feeding is the science of supplying birds with nutrients and feed required for maintenance, growth, reproduction, and production.

It includes nutrient requirements, feed ingredients, ration formulation, feeding systems, water management, nutrient deficiencies, and feeding disorders.

Proper nutrition is essential for growth, health, productivity, and profit.

Importance of Poultry Nutrition

  • Supports growth
  • Maintains health
  • Improves feed efficiency
  • Supports egg production
  • Improves meat yield
  • Strengthens immunity
  • Improves profitability
Nutrition directly affects productivity, health, and profit.

Major Nutrients

Nutrients are chemical substances in feed required by birds for body maintenance, growth, and production.

  • Water
  • Carbohydrates
  • Proteins
  • Fats
  • Vitamins
  • Minerals
Nutrients are essential for life, growth, and production.

Water

Water is the most important nutrient in poultry nutrition.

It supports digestion, nutrient transport, temperature regulation, waste removal, metabolism, and blood circulation.

Water deficiency quickly reduces feed intake, growth, and egg production.

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the main energy source in poultry diets.

Common sources include corn, wheat, rice bran, and sorghum.

Carbohydrates supply energy for growth, movement, and egg production.

Proteins

Proteins are needed for growth, tissue repair, muscle development, feather formation, enzymes, and egg production.

Protein is essential for growth and tissue formation.

Amino Acids

Amino acids are the building blocks of protein.

Important amino acids in poultry include lysine, methionine, tryptophan, and threonine.

Methionine is commonly the first limiting amino acid in corn-soy poultry diets.

Amino acids are the true functional units of protein.

Fats

Fats are concentrated energy sources.

They improve palatability, supply essential fatty acids, reduce feed dustiness, and help absorb fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, and K.

Fats provide concentrated energy.

Vitamins

Vitamins are organic compounds needed in small amounts for metabolism, immunity, reproduction, and health.

  • Vitamin A = vision and immunity
  • Vitamin D3 = calcium absorption and bone formation
  • Vitamin E = reproduction and antioxidant protection
  • Vitamin K = blood clotting
Poultry specifically need Vitamin D3 more efficiently than D2.

Minerals

Minerals are inorganic nutrients needed for bones, eggshells, nerve function, enzyme activity, and metabolism.

Important minerals include calcium, phosphorus, manganese, iron, salt, zinc, copper, iodine, and selenium.

Minerals support structure, metabolism, and eggshell formation.

Calcium and Phosphorus

Calcium is critical for bone development and eggshell formation.

Phosphorus supports bone development and energy metabolism.

In broilers, the Ca:P ratio is closer to about 2:1. In layers, the ratio is much higher, about 10:1 to 12:1, because eggshell production requires high calcium.

Layers need high calcium for strong eggshells.

Feed Ingredients

Feed ingredients are raw materials used to formulate poultry diets.

  • Energy feeds = corn, wheat, rice bran, sorghum
  • Protein feeds = soybean meal, fish meal, copra meal, meat meal
  • Mineral supplements = limestone, dicalcium phosphate, salt
  • Vitamin supplements = vitamin premixes
Feed ingredients are the building blocks of poultry diets.

Grit

Grit refers to small insoluble stones or particles that help the gizzard grind feed.

It is especially useful when birds consume whole grains or fibrous feeds.

Grit is not a nutrient, but it helps the gizzard grind feed.

Ration and Balanced Ration

Ration is the amount of feed given to a bird in 24 hours.

Balanced ration contains all required nutrients in proper amounts and proportions for the bird’s age and production stage.

Balanced ration supports efficient production.

Ration Formulation

Ration formulation is the process of combining feed ingredients to meet nutrient requirements at practical cost.

Ration formulation ensures nutritional balance and economy.

Types of Poultry Rations

  • Starter ration = fed to chicks; high protein for early growth
  • Grower ration = fed during growing stage; supports body development
  • Finisher ration = fed to broilers before market; supports rapid weight gain
  • Layer ration = fed to laying hens; high calcium for eggshell formation
Poultry rations vary by age and production purpose.

Feeding Systems

Feeding systems are methods used to provide feed to birds.

  • Ad libitum feeding = feed is always available
  • Restricted feeding = feed intake is controlled
Feeding system affects intake, growth, and efficiency.

Water Management

Water must always be clean, fresh, cool, and available.

Poor water quality or lack of water reduces feed intake, growth, egg production, and health.

Water management is essential for poultry performance.

Feed Conversion Ratio

Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) is the amount of feed required to produce one unit of body weight gain.

FCR = Feed Intake ÷ Weight Gain

Lower FCR = better feed efficiency.

Nutrient Deficiency

Nutrient deficiency occurs when birds do not receive enough essential nutrients.

Deficiency may result from poor feed quality, unbalanced ration, poor feed intake, poor digestion, or nutrient imbalance.

Board exams often give the symptom first, then ask the missing nutrient.

Protein and Amino Acid Deficiencies

  • Protein deficiency = slow growth, poor feathering, weak birds, low egg production
  • Methionine deficiency = poor feathering, slow growth, poor feed efficiency
  • Lysine deficiency = poor growth, poor muscling, reduced breast muscle
Poor feathering often suggests protein or methionine deficiency.

Vitamin Deficiencies

  • Vitamin A deficiency = xerophthalmia, blindness, poor immunity
  • Vitamin D3 deficiency = rickets, soft bones, weak legs, poor eggshell quality
  • Vitamin E deficiency = encephalomalacia, crazy chick disease, poor coordination, low hatchability
  • Vitamin K deficiency = hemorrhage, prolonged bleeding, poor clotting
  • Thiamine deficiency = weakness, nervous signs, star-gazing
  • Riboflavin deficiency = curled-toe paralysis, poor growth, leg weakness
  • Niacin deficiency = leg weakness, enlarged hock joints
Vitamin deficiencies are high-yield board exam questions.

Mineral Deficiencies

  • Calcium deficiency = thin eggshells, soft-shelled eggs, weak bones
  • Phosphorus deficiency = poor growth, weak bones, poor feed efficiency
  • Manganese deficiency = perosis, slipped tendon, leg deformities
  • Iron deficiency = anemia, pale comb, weakness
  • Salt deficiency = poor appetite, weakness, poor growth
Soft shell = calcium. Perosis = manganese. Pale comb = iron.

Water Deficiency

Water deficiency causes low feed intake, dehydration, low egg production, poor growth, and possible death.

Sudden drop in intake may indicate water problem.

Feeding Disorders

  • Obesity / overfeeding = fatty liver, poor reproduction, reduced egg production
  • Moldy feed = mycotoxicosis, poor growth, liver damage, low egg production
  • Rancid feed = low intake, poor growth, poor performance
  • Nutrient imbalance = poor growth, poor egg production, metabolic disorders
Feeding disorders reduce performance and profit.

Aflatoxin

Aflatoxin is a common mold toxin, often associated with moldy corn and produced by Aspergillus flavus.

It mainly damages the liver and reduces growth, immunity, and egg production.

Aflatoxin = mold toxin that attacks the liver.

Salt Excess

Too much salt can cause excessive water intake and wet droppings.

Wet droppings worsen litter quality and may increase ammonia problems.

Too much salt = wet droppings.

Board Exam Master Concept

Board questions commonly use:

Symptom first → identify deficiency → identify missing nutrient
  • Curled toe = riboflavin
  • Soft shell = calcium
  • Rickets = Vitamin D3 / Calcium / Phosphorus
  • Perosis = manganese
  • Crazy chick = Vitamin E
  • Star-gazing = thiamine
