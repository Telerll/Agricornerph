Health Management in Dairy Cattle

This lesson covers disease prevention, sanitation, vaccination, biosecurity, deworming, milk hygiene, calf health, stress control, and herd health management in dairy cattle production.

Health Management in Dairy Cattle

Health Management in Dairy Cattle

Health management refers to the prevention, control, and treatment of diseases to maintain productivity and animal welfare.

Good health management improves milk production, reproductive efficiency, longevity, and profitability.

Good health management improves dairy productivity.

Importance of Health Management

Proper health management reduces:

  • Disease occurrence
  • Mortality
  • Production losses
  • Veterinary expenses
Disease prevention is more economical than treatment.

Objectives of Health Management

  • Disease prevention
  • Early disease detection
  • Proper treatment
  • Herd health maintenance
Herd health management improves productivity.

Disease

Disease refers to any abnormal condition affecting normal body function.

Disease reduces productivity.

Signs of Healthy Dairy Cattle

  • Alertness
  • Good appetite
  • Normal temperature
  • Shiny hair coat
  • Proper milk production
Healthy animals are active and productive.

Signs of Sick Dairy Cattle

  • Loss of appetite
  • Weakness
  • Fever
  • Diarrhea
  • Coughing
  • Abnormal milk production
Early disease detection improves treatment success.

Biosecurity

Biosecurity refers to measures preventing entry and spread of diseases.

Biosecurity prevents disease transmission.

Sanitation

Sanitation involves maintaining cleanliness in:

  • Housing
  • Milking areas
  • Feeding equipment
  • Water sources
Sanitation reduces disease occurrence.

Hygiene

Hygiene refers to practices promoting cleanliness and health.

Proper hygiene improves milk quality and animal health.

Vaccination

Vaccination stimulates immunity against infectious diseases.

Vaccination prevents infectious diseases.

Vaccine

A vaccine is a biological preparation that stimulates immune response.

Vaccines provide immunity.

Immunity

Immunity is the ability to resist disease.

Immunity protects animals from infection.

Active Immunity

Active immunity develops after vaccination or natural infection.

Active immunity develops inside the body.

Passive Immunity

Passive immunity is obtained through antibodies from colostrum.

Colostrum provides passive immunity.

Colostrum

Colostrum is the first milk rich in antibodies.

  • Disease protection
  • Nutrient supply
  • Improves calf survival
Colostrum is essential for calf survival.

Deworming

Deworming controls internal parasites.

Deworming improves animal performance.

Internal Parasites

Internal parasites live inside the animal body.

  • Roundworms
  • Tapeworms
  • Liver flukes
Internal parasites reduce productivity.

External Parasites

External parasites live outside the body.

  • Ticks
  • Lice
  • Mites
External parasites cause irritation and disease transmission.

Quarantine

Quarantine is the isolation of newly acquired or sick animals.

Quarantine prevents disease spread.

Isolation

Isolation separates sick animals from healthy animals.

Isolation controls disease transmission.

Disinfection

Disinfection destroys harmful microorganisms in facilities and equipment.

Disinfection improves sanitation.

Clean Milk Production

Clean milk production ensures milk is free from:

  • Dirt
  • Bacteria
  • Contaminants
Clean milk improves food safety.

Mastitis Prevention

Mastitis prevention includes:

  • Proper milking hygiene
  • Udder sanitation
  • Teat dipping
Good udder care prevents mastitis.

Teat Dipping

Teat dipping involves dipping teats in disinfectant after milking.

Teat dipping reduces mastitis infection.

Foot Care

Foot care prevents:

  • Lameness
  • Hoof diseases
Healthy feet improve mobility.

Hoof Trimming

Hoof trimming maintains proper hoof shape and function.

Hoof trimming prevents lameness.

Proper Housing

Proper housing should be:

  • Clean
  • Dry
  • Ventilated
  • Comfortable
Proper housing improves herd health.

Ventilation

Good ventilation removes:

  • Excess moisture
  • Heat
  • Harmful gases
Ventilation improves animal comfort.

Stress Management

Stress reduces:

  • Milk production
  • Immunity
  • Reproductive efficiency
Stress negatively affects productivity.

Heat Stress

Heat stress occurs when animals cannot effectively dissipate body heat.

  • Panting
  • Reduced feed intake
  • Reduced milk production
Heat stress lowers milk production.

Prevention of Heat Stress

  • Shade provision
  • Ventilation
  • Adequate water supply
Heat stress prevention improves performance.

Nutrition and Health

Proper nutrition strengthens immunity and disease resistance.

Nutrition supports herd health.

Importance of Clean Water

Clean water prevents disease transmission and dehydration.

Clean water is essential for health.

Herd Health Program

A herd health program includes:

  • Vaccination
  • Deworming
  • Sanitation
  • Disease monitoring
Herd health programs improve productivity.

Veterinary Care

Veterinary care involves diagnosis, treatment, and disease prevention.

Veterinary services support herd health.

Disease Monitoring

Disease monitoring involves regular observation of animal condition.

Monitoring helps early disease detection.

Record Keeping

Health records include:

  • Vaccination records
  • Treatment records
  • Disease history
Record keeping improves health management.

Reproductive Health

Proper reproductive health management improves:

  • Fertility
  • Conception
  • Calving performance
Reproductive health supports productivity.

Calf Health Management

Calf health management includes:

  • Colostrum feeding
  • Vaccination
  • Sanitation
Healthy calves improve future herd productivity.

Animal Welfare

Animal welfare includes proper:

  • Nutrition
  • Housing
  • Handling
  • Medical care
Good welfare improves productivity.

Humane Handling

Humane handling reduces stress and injuries.

Proper handling improves animal welfare.

Disease Prevention

Disease prevention is more effective and economical than treatment.

Prevention is better than cure.

Zoonotic Diseases

Zoonotic diseases are transmissible from animals to humans.

  • Tuberculosis
  • Rabies
Zoonotic diseases affect public health.

Milk Hygiene

Milk hygiene involves sanitary handling of milk during milking and storage.

Milk hygiene improves milk safety.

Importance of Early Detection

Early disease detection improves:

  • Treatment success
  • Recovery
  • Productivity
Early detection reduces production losses.

Importance of Proper Management

Proper management improves:

  • Health
  • Milk yield
  • Reproductive efficiency
Good management improves profitability.

Board Exam Trap Terms

  • Biosecurity = disease prevention measures
  • Vaccination = stimulation of immunity
  • Immunity = resistance to disease
  • Colostrum = first milk rich in antibodies
  • Deworming = parasite control
  • Quarantine = isolation of new animals
  • Isolation = separation of sick animals
  • Mastitis = udder inflammation
  • Teat dipping = teat disinfection after milking
  • Zoonotic disease = transmissible to humans
These are common board recall terms in dairy health management.
