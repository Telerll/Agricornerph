Breeds of Swine

Breeds of Swine

Breeds of swine refers to the different genetically distinct groups of pigs developed for specific production traits such as growth, reproduction, carcass quality, and adaptability.

Each breed has unique characteristics that make it useful for meat production, breeding, maternal ability, growth performance, and carcass quality.

Swine breeds differ in appearance, performance, and production value.

Breed

A breed is a group of animals with similar inherited characteristics that distinguish them from other groups of the same species.

These traits are consistently passed from parents to offspring.

Breed is a genetically distinct group with recognizable traits.

Importance of Swine Breeds

Swine breeds are important because breed selection affects:

  • Growth rate
  • Fertility
  • Litter size
  • Feed efficiency
  • Carcass quality
  • Adaptability
  • Profitability
Proper breed selection improves production efficiency.

Classification of Swine Breeds

Swine breeds are commonly classified according to:

  • Origin
  • Body type
  • Production use
  • Economic value
Swine breeds are classified by source and production purpose.

Major Swine Breeds

  • Large White
  • Landrace
  • Duroc
  • Hampshire
  • Berkshire
  • Pietrain

These are major economically important swine breeds.

Large White (Yorkshire)

Large White, also called Yorkshire, is one of the most popular and widely used swine breeds in the world.

  • White body color
  • Erect ears
  • Strong mothering ability
  • Good milk production
  • Adaptability
Large White is known for maternal ability and is called the Mother Breed.

Landrace

Landrace is a white breed known for its long body and excellent reproductive traits.

  • White body color
  • Drooping ears
  • Long body
  • Prolific litter size
  • Strong maternal traits
Landrace is known for body length, prolificacy, and Bacon Type traits.

Duroc

Duroc is a popular breed known for growth rate, feed efficiency, and hardiness.

  • Red body color
  • Drooping ears
  • Fast growth
  • Good feed conversion
  • Muscular body
Duroc is a strong sire breed known for growth and hardiness.

Hampshire

Hampshire is a black breed known for leanness and muscularity.

  • Black body
  • White belt across shoulders and forelegs
  • Erect ears
  • Lean carcass
  • Muscular build
Hampshire is known for lean carcass and white belt marking.

Berkshire

Berkshire is a black breed known for superior pork quality.

  • Black body
  • Six white points
  • Erect ears
  • Excellent meat quality
  • Flavorful pork
Berkshire is known for premium pork quality and flavor.

Pietrain

Pietrain is a heavily muscled breed known for extremely lean carcass.

  • White body with black spots
  • Erect ears
  • Extreme muscling
  • High lean yield
  • Low backfat
Pietrain is known for Meat Type traits, extreme muscling, and lean carcass.

Chester White

Chester White is a white breed known for durability and mothering ability.

  • White body
  • Drooping ears
  • Maternal ability
  • Soundness
Chester White is known for durability and maternal traits.

Poland China

Poland China is a large black breed known for size and feed efficiency.

  • Black body
  • Six white points
  • Drooping ears
  • Large size
Poland China is known for size, growth, and drooping ears.

Maternal Breed

A maternal breed is a breed mainly valued for reproductive and mothering ability.

  • Fertility
  • Litter size
  • Milk production
  • Mothering ability
Maternal breeds are used for reproduction and piglet production.

Sire Breed

A sire breed is a breed mainly valued for growth, muscling, and carcass quality.

  • Growth rate
  • Feed efficiency
  • Muscling
  • Carcass quality
Sire breeds are used for meat and carcass improvement.

Terminal Breed

A terminal breed is a breed used mainly in final crossbreeding to produce market pigs.

Terminal breeds improve meat production traits.

Crossbreeding

Crossbreeding is the mating of animals from different breeds to combine desirable traits.

Crossbreeding combines breed strengths.

Hybrid Vigor (Heterosis)

Hybrid vigor or heterosis is the improved performance of crossbred offspring over purebred parents.

Hybrid vigor improves performance in crossbreds.

Purebred

A purebred is an animal whose parents belong to the same breed.

Purebred animals maintain breed purity.

Board Exam Trap Terms

Large White vs. Landrace: Large White has erect ears; Landrace has drooping ears.

Easiest distinction = ear position.

Duroc vs. Hampshire: Duroc is red; Hampshire is black with white belt.

Easiest distinction = coat color and white belt.

Berkshire vs. Poland China: Both may have six white points.

  • Berkshire = erect ears
  • Poland China = drooping ears
When both have white points, use ear carriage to identify them.

Maternal vs. Sire Breed: Maternal = reproduction traits; Sire = growth and carcass traits.

Maternal = mother traits, sire = meat traits.

Advanced Board Exam Trap Concepts

Large White / Yorkshire is often called the Mother Breed because of its strong maternal ability, milk production, and adaptability.

Large White = classic Mother Breed.

Landrace is classically known as the Bacon Type breed because of its long body, long side, and long loin.

Landrace = Bacon Type breed.

Pietrain is known as the Meat Type breed because of its extreme muscling, high lean yield, and low backfat.

Pietrain = Meat Type breed.

Pietrain is also commonly associated with Porcine Stress Syndrome (PSS) and the halothane gene.

PSS may cause stress sensitivity, sudden death, and pale, soft, exudative pork.

Pietrain = PSS / halothane gene board trap.

Board Exam Master Concept

Swine breed questions usually test breed identification, body color, ear type, maternal vs sire use, and carcass vs reproductive value.

Board exams commonly test breed recognition and breed function.
