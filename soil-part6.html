💧 Part 6: Soil Water

1. Soil Water

  • Soil water is the water held in the pore spaces of the soil.
  • It is essential for plant growth and many biological and chemical processes in the soil.
  • It serves as a solvent and transport medium for nutrients.

2. Importance of Soil Water

  • Supplies water needed by plants for growth and development
  • Helps dissolve nutrients so roots can absorb them
  • Supports microbial activity and chemical reactions in the soil
  • Regulates soil temperature

3. Forms of Soil Water

Gravitational Water

  • Water that drains freely through the soil due to gravity
  • Found in large pores after heavy rain or irrigation
  • Moves downward quickly
Gravitational water is not readily available to plants.

Capillary Water

  • Water held in the small pores by capillary forces
  • Moves in all directions
Capillary water is the main form of water available to plants.

Hygroscopic Water

  • Thin film of water tightly held around soil particles
  • Held by strong adhesion forces
Hygroscopic water is not available to plants.

4. Soil Moisture Constants

Field Capacity (FC)

  • Amount of water remaining after excess water has drained
  • Represents the upper limit of available water
Field Capacity is the ideal moisture condition for plant growth.

Permanent Wilting Point (PWP)

  • Soil moisture level where plants cannot recover from wilting
  • Water becomes unavailable to plants

Available Water Capacity (AWC)

  • Water available to plants
  • Formula: AWC = FC − PWP

Saturation

  • All pores are filled with water
Saturation means no air in the soil, which causes poor root respiration.

5. Soil Water Movement

Infiltration

  • Entry of water into the soil surface

Percolation

  • Downward movement of water through the soil profile

Capillary Rise

  • Upward movement of water through small pores

Lateral Flow

  • Sideways movement of water in the soil

6. Soil Water Potential

  • Soil water potential is the energy status of water in soil.
  • It controls the direction and movement of water.

Types of Soil Water Potential

  • Gravitational potential
  • Matric potential
  • Osmotic potential

7. Soil Moisture Tension

  • Soil moisture tension is the force required by plant roots to extract water from the soil.
Wet soil = low tension | Dry soil = high tension

8. Hydraulic Conductivity

  • Hydraulic conductivity is the ability of soil to transmit water.
  • Sandy soils usually have high conductivity.
  • Clay soils usually have low conductivity.

9. Unsaturated Flow

  • Unsaturated flow is the movement of water when soil is not fully saturated.
  • It is mainly controlled by capillary forces.

10. Evaporation

  • Evaporation is the loss of water from the soil surface to the atmosphere.

11. Transpiration

  • Transpiration is the loss of water from plant leaves.

Evapotranspiration (ET)

  • Combined loss of water from soil and plants
  • Important in irrigation planning

12. Soil Moisture Zones

Saturation Zone

  • All pores are filled with water
Poor condition for roots because there is little or no air.

Aeration Zone

  • Both air and water are present in the soil pores
Best condition for plant growth.

13. Factors Affecting Soil Water

  • Texture – clay holds more water than sand
  • Structure – better structure improves movement and storage
  • Organic matter – increases water retention
  • Soil depth – deeper soil stores more water
  • Compaction – reduces water movement and infiltration

14. Water Holding Capacity

  • Water holding capacity is the ability of soil to retain water.
  • It is influenced by texture, structure, and organic matter.

15. Soil Drainage

  • Soil drainage is the removal of excess water from the soil.

Types of Drainage

  • Natural drainage
  • Artificial drainage
Poor drainage can lead to waterlogging.

16. Soil Moisture Management

  • Proper irrigation maintains moisture
  • Mulching reduces evaporation
  • Organic matter improves water retention
  • Drainage prevents excess water

17. Waterlogging

  • Waterlogging happens when soil becomes saturated for a long period.

Effects of Waterlogging

  • Reduced aeration
  • Poor root respiration
  • Root damage
  • Poor plant growth

18. Soil Water Retention Curve

  • Shows the relationship between water content and tension.
  • Used to determine field capacity and permanent wilting point.

19. Available vs Unavailable Water

  • Available water – between FC and PWP
  • Unavailable water – hygroscopic water and water below PWP

20. Irrigation Requirement

  • The amount of water needed to restore the soil to field capacity.
  • Depends on crop type, soil type, and climate.

21. Drought Stress

  • Drought stress occurs when soil moisture is below plant needs.

Effects of Drought Stress

  • Wilting
  • Reduced growth
  • Low yield

Final Master Summary

Gravitational → drains → not available
Capillary → usable water
Hygroscopic → unavailable

FC → upper limit
PWP → lower limit
AWC → usable water

Wet soil → low tension
Dry soil → high tension

ET ↑ → soil water ↓
