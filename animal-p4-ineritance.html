Mendelian Inheritance

Mendelian inheritance is the basic pattern of inheritance that explains how traits are passed from parents to offspring according to the laws discovered by Gregor Mendel.

Mendel is the Father of Genetics.

Gregor Mendel

Gregor Mendel was an Austrian monk who discovered the basic laws of heredity through experiments on pea plants.

He studied traits such as:

  • Seed color
  • Flower color
  • Plant height
Mendel established the laws of inheritance.

Importance of Mendelian Genetics

Mendelian genetics is important because it explains:

  • How simple traits are inherited
  • How genes are transmitted
  • The basis of animal breeding and selection
Mendelian genetics is the foundation of inheritance.

Mendel’s Law of Dominance

Law of Dominance states that when two different alleles are present, one may mask the expression of the other.

  • Dominant allele = expressed
  • Recessive allele = hidden

Example:

  • B = black coat
  • b = red coat
Dominant alleles mask recessive alleles.

Dominant Trait

Dominant trait is expressed even when only one dominant allele is present.

Example:

  • BB = black
  • Bb = black
One dominant allele is enough for expression.

Recessive Trait

Recessive trait is expressed only when both alleles are recessive.

Example:

  • bb = red
Recessive traits require two recessive alleles.

Genotype

Genotype is the genetic combination of alleles.

Examples:

  • BB = homozygous dominant
  • Bb = heterozygous
  • bb = homozygous recessive
Genotype refers to allele combination.

Phenotype

Phenotype is the observable expression of the genotype.

Example:

  • BB = black
  • Bb = black
  • bb = red
Phenotype is the visible trait.

Homozygous

Homozygous means the two alleles are the same.

  • BB = homozygous dominant
  • bb = homozygous recessive
Homozygous = same alleles.

Heterozygous

Heterozygous means the two alleles are different.

Example:

  • Bb
Heterozygous = different alleles.

Mendel’s Law of Segregation

Law of Segregation states that allele pairs separate during gamete formation.

Each gamete receives only one allele.

Example:

  • Bb parent produces B and b gametes
Alleles separate during meiosis.

Punnett Square

Punnett Square is a diagram used to predict possible offspring genotypes.

It shows:

  • Genotype ratio
  • Phenotype ratio
Punnett Square predicts inheritance outcomes.

Monohybrid Cross

Monohybrid cross involves one trait only.

Example:

  • Bb × Bb

Punnett result:

  • BB
  • Bb
  • Bb
  • bb
Monohybrid cross studies one trait.

Genotypic Ratio

The genotypic ratio in a monohybrid cross Bb × Bb is:

1 BB : 2 Bb : 1 bb

Phenotypic Ratio

The phenotypic ratio in a monohybrid cross Bb × Bb is:

3 dominant : 1 recessive

Example:

  • 3 black
  • 1 red

Mendel’s Law of Independent Assortment

Law of Independent Assortment states that genes for different traits assort independently during gamete formation.

This applies when genes are on different chromosomes.

If genes are on the same chromosome, this is called gene linkage, which may alter expected ratios.

Inheritance of one trait does not always affect another.

Dihybrid Cross

Dihybrid cross involves two traits.

Example:

  • BbYy × BbYy
Dihybrid cross studies inheritance of two traits at the same time.

Dihybrid Ratio

The classic phenotypic ratio in the F₂ generation of a dihybrid cross is:

9 : 3 : 3 : 1

This applies when both parents are heterozygous BbYy × BbYy and traits assort independently.

Test Cross

Test cross is used to determine the genotype of an individual showing a dominant trait.

Method:

  • Cross unknown dominant with homozygous recessive

Example:

  • Unknown black B_ × red bb

Results:

  • If unknown = BB, all offspring = black
  • If unknown = Bb, some offspring = red
Test cross reveals hidden genotype.

Non-Mendelian Inheritance

Not all traits follow simple Mendelian patterns.

Some traits show non-Mendelian inheritance.

Not all traits follow complete dominance.

Incomplete Dominance

Incomplete dominance occurs when the heterozygous phenotype is intermediate or blended.

Example:

  • Red × White = Pink
Incomplete dominance = blended trait.

Codominance

Codominance occurs when both alleles are expressed equally in the heterozygote.

Example:

  • Roan coat color in cattle
Codominance = both alleles are fully expressed.

Board Exam Master Concept

Mendelian inheritance explains simple inheritance through:

  • Dominance
  • Segregation
  • Independent assortment

Non-Mendelian inheritance explains exceptions such as:

  • Incomplete dominance
  • Codominance
Mendel’s laws explain basic inheritance patterns.
