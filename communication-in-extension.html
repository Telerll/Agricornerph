Communication in Extension

Meaning of Communication

  • Communication is the process of sharing ideas, information, experiences, and feelings between individuals or groups.
  • In extension work, communication serves as the bridge between research institutions, extension workers, and farmers.

Communication in Extension

  • Extension is primarily a communication process.
  • It involves transferring knowledge, technologies, and innovations to rural people.
  • Effective communication helps farmers understand, accept, and apply recommended practices.
  • Communication also allows extension workers to receive feedback and understand the needs and problems of farmers.

Importance of Communication in Extension

  • Helps farmers acquire new knowledge and skills.
  • Encourages adoption of modern agricultural technologies.
  • Strengthens farmer participation in development programs.
  • Builds trust and cooperation between extension workers and clientele.
  • Facilitates behavioral change among rural people.

Characteristics of Effective Communication

  • Clear
  • Simple
  • Accurate
  • Timely
  • Relevant
  • Understandable
  • Feedback-oriented

Role of Extension Worker in Communication

  • Acts as communicator and educator.
  • Interprets scientific information into practical knowledge.
  • Encourages participation and discussion.
  • Identifies communication barriers among clientele.
  • Helps farmers make informed decisions.
