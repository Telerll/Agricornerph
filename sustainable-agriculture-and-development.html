Framework for Sustainable Development

Meaning

The framework for sustainable development refers to the guiding principles that ensure development activities meet present human needs without destroying the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

1. Economically Viable

Development activities should provide economic benefits, generate income, create livelihood opportunities, and remain financially sustainable over time.

2. Ecologically Sound

Development must protect the environment and conserve natural resources such as soil, water, forests, and biodiversity.

3. Socially Just and Humane

Development should promote fairness, equality, respect for human dignity, and equal access to opportunities and benefits.

4. Culturally Acceptable / Appropriate

Programs and technologies should fit the traditions, beliefs, values, and practices of the community.

5. Grounded in Holistic Science

Development should be based on integrated scientific understanding, considering environmental, economic, social, technological, and cultural factors together.

6. Resilient and Low Risk

Development systems should withstand climate change, disasters, pests, diseases, and economic problems while reducing possible risks.

7. Biodiversity-Oriented

Development should protect and promote the variety of plants, animals, microorganisms, and ecosystems to maintain ecological balance.

8. Productive

Development should maintain or improve productivity so food, livelihood, and other human needs can be continuously met.

9. Participatory

People should actively participate in planning, decision-making, implementation, monitoring, and evaluation of development programs.

Sustainable Agriculture

Definition

Sustainable agriculture is maintaining or prolonging the productive capacity of the natural resource base to meet human needs.

It is also the conservation of the natural resource base and the orientation of technological and institutional change that ensures the continued satisfaction of human needs for present and future generations.

It Involves

  • Conservation of natural resources – Proper protection and management of soil, water, forests, biodiversity, and other natural resources.
  • Proper technological orientation – Use of efficient, practical, environment-friendly, and locally appropriate technologies.
  • Institutional change – Improvement of policies, organizations, and institutions that support sustainable development.
  • Continuous satisfaction of human needs – Ensuring that present and future generations can continuously meet their needs.

Components of Agriculture Development

1. Governance

Governance refers to leadership, policies, planning, decision-making, and management systems in agriculture.

2. Research

Research develops new knowledge, technologies, and innovations to improve crop production, livestock production, pest management, and farming systems.

3. Extension / Education

Extension and education involve the dissemination of agricultural knowledge, skills, and technologies to farmers and rural communities.

4. Marketing

Marketing includes promoting, pricing, distributing, and selling agricultural products so farmers can access markets and improve income.

5. Production

Production refers to actual farming activities involved in producing crops, livestock, fishery products, and other agricultural commodities.

6. Supply

Supply refers to the provision of agricultural inputs and resources such as seeds, fertilizers, machinery, feeds, labor, and capital.
