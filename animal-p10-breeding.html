Breeding and Reproduction in Beef Cattle

This lesson covers the breeding principles, reproductive terms, mating systems, reproductive management, calf production, and board exam recall points in beef cattle.

Breeding and Reproduction in Beef Cattle

Breeding and Reproduction

Breeding and reproduction are essential components of beef cattle production because they determine herd improvement, calf production, genetic progress, productivity, and profitability.

Efficient reproduction increases the number of healthy calves produced per year.

Reproductive efficiency affects herd productivity.

Objectives of Breeding

The objectives of breeding in beef cattle include:

  • Improving growth rate
  • Increasing feed efficiency
  • Improving carcass quality
  • Increasing fertility
  • Improving adaptability
  • Improving disease resistance
Breeding improves genetic performance.

Reproduction

Reproduction is the biological process by which cattle produce offspring.

The reproductive process involves mating, fertilization, gestation, and parturition.

Reproduction produces offspring.

Male and Female Cattle Terms

Common reproductive terms include:

  • Bull = mature intact male cattle
  • Steer = castrated male cattle
  • Cow = mature female cattle that has calved
  • Heifer = young female cattle that has not calved
  • Calf = young cattle
Bull = mature male; Cow = mature female.

Puberty

Puberty refers to the stage when cattle become sexually mature and capable of reproduction.

Puberty is affected by breed, nutrition, genetics, and management.

Puberty = sexual maturity.

Estrous Cycle

The estrous cycle is the reproductive cycle occurring between heat periods.

The average estrous cycle in cattle is about 21 days.

Estrous cycle in cattle = about 21 days.

Estrus

Estrus or heat is the period when the female is sexually receptive to the male.

Signs of estrus include:

  • Mounting behavior
  • Restlessness
  • Mucus discharge
  • Swollen vulva
  • Standing to be mounted
Estrus = heat period.

Ovulation

Ovulation refers to the release of the egg cell from the ovary.

Ovulation usually occurs shortly after estrus.

Ovulation = release of egg cell.

Fertilization

Fertilization occurs when the sperm cell unites with the egg cell.

Fertilization usually takes place in the oviduct.

Fertilization = union of sperm and egg.

Gestation Period

Gestation period refers to the length of pregnancy from conception to birth.

The average gestation period in cattle is approximately 283 days.

Gestation period in cattle = about 283 days.

Parturition

Parturition refers to the process of giving birth.

Parturition in cattle is called calving.

Calving = parturition in cattle.

Colostrum

Colostrum is the first milk produced after parturition.

It is rich in antibodies, provides immunity, and improves calf survival.

Colostrum provides passive immunity.

Lactation

Lactation refers to milk production after calving.

Milk is important for calf nutrition, growth, and survival.

Lactation begins after calving.

Weaning

Weaning is the process of separating calves from their mothers.

Its objectives are to encourage independent feeding and prepare calves for the growth stage.

Weaning separates calf from cow.

Breeding Age

Breeding age refers to the appropriate age for first mating.

Proper breeding age improves fertility, reproductive performance, and animal health.

Proper breeding age improves reproductive success.

Natural Mating

Natural mating involves direct mating between bull and cow.

It is simple and requires less technical skill, but it may increase the risk of disease transmission and provides limited breeding control.

Natural mating = direct mating.

Artificial Insemination

Artificial insemination (AI) is the introduction of semen into the reproductive tract of the female using artificial methods.

Advantages include rapid genetic improvement, reduced disease transmission, and wider use of superior sires.

Disadvantages include the need for skill and proper heat detection.

AI = artificial introduction of semen.

Heat Detection

Heat detection involves identifying females in estrus.

Accurate heat detection improves conception rate and breeding efficiency.

Proper heat detection improves fertility.

Conception Rate

Conception rate refers to the percentage of females that become pregnant after breeding.

Factors affecting conception include nutrition, semen quality, timing, and animal health.

Conception rate measures breeding success.

Fertility

Fertility refers to the ability to reproduce successfully.

Good fertility improves herd productivity and calf production.

Fertility affects reproductive efficiency.

Pregnancy Diagnosis

Pregnancy diagnosis determines whether the female is pregnant.

Methods include:

  • Rectal palpation
  • Ultrasound
  • Observation
Pregnancy diagnosis confirms conception.

Calving Interval

Calving interval refers to the time between successive calvings.

Short calving interval improves productivity.

Short calving interval increases efficiency.

Calving Difficulty

Calving difficulty or dystocia refers to difficult birth.

Causes may include large calf, narrow pelvis, or poor fetal position.

Dystocia = difficult calving.

Reproductive Efficiency

Reproductive efficiency refers to the ability to produce healthy calves regularly.

Important indicators include conception rate, calving interval, and fertility.

Reproductive efficiency affects profitability.

Selection of Breeding Stock

Breeding animals should possess good fertility, strong conformation, good growth performance, and disease resistance.

Good breeding stock improves herd quality.

Crossbreeding

Crossbreeding involves mating different breeds to combine desirable traits.

Advantages include hybrid vigor, adaptability, and improved growth.

Crossbreeding improves performance.

Hybrid Vigor

Hybrid vigor or heterosis refers to improved performance of crossbred offspring.

Traits improved may include survivability, fertility, and growth rate.

Hybrid vigor improves productivity.

Inbreeding

Inbreeding involves mating closely related animals.

Excessive inbreeding may cause reduced fertility, lower vigor, and increased genetic defects.

Excessive inbreeding reduces performance.

Linebreeding

Linebreeding is a mild form of inbreeding used to maintain desirable traits.

Linebreeding maintains genetic traits.

Selection

Selection involves choosing superior animals for breeding.

Selection criteria include growth, fertility, carcass quality, and feed efficiency.

Selection improves genetic quality.

Culling

Culling refers to removing inferior or unproductive animals from the herd.

Reasons for culling include infertility, disease, and poor performance.

Culling improves herd quality.

Breeding Soundness Examination

Breeding soundness examination evaluates the reproductive capability of breeding animals.

Breeding soundness ensures reproductive fitness.

Semen Evaluation

Semen evaluation assesses sperm motility, sperm concentration, and sperm morphology.

Good semen quality improves conception.

Bull Management

Proper bull management includes good nutrition, health care, exercise, and reproductive evaluation.

Proper bull management improves breeding performance.

Cow Management

Proper cow management includes nutrition, reproductive care, disease prevention, and calving management.

Proper cow management improves fertility.

Nutrition and Reproduction

Good nutrition is essential for fertility, pregnancy, milk production, and calf growth.

Nutritional deficiency may reduce reproductive performance.

Nutrition affects reproduction.

Reproductive Disorders

Common reproductive disorders include:

  • Retained placenta
  • Abortion
  • Infertility
  • Dystocia
Reproductive disorders reduce productivity.

Abortion

Abortion refers to premature loss of fetus before full-term pregnancy.

Causes may include disease, stress, and nutritional problems.

Abortion causes reproductive loss.

Retained Placenta

Retained placenta occurs when fetal membranes fail to detach after calving.

Retained placenta is a postpartum disorder.

Importance of Reproductive Management

Good reproductive management improves calf crop, herd productivity, and farm profitability.

Reproductive management improves production efficiency.

Record Keeping in Breeding

Breeding records may include:

  • Breeding dates
  • Calving dates
  • Pregnancy status
  • Sire information
Record keeping improves herd management.

Economic Importance of Breeding

Efficient breeding programs improve meat production, genetic improvement, and farm income.

Breeding affects profitability.
