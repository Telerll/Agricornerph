Accounting in Cooperatives

1. Accounting as a Language

Accounting has been called the “Language of Business.”

2. Basic Accounting Principles or Concepts

Money as Common Denominator
Money is used to express all business transactions in one measurable form so they can be added and compared.
Business Entity Principle
The cooperative is separate from its members and is treated as its own entity.
Value Equals Cost Principle
Assets are recorded based on their purchase cost.
Dual Aspect Principle
Assets = Liabilities + Equity
Accrual Principle
Income is recorded when earned, not when cash is received.

3. Balance Sheet Concept

The balance sheet shows the financial position of the cooperative at a specific date, showing where money came from and where it is used.

4. Accounting Conventions

Consistency – same method must always be used
Conservatism – choose lower value when uncertain
Materiality – ignore small irrelevant items

5. Growth and Expansion

Horizontal Integration – similar businesses combined
Vertical Integration – expansion across production stages
Diversification – adding new services
Consolidation – forming new organization
Merger – one absorbs another

6. Member Volume Strategies

1. Increase quality members
2. Provide economical services
3. Use effective volume strategies

7. Pooling Method

Members’ products are combined, sold collectively, and income is distributed after expenses.

Objectives of Pooling

1. Reduce marketing risk
2. Improve planning
3. Reduce distribution cost

8. Capital

Equity Capital – from members
Loan Capital – from external lenders

9. Equity Capital Methods

Sale of shares (common & preferred)
Member contributions
Capital retains
Deferred patronage refund

10. Reserves

Valuation – bad debts & depreciation
Liquidity – emergencies
Capital – expansion & investment

11. Loan Capital Methods

Borrowings
Deferred payments
Trade credit
Bonds & debentures
Donations, grants, gifts
