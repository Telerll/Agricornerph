Introduction to Beef Cattle Production

Beef Cattle Production covers the principles, systems, management practices, feeding, breeding, and health care involved in raising cattle primarily for meat production.

Introduction to Beef Cattle

Beef Cattle

Beef cattle are cattle raised primarily for meat production. They are important livestock animals used for supplying beef and other by-products needed by humans.

Beef cattle are commonly raised in ranches, pasture areas, and integrated farming systems.

Beef cattle = cattle raised for meat production.

Scientific Classification

Domestic cattle are scientifically classified as Bos taurus or Bos indicus depending on breed type and origin.

  • Bos taurus = European cattle
  • Bos indicus = Zebu cattle
Bos taurus = European breeds
Bos indicus = Zebu breeds

Importance of Beef Cattle

Beef cattle contribute significantly to food production, livelihood, agriculture, and the economy.

  • Source of meat
  • Source of income
  • Source of leather and hide
  • Source of manure
  • Draft power in some areas
Beef cattle are important sources of meat and livelihood.

Characteristics of Beef Cattle

General Characteristics

  • Large body size
  • Heavy muscling
  • Fast growth rate
  • Efficient feed conversion
  • Good carcass quality

Beef cattle are selected for traits that improve meat production and profitability.

Beef cattle are selected mainly for meat traits.

Ruminant Animal

Cattle are classified as ruminants because they possess a four-compartment stomach and chew cud.

  • Rumen
  • Reticulum
  • Omasum
  • Abomasum
Cattle are ruminant animals.

Production Systems

Extensive System

Extensive production system involves raising cattle in large pasture areas with minimal supplementation and lower management intensity.

Extensive system = pasture-based production.

Intensive System

Intensive production system involves confinement feeding, close management, and higher input levels to maximize production.

Intensive system = confinement and high-input production.
