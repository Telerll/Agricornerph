Marketing

Marketing refers to the different activities and services involved in transferring products from producers to final consumers.

It developed because of specialization in production and trading activities.

Market

A market is a place where buyers and sellers exchange products and services.

It may also refer to a group of buyers and sellers with facilities that allow trading activities to occur.

A market can also be a wide geographic area where supply and demand interact to determine prices.

Elements of a Market

1. Buyers

a. Ultimate Buyers

These are consumers who buy products for personal use and satisfaction.

b. Industrial Buyers

These buyers purchase products for resale or for conversion into another form.

2. Sellers

Sellers are individuals or businesses that provide products and services.

3. Trading Facilities

These are facilities or systems that support exchange and trading activities.

Point of Production

This refers to the place of first sale by farmers, commonly at the farm or farmer’s residence.

Point of Consumption

This is the stage where marketing activities end or where the final purchase occurs.

Services

Services are activities performed on products that change their form, place, time, or characteristics.

These services add value to products and involve corresponding costs.

Some services involve physical handling such as transportation, packaging, and processing.

Other services may not directly involve physical handling, such as advertising and assumption of risks.

Production Services

These are services added before the point of production.

Marketing Services

These are services added after the point of production.

The Marketing Services

  1. Processing
  2. Transportation
  3. Storage
  4. Buying and Selling
