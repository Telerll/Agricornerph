❤️ Circulatory System of Farm Animals

Circulatory System

Circulatory system is the body system responsible for transporting blood, nutrients, gases, hormones, and wastes throughout the body.

Functions of the Circulatory System

Transport

Transport is the movement of oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products in the body.

Gas Exchange

Gas exchange is the transport of oxygen (O₂) to tissues and carbon dioxide (CO₂) away from tissues.

Protection

Protection is the defense against disease through white blood cells.

Regulation

Regulation is the control of body temperature, pH, and fluid balance.

Components of the Circulatory System

Heart

Heart is a muscular organ that pumps blood throughout the body.

Blood Vessels

Blood vessels are tubes that carry blood throughout the body.

Blood

Blood is the fluid that transports substances in the body.

Structure of the Heart

Right Atrium

Right atrium receives deoxygenated blood from the body.

Right Ventricle

Right ventricle pumps blood to the lungs.

Left Atrium

Left atrium receives oxygenated blood from the lungs.

Left Ventricle

Left ventricle pumps oxygenated blood to the body.

Heart Valves

Tricuspid Valve

Tricuspid valve is located between the right atrium and right ventricle.

It prevents backflow of blood.

Mitral (Bicuspid) Valve

Mitral valve is located between the left atrium and left ventricle.

It prevents backflow of blood.

Heart valves ensure one-way flow of blood.

Blood Vessels

Arteries

Arteries are blood vessels that carry blood away from the heart.

They usually carry oxygenated blood.

Veins

Veins are blood vessels that carry blood toward the heart.

They usually carry deoxygenated blood.

Capillaries

Capillaries are very small blood vessels where exchange of gases, nutrients, and wastes occurs.

Important Exception

  • Pulmonary artery carries deoxygenated blood to the lungs.
  • Pulmonary vein carries oxygenated blood to the heart.
Pulmonary artery = deoxygenated
Pulmonary vein = oxygenated

Blood Composition

Plasma

Plasma is the liquid portion of blood that carries nutrients and wastes.

Red Blood Cells (RBC)

Red blood cells transport oxygen using hemoglobin.

White Blood Cells (WBC)

White blood cells defend the body against infection.

Platelets

Platelets help in blood clotting.

Types of Circulation

Pulmonary Circulation

Pulmonary circulation is the movement of blood between the heart and lungs.

Systemic Circulation

Systemic circulation is the movement of blood between the heart and the rest of the body.

Blood Flow Pathway

Body → Right Atrium → Tricuspid Valve → Right Ventricle → Lungs → Left Atrium → Mitral Valve → Left Ventricle → Body

Heartbeat and Pulse

Heartbeat

Heartbeat is the rhythmic contraction and relaxation of the heart.

Pulse

Pulse is the expansion of arteries caused by the heartbeat.

Oxygen Transport

Oxygen is carried by hemoglobin in red blood cells.

Carbon dioxide is transported back to the lungs for removal.

Importance in Animal Science

  • Supplies nutrients to tissues
  • Removes waste products
  • Supports growth and production
  • Important in animal health and performance

Circulatory System in Livestock Production

Efficient circulation improves growth rate, feed efficiency, and nutrient distribution.

About 400–500 liters of blood must pass through the udder to produce 1 liter of milk.

Exam Memory Tricks

Arteries = Away from heart
Veins = Toward heart
Capillaries = Exchange point

Final Summary

Circulatory system = transport system

Heart = pump
Blood = transport fluid
Vessels = pathways

RBC = oxygen
WBC = defense
Platelets = clotting
