Animal Breeding and Genetics

Animal Breeding and Genetics focuses on heredity, inheritance, genetic improvement, breeding systems, and the scientific principles used to improve livestock productivity, performance, and profitability across generations.

Overview

This section covers the genetic foundation of animal improvement and the practical breeding systems used in livestock production. It explains how traits are inherited, how animals differ, how breeders select superior animals, and how breeding decisions improve future generations.

The first topic focuses on Animal Genetics, which explains heredity, genes, chromosomes, inheritance, and variation. The second topic focuses on Animal Breeding, which applies genetic principles in practical livestock improvement through selection, mating systems, and breeding methods.

Topics Covered

Animal Genetics

Covers heredity, variation, genes, DNA, chromosomes, Mendelian and non-Mendelian inheritance, sex determination, chromosome numbers, gene action, mutation, heritability, and genetic principles used in animal science.

Animal Breeding
