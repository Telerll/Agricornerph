Animal Breeding and Genetics

This part covers the scientific foundation of animal improvement, including heredity, inheritance, variation, genetic principles, selection, breeding systems, and practical livestock improvement.

Overview

Animal Breeding and Genetics explains how traits are inherited and how those inherited traits can be improved through planned selection and mating. This part is divided into two major subtopics: Animal Genetics and Animal Breeding.

Animal Genetics focuses on genes, DNA, chromosomes, Mendelian and non-Mendelian inheritance, sex determination, chromosome numbers, mutation, heritability, and variation. Animal Breeding focuses on selection, culling, mating systems, breeding value, progeny testing, crossbreeding, heterosis, and modern breeding technologies.

Topics Covered

Animal Genetics

Study heredity, variation, genes, DNA, chromosomes, genotype, phenotype, Mendelian inheritance, non-Mendelian inheritance, sex chromosomes, chromosome numbers, heritability, mutation, and genetic board traps.

Animal Breeding

Study selection, culling, breeding systems, purebreeding, inbreeding, linebreeding, outcrossing, crossbreeding, heterosis, grading up, progeny testing, EBV, AI, ET, genomics, and breeding board traps.
