Animal Breeding and Genetics

Animal Breeding and Genetics focuses on heredity, inheritance, genetic improvement, and breeding systems used to improve livestock productivity, performance, and profitability across generations.

Overview

This section explains the scientific foundation of animal improvement. It covers how traits are inherited, why animals differ, how superior animals are selected, and how breeding systems improve future generations.

Animal Genetics focuses on heredity, genes, chromosomes, inheritance, and variation. Animal Breeding focuses on selection, culling, mating systems, and practical genetic improvement in livestock production.

Topics Covered

Animal Genetics

Covers heredity, variation, genes, DNA, chromosomes, Mendelian and non-Mendelian inheritance, sex determination, chromosome numbers, gene action, mutation, heritability, and genetic principles used in animal science.

Animal Breeding

Covers breeding principles, selection, culling, breeding systems, inbreeding, crossbreeding, heterosis, pedigree, progeny testing, breeding value, selection methods, and modern breeding technologies.
