Beef Cattle Production Final Integration

Introduction

Beef cattle production refers to the raising and management of cattle primarily for meat production.

Beef cattle are raised mainly for meat production.

Importance of Beef Cattle

  • Food supply
  • Nutrition
  • Livelihood
  • Agricultural economy
  • Leather and hide
  • Manure
Beef cattle contribute to food security and economy.

Characteristics of Beef Cattle

  • Large body size
  • Fast growth
  • Muscular body
  • Efficient feed conversion
  • Good carcass traits
Beef cattle are selected for meat production traits.

Parts of Beef Cattle

  • Muzzle
  • Forehead
  • Horn
  • Neck
  • Withers
  • Brisket
  • Back
  • Loin
  • Rump
  • Flank
  • Udder
  • Tail
  • Hoof
Knowledge of body parts is important in cattle evaluation.

Major Beef Cattle Breeds

Hereford

Red body with white face; known as a hardy beef breed.

Hereford = white face breed.

Angus

Naturally polled; commonly black or red; known for excellent meat quality.

Angus = naturally hornless breed.

Brahman

Has hump over shoulder, loose skin, and strong heat tolerance.

Brahman = hump + heat tolerance.

Shorthorn

Dual-purpose breed; may be red, white, or roan.

Shorthorn = dual-purpose breed.

Charolais

White or cream colored breed with large muscular body.

Charolais = large white breed.

Production Systems

Extensive System

Animals graze over large pasture areas with minimal inputs.

Extensive system uses large grazing land.

Semi-Intensive System

Combination of grazing and supplemental feeding.

Semi-intensive combines grazing and feeding.

Intensive System

Animals are confined and provided with complete feeds.

Intensive system uses confinement feeding.

Feedlot System

Uses high-energy rations for rapid fattening.

Feedlot system improves weight gain.

Feeding and Nutrition

Major Nutrients

  • Water
  • Carbohydrates
  • Protein
  • Fats
  • Minerals
  • Vitamins
Nutrients support growth and production.

Water

Water is the most essential nutrient.

Water is essential for life.

Roughages

Bulky feeds high in fiber such as grasses, hay, and silage.

Roughages are high-fiber feeds.

Concentrates

Feeds rich in digestible nutrients such as corn, rice bran, and soybean meal.

Concentrates are nutrient-dense feeds.

Hay and Silage

  • Hay = dried preserved forage
  • Silage = fermented forage preserved under anaerobic conditions
Hay = dried forage; Silage = fermented forage.

Grazing and Rotational Grazing

Grazing allows cattle to feed directly on pasture, while rotational grazing moves cattle from one pasture area to another to prevent overgrazing.

Rotational grazing prevents overgrazing.

Breeding and Reproduction

Cattle Terms

  • Bull = mature intact male cattle
  • Cow = mature female cattle
  • Heifer = young female cattle
  • Steer = castrated male cattle
  • Calf = young cattle
Proper terminology is important in boards.

Reproductive Terms

  • Estrus = heat period
  • Estrous cycle = about 21 days
  • Ovulation = release of egg
  • Gestation period = about 283 days
  • Calving = parturition in cattle
Gestation period in cattle = 283 days.

Artificial Insemination

Artificial introduction of semen into the female reproductive tract.

AI improves genetics.

Pregnancy Diagnosis

  • Rectal palpation
  • Ultrasound
Pregnancy diagnosis confirms conception.

Colostrum and Weaning

  • Colostrum = first milk rich in antibodies
  • Weaning = separation of calf from dam
Colostrum provides passive immunity.

Health Management and Common Diseases

Health Management

  • Vaccination
  • Deworming
  • Biosecurity
  • Quarantine
  • Sanitation
Proper health management improves productivity.

Common Diseases

  • Anthrax = caused by Bacillus anthracis
  • Foot-and-mouth disease = highly contagious viral disease
  • Rabies = viral disease affecting nervous system
  • Mastitis = inflammation of udder
  • Bloat = gas accumulation in rumen
  • Foot rot = infectious hoof disease
  • Liver fluke = internal parasite affecting liver
  • Mange = skin disease caused by mites
Disease control reduces economic loss.

General Management Practices

Identification Methods

  • Ear tagging
  • Branding
  • Tattooing
Identification improves record keeping.

Common Management Practices

  • Castration = removal of testes
  • Dehorning = removal of horns
  • Sanitation = cleanliness of facilities
  • Record keeping = breeding, health, and feeding records
  • Animal welfare = humane treatment and proper care
Good management increases profitability.

Housing

Good housing should be clean, dry, ventilated, and spacious.

Proper housing improves welfare.
