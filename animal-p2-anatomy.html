📘 Anatomy & Physiology (Basic Concepts)

Anatomy

Anatomy is the study of the form and structure of the body and its parts.

Physiology

Physiology is the study of the function of body parts and organ systems.

It explains how organs work and how life processes happen.

Homeostasis

Homeostasis is the maintenance of a stable internal environment in the body.

It keeps body conditions normal despite changes outside the body.

External Anatomy

External anatomy refers to body structures found outside the body that are visible or form part of the outer body covering.

Includes:

  • Protective covering such as feathers, hair, horns, and skin
  • Digestive openings such as mouth, beak, bill, snout, and muzzle
  • Sense organs such as skin, nostrils, eyes, and ears
  • Locomotory parts such as feet, wings, and fins

Internal Anatomy

Internal anatomy refers to the organs and organ systems found inside the body.

These organs perform functions related to survival, growth, and reproduction.

Main Internal Organs Commonly Found In:

  • Thoracic cavity – lungs and heart
  • Abdominal cavity – stomach, small intestine, pancreas, liver, spleen, large intestine, kidneys, and ovaries
  • Pelvic cavity – reproductive organs

Levels of Body Organization

Cell

Cell is the basic unit of life.

It is the smallest living part of the body that can perform life functions.

Tissue

Tissue is a group of similar cells that perform a common function.

Organ

Organ is a structure made up of different tissues working together to perform a specific function.

Organ System

Organ system is a group of organs working together to carry out major body functions.

Basic Terms Used in Animal Physiology

Parturition

Parturition is the act of giving birth.

Calving

Calving is parturition in cows.

Farrowing

Farrowing is parturition in sows.

Foaling

Foaling is parturition in mares.

Kidding

Kidding is parturition in goats.

Lambing

Lambing is parturition in ewes.

Conception

Conception is the act of fertilization.

Dam

Dam is the female parent.

Fecundity / Prolificacy

Fecundity or prolificacy is the ability to produce offspring.

Fertility

Fertility is the ability to produce fertile ova and to support proper development for cell division and embryonic growth.

Gestation

Gestation is the period from conception to birth.

Lactation

Lactation is the production of milk.

Ovulation

Ovulation is the release of the ovum or egg cell.

Puberty

Puberty is sexual maturity, shown by the first heat or ovulation.

Estrus

Estrus is the period when the female is sexually receptive.

Sterility

Sterility is the inability to produce a normal young.

Transmission Ability

Transmission ability is the ability of an animal to pass on desirable or undesirable traits to its offspring.

Weaning

Weaning is the process of separating the young from its dam.

Why Anatomy and Physiology Are Important

  • Help us understand the structure and function of the animal body
  • Help in proper feeding, breeding, and management
  • Help identify body systems related to growth, reproduction, and health
  • Serve as the foundation for studying the major organ systems
Anatomy = structure
Physiology = function
