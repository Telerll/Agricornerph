🧪 Soil Science Review

Review the core concepts of soil science including soil formation, horizons, texture, structure, pH, and water-holding capacity.

1. Soil Formation

div class="section-box">

Soil is formed through the interaction of five factors known as CLORPT:

  • C - Climate
  • O - Organisms
  • R - Relief
  • P - Parent Material
  • T - Time
Tip: CLORPT is one of the most basic and important concepts in Soil Science.

2. Soil Horizons

Soil is arranged into layers called horizons:

  • O Horizon - rich in organic matter
  • A Horizon - topsoil
  • B Horizon - subsoil
  • C Horizon - parent material
Remember: O = organic, A = topsoil, B = subsoil, C = parent material.

3. Soil Texture

Soil texture refers to the proportion of sand, silt, and clay.

  • Sand - largest particles, fast drainage
  • Silt - medium particles, smooth feel
  • Clay - smallest particles, high water-holding capacity
Exam Tip: Clay holds the most water, while sandy soil drains the fastest.

4. Soil Structure

Soil structure refers to the arrangement of soil particles into aggregates.

  • Granular
  • Blocky
  • Prismatic
  • Platy

Good soil structure improves aeration, water infiltration, and root growth.

5. Soil pH

Soil pH measures how acidic or alkaline the soil is.

  • pH 7 = neutral
  • Below 7 = acidic
  • Above 7 = alkaline
Quick Recall: Neutral soil pH = 7

6. Soil Color

Soil color may be affected by:

  • Organic matter
  • Moisture content
  • Iron compounds

Dark soils often contain more organic matter.
