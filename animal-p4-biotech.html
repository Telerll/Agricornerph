Biotechnology in Animal Breeding

Biotechnology in animal breeding is the application of scientific and laboratory techniques to improve animal reproduction, genetics, and production.

It uses biological tools to accelerate genetic improvement beyond conventional breeding.

Biotechnology improves breeding faster and more precisely.

Importance of Biotechnology

  • Accelerate genetic improvement
  • Improve reproductive efficiency
  • Multiply superior animals faster
  • Improve disease resistance
  • Support modern livestock production
Biotechnology increases speed and precision of breeding.

Major Biotechnologies in Animal Breeding

  • Artificial Insemination (AI)
  • Estrus Synchronization

Biotechnology improves both reproduction and selection.

Artificial Insemination (AI)

Artificial insemination (AI) is the deposition of semen into the female reproductive tract by artificial means instead of natural mating.

It allows one superior sire to breed many females without direct mating.

AI allows rapid spread of superior male genetics.

Estrus Synchronization

Estrus synchronization is the control of the estrous cycle so females come into heat at the same time.

This makes breeding and artificial insemination easier to schedule and manage.

Synchronization makes breeding more efficient.

Embryo Transfer (ET)

Embryo transfer (ET) is the collection of embryos from a superior donor female and transfer to recipient females.

This allows one elite female to produce more offspring than normal.

ET spreads superior female genetics.

MOET

Multiple Ovulation Embryo Transfer (MOET) stimulates a superior female to release many eggs, which are fertilized and transferred to recipient females.

This greatly increases the reproductive output of elite dams.

MOET multiplies elite female genetics rapidly.

In Vitro Fertilization (IVF)

IVF is fertilization of egg and sperm outside the body under laboratory conditions.

The embryo is later transferred into a recipient female.

IVF allows embryo production in the laboratory.

Sexed Semen

Sexed semen is semen processed to increase the probability of producing offspring of a desired sex.

This is useful when producers prefer females (dairy) or males (beef).

Sexed semen controls offspring sex probability.

Cloning

Cloning is the production of a genetically identical copy of an animal.

It is used to replicate elite animals with valuable genetics.

Cloning produces genetic copies.

Marker-Assisted Selection (MAS)

MAS uses DNA markers linked to desirable traits to help identify superior animals early.

It improves selection accuracy before full performance is expressed.

MAS selects animals using DNA clues.

Genomic Selection

Genomic selection uses thousands of DNA markers across the genome to predict genetic merit.

It is faster and more accurate than conventional selection.

Genomic selection predicts genetic merit earlier and more accurately.

Gene Editing and CRISPR

Gene editing is the direct modification of DNA to alter specific traits.

CRISPR is a precise tool used to cut and modify DNA at specific target sites.

This allows correction of undesirable genes or introduction of useful traits such as disease resistance.

CRISPR directly targets specific genetic problems.

Biotechnology and Genetic Gain

Biotechnology improves genetic gain by:

  • Shortening generation interval
  • Improving selection accuracy
  • Multiplying superior animals faster
  • Increasing selection intensity
Biotechnology accelerates genetic progress.
