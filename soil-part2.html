🌧 Part 2: Weathering & Soil Formation

Rocks

  • Rocks are natural aggregates of minerals.
  • They serve as the parent material of soils.
  • The study of rocks is called Petrology.

General Classification of Rocks

1. Igneous Rocks

  • Formed from the cooling and solidification of molten magma.
  • Examples: Granite, Basalt

2. Sedimentary Rocks

  • Formed from deposited and compacted weathered materials.
  • Examples: Sandstone, Limestone

3. Metamorphic Rocks

  • Formed when rocks are changed by heat and pressure.
  • Examples: Marble, Slate

Modes of Rock Formation

1. Extrusive (Volcanic)

  • Magma reaches the surface and cools rapidly.
  • Usually forms fine-grained rocks.

2. Intrusive (Plutonic)

  • Magma cools slowly beneath the Earth's surface.
  • Usually forms coarse-grained rocks.

Weathering

  • Weathering is the breakdown of rocks and minerals at or near the Earth's surface.
  • It is the first step in soil formation.

Types of Weathering

1. Physical Weathering

  • Breakdown of rocks into smaller pieces without changing chemical composition.
  • Caused by temperature changes, freezing and thawing, and pressure changes.

2. Chemical Weathering

  • Breakdown of rocks accompanied by chemical changes in minerals.
  • Leads to the formation of new minerals and soluble substances.

Processes of Chemical Weathering

  • Hydrolysis – reaction of minerals with water
  • Oxidation – reaction with oxygen
  • Solution – dissolution of minerals in water
  • Hydration – absorption of water by minerals
  • Carbonation – reaction with carbon dioxide in water

Stages of Weathering

  • Disintegration of rocks
  • Formation of smaller particles
  • Development of soil materials
  • Formation of soil horizons over time

Soil Formation

  • Soil is formed from the combined effects of weathering and soil-forming factors.
  • The process of soil formation is called Pedogenesis.

Factors of Soil Formation (CLORPT)

  • C – Climate
  • L – Living Organisms
  • O – Relief or Topography
  • R – Parent Material
  • P – sometimes represented in sequence as part of parent material concept
  • T – Time
Remember: CLORPT = Climate, Organisms, Relief, Parent Material, Time

Importance of Soil Formation Factors

  • Climate affects temperature and rainfall.
  • Organisms contribute organic matter and biological activity.
  • Relief affects drainage, erosion, and soil depth.
  • Parent Material determines initial mineral composition.
  • Time allows soil horizons to develop.
