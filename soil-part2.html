🌧 Part 2: Weathering & Soil Formation

Rocks

  • Rocks are natural aggregates of one or more minerals.
  • They serve as the parent material of soils.
  • The study of rocks is called Petrology.
  • Weathering of rocks leads to the formation of soil particles.

Classification of Rocks

1. Igneous Rocks

  • Formed from the cooling and solidification of molten magma.
  • Primary source of most minerals in soil.

Types of Igneous Rocks

  • Intrusive (Plutonic) – magma cools slowly beneath the Earth's surface → coarse texture
  • Extrusive (Volcanic) – lava cools rapidly on the surface → fine texture

Examples

  • Granite – coarse-grained, light colored
  • Basalt – fine-grained, dark colored
Igneous rocks = primary rocks (source of soil minerals)

Sedimentary Rocks

  • Formed from the accumulation, compaction, and cementation of weathered rock materials.
  • Often layered or stratified.
  • May contain fossils.

Types of Sedimentary Rocks

  • Clastic – formed from rock fragments (e.g., sandstone)
  • Chemical – formed from precipitation of minerals (e.g., limestone)
  • Organic – formed from plant/animal remains

Examples

  • Sandstone
  • Limestone
  • Shale

Metamorphic Rocks

  • Formed when existing rocks are altered by heat, pressure, or chemical processes.
  • Do not melt, but change in structure and composition.

Examples

  • Marble (from limestone)
  • Slate (from shale)
Metamorphic = changed form due to heat and pressure

Importance of Rocks in Soil Formation

  • Determine soil mineral composition.
  • Influence soil fertility.
  • Affect soil texture and structure.
  • Control rate of weathering and soil development.
