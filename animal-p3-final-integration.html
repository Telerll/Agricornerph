Part 3 Final Integration

The Master Concept

Animal Nutrition is not just feeding animals.

It is the science of converting feed into:

  • Growth
  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Reproduction
  • Profit
Animal nutrition converts feed into performance and profit.

The Golden Rule

Animals will always follow this priority:

Maintenance → Production

Animals will always:

  • Survive first
  • Produce second

If nutrients are not enough:

  • No growth
  • No milk
  • No eggs

The Nutrition Flow

Feed → Digestion → Absorption → Metabolism → Production

Step 1: Feed

Feed contains nutrients:

  • Energy
  • Protein
  • Vitamins
  • Minerals
  • Water

Step 2: Digestion

Feed is broken down by enzymes such as:

  • Amylase
  • Pepsin
  • Lipase

Step 3: Absorption

Nutrients are absorbed mainly in the small intestine through villi and microvilli.

Step 4: Metabolism

Absorbed nutrients are converted into energy, body tissues, or production outputs.

Step 5: Production

  • Growth
  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Reproduction
If one step fails, production drops.

The 6 Essential Nutrients

Animals need six essential nutrients:

  • Water
  • Carbohydrates
  • Fats
  • Protein
  • Vitamins
  • Minerals
Lack of one nutrient reduces performance.

Energy is the #1 Limiting Factor

Energy controls:

  • Growth
  • Milk yield
  • Egg production

Main sources:

  • Carbohydrates
  • Fats (2.25× more energy)
No energy = no production.

Protein = Growth Builder

Protein is needed for:

  • Muscle
  • Enzymes
  • Hormones

Key board concept:

  • Limiting amino acids = Lysine and Methionine
If one essential amino acid is lacking, the rest cannot be fully used.

Minerals and Vitamins = Regulators

They regulate:

  • Bone formation
  • Metabolism
  • Immunity
  • Reproduction

Key board facts:

  • Calcium : Phosphorus = 2 : 1
  • Fat-soluble vitamins = KADE
Vitamins and minerals regulate body function.

Water = The Master Nutrient

Water controls:

  • Intake
  • Digestion
  • Temperature
  • Production
Animals die faster without water than without feed.

Digestion Type Matters

Monogastric

  • Simple stomach
  • Relies mainly on enzymes

Ruminant

  • Four stomach compartments
  • Uses microbial fermentation
  • Can use NPN such as urea

Hindgut Fermenter

  • Fermentation occurs in cecum
  • Example: rabbit
  • Coprophagy occurs
Digestion system determines feeding strategy.

Feed Classification

Roughage

  • High fiber
  • Low energy

Concentrates

  • High energy or protein
  • Low fiber
Proper balance between roughage and concentrates is essential.

Ration Formulation

The goal of ration formulation is to meet nutrient requirements at the lowest possible cost.

Common tools:

  • Pearson Square
  • Trial and Error

Feeding systems:

  • Ad libitum = free choice feeding
  • Restricted feeding = controlled intake
Good ration formulation balances nutrition and cost.

Feeding Standards

Animals need nutrients for:

  • Maintenance
  • Growth
  • Reproduction
  • Lactation
  • Work

Requirements change depending on:

  • Age
  • Species
  • Environment
  • Production stage
Feeding must match the animal’s requirement.

Feed Additives and Supplements

Additives

Improve feed performance.

  • Enzymes
  • Probiotics

Supplements

Supply nutrients.

  • Vitamins
  • Minerals
Additives optimize feed, supplements correct nutrition.

Water and Production Link

Less water → Less feed → Less growth → Less production

Water shortage is one of the fastest causes of production decline.

Board Exam High-Yield Concepts

  • Fats = 2.25× more energy
  • Lysine and Methionine = limiting amino acids
  • Calcium : Phosphorus = 2 : 1
  • Urea = ruminants only
  • Silage = lactic acid fermentation
  • Probiotics vs Prebiotics
  • Phase feeding
  • Negative energy balance

The Business Mindset

Feed accounts for about 60–70% of total production cost.

If you master nutrition, you can:

  • Control cost
  • Improve efficiency
  • Maximize profit
Right nutrition = maximum performance + maximum profit.
