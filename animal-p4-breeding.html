Selection and Breeding Value

Selection

Selection is the process of choosing superior animals to become parents of the next generation.

Its purpose is to improve the genetic quality of future offspring.

Selection is the foundation of genetic improvement.

Importance of Selection

Selection is important because it:

  • Increases productivity
  • Improves efficiency
  • Enhances profitability
  • Removes inferior animals from breeding
Selection improves the herd genetically.

Basis of Selection

Animals are selected based on:

  • Phenotype
  • Pedigree
  • Performance records
  • Progeny performance
  • Breeding value
Selection uses records to identify superior genetics.

Phenotypic Selection

Phenotypic selection is selection based on observable traits.

Examples:

  • Body size
  • Growth rate
  • Udder conformation
  • Coat quality
Phenotype is the simplest basis of selection.

Pedigree Selection

Pedigree selection is selection based on the performance of ancestors.

Used when:

  • The trait is difficult to measure early
  • The trait is sex-limited
  • The animal is still young
Pedigree helps predict genetic potential.

Performance Selection

Performance selection is selection based on the animal’s own production record.

Examples:

  • Milk yield
  • Egg production
  • Body weight
  • Feed efficiency
Own performance reflects production ability.

Progeny Testing

Progeny testing is selection based on the performance of offspring.

It is commonly used in sires to evaluate:

  • Milk production
  • Growth
  • Carcass quality

It is one of the most accurate methods of selection, but it increases generation interval because breeders must wait for offspring to grow and perform.

Offspring performance reveals parent genetic merit.

Family Selection

Family selection is selection based on the average performance of relatives.

Examples:

  • Littermates
  • Full siblings
  • Half siblings
Relatives help estimate genetic value.

Mass Selection

Mass selection is selection based on individual phenotype only.

It is simple and commonly used in livestock improvement.

Mass selection is fast and practical.

Independent Culling Levels

Independent culling levels is a method where animals must meet minimum standards in several traits.

Animals failing one trait are rejected.

One poor trait can eliminate an animal.

Selection Index

Selection index combines several traits into one numerical value for ranking animals.

Traits may include:

  • Growth
  • Fertility
  • Milk yield
  • Feed efficiency

It is one of the most efficient methods for improving several traits at the same time.

Selection index is the most efficient multi-trait selection method.

Breeding Value

Breeding value (BV) is the genetic worth of an animal as a parent.

It estimates the value of genes the animal can pass to offspring.

Breeding value predicts genetic usefulness.

Estimated Breeding Value

Estimated Breeding Value (EBV) is the predicted breeding value calculated using records and statistical methods.

It estimates the animal’s genetic merit in practical breeding.

In some industries, especially beef and swine, Expected Progeny Difference (EPD) is also used.

EPD = ½ EBV

This is because a parent passes only half of its genes to its offspring.
EBV estimates parent merit.
EPD predicts offspring performance.

Phenotype vs Breeding Value

Phenotype is what the animal shows.

Breeding value is what the animal can pass genetically.

Good appearance does not always mean good genetics.

Accuracy of Selection

Selection accuracy depends on:

  • Heritability
  • Quality of records
  • Number of records
  • Quality of pedigree
  • Progeny information

As the number of records and progeny increases, the accuracy of EBV becomes higher and may approach 1.0.

Better records = better selection decisions.

Selection Intensity

Selection intensity refers to how strict selection is.

The fewer animals selected, the greater the intensity.

Stricter selection = greater genetic gain.

Genetic Gain

Genetic gain is the improvement achieved through selection.

It depends on:

  • Variation
  • Heritability
  • Selection intensity
  • Generation interval
Stronger selection improves progress.

Culling

Culling is the removal of inferior animals from the breeding population.

Reasons for culling:

  • Poor fertility
  • Low production
  • Poor health
  • Undesirable traits
Culling removes poor genetics.

Selection in Practice

In practical breeding, animals are selected to:

  • Improve production
  • Improve efficiency
  • Improve health
  • Improve adaptability
Practical selection balances production and function.

Board Exam Master Concept

Selection identifies superior animals.

Breeding value predicts genetic worth.

Together, they drive genetic improvement.

Selection chooses. Breeding value predicts.
