📊 Part 6: Farm Practices & Management

Farm Practices

  • Farm practices are the activities carried out to ensure good crop growth and production.
  • These include land preparation, planting, irrigation, fertilization, weeding, and harvesting.

Land Preparation

  • Land preparation makes the soil suitable for planting.
  • It helps control weeds, improve soil aeration, and provide a good seedbed.

Common Steps

  • Clearing
  • Plowing
  • Harrowing
  • Leveling

Planting

  • Planting is the placement of seeds or seedlings in the field.
  • Proper spacing, depth, and timing are important for good establishment.

Water Management

  • Water is essential for crop growth and development.
  • Proper irrigation ensures adequate moisture for the crop.
  • Drainage is also important to prevent waterlogging.

Nutrient Management

  • Includes the use of organic and inorganic fertilizers.
  • Helps provide the nutrients needed for healthy plant growth.
  • Should be based on crop needs and soil condition.

Weed Management

  • Weeds compete with crops for light, water, nutrients, and space.
  • Control methods include:
    • Manual weeding
    • Mechanical control
    • Cultural methods
    • Chemical control

Pest and Disease Management

  • Pests and diseases can reduce crop yield and quality.
  • Management includes prevention, monitoring, and control measures.
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM) is recommended.

Harvesting

  • Harvesting is the collection of mature crop produce.
  • It should be done at the proper stage of maturity.
  • Proper harvesting reduces losses and preserves quality.

Postharvest Handling

  • Includes cleaning, sorting, grading, storage, and transport.
  • Proper handling helps maintain quality and reduces spoilage.

Farm Management

  • Farm management is the process of planning, organizing, directing, and controlling farm resources.
  • It aims to achieve maximum productivity and profit.

Functions of Farm Management

  • Planning – deciding what to do and how to do it
  • Organizing – arranging resources efficiently
  • Directing – guiding farm operations
  • Controlling – checking if goals are achieved
Good farm management = efficient production + higher profit
