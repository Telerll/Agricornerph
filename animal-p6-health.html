Poultry Health and Disease Management

Poultry Health and Disease Management

Poultry health and disease management is the branch of poultry science concerned with maintaining bird health, preventing disease, controlling infections, and improving flock performance.

It includes health management, disease prevention, sanitation, vaccination, biosecurity, diagnosis, treatment, and parasite control.

Poultry health management protects flock productivity and survival.

Poultry Health

Poultry health refers to the state of physical well-being and normal body function in birds.

  • Active and alert
  • Feeding well
  • Growing normally
  • Free from disease
Healthy birds are active, productive, and disease-free.

Disease

Disease is any abnormal condition that disrupts normal body function and reduces health or productivity.

Disease may affect growth, egg production, feed efficiency, reproduction, and survival.

Disease reduces health and productivity.

Causes of Disease

  • Bacteria
  • Viruses
  • Fungi
  • Parasites
  • Nutritional deficiencies
  • Environmental stress
  • Toxins
Disease may be infectious or non-infectious.

Infectious and Non-Infectious Disease

Infectious disease is caused by pathogenic microorganisms and can spread between birds.

Non-infectious disease is caused by factors such as nutrition, environment, toxins, and management.

Infectious diseases spread; non-infectious diseases do not spread bird to bird.

Signs of Healthy Birds

  • Bright eyes
  • Clean nostrils
  • Smooth feathers
  • Active movement
  • Good appetite
  • Normal droppings
Healthy birds are active and alert.

Signs of Sick Birds

  • Dullness
  • Ruffled feathers
  • Drooping wings
  • Poor appetite
  • Diarrhea
  • Coughing
  • Nasal discharge
  • Weakness
Sick birds show poor activity and abnormal behavior.

Disease Prevention

Disease prevention is more effective and cheaper than treatment.

  • Sanitation
  • Vaccination
  • Biosecurity
  • Proper nutrition
  • Good ventilation
Prevention is the best disease control strategy.

Sanitation and Disinfection

Sanitation is the maintenance of cleanliness in poultry houses, equipment, and surroundings.

Disinfection is the use of chemicals to destroy disease-causing microorganisms.

Sanitation reduces disease organisms; disinfection kills pathogens.

Biosecurity

Biosecurity is the set of preventive measures used to prevent disease from entering or spreading in a flock.

  • Restrict visitors
  • Disinfect footwear
  • Isolate new birds
  • Control rodents
  • Clean equipment
Biosecurity prevents disease entry and spread.

Vaccination

Vaccination is the administration of vaccines to stimulate immunity against disease.

Vaccine is a biological preparation used to stimulate immunity.

Vaccination protects birds from specific diseases.

Immunity

Immunity is the ability of the bird to resist infection.

Types of immunity include natural immunity, acquired immunity, active immunity, and passive immunity.

Immunity protects birds from disease.

Diagnosis and Treatment

Diagnosis is the identification of disease based on signs, lesions, and tests.

Treatment is the use of medications or management practices to control disease after infection.

Diagnosis identifies disease; treatment controls disease after infection.

Isolation and Quarantine

Isolation is the separation of sick birds from healthy birds.

Quarantine is the restriction of movement of birds exposed to disease or newly introduced birds.

Isolation prevents spread; quarantine prevents introduction and spread.

Stress and Stressors

Stress is any factor that disturbs normal body function and reduces resistance.

Common stressors include heat, overcrowding, poor ventilation, poor nutrition, transport, and sudden management changes.

Stress lowers immunity and predisposes birds to disease.

Common Poultry Diseases

Common poultry diseases of economic importance include:

  • Newcastle disease
  • Fowl pox
  • Infectious bursal disease
  • Coccidiosis
  • Infectious bronchitis
  • Avian influenza
  • Marek’s disease
These diseases can reduce growth, production, and survival.

Newcastle Disease

Newcastle disease is a highly contagious viral disease affecting the respiratory, nervous, and digestive systems.

  • Coughing
  • Twisting of neck
  • Paralysis
  • Green diarrhea
Newcastle disease = twisting neck and respiratory signs.

Fowl Pox

Fowl pox is a viral disease causing wart-like lesions on the skin and mouth.

Fowl pox causes skin lesions.

Infectious Bursal Disease

Infectious bursal disease (IBD), also called Gumboro disease, is a viral disease affecting the bursa and immune system.

IBD damages immunity in young birds.

Coccidiosis

Coccidiosis is a protozoan disease affecting the intestines.

  • Bloody diarrhea
  • Poor growth
  • Weakness
Coccidiosis causes bloody diarrhea.

Infectious Bronchitis

Infectious bronchitis is a viral respiratory disease.

  • Coughing
  • Sneezing
  • Poor egg production
Infectious bronchitis affects respiration and egg production.

Avian Influenza

Avian influenza is a serious contagious viral disease affecting respiratory and systemic health.

Avian influenza is a major contagious viral disease.

Marek’s Disease

Marek’s disease is a viral disease causing paralysis and tumors.

Marek’s disease causes paralysis.

Parasites

Parasites are organisms that live on or inside the bird and cause harm.

  • External parasites = lice, mites, ticks
  • Internal parasites = roundworms, tapeworms, cecal worms
Parasites reduce health and performance.

Deworming

Deworming is the control of internal parasites using anti-parasitic drugs.

Deworming controls internal worms.

Mortality and Morbidity

Mortality is the death rate in a flock.

Morbidity is the rate of disease occurrence in a flock.

Mortality = death rate. Morbidity = disease rate.

Necropsy

Necropsy is the postmortem examination of a dead bird to determine cause of death.

Necropsy helps diagnose disease after death.

Board Exam Master Concept

Poultry health management focuses on prevention, sanitation, vaccination, biosecurity, and early diagnosis.

Prevention is the most effective disease control tool.
