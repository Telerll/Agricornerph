Common Characteristics of Cooperatives

Below are the expanded characteristics of cooperatives:

1. Common Interest Among Members
Members are united by at least one shared interest such as livelihood, farming, savings, credit, or business opportunities. This motivates them to work together.

2. Improvement of Economic and Social Conditions
Cooperatives aim to improve members’ quality of life by increasing income, reducing costs, and providing better services.

3. Joint Ownership and Control
The cooperative is owned and controlled by members equally, following “one member, one vote.”

4. Jointly Owned Enterprise
Members pool resources and use a common organization to provide goods and services efficiently.
