Common Characteristics of Cooperatives

Expanded Lesson Content
1. Common Interest Among Members
Members are united by at least one shared interest such as livelihood, farming, savings, credit, or business opportunities.
2. Improvement of Economic and Social Conditions
Cooperatives aim to improve members’ quality of life by increasing income, reducing costs, and providing better services.
3. Joint Ownership and Control
Cooperatives are owned and controlled equally by members following the principle of one member, one vote.
4. Jointly Owned Enterprise
Members pool resources to operate a common enterprise that provides goods and services efficiently.
