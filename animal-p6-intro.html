Introduction to Poultry Production

Poultry Production

Poultry production is the branch of animal science concerned with the breeding, raising, management, and utilization of domesticated birds for food, income, and other useful products.

It deals with the scientific production of birds for:

  • Meat
  • Eggs
  • Breeding stock
  • Feathers
  • Manure
  • Industrial by-products

Poultry production includes:

  • Breeding
  • Feeding
  • Housing
  • Health care
  • Production management
  • Marketing
Poultry production is the science and business of raising birds for food and profit.

Poultry

Poultry refers to domesticated birds raised under human care for economic and agricultural purposes.

These birds are kept mainly for:

  • Meat production
  • Egg production
  • Breeding and multiplication
  • Feathers and by-products
  • Income generation

Unlike wild birds, poultry are selected and managed for production efficiency.

Poultry are domesticated birds raised for useful economic purposes.

Importance of Poultry Production

Poultry production is one of the most important branches of livestock production because it contributes greatly to:

  • Human nutrition
  • Food supply
  • Employment
  • Household income
  • National economy

Poultry provides affordable and accessible animal protein in the form of meat and eggs.

It is especially important in developing countries because poultry can be raised in both:

  • Backyard systems
  • Commercial systems
Poultry plays a major role in food security and livelihood.

Poultry Industry

Poultry industry is the agricultural sector involved in the breeding, production, processing, distribution, and marketing of poultry and poultry products.

It includes:

  • Hatcheries
  • Breeder farms
  • Broiler farms
  • Layer farms
  • Feed mills
  • Poultry processing plants
  • Egg distribution systems
  • Poultry product marketing

The poultry industry is one of the fastest-growing sectors in animal agriculture because poultry are highly efficient and profitable.

Poultry industry covers the full chain from breeding to market.

Why Poultry is Economically Important

Poultry is economically important because:

  • Birds require less capital than large livestock
  • Birds need less space
  • Birds grow rapidly
  • Birds reproduce quickly
  • Birds give fast returns
  • Birds efficiently convert feed into food

Because of these advantages, poultry is often considered one of the most economical livestock enterprises.

Poultry is one of the most profitable and efficient livestock enterprises.

Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR)

Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) is the amount of feed required to produce one unit of body weight gain.

FCR = Feed Consumed ÷ Weight Gain

In modern broiler production, FCR is commonly around 1.5 to 1.7.

This means about 1.5–1.7 kg of feed produces 1 kg of live weight gain.

Poultry has one of the best feed conversion ratios in livestock.

Common Poultry Species

Common poultry species include:

  • Chicken
  • Duck
  • Turkey
  • Quail
  • Goose

These species differ in:

  • Body size
  • Growth rate
  • Production purpose
  • Management needs
Poultry includes several species raised for different purposes.

Chicken

Chicken is the most important and widely raised poultry species in the world.

It is the dominant poultry species in commercial production because it grows fast, reproduces efficiently, and adapts well to different production systems.

Chicken is raised mainly for:

  • Meat production or broilers
  • Egg production or layers
Chicken is the most economically important poultry species.

Broilers and Layers

Commercial chickens are not managed as true dual-purpose birds.

In commercial production:

  • Broilers are specialized for meat
  • Layers are specialized for eggs

These differ in:

  • Genetics
  • Nutrition
  • Growth rate
  • Management
  • Housing

Backyard chickens may be dual-purpose, but commercial poultry is highly specialized.

Broilers = meat birds. Layers = egg birds.

Duck

Duck is an important poultry species raised for both meat and eggs.

Ducks are hardy birds, tolerant to wet conditions, and more resistant to some diseases than chickens.

Two important duck types:

  • Pekin duck = common meat duck
  • Pateros duck = important native duck in the Philippines, widely used in balut production
Ducks are hardy birds raised for meat, eggs, and balut production.

Turkey

Turkey is a large poultry species mainly raised for meat production.

Turkeys grow larger than chickens and produce a higher meat yield per bird.

Turkey is a large poultry meat bird.

Quail

Quail is a small poultry species raised for both eggs and meat.

Quail are known for:

  • Rapid maturity
  • Small body size
  • Minimal space requirement
  • High egg productivity
Quail are small but highly productive birds.

Goose

Goose is raised mainly for:

  • Meat
  • Feathers
  • Specialty products

Geese are larger birds and are less common than chickens and ducks in commercial systems.

Geese provide meat, feathers, and specialty products.

Poultry Products

Poultry provide both primary and secondary products.

Primary products:

  • Eggs
  • Meat

Secondary products:

  • Feathers
  • Manure
  • Breeding stock
  • Industrial by-products
Poultry provides both food and useful by-products.

Eggs

Eggs are one of the most important poultry products and one of the most complete natural foods.

They are highly valued because they provide:

  • High-quality protein
  • Essential amino acids
  • Vitamins
  • Minerals
  • Energy

Egg production is one of the most important economic functions of poultry.

Eggs are one of the most valuable poultry products.

Poultry Meat

Poultry meat is one of the most widely consumed animal protein sources in the world.

It is popular because it is:

  • Affordable
  • Nutritious
  • Tender
  • Easy to cook
  • Widely available
Poultry meat is an important and affordable protein source.

Feathers

Feathers are poultry by-products used for:

  • Stuffing materials
  • Feather meal
  • Industrial applications

Although secondary in value, feathers still have economic use.

Feathers are useful poultry by-products.

Poultry Manure

Poultry manure is an important by-product used as:

  • Organic fertilizer
  • Soil conditioner
  • Nutrient source in agriculture

It has agricultural value because it is rich in nitrogen and minerals.

Poultry manure is a valuable agricultural by-product.

Characteristics of Poultry

Poultry have several biological and economic characteristics that make them efficient production animals:

  • Fast growth
  • Early maturity
  • High feed efficiency
  • High reproductive rate
  • Short generation interval
  • Efficient space utilization
Poultry are biologically efficient production animals.

Fast Growth

Poultry grow rapidly compared with many other livestock species.

This allows faster turnover and quicker meat production.

Poultry have rapid growth rate.

Early Maturity

Poultry reach market or reproductive age quickly.

This allows early egg production and faster income return.

Poultry mature early and produce quickly.

High Feed Efficiency

Poultry efficiently convert feed into meat or eggs.

This means less feed is needed per unit of product.

Poultry have efficient feed conversion.

High Reproductive Rate

Poultry reproduce quickly and produce many offspring within a short period.

Poultry multiply rapidly.

Short Generation Interval

Poultry have short production and breeding cycles, allowing faster genetic and production progress.

Poultry have short generation interval.

Photoperiodism

Photoperiodism is the response of poultry, especially layers, to light duration.

Light stimulates the pituitary gland, which influences reproductive hormones and egg production.

In layer production, artificial lighting is often used to maintain about 14–16 hours of light per day to support egg laying.

Light directly affects egg production in layers.

Commercial Poultry Strains

Modern commercial poultry production uses highly selected genetic strains.

Common broiler strains include:

  • Cobb
  • Arbor Acres
  • Ross

These strains are selected for:

  • Rapid growth
  • High feed efficiency
  • Good carcass yield
Commercial poultry relies on specialized genetic strains.
