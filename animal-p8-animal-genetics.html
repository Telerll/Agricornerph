Animal Genetics

Animal genetics is the branch of animal science that studies heredity, inheritance, variation, chromosomes, gene action, and transmission of traits in animals.

It explains how traits are passed from parents to offspring, why offspring resemble their parents, why variation occurs, and how inheritance affects productivity.

Animal genetics = study of heredity, inheritance, variation, chromosomes, and gene action in animals.

Importance of Animal Genetics

  • Explains inheritance of traits
  • Explains variation among animals
  • Helps identify desirable traits
  • Helps eliminate undesirable traits
  • Provides scientific basis for breeding
  • Improves animal productivity
Animal genetics is the scientific foundation of animal improvement.

Basic Genetic Concepts

  • Genetics = study of heredity and variation
  • Heredity = transmission of traits
  • Variation = differences among individuals
  • Genetic Variation = inherited variation
  • Environmental Variation = non-inherited variation
Genetics explains heredity and variation in animals.

Gene, DNA, and Chromosome

  • Gene = unit of heredity
  • DNA = genetic material
  • Chromosome = carries genes
  • Genome = complete genetic makeup
  • Locus = gene position
  • Allele = alternative gene form
Genes are carried by DNA, and DNA is organized into chromosomes.

DNA Structure

DNA is made of nucleotides composed of sugar, phosphate, and nitrogen bases.

  • A = Adenine
  • T = Thymine
  • G = Guanine
  • C = Cytosine

Base pairing:

  • A pairs with T
  • G pairs with C
DNA = double helix with A-T and G-C base pairing.

Genotype and Phenotype

  • Genotype = genetic makeup
  • Phenotype = observable trait
Genotype is inherited, phenotype is expressed.

Gene Action

  • Dominant = expressed
  • Recessive = hidden unless homozygous
  • Homozygous = same alleles
  • Heterozygous = different alleles
Gene action determines trait expression.

Mendelian Genetics

  • Gregor Mendel = Father of Genetics
  • Law of Segregation = allele separation
  • Law of Independent Assortment = traits assort independently
Mendel established the basic laws of inheritance.

Non-Mendelian Genetics

  • Incomplete Dominance = blended phenotype
  • Codominance = both expressed
  • Multiple Alleles = many gene forms
  • Lethal Genes = cause death
  • Sex-Linked = sex chromosome inheritance
  • Sex-Limited = one sex only
  • Sex-Influenced = affected by sex
  • Polygenic = many genes
  • Pleiotropy = one gene affects many traits
  • Epistasis = one gene masks another
  • Maternal Effect = maternal influence
  • Cytoplasmic Inheritance = extranuclear inheritance
  • Genomic Imprinting = parent-of-origin effect
  • Penetrance = proportion expressed
  • Expressivity = degree expressed
Non-Mendelian genetics explains complex inheritance beyond simple dominance.

Sex Determination

  • Sex Chromosomes = determine sex
  • Autosomes = non-sex chromosomes
  • Mammals = XX / XY
  • Birds = ZZ / ZW
Mammals = male determines sex; Birds = female determines sex.

Chromosome Number in Farm Animals (2n)

  • Cattle = 60 (XX / XY)
  • Carabao = 48 (XX / XY)
  • Goat = 60 (XX / XY)
  • Sheep = 54 (XX / XY)
  • Swine = 38 (XX / XY)
  • Horse = 64 (XX / XY)
  • Chicken = 78 (ZZ / ZW)
  • Duck = 80 (ZZ / ZW)
  • Turkey = 82 (ZZ / ZW)
  • Quail = 78 (ZZ / ZW)
Memorize chromosome numbers exactly — very common board recall topic.

Trait Classification

  • Qualitative Traits = simple traits
  • Quantitative Traits = measurable traits
  • Heritability = genetic control
  • Repeatability = consistency
Quantitative traits are economically important in livestock production.

Population and Cytogenetics

  • Population Genetics = genetics of groups
  • Gene Frequency = allele proportion
  • Genotype Frequency = genotype proportion
  • Cytogenetics = chromosome genetics
  • Mutation = sudden genetic change
  • Chromosomal Abnormalities = chromosome defects
Population and cytogenetics explain genetic changes in individuals and populations.
