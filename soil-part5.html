⚙️ Part 5: Advanced Soil Physical Properties

1. Soil Density

Particle Density

  • Particle density refers to the mass of soil solids per unit volume of solids only, excluding pore spaces.
  • It does not include air or water.
  • The average value for most mineral soils is 2.65 g/cm³.
  • It depends on the mineral composition of the soil, especially quartz-rich soils.
Particle density is relatively constant because mineral composition does not easily change.

Bulk Density

  • Bulk density refers to the mass of dry soil per total volume, including pore spaces.
  • It is more important in field conditions than particle density.

Factors Affecting Bulk Density

  • Soil compaction
  • Soil structure
  • Organic matter content
High bulk density = compact soil = restricted root growth.

2. Soil Porosity

  • Porosity is the percentage of the total soil volume occupied by pore spaces.
  • These pores may contain air or water.

Types of Pores

Macropores

  • Large pores
  • Allow air movement
  • Important for drainage and aeration

Micropores

  • Small pores
  • Hold water for plant use
Ideal soil condition = balanced macropores and micropores.

3. Soil Permeability

  • Soil permeability refers to the rate at which water moves through the soil profile.

Types of Permeability

  • Rapid – common in sandy soils
  • Moderate – common in loamy soils
  • Slow – common in clay soils

Importance of Soil Permeability

  • Affects drainage
  • Determines irrigation efficiency
  • Influences root health

4. Soil Infiltration

  • Soil infiltration refers to the rate at which water enters the soil surface.

Factors Affecting Infiltration

  • Soil texture
  • Soil structure
  • Organic matter content
  • Soil compaction
Low infiltration = runoff and erosion.

5. Soil Consistence

  • Soil consistence refers to the resistance of soil to deformation or rupture.
  • It depends on the moisture content of the soil.

Consistence at Different Moisture Conditions

Dry Condition

  • Loose
  • Soft
  • Slightly hard
  • Hard
  • Very hard

Moist Condition

  • Loose
  • Very friable
  • Friable
  • Firm
  • Very firm

Wet Condition

  • Described by stickiness and plasticity
  • Non-sticky to very sticky
  • Non-plastic to very plastic
Clay soils are usually sticky and plastic when wet.

6. Soil Color

  • Soil color is one of the most visible physical properties of soil.
  • It provides clues about organic matter content, drainage condition, and mineral composition.

Common Soil Colors and Meaning

  • Dark – high organic matter
  • Red / Yellow – presence of iron oxides
  • Gray / Bluish – poor drainage and waterlogging

Importance of Soil Color

  • Helps identify soil horizons
  • Indicates drainage and aeration status
  • Useful in soil classification and field description
Dark color = more organic matter | Gray color = poor drainage

7. Soil Temperature

  • Soil temperature affects seed germination, root growth, microbial activity, and nutrient availability.

Factors Affecting Soil Temperature

  • Sunlight exposure
  • Soil moisture
  • Soil color
  • Vegetation cover
Warm soil = faster biological activity | Cold soil = slower plant growth

8. Soil Air

  • Soil air occupies the pore spaces not filled with water.

Composition of Soil Air

  • Lower oxygen (O₂) than atmospheric air
  • Higher carbon dioxide (CO₂)

Importance of Soil Air

  • Essential for root respiration
  • Supports microbial activity

9. Soil Compaction

  • Compaction is the compression of soil particles that reduces pore space.

Causes of Compaction

  • Heavy machinery
  • Over-tillage

Effects of Compaction

  • Reduced aeration
  • Poor drainage
  • Restricted root growth
Compaction increases bulk density and decreases porosity.

10. Soil Tilth

  • Soil tilth refers to the physical condition of soil in relation to its ease of cultivation.

Characteristics of Good Tilth

  • Good soil structure
  • Proper moisture content
  • Easy to work and cultivate
Good tilth = ideal condition for plant growth.

11. Soil Structure Stability

  • Soil structure stability refers to the ability of soil aggregates to resist breakdown.

Importance of Soil Structure Stability

  • Prevents soil erosion
  • Maintains good soil structure
  • Improves water infiltration

Important Relationships

  • Bulk Density ↑ → Porosity ↓
  • Porosity ↑ → Air + Water ↑
  • Compaction ↑ → Root Growth ↓
Soil physical properties strongly affect root environment and plant growth.
