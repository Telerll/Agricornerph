⚙️ Part 5: Advanced Physical Properties

1. Soil Physical Properties

  • Soil physical properties are the characteristics of soil that can be observed or measured without changing its composition.
  • These properties affect water movement, root growth, and soil management.

2. Soil Color

Soil color is one of the most visible physical properties of soil.

It indicates organic matter content, drainage condition, and mineral composition.

Color Interpretation

  • Dark – high organic matter
  • Red / Yellow – presence of iron oxides
  • Gray / Bluish – poor drainage

Importance of Soil Color

  • Helps identify soil horizons
  • Indicates drainage and aeration
  • Useful in soil classification
Soil color is an indicator of soil condition.

3. Soil Density

Particle Density

  • Mass of soil solids per unit volume, excluding pores
  • Average is about 2.65 g/cm³
  • Depends on mineral composition

Bulk Density

  • Mass of dry soil per total volume, including pore spaces

Factors Affecting Bulk Density

  • Compaction
  • Soil structure
  • Organic matter
High bulk density means compact soil and poor root growth.

4. Soil Porosity

Porosity is the percentage of pore space in soil.

Types of Pores

  • Macropores – large pores for air and drainage
  • Micropores – small pores for water retention
Ideal soil has a balance of macropores and micropores.

5. Soil Permeability

Permeability is the rate at which water moves through soil.

Types of Permeability

  • Rapid – sandy soils
  • Moderate – loam
  • Slow – clay
Permeability affects drainage, irrigation, and root health.

6. Soil Infiltration

Infiltration is the rate at which water enters the soil surface.

Factors Affecting Infiltration

  • Texture
  • Structure
  • Organic matter
  • Compaction
Low infiltration leads to runoff and erosion.

7. Soil Consistency

Soil consistency is the resistance of soil to deformation or rupture.

It depends on soil moisture condition.

Dry Soil

  • Loose
  • Soft
  • Hard
  • Very hard

Moist Soil

  • Friable
  • Firm
  • Very firm

Wet Soil

  • Sticky
  • Plastic
Clay soils are usually sticky and plastic when wet.

8. Soil Temperature

Soil temperature affects biological and plant processes.

Influences of Soil Temperature

  • Seed germination
  • Root growth
  • Microbial activity

Factors Affecting Soil Temperature

  • Sunlight
  • Moisture
  • Soil color
  • Vegetation cover

9. Soil Air

Soil air occupies pore spaces not filled with water.

Characteristics of Soil Air

  • Less oxygen than the atmosphere
  • More carbon dioxide than the atmosphere
Soil air is important for root respiration and microbial activity.

10. Soil Compaction

Soil compaction is the compression of soil particles.

Causes of Soil Compaction

  • Heavy machinery
  • Over-tillage

Effects of Soil Compaction

  • Increased bulk density
  • Reduced porosity
  • Poor aeration
  • Restricted root growth

11. Soil Tilth

Soil tilth is the physical condition of soil for cultivation.

Characteristics of Good Tilth

  • Good structure
  • Proper moisture
  • Easy to work
Good tilth is the best condition for plant growth.

12. Soil Structure Stability

Soil structure stability is the ability of soil aggregates to resist breakdown.

Importance of Soil Structure Stability

  • Prevents erosion
  • Improves infiltration
  • Maintains soil structure

13. Water Holding Capacity

Water holding capacity is the ability of soil to retain water against gravity.

Factors Affecting Water Holding Capacity

  • Texture
  • Structure
  • Organic matter

14. Relationship of Physical Properties

High bulk density → low porosity
Good structure → high infiltration
Clay soil → high water holding, low permeability
Sandy soil → low water holding, high permeability
Compaction → reduces infiltration and root growth

Final Summary

Color → indicates condition
Density → shows compaction level
Porosity → air and water space
Permeability → water movement
Consistency → resistance of soil
Temperature → affects biological activity
Compaction → soil problem
Tilth → ideal soil condition
