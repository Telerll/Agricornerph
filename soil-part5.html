⚙️ Part 5: Advanced Soil Physical Properties

Soil Density

Particle Density

  • Mass of soil solids per unit volume of solids.
  • Average value: 2.65 g/cm³
  • Not greatly affected by soil structure.

Bulk Density

  • Mass of dry soil per unit volume including pore spaces.
  • Affected by compaction and soil structure.
  • High bulk density = compact soil, poor root growth.
High bulk density = low porosity

Soil Porosity

  • Percentage of pore spaces in soil.
  • Determines water and air storage.

Types of Pores

  • Macropores – large pores for air movement and drainage
  • Micropores – small pores that hold water
Good soil = balance of macro and micro pores

Soil Consistence

  • Resistance of soil to deformation or breaking.

Conditions

  • Dry – hard or soft
  • Moist – friable or firm
  • Wet – sticky and plastic

Soil Color

  • Indicates organic matter and mineral content.

Common Colors

  • Dark – high organic matter
  • Red/Yellow – iron oxides
  • Gray – poor drainage
Dark soil = usually more fertile

Soil Temperature

  • Affects seed germination and root growth.
  • Influences microbial activity.

Factors Affecting Temperature

  • Sunlight
  • Soil moisture
  • Color
  • Vegetation cover

Soil Air

  • Air present in soil pores.
  • Important for root respiration.

Composition

  • Less oxygen than atmosphere
  • More carbon dioxide
Poor aeration = poor root growth

Soil Compaction

  • Compression of soil reducing pore space.
  • Caused by heavy machinery or repeated tillage.

Effects

  • Reduced root growth
  • Poor drainage
  • Low aeration

Soil Tilth

  • Physical condition of soil related to ease of cultivation.
  • Good tilth = ideal for plant growth.

Characteristics

  • Good structure
  • Proper moisture
  • Easy to work
Good tilth = good crop growth

Summary

  • Soil physical properties affect plant growth.
  • Balance of air, water, and structure is essential.
Soil condition = root environment
