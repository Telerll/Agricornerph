⚙️ Part 5: Advanced Physical Properties

Soil Color

  • Soil color is one of the most visible physical properties of soil.
  • It gives clues about organic matter content, drainage condition, and mineral composition.
  • Dark soils usually contain more organic matter.
  • Red, yellow, or brown colors are often due to iron compounds.
  • Gray or bluish colors may indicate poor drainage.

Importance of Soil Color

  • Helps in identifying soil horizons.
  • Indicates drainage and aeration status.
  • Useful in soil classification and field description.
Dark color = more organic matter | Gray color = poor drainage

Bulk Density

  • Bulk density is the mass of dry soil per unit bulk volume, including pore spaces.
  • It is usually expressed in grams per cubic centimeter (g/cm³).
  • High bulk density means the soil is compact.
  • Low bulk density means the soil is loose and porous.

Importance of Bulk Density

  • Affects root penetration.
  • Influences water infiltration and drainage.
  • Indicates degree of soil compaction.

Particle Density

  • Particle density is the mass of soil solids per unit volume of soil solids only.
  • It does not include pore spaces.
  • Average particle density of mineral soils is about 2.65 g/cm³.

Porosity

  • Porosity is the percentage of the total soil volume occupied by pore spaces.
  • These pore spaces may contain air or water.
  • Porosity is influenced by soil texture, structure, and bulk density.
High porosity means more space for air and water.

Soil Consistency

  • Soil consistency is the degree of resistance of soil to deformation or rupture.
  • It varies depending on moisture content.
  • Used to describe the feel and behavior of soil when dry, moist, or wet.

Consistency at Different Moisture Conditions

When Dry

  • Loose
  • Soft
  • Slightly hard
  • Hard
  • Very hard

When Moist

  • Loose
  • Very friable
  • Friable
  • Firm
  • Very firm

When Wet

  • Described in terms of stickiness and plasticity
  • Non-sticky to very sticky
  • Non-plastic to very plastic

Soil Temperature

  • Soil temperature affects seed germination, root growth, microbial activity, and nutrient availability.
  • It is influenced by soil color, moisture, vegetation cover, and climate.

Soil Air

  • Soil air occupies the pore spaces not filled with water.
  • It contains less oxygen and more carbon dioxide than atmospheric air.
  • Good aeration is necessary for root respiration and microbial activity.

Compaction

  • Compaction is the pressing together of soil particles, reducing pore space.
  • It increases bulk density and decreases porosity.
  • Compacted soils restrict root growth, aeration, and water movement.

Good Physical Condition of Soil

  • Has favorable bulk density and porosity.
  • Allows free movement of air and water.
  • Promotes easy root penetration and healthy plant growth.
