🌬 Respiratory System of Farm Animals

Respiratory System

Respiratory system is the body system responsible for the intake of oxygen and removal of carbon dioxide.

Functions of the Respiratory System

Gas Exchange

Gas exchange is the process of taking in oxygen (O₂) and removing carbon dioxide (CO₂).

Respiration

Respiration is the cellular process of using oxygen to release energy from nutrients.

Regulation

Regulation is the control of blood pH through carbon dioxide levels.

Thermoregulation

Thermoregulation is the regulation of body temperature through breathing.

Parts of the Respiratory System

Nostrils (Nares)

Nostrils are the external openings where air enters the body.

Nasal Cavity

Nasal cavity warms, moistens, and filters incoming air.

Pharynx

Pharynx is the passageway for air from the nasal cavity to the larynx.

Larynx

Larynx controls airflow and produces sound.

Trachea

Trachea is the tube that carries air to the lungs.

Bronchi

Bronchi are the main branches of the trachea entering the lungs.

Bronchioles

Bronchioles are smaller airways distributing air within the lungs.

Lungs

Lungs are the main organs of respiration.

Alveoli

Alveoli are tiny air sacs where gas exchange occurs.

Process of Breathing

Inhalation (Inspiration)

Inhalation is the intake of air into the lungs.

Exhalation (Expiration)

Exhalation is the release of air from the lungs.

Mechanism of Breathing (Mammals)

Diaphragm

Diaphragm is a dome-shaped muscle separating the thoracic cavity and abdominal cavity.

Intercostal Muscles

Intercostal muscles are muscles between the ribs that assist in breathing.

Mechanism

  • Contraction of diaphragm and intercostal muscles expands the thoracic cavity
  • Expansion creates negative pressure
  • Air is drawn into the lungs
Breathing in mammals is pressure-driven.

Gas Exchange Process

Oxygen diffuses from the alveoli into the blood.

Carbon dioxide diffuses from the blood into the alveoli.

Gas exchange occurs between the alveoli and capillaries.

Respiratory System in Farm Animals

  • Supplies oxygen for metabolism
  • Removes carbon dioxide
  • Supports growth and production

Poultry Respiratory System

Air Sacs

Air sacs are extensions of the lungs that store and move air.

Birds have 9 air sacs.

Key Feature

  • Airflow is unidirectional or one-way
  • Birds do not have a diaphragm

Two-Cycle Breathing Mechanism

  • 1st inhalation → air enters air sacs
  • 1st exhalation → air moves into lungs
  • 2nd inhalation → air moves from lungs to air sacs
  • 2nd exhalation → air leaves the body
Poultry respiration is more efficient because of continuous one-way airflow.

Sound Production

Mammals

Sound is produced in the larynx.

Poultry

Sound is produced in the syrinx, located at the base of the trachea.

Thermoregulation

Panting

Panting is rapid breathing used to release excess body heat.

It is important in cattle and swine because they have limited sweating ability.

Importance in Animal Science

  • Affects animal health
  • Important in growth and production
  • Supports metabolism
  • Critical in heat stress management

Respiratory System and Production

  • Good respiration improves metabolism
  • Better oxygen supply supports better growth
  • Important in stress and environmental adaptation

Board Exam Comparison

Mammals: diaphragm + intercostal muscles
Poultry: no diaphragm, uses air sacs

Mammals: bidirectional airflow
Poultry: unidirectional airflow

Mammals: larynx
Poultry: syrinx

Final Summary

Respiratory system = O₂ in, CO₂ out

Alveoli = gas exchange

Mammals: diaphragm + intercostal muscles
Poultry: air sacs + one-way airflow

Panting = heat regulation
