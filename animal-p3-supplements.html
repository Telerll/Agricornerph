Feed Additives and Supplements

Feed Additives and Supplements

Feed additives and supplements are substances added to animal feed to improve nutrient value, growth, health, feed efficiency, and overall production.

They are added in small amounts but can greatly affect performance.

Additives improve feed performance, while supplements improve nutrient supply.

Difference Between Additives and Supplements

Feed Additives

Feed additives are substances added mainly to improve feed quality, digestion, health, or performance.

They are not always nutrients themselves, but they improve how feed works.

Examples:

  • Probiotics
  • Enzymes
  • Antioxidants
  • Preservatives
Additives improve feed function.

Feed Supplements

Feed supplements are nutrient-rich materials added to correct deficiencies or improve nutrient supply.

They directly provide nutrients.

Examples:

  • Vitamin premix
  • Mineral premix
  • Protein supplement
  • Calcium supplement
Supplements improve nutrient content.

Importance of Feed Additives and Supplements

They are used to:

  • Improve growth
  • Improve feed efficiency
  • Improve health
  • Prevent deficiency
  • Improve reproduction
  • Improve production
Feed additives and supplements improve performance and profitability.

Probiotics

Probiotics are live beneficial microorganisms added to feed to improve gut health and digestion.

They help:

  • Balance intestinal microbes
  • Improve digestion
  • Improve immunity
  • Reduce diarrhea

Examples:

  • Lactobacillus
  • Bacillus
  • Saccharomyces
Probiotics = beneficial microbes.

Prebiotics

Prebiotics are non-digestible feed ingredients that stimulate the growth of beneficial gut bacteria.

They serve as food for probiotics.

They help:

  • Improve gut health
  • Support beneficial bacteria
  • Improve digestion
Prebiotics = food for good bacteria.

Enzymes

Enzymes are biological catalysts added to feed to improve nutrient digestion.

They help break down feed components the animal cannot digest efficiently.

Examples:

  • Phytase
  • Xylanase
  • Protease
Enzymes improve nutrient digestibility.

Phytase

Phytase is an enzyme that breaks down phytate and releases phosphorus from plant feeds.

It helps:

  • Improve phosphorus availability
  • Reduce phosphorus waste
  • Improve mineral use
Phytase improves phosphorus utilization.

Antibiotics

Antibiotics were used as growth promoters and disease preventives in feed.

They improve:

  • Growth
  • Feed efficiency
  • Disease control

Important note:

  • Overuse may cause antimicrobial resistance
  • Use is now restricted in many areas
Antibiotic growth promoters are being reduced or phased out.

Antioxidants

Antioxidants are additives that prevent oxidation and protect feed nutrients from spoilage.

They protect:

  • Fats
  • Vitamins
  • Feed quality
Antioxidants prevent feed rancidity.

Preservatives

Preservatives are additives used to prevent spoilage and extend feed shelf life.

They help:

  • Prevent mold growth
  • Reduce spoilage
  • Improve storage stability
Preservatives protect feed quality during storage.

Mold Inhibitors

Mold inhibitors are additives that prevent fungal growth in stored feed.

They reduce:

  • Mold contamination
  • Spoilage
  • Toxin risk
Mold inhibitors improve feed safety.

Mycotoxin Binders

Mycotoxin binders are additives that bind harmful toxins produced by molds and reduce their absorption in the animal.

They help reduce effects of:

  • Aflatoxin
  • Ochratoxin
  • Fumonisin
Mycotoxin binders reduce toxin absorption.

Coccidiostats

Coccidiostats are feed additives used to prevent coccidiosis, especially in poultry.

They help:

  • Reduce intestinal damage
  • Improve growth
  • Improve feed efficiency
Coccidiostats help control coccidiosis.

Growth Promoters

Growth promoters are substances added to improve growth rate and feed conversion.

Examples:

  • Probiotics
  • Enzymes
  • Formerly antibiotics
Growth promoters improve performance.

Vitamin Supplements

Vitamin supplements are added to prevent vitamin deficiency and support normal body function.

They improve:

  • Growth
  • Immunity
  • Reproduction
  • Metabolism
Vitamin supplements prevent deficiency.

Mineral Supplements

Mineral supplements are added to correct mineral deficiency and improve growth, bone health, and reproduction.

Examples:

  • Calcium
  • Phosphorus
  • Salt
  • Trace minerals
Mineral supplements support structural and metabolic functions.

Protein Supplements

Protein supplements are added to improve protein intake and amino acid supply.

Examples:

  • Soybean meal
  • Fish meal
  • Urea, for ruminants only
Protein supplements improve growth and tissue development.

Non-Protein Nitrogen (NPN)

Non-protein nitrogen (NPN) refers to nitrogen sources that are not true protein but can be used by rumen microbes to produce microbial protein.

Example:

  • Urea

Used only in:

  • Ruminants
NPN is useful only in ruminants.

Buffers

Buffers are additives used to stabilize rumen pH and reduce acidosis.

Example:

  • Sodium bicarbonate

Used in:

  • Dairy cattle
  • Feedlot cattle
Buffers help maintain rumen stability.

Palatability Enhancers

Palatability enhancers are additives used to improve taste and feed acceptance.

They help:

  • Increase feed intake
  • Improve acceptance
  • Reduce feed refusal
Palatability affects intake.

Board Exam Last-Minute Power Facts

Ionophores

Ionophores are feed additives that alter rumen microbial activity to improve feed efficiency and energy use.

Common Example:

  • Rumensin or Monensin

Functions:

  • Improve feed efficiency
  • Improve energy utilization
  • Reduce bloat risk
  • Help control coccidiosis

Commonly used in cattle.

Ionophores improve rumen efficiency and growth performance.

Anthelmintics

Anthelmintics are deworming agents added to feed or given directly to control internal parasites.

They help:

  • Remove worms
  • Improve nutrient use
  • Improve growth
  • Improve feed efficiency
Anthelmintics = dewormers.

Pellet Binders

Pellet binders are additives used to help feed particles stick together during pelleting.

They help:

  • Maintain pellet shape
  • Reduce feed dust
  • Reduce feed loss
  • Improve pellet quality

Common in:

  • Poultry feed
  • Fish feed
Pellet binders improve pellet stability.

Urea Safety Rule

Urea is a non-protein nitrogen source used only in ruminants.

Safety limits:

  • Do not exceed 1% of total ration
  • Do not exceed 1/3 of total protein source

Too much urea causes:

  • Ammonia toxicity
  • Rapid poisoning
  • Possible sudden death
Excess urea can kill very quickly.

Board Exam Master Concept

Feed additives and supplements are not the same.

  • Additives improve feed function
  • Supplements improve nutrient supply

Both improve:

  • Efficiency
  • Health
  • Productivity
  • Profitability
Additives optimize feed, while supplements correct nutrition.
