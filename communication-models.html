Expanded Terms in Communication Models

Berlo’s Model Terms

1. Source

The originator or sender of the message. This is the person who starts the communication process.

Examples:

  • Extension worker
  • Teacher
  • Researcher

2. Communication Skills

The ability to speak, write, listen, read, and understand effectively. Better communication skills improve message delivery.

3. Attitudes

Refers to feelings, beliefs, opinions, or mindset of the source or receiver.

Positive attitudes improve communication effectiveness.

4. Knowledge

Refers to the information and understanding possessed by the communicator.

A knowledgeable communicator becomes more credible.

5. Social System

Refers to the social environment, customs, traditions, and societal structure affecting communication.

Examples:

  • Religion
  • Family structure
  • Community norms

6. Culture

Shared beliefs, practices, language, and traditions of a group of people.

Culture influences how messages are interpreted.

7. Message

The information, idea, thought, or feeling transmitted from source to receiver.

8. Content

Refers to the actual information included in the message.

9. Elements

Supporting parts of the message such as gestures, tone, symbols, or illustrations.

10. Treatment

Refers to the style or manner used in presenting the message.

Examples:

  • Formal
  • Friendly
  • Persuasive

11. Structure

Refers to the organization or arrangement of the message.

12. Code

Symbols, words, signs, or language used in communication.

13. Channel

The medium through which the message travels.

14. Hearing

Communication received through listening.

15. Seeing

Communication received visually through observation.

16. Touching

Communication involving physical contact.

17. Smelling

Communication involving odor perception.

18. Tasting

Communication involving taste perception.

19. Receiver

The person or group receiving and interpreting the message.

Lasswell’s Model Terms

1. Who

Refers to the communicator or source.

2. Says What

Refers to the content or message communicated.

3. In Which Channel

Refers to the medium used in transmitting the message.

Examples:

  • Radio
  • Television
  • Social media
  • Seminar

4. To Whom

Refers to the audience or receiver.

5. With What Effect

Refers to the outcome or impact of communication.

Examples:

  • Change in attitude
  • Increased awareness
  • Adoption of technology

Shannon and Weaver’s Model Terms

1. Encoder

Device or process converting ideas into signals or codes.

Examples:

  • Human speech
  • Telephone microphone

2. Decoder

Device or process translating signals back into understandable form.

Examples:

  • Human hearing
  • Radio receiver

3. Noise

Any factor interfering with communication clarity or accuracy.

Examples:

  • Static noise
  • Misunderstanding
  • Distractions
  • Poor internet connection

4. Information Model

A communication model emphasizing transmission of information from sender to receiver.

5. Linear Communication

One-way communication flow without immediate feedback.

6. Feedback

Response or reaction from the receiver back to the source.

Feedback indicates whether communication was successful.

General Communication Terms

1. Feedback Loop

Continuous exchange of messages and responses between sender and receiver.

2. Media

Tools or platforms used to communicate information.

Examples:

  • Newspapers
  • Television
  • Facebook
  • Radio

3. Mass Communication

Communication intended for large audiences using mass media.

4. Communication Breakdown

Failure of communication due to misunderstanding or barriers.

5. Audience

Group of people intended to receive the message.

6. Persuasion

Process of influencing attitudes, beliefs, or behaviors.

7. Interpretation

Meaning assigned by the receiver to the message.

8. Communication Process

Step-by-step flow of sending, receiving, and interpreting messages.
