Communication Models

1. Berlo’s Model

Berlo’s Model focuses on the individual characteristics of communication and emphasizes the relationship between the source and the receiver.

The more developed the communication skills of both source and receiver, the more effective communication becomes.

Components of Berlo’s Model

Source

  • Communication skills
  • Attitudes
  • Knowledge
  • Social system
  • Culture

Message

  • Content
  • Elements
  • Treatment
  • Structure
  • Code

Channel

  • Hearing
  • Seeing
  • Touching
  • Smelling
  • Tasting

Receiver

  • Communication skills
  • Attitudes
  • Knowledge
  • Social system
  • Culture

Explanation of Berlo’s Model

  • The source creates messages based on communication skills, attitudes, knowledge, and cultural background.
  • Messages are transmitted through channels.
  • The receiver interprets messages according to his/her own characteristics.
  • The major limitation of the model is the absence of feedback.

2. Lasswell’s Model

Harold Lasswell summarized communication using the statement:

“Who says what in which channel to whom with what effects?”

Components of Lasswell’s Model

  • Who – Source or communicator
  • Says What – Message
  • In Which Channel – Medium or channel used
  • To Whom – Receiver or audience
  • With What Effect – Result or effect of communication

Explanation of Lasswell’s Model

  • Lasswell’s model focuses on cause-and-effect relationships.
  • It explains the influence of media and communication on audiences.
  • Commonly used in mass communication and media studies.
  • A limitation of the model is that communication is mostly one-way with limited feedback.

3. Shannon and Weaver’s Model

Shannon and Weaver developed a general communication model commonly known as the Information Model.

Components of Shannon and Weaver’s Model

  • Source
  • Encoder
  • Channel
  • Message
  • Decoder
  • Receiver
  • Noise

Explanation of Components

  • Source – Originator of information.
  • Encoder – Converts ideas into signals or codes.
  • Channel – Medium through which the message travels.
  • Message – Information being transmitted.
  • Decoder – Converts signals into understandable form.
  • Receiver – Final recipient of the message.
  • Noise – Factors that interfere with communication.

Examples of Noise

  • Poor signal
  • Loud environment
  • Distractions
  • Misunderstanding

Importance of Shannon and Weaver’s Model

  • Helps explain communication breakdowns and barriers.
  • Useful in analyzing technical and human communication systems.
  • Emphasizes the role of noise in affecting communication effectiveness.

Limitation of Shannon and Weaver’s Model

  • Originally intended for technical and mechanical communication systems.
  • Communication is mostly viewed as linear or one-way.
