Sheep Breeds

Sheep breeds are classified according to their primary economic purpose, adaptability, body size, wool quality, and meat-producing ability. Sheep are raised mainly for meat, wool, milk, and skin, although in the Philippine setting they are more commonly associated with meat production and small-scale livelihood systems.

A breed is a group of animals with similar and heritable characteristics such as body conformation, wool type, color, growth rate, and production performance that distinguish them from other groups of the same species.

In sheep production, breed selection is important because different breeds vary in body size, growth rate, wool production, meat quality, reproductive efficiency, climate adaptability, and disease resistance.

Sheep breeds are groups of sheep classified according to their inherited characteristics and production purpose.

Classification of Sheep Breeds

Sheep breeds are commonly classified according to their primary use:

  • Wool breeds
  • Meat breeds
  • Dual-purpose breeds
  • Hair sheep breeds

This classification is important because it determines the management system, feeding program, and economic purpose of the flock.

Sheep breeds are classified based on production purpose.

Wool Breeds

Wool breeds are sheep primarily raised for wool production. These breeds produce heavy fleece and are usually characterized by dense wool covering, large body frame, and relatively slower maturity compared with meat breeds.

Wool breeds are more common in temperate regions because their wool helps protect them from cold climates.

Examples include Merino and Rambouillet.

Wool breeds are primarily raised for fleece production.

Merino

The Merino is one of the most famous and economically important wool breeds in the world.

It originated in Spain and is best known for producing fine, soft, high-quality wool. Merino sheep are considered the world’s leading fine-wool breed.

  • Medium body size
  • White face
  • White fleece
  • Fine, dense wool
  • Excellent wool quality
  • Hardy and adaptable
Merino = fine wool breed.

Rambouillet

The Rambouillet originated in France and was developed from Merino sheep.

It is a large wool breed known for fine wool production, larger body size than Merino, better meat conformation, and adaptability.

  • Large body frame
  • White face
  • White fleece
  • Fine wool
  • Hardy and adaptable
Rambouillet = large fine-wool breed.

Meat Breeds

Meat breeds are sheep raised primarily for lamb and mutton production. These breeds are characterized by fast growth, muscular body, good carcass quality, rapid maturity, and efficient feed conversion.

Examples include Suffolk, Hampshire, and Dorset.

Meat breeds are raised primarily for meat production.

Suffolk

The Suffolk is one of the most popular meat breeds in the world.

It originated in England and is widely used in commercial meat production because of its rapid growth, large size, muscular build, and high meat yield.

  • Black face
  • Black legs
  • No wool on face and legs
  • Large body
  • Fast growth
  • Excellent carcass quality
Suffolk = popular meat breed.

Hampshire

The Hampshire is a meat breed known for rapid growth and muscular carcass.

It originated in England and is widely used in lamb production.

  • Dark face
  • Wool cap on head
  • Muscular body
  • Rapid growth
  • Strong meat conformation
Hampshire = muscular meat breed.

Dorset

The Dorset is a meat breed known for good carcass quality and excellent reproductive performance.

It is especially valued because ewes may breed out of season, making it useful for year-round lamb production.

  • White face
  • Medium to large body
  • Fast growth
  • Good mothering ability
  • Good fertility
Dorset = meat breed with out-of-season breeding ability.

Dual-Purpose Breeds

Dual-purpose breeds are sheep raised for both meat and wool. These breeds are useful because they provide two sources of income: wool and meat.

Examples include Corriedale and Columbia.

Dual-purpose breeds produce both meat and wool.

Corriedale

The Corriedale is a dual-purpose breed developed for both wool and meat production.

It is one of the most common dual-purpose sheep breeds.

  • Medium to large body
  • White face
  • Good wool production
  • Good carcass quality
  • Adaptable
Corriedale = dual-purpose breed.

Columbia

The Columbia is a large dual-purpose breed developed in the United States.

It is known for large body size, good growth, good wool, and adaptability.

Columbia = large dual-purpose breed.

Hair Sheep Breeds

Hair sheep breeds are sheep that produce hair instead of heavy wool fleece.

These breeds are more adapted to tropical and subtropical climates because they tolerate heat better, have lower parasite burden, do not require shearing, and adapt well to humid climates.

Examples include Dorper and Katahdin.

Hair sheep are tropical-adapted sheep with hair instead of wool.

Dorper

The Dorper is a popular meat breed developed in South Africa.

It is a hair sheep known for rapid growth, excellent meat quality, heat tolerance, drought tolerance, and adaptability.

  • White body
  • Black head (common type)
  • Muscular body
  • Good growth
Dorper = tropical meat-type hair sheep.

Katahdin

The Katahdin is a hair sheep breed developed in the United States.

It is known for parasite resistance, heat tolerance, low maintenance, and no shearing required.

Katahdin = low-maintenance hair sheep.
