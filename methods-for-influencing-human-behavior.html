Methods for Influencing Human Behavior

1. Advice

Used if:

  • Farmers agree with the extension agent about their problems and the criteria for choosing the correct solution.
  • The extension agent has enough information to solve the problem scientifically or in practice.
  • Farmers are confident that the extension worker can help them.
  • The extension agent believes farmers cannot or need not solve the problem alone.
  • Farmers have sufficient means to carry out the advice.

2. Compulsion or Coercion

  • A party forces somebody to do something.
  • It involves power and authority.
  • Example: government regulations on traffic rules, public health, environmental policies, and similar rules.

3. Exchange

Exchange of goods and services between two individuals or groups.

Applicable if:

  • Each party considers the transaction favorable.
  • Each has goods or services desired by the other.
  • Each can deliver his/her part when the exchange has been delivered by the other.

4. Openly Influencing Farmer’s Knowledge Level and Attitude

Applicable if we believe that the farmer cannot solve his/her own problems because of insufficient or incorrect knowledge and/or because attitudes do not match goals.

  • Farmers can solve their own problems if they gain more knowledge or improved attitudes.
  • We help farmers collect better knowledge or influence their attitudes.
  • Farmers trust our expertise and are prepared to cooperate.

5. Manipulation

Influencing farmer’s knowledge level and attitudes without the farmer being aware of it.

Possible if we believe:

  • It is necessary and desirable for farmers to change behavior in a certain direction.
  • It is unnecessary or undesirable for farmers to make independent decisions.
  • We can control techniques for influencing farmers without them being aware of it.
  • Farmers do not actively object to being influenced in this manner.

6. Providing Means

Applied under the following conditions:

  • Farmers are trying to achieve goals considered appropriate by the extension agent.
  • Farmers do not have the means available to achieve those goals or do not wish to risk using those means.

7. Providing Service

May involve taking over certain tasks from farmers.

Possible when:

  • We have knowledge and/or means to perform the tasks better or more economically than farmers.
  • We agree with farmers that it is useful to perform those tasks.
  • We are prepared to perform the tasks for them.

8. Changing Farmer’s Social and/or Economic Structure

This applies when we agree with farmers that they could perform optimally, but they cannot behave this way because of barriers in the economic and/or social structure.

Possible when:

  • We consider changes in these structures desirable.
  • We have the freedom to work toward these changes.
  • We are in a position to implement changes through power or conviction.
