Introduction

Weed Science is the study of weeds, their identification, biology, ecology, dissemination, competition, and management. Weeds are plants growing where they are not wanted and are major causes of yield loss in agriculture.

Weed Classification

Life Cycle

  • Annual – completes life cycle in one season
  • Biennial – completes in two seasons
  • Perennial – lives more than two years

Morphology

  • Sedges – triangular stems
  • Grasses – round stems
  • Broadleaf – wide leaves

Weed Reproduction

  • Sexual reproduction (seeds)
  • Asexual reproduction (vegetative structures)
  • Apomixis – seed without fertilization
  • Seed bank – soil reserve of weed seeds

Weed Dissemination

  • Anemochory – wind
  • Hydrochory – water
  • Zoochory – animals
  • Autochory – explosive self-dispersal
  • Human activities – machinery, seeds, transport

Weed Characteristics

  • High seed production
  • Seed dormancy
  • Rapid growth
  • Wide adaptability
  • Vegetative reproduction
  • Strong competition

Crop-Weed Competition

Weeds compete with crops for water, nutrients, light, space, and carbon dioxide. Early weed competition causes the greatest yield loss.

Weed Control Methods

  • Preventive
  • Cultural
  • Mechanical
  • Physical
  • Biological
  • Chemical
  • Integrated Weed Management (IWM)

Herbicides (Quick Review)

  • Selective – kills specific weeds (2,4-D)
  • Non-selective – kills most plants (Glyphosate, Paraquat)
  • Contact – kills touched parts only
  • Systemic – moves throughout plant
  • Pre-emergence – before weeds emerge
  • Post-emergence – after weeds emerge

Major Weeds in the Philippines

  • Cyperus rotundus – Purple Nutsedge
  • Imperata cylindrica – Cogon Grass
  • Echinochloa crus-galli – Barnyard Grass
  • Eleusine indica – Goosegrass
  • Rottboellia cochinchinensis – Itchgrass
  • Mimosa pudica – Makahiya
  • Chromolaena odorata – Hagonoy
  • Monochoria vaginalis – Pickerel Weed

Board Exam Key Points

✔ Sedges have edges (triangular stems)
✔ Glyphosate = systemic, non-selective
✔ Paraquat = contact, non-selective
✔ 2,4-D = selective, systemic
✔ Seed bank ensures weed recurrence
✔ Early weed competition = highest yield loss
✔ IWM combines all control methods

Summary

Weed Science integrates the study of weed biology, classification, reproduction, dissemination, ecology, competition, and control. Weeds persist due to their adaptability, seed dormancy, and reproductive efficiency. Effective management requires Integrated Weed Management combining multiple control strategies to reduce weed impact on agriculture.
