Problems in Agricultural Marketing

Agricultural marketing faces several problems due to the nature of farm products, production systems, and market conditions.

1. Product Characteristics

a. Perishability

Many agricultural products spoil quickly, requiring fast transportation, storage, and handling to prevent losses.

b. Seasonality

Production is seasonal, causing uneven supply and price fluctuations throughout the year.

c. Bulkiness

Agricultural goods are often bulky and expensive to transport relative to value.

d. Heterogeneity

Products vary in quality, size, and appearance, making standardization difficult.

2. Market Structure Problems

Many small producers face weak bargaining power compared to traders and middlemen.

3. Inadequate Market Information

Farmers often lack updated information about prices and market demand.

4. Poor Infrastructure

Lack of roads, storage facilities, and transportation increases marketing costs.

5. Marketing Inefficiency

Inefficient systems lead to high costs and low returns for farmers.
