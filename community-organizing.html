Community Organizing

Community

Community is defined as an organization of families and individuals settled in a fairly compact and contiguous geographical area with significant elements of common life.

Elements

  • Manners – A way of doing something.
  • Customs – The social convention carried on by tradition.
  • Tradition – Handing down customs, beliefs, and practices from generation to generation.
  • Mode of Speech – The language of a certain people or group.

Characteristics of Community

1. A Common System of Values

Values are concepts with various shades of meaning. They guide what ought to be done and relate to the ultimate ends, goals, or purposes of social action.

2. Normatively Defined Relation

Norms are accepted ways of behaving that govern group life in all societies. These are rules or standards of behavior shared by members of a social group.

3. Interdependence

Interdependence means needing and depending upon each other. It refers to relationships that exist between and among people in the community.

4. Recognition of Belongings

Everybody wants to feel liked and accepted by others. Association with others makes people feel that they have unity and belongingness.

5. System of Stratification

Stratification is the way a large group of people is divided into classes based on occupation, role in production, relation to resources, and share of social wealth.

6. Locality

Locality refers to the area occupied by closely knit people and may vary according to the term of reference used.

Community Organizing

Definition

  • It is a development strategy for building people’s capabilities toward self-determination and self-reliance.
  • It is a method of educating, organizing, and mobilizing people toward their own development.
  • It is both a method and a process.

Community Organizing as a Process

  • Educating people to understand their critical consciousness and existing conditions.
  • Organizing people to work collectively and efficiently on their problems.
  • Mobilizing people to develop readiness to respond and take action on immediate and long-term problems.

Three Major Components of Community Organizing

  1. Training and Education
  2. Organization Building
  3. Mobilization

Tasks and Functions of a Community Organizer

1. As a Facilitator

  • Enhances individual and group strengths and minimizes weaknesses and conflicts.
  • Heightens group unity.
  • Assists individuals and groups to respond to common interests.

2. As a Trainer

  • Assesses training needs.
  • Plans and conducts educational programs to enhance capabilities.
  • Assists leaders in training others.
  • Engages in praxis or action-reflection-action through continuous dialogue with people.

3. As an Advocate

  • Analyzes and articulates critical issues.
  • Assists others to reflect upon issues.
  • Evokes and provokes meaningful discussions and actions.

4. As a Researcher

  • Conducts social analysis.
  • Engages in participatory technology development.
  • Simplifies and enriches appropriate research concepts and skills.
  • Engages in social integration to understand social phenomena from the eyes of the people.

5. As a Planner

  • Conducts analysis of area resources and potentials.
  • Assists local groups in planning for their common good.
  • Systematizes group actions.

6. As a Catalyst

  • Initiates debates and actions regarding critical problems.
  • Monitors and nurtures growth of individuals and groups.

Community Organization

Definition

  • It is the process of bringing about and maintaining adjustment between social welfare needs and resources in a geographical area or special field of service.
  • It is a process where the community identifies its needs and objectives, ranks them, develops confidence, and takes action to meet them.
  • It is a systematic, planned, and liberating change process that transforms a deprived community into an organized, conscious, empowered, self-reliant, and just human entity.

Community Organization: Values, Goals and Principles

I. Values of Community Organization

  1. Human Rights
  2. Social Justice
  3. Social Responsibility

II. Goals of Community Organizing

1. People’s Empowerment

  • Community organizing aims at achieving effective power for the people.
  • People learn to overcome powerlessness and develop the capacity to control their situation.

2. Building Relatively Permanent Structures and People’s Organization

  • Community organizing aims to establish and sustain organizational structures that serve the needs and aspirations of the people.

3. Building Alliances

  • Community organizing seeks to create a conducive environment for solidarity, creativity, and equitable distribution of power and resources.

4. Improved Quality of Life / Standard of Living

5. Popular Democracy

6. National Social Transformation

III. Principles of Community Organizing

  1. People’s Participation
  2. Self-Determination
  3. Experiential Learning
  4. Self-Reliance

Organizing Approaches

1. Issue-Based Approach

This organizing approach revolves around issues or problems that are felt by a significant number of people in the community.

  • Requires people to negotiate from a position of strength.
  • Usually involves issues outside the community for resolution.
  • Has a high probability of being resolved if collectively acted upon.

2. Socio-Economic Project-Based Approach

This organizing approach centers around the introduction of socio-economic projects.

  • Health care
  • Income-generating projects
  • Enterprise development
  • MPC formation

3. Church / Faith-Based Approach

This organizing approach is used by organizers within the structures of a church or within components of church-based programs.
