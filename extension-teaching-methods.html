EXTENSION TEACHING METHODS (ETM)

Extension Teaching Methods (ETM) are the various ways and techniques used by extension workers to communicate knowledge, skills, and innovations to farmers, homemakers, youth, and rural communities. These methods help influence behavior, improve practices, and encourage adoption of new technologies.

ETM According to Form

1. Written Form

These are methods delivered through printed or written materials.

  • Bulletins
  • Circular letters
  • Leaflets
  • News articles
  • Personal letters

2. Spoken Form

Methods that rely on oral communication.

  • Meetings
  • Farm and home visits
  • Office calls
  • Radio calls

3. Visual or Object Form

Methods using visual materials or actual objects.

  • Exhibits
  • Motion pictures
  • Posters
  • Result demonstrations
  • Slides
  • Television

4. Spoken and Visual Object Form

Combination of oral explanation and visual presentation.

  • Demonstration
  • Meetings with motion pictures
  • Slides presentation
  • Visual aids

ETM According to Number of Clientele

1. Individual Approach

This method focuses on one person or one family at a time.

  • Farm and home visits
  • Office calls
  • Personal letters
  • Result demonstrations

Models Under Individual Approach

Participatory Model: The farmer and extension worker solve problems together.

Counseling Model: Uses empathy and understanding to guide the farmer.

Diagnosis-Prescription Model: The extension worker analyzes the problem and recommends solutions.

2. Group Approach

This method teaches several people at the same time.

  • Meetings
  • Method demonstrations
  • Leadership training
  • Lectures
  • Film forums
  • Field meetings
  • Symposium
  • Panel discussion
  • Group discussion
  • Extension classes
  • Seminar-workshops
  • Educational tours
  • Field days
  • Camping
  • Achievement days
  • Role playing
  • Puppet shows and skits

3. Mass Media Approach

Uses communication channels that reach large audiences.

  • Bulletins
  • Pamphlets
  • Circulars
  • Leaflets
  • News stories
  • Posters
  • Exhibits
  • Radio
  • Television
  • Films

Operational Description of ETM

Individual Approach

1. Farm and Home Visit

An extension worker personally visits the farm or home to gather information, give advice, and encourage improved practices.

2. Office Calls

Farmers visit the extension office for assistance or consultation. This shows willingness to solve problems and learn from experts.

3. Letters and Correspondence

Letters are used to answer inquiries, provide recommendations, and disseminate information about crops, seeds, and pest control.

4. Result Demonstration

This shows the actual result of improved farming practices. It proves effectiveness and encourages farmers to adopt better practices.

Group Approach

1. Meeting

An organized gathering for planning, coordination, discussion, and decision-making.

2. Method Demonstration

Shows how a skill or practice is properly done. Farmers learn by seeing, hearing, discussing, and doing.

3. Lecture

A specialist presents technical information to an audience. It is mostly one-way communication, but questions may follow afterward.

4. Discussion Following a Lecture

Also called lecture forum. It clarifies information and encourages participation.

5. Film Forum

Discussion before and after showing an educational film. It makes learning more interesting and improves understanding.

6. Symposium

A series of talks by different speakers on one topic. It presents different viewpoints and broadens understanding.

7. Panel Discussion

A small group of experts, usually 2 to 8 speakers, discusses a topic before an audience with the guidance of a moderator.

8. Group Discussion

Participants discuss a topic in smaller groups.

  • Brainstorming: Free sharing of ideas and solutions.
  • Open Forum: Questions and clarifications are entertained.
  • Phillips 66: 6 persons discuss for 6 minutes.

9. Extension Classes

Short-term courses on agriculture and family living for farmers and youth.

10. Seminar-Workshop

A training approach involving lectures and practical activities where participants actively perform tasks and exercises.

11. Program Planning Meetings

Meetings conducted for extension program planning with extension workers, community leaders, and organized groups.

12. Educational Tours

Visits to research stations, institutions, or successful farms to observe modern technologies and learn through actual exposure.

13. Field Days

Large gatherings where demonstrations are presented publicly to create awareness and encourage adoption of innovations.

14. Camps

Special group activities commonly used in youth programs for leadership, cooperation, and skills development.

15. Achievement Days and Rallies

Events recognizing accomplishments and successful projects through exhibits, demonstrations, and awarding ceremonies.

16. Role Playing

A dramatization method where participants act out situations using assumed roles. It helps develop communication, problem-solving, and understanding of human behavior.

Mass Media

Mass media includes publications and communication materials used to disseminate agricultural information to large audiences.

1. Posters

Visual materials designed to attract attention. A good poster clearly states what, where, and when.

2. Exhibits

Displays that communicate ideas quickly through visual presentation. They help people understand faster than long explanations.

3. Radio

Radio is one of the fastest and most powerful communication tools. It reaches remote rural areas, promotes adoption of innovations, and increases agricultural awareness.

Media Combination and Use of Audio-Visual Aids

Combining mass media with group discussion creates stronger behavioral change.

  • Video tapes
  • Sound slides
  • Educational films

Audio-visual materials improve learning effectiveness, increase interest, and make lessons more memorable. They should be carefully organized, synchronized, and pretested before use.

Use of Folk Media

Folk media refers to traditional and community-based communication methods. It is participatory, uses local language, and is easily understood by grassroots communities.

Folk media motivates people to change behavior, communicates local concerns effectively, and complements modern communication methods.

Example: Barangay Integrated Development Approach for Nutrition Improvement (BIDANI)
