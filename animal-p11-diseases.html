Common Diseases and Disorders in Dairy Cattle

This lesson covers common infectious, non-infectious, metabolic, parasitic, reproductive, and management-related diseases and disorders affecting dairy cattle.

Common Diseases and Disorders in Dairy Cattle

Common Diseases and Disorders

Diseases and disorders reduce milk production, reproductive efficiency, growth, and profitability.

Disease control is essential in dairy production.

Disease

Disease refers to abnormal conditions affecting body function and productivity.

Disease reduces animal performance.

Infectious Diseases

Infectious diseases are caused by microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites.

Infectious diseases can spread between animals.

Non-Infectious Diseases

Non-infectious diseases are caused by nutritional deficiency, metabolic disorders, or environmental stress.

Non-infectious diseases are not contagious.

Mastitis

Mastitis is inflammation of the udder. It may be caused by bacterial infection and poor milking hygiene.

  • Swollen udder
  • Abnormal milk
  • Reduced milk yield
Mastitis is one of the most important dairy diseases.

Clinical Mastitis

Clinical mastitis shows visible signs such as clots in milk, swelling, and pain.

Clinical mastitis shows obvious symptoms.

Subclinical Mastitis

Subclinical mastitis has no visible signs but reduces milk production.

Subclinical mastitis is difficult to detect.

Prevention of Mastitis

  • Teat dipping
  • Udder sanitation
  • Proper milking hygiene
Proper milking hygiene prevents mastitis.

Milk Fever

Milk fever is a metabolic disorder caused by low blood calcium after calving.

  • Weakness
  • Inability to stand
  • Muscle tremors
Milk fever is associated with calcium deficiency.

Prevention of Milk Fever

Milk fever may be prevented through proper mineral supplementation and balanced feeding.

Proper nutrition prevents milk fever.

Ketosis

Ketosis is a metabolic disorder caused by negative energy balance.

  • Reduced appetite
  • Weight loss
  • Decreased milk yield
Ketosis occurs during energy deficiency.

Prevention of Ketosis

Adequate energy intake during lactation helps prevent ketosis.

Proper feeding prevents ketosis.

Bloat

Bloat is accumulation of gas in the rumen.

  • Swollen abdomen
  • Breathing difficulty
Severe bloat may cause death.

Causes of Bloat

  • Excessive legume feeding
  • Rapid fermentation
Improper feeding may cause bloat.

Acidosis

Acidosis is excessive acidity in the rumen, commonly associated with excessive grain feeding.

  • Diarrhea
  • Reduced appetite
Excess concentrates may cause acidosis.

Laminitis

Laminitis is inflammation of the hoof tissues.

Laminitis causes lameness.

Pneumonia

Pneumonia is inflammation of the lungs.

  • Coughing
  • Fever
  • Difficulty breathing
Pneumonia affects the respiratory system.

Prevention of Pneumonia

  • Proper ventilation
  • Vaccination
  • Sanitation
Good housing reduces pneumonia.

Foot Rot

Foot rot is an infectious disease affecting the hooves.

  • Swelling
  • Lameness
  • Foul odor
Foot rot affects mobility.

Prevention of Foot Rot

  • Clean flooring
  • Foot baths
  • Hoof care
Proper foot care prevents foot rot.

Brucellosis

Brucellosis is a bacterial disease affecting reproduction.

  • Abortion
  • Infertility
Brucellosis causes reproductive losses.

Tuberculosis

Tuberculosis is a chronic bacterial disease affecting lungs and other organs.

Tuberculosis is zoonotic.

Anthrax

Anthrax is an acute bacterial disease caused by Bacillus anthracis.

Anthrax is highly fatal and zoonotic.

Rabies

Rabies is a viral disease affecting the nervous system.

Rabies is transmissible to humans.

Foot-and-Mouth Disease

FMD is a highly contagious viral disease.

  • Mouth lesions
  • Salivation
  • Lameness
FMD spreads rapidly.

Blackleg

Blackleg is a bacterial disease affecting muscles.

Blackleg commonly affects young cattle.

Johne’s Disease

Johne’s disease is a chronic intestinal disease.

  • Chronic diarrhea
  • Weight loss
Johne’s disease reduces productivity.

Liver Fluke

Liver fluke is an internal parasite affecting the liver.

Liver fluke damages liver tissue.

Roundworms

Roundworms are internal parasites affecting digestion and growth.

Heavy infestation reduces productivity.

External Parasites

  • Ticks
  • Lice
  • Mites
External parasites cause irritation and disease transmission.

Tick Infestation

Ticks suck blood and transmit diseases.

Tick control improves animal health.

Mange

Mange is a skin disease caused by mites.

  • Itching
  • Hair loss
  • Skin irritation
Mange affects skin health.

Retained Placenta

Retained placenta occurs when fetal membranes fail to expel after calving.

Retained placenta may cause infection.

Dystocia

Dystocia refers to difficult calving.

Dystocia requires immediate assistance.

Abortion

Abortion is premature expulsion of fetus.

Abortion causes reproductive losses.

Diarrhea

Diarrhea is excessive watery feces caused by infection, poor feeding, or stress.

Severe diarrhea causes dehydration.

Calf Scours

Calf scours is diarrhea in young calves.

Calf scours may cause calf mortality.

Prevention of Calf Scours

  • Colostrum feeding
  • Sanitation
  • Proper nutrition
Good calf management prevents scours.

Lameness

Lameness affects movement and productivity.

  • Hoof diseases
  • Injuries
  • Poor flooring
Lameness reduces milk production.

Heat Stress

Heat stress occurs when cattle cannot effectively release body heat.

  • Panting
  • Reduced feed intake
  • Reduced milk production
Heat stress lowers productivity.

Prevention of Heat Stress

  • Shade
  • Ventilation
  • Water supply
Proper cooling reduces heat stress.

Nutritional Deficiency Disorders

Deficiency disorders result from inadequate nutrients.

Deficiencies reduce animal performance.

Prevention of Diseases

  • Vaccination
  • Sanitation
  • Proper feeding
  • Biosecurity
  • Quarantine
Prevention is better than cure.

Biosecurity

Biosecurity refers to disease prevention measures.

Biosecurity prevents disease spread.

Vaccination

Vaccination stimulates immunity against diseases.

Vaccination protects animals from infection.

Zoonotic Diseases

Zoonotic diseases are transmissible from animals to humans.

  • Rabies
  • Anthrax
  • Tuberculosis
Zoonotic diseases affect public health.

Importance of Disease Control

Disease control improves milk yield, herd health, and profitability.

Disease control improves dairy productivity.
