Introduction to Goat and Sheep Production

Goat

Goat is scientifically known as Capra hircus. Goats are small ruminants raised for meat, milk, skin, manure, and livelihood.

Goat scientific name = Capra hircus

Sheep

Sheep is scientifically known as Ovis aries. Sheep are raised for meat, wool, milk, and skin production.

Sheep scientific name = Ovis aries

Feeding Behavior

  • Goat = browser
  • Sheep = grazer
  • Goat = biological herbicide
Goat feeds on shrubs and leaves while sheep mainly graze on grasses.

Major Goat Breeds

Anglo-Nubian

  • Roman nose
  • Pendulous ears
  • Meat and milk producer
Anglo-Nubian = Roman nose + pendulous ears

Boer Goat

  • Meat-type goat
  • Fast growing
  • Heavy muscling
Boer = meat-type goat

Saanen

  • Dairy goat
  • White color
  • High milk production
Saanen = white dairy goat

La Mancha

  • Very small ears
  • Dairy goat breed
La Mancha = small ears

Breeding and Reproduction

Important Terms

  • Buck = mature male goat
  • Doe = mature female goat
  • Kid = young goat
  • Ram = mature male sheep
  • Ewe = mature female sheep
  • Lamb = young sheep

Reproductive Terms

  • Estrus = heat period
  • Ovulation = release of egg
  • Puberty = sexual maturity
  • Flushing = increased feeding before breeding

Gestation Period

  • Goat = about 150 days
  • Sheep = about 147–150 days
Kidding = goat parturition
Lambing = sheep parturition

Feed Resources and Feeding Management

Feed Resources

  • Roughages = bulky high-fiber feeds
  • Concentrates = nutrient-rich feeds
  • Forages = edible plant materials

Feed Preservation

  • Hay = dried forage
  • Silage = fermented forage

Important Nutrients

  • Water = most essential nutrient
  • Protein = body-building nutrient
  • Carbohydrates = energy source

Special Feeding Practices

  • Colostrum = first milk rich in antibodies
  • Creep feeding = supplemental feeding for young animals

Health Management

Common Diseases

  • Anthrax = Bacillus anthracis
  • Mastitis = udder inflammation
  • Foot rot = hoof infection
  • Pneumonia = lung inflammation
  • Rabies = nervous system disease
  • FMD = highly contagious viral disease

Parasitic Diseases

  • Liver fluke = liver parasite
  • Mange = mite infestation

Metabolic Disorders

  • Bloat = gas accumulation in rumen
  • Ketosis = energy deficiency disorder

Disease Prevention

  • Vaccination = immunity stimulation
  • Deworming = parasite control
  • Quarantine = isolation
  • Biosecurity = disease prevention

General Management Practices

  • Castration = removal of testes
  • Dehorning = horn removal
  • Docking = tail shortening
  • Weaning = separation from mother

Housing Management

Good housing should be:

  • clean
  • dry
  • well ventilated
  • elevated

Zoonotic Diseases

Zoonotic diseases are transmissible from animals to humans.

  • Anthrax
  • Rabies
Zoonotic diseases infect humans.
