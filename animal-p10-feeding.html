Feed Resources and Feeding Management in Beef Cattle

This lesson covers beef cattle nutrients, feed resources, roughages, concentrates, feeding systems, calf feeding, supplementation, feed quality, and board exam recall points.

Feed Resources and Feeding Management

Feed Resources and Feeding Management

Feed resources and feeding management are essential components of beef cattle production because nutrition directly affects growth, reproduction, health, feed efficiency, carcass quality, and profitability.

Proper feeding management improves productivity and animal performance.

Nutrition greatly affects cattle productivity.

Importance of Feeding

Feeding provides nutrients necessary for maintenance, growth, reproduction, lactation, body repair, and energy production.

Feeding supports life and production.

Nutrients

Nutrients are substances required by animals for normal body functions and productivity.

  • Water
  • Carbohydrates
  • Proteins
  • Fats
  • Minerals
  • Vitamins
Nutrients are essential for body functions.

Water

Water is the most essential nutrient in cattle production.

It is needed for digestion, nutrient transport, temperature regulation, waste removal, and metabolism.

Cattle should always have access to clean water.

Water is the most essential nutrient.

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the primary source of energy.

Common sources include grasses, grains, silage, and hay.

Carbohydrates provide energy.

Protein

Protein is essential for muscle growth, tissue repair, enzyme production, and milk production.

Protein deficiency may reduce growth and productivity.

Protein is important for growth and repair.

Fats

Fats provide concentrated energy.

They are used for energy storage, insulation, and absorption of fat-soluble vitamins.

Fats provide concentrated energy.

Minerals

Minerals are required for bone formation, reproduction, metabolism, and nerve function.

  • Calcium
  • Phosphorus
  • Salt
  • Magnesium
Minerals are important for body functions.

Vitamins

Vitamins help regulate body processes and maintain health.

  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin D
  • Vitamin E
Vitamins regulate body functions.

Feed Resources

Feed resources refer to materials used as animal feed.

  • Pasture grasses
  • Legumes
  • Crop residues
  • Hay
  • Silage
  • Concentrates
Feed resources supply nutrients.

Roughages

Roughages are bulky feeds high in fiber and low in digestible nutrients.

  • Grasses
  • Hay
  • Silage
  • Crop residues
Roughages are high-fiber feeds.

Concentrates

Concentrates are feeds rich in digestible nutrients and low in fiber.

  • Corn
  • Rice bran
  • Soybean meal
Concentrates are nutrient-dense feeds.

Forages

Forages are edible plant materials consumed by cattle.

  • Pasture grasses
  • Legumes
  • Forage crops
Forages are major cattle feed sources.

Pasture Grasses

Pasture grasses are important feed resources in grazing systems.

  • Napier grass
  • Guinea grass
  • Para grass
Pasture grasses support grazing systems.

Legumes

Legumes are forage plants high in protein.

  • Ipil-ipil
  • Centrosema
  • Stylo
Legumes improve protein supply.

Crop Residues

Crop residues are agricultural by-products used as feeds.

  • Rice straw
  • Corn stover
  • Sugarcane tops
Crop residues can be used as roughage.

Hay

Hay is dried forage preserved for future feeding.

It is useful as feed reserve during dry season and is easier to store.

Hay = dried preserved forage.

Silage

Silage is fermented forage stored under anaerobic conditions.

It helps preserve feed for long-term use while maintaining nutrient quality.

Silage = fermented forage.

Supplemental Feeding

Supplemental feeding provides additional nutrients beyond forage intake.

Supplementation may include concentrates, minerals, and protein supplements.

Supplementation improves productivity.

Feeding Systems

Common feeding systems include:

  • Grazing system
  • Stall feeding
  • Zero grazing
  • Feedlot feeding
Feeding systems vary with management.

Grazing System

Grazing allows cattle to harvest forage directly from pasture.

It reduces feeding cost and allows natural feeding behavior.

Grazing reduces feeding cost.

Stall Feeding

Stall feeding involves feeding cattle in confinement using harvested feeds.

It allows controlled feeding and reduces pasture damage.

Stall feeding allows controlled feeding.

Zero Grazing

Zero grazing refers to confinement feeding where forage is cut and brought to animals.

Zero grazing = cut-and-carry system.

Feedlot Feeding

Feedlot feeding involves intensive feeding using high-energy rations for rapid growth and fattening.

Feedlot feeding improves weight gain.

Feed Intake

Feed intake refers to the amount of feed consumed by the animal.

It is affected by body size, feed quality, temperature, and health condition.

Feed intake affects growth performance.

Feed Conversion Efficiency

Feed conversion efficiency refers to the ability to convert feed into body weight gain.

Better feed efficiency reduces production cost.

Balanced Ration

A balanced ration contains all nutrients in proper amounts needed by the animal.

Balanced ration supports optimum production.

Maintenance Requirement

Maintenance requirement refers to nutrients needed to maintain normal body functions without gain or loss.

Maintenance nutrients sustain life processes.

Growth Requirement

Growth requirement refers to nutrients needed for body weight gain and muscle development.

Growth nutrients support weight gain.

Lactation Requirement

Lactation requirement refers to nutrients needed for milk production.

Lactation increases nutrient requirement.

Reproductive Requirement

Reproductive requirement refers to nutrients needed for breeding and pregnancy.

Proper nutrition improves fertility.

Feeding Calves

Calves require colostrum, milk, starter feeds, and clean water.

Proper calf feeding improves growth.

Colostrum Feeding

Colostrum should be given immediately after birth because it provides antibodies and immunity.

Colostrum protects calves from disease.

Creep Feeding

Creep feeding refers to supplemental feeding of young calves.

It improves growth and supports earlier weaning.

Creep feeding improves calf growth.

Mineral Supplementation

Mineral supplementation prevents nutritional deficiencies.

Mineral supplements may include salt blocks and mineral mixes.

Mineral supplementation prevents deficiencies.

Feeding Frequency

Feeding frequency depends on production system, feed type, and animal age.

Proper feeding schedule improves intake.

Feed Quality

Good feed quality improves digestibility, growth, health, and productivity.

High-quality feed improves performance.

Feed Storage

Proper feed storage prevents spoilage, mold growth, and nutrient loss.

Proper storage preserves feed quality.

Feed Additives

Feed additives are substances added to improve feed efficiency and animal performance.

  • Vitamins
  • Minerals
  • Probiotics
Feed additives improve performance.

Nutritional Deficiency

Nutritional deficiency occurs when nutrients are insufficient.

It may cause poor growth, low fertility, and weak immunity.

Nutritional deficiency reduces productivity.

Overfeeding

Overfeeding may cause obesity, digestive disorders, and economic loss.

Excess feeding may reduce efficiency.

Underfeeding

Underfeeding may result in weight loss, poor growth, and reproductive problems.

Underfeeding reduces productivity.

Feed Cost

Feed cost is one of the largest expenses in cattle production.

Efficient feeding management reduces production cost.

Feed management affects profitability.

Importance of Feeding Management

Proper feeding management improves growth rate, feed efficiency, carcass quality, reproductive performance, and profitability.

Feeding management improves productivity.

Board Exam Trap Terms

  • Roughages = high-fiber feeds
  • Concentrates = nutrient-dense feeds
  • Hay = dried forage
  • Silage = fermented forage
  • Zero grazing = cut-and-carry system
  • Colostrum = first milk
  • Creep feeding = supplemental feeding for calves
  • Balanced ration = complete nutrient supply
  • Feed conversion efficiency = feed-to-gain efficiency
  • Maintenance requirement = nutrients for body maintenance
These are common board recall terms in feeding management.
