Philippine Agriculture

Spanish Era

Spanish Agricultural Contributions

  • Introduced and acclimatized the myrrh birds (Martinez) from China to fight migratory locust infestation in the Philippines.
  • The Plan General Economico included income-generating monopolies such as tobacco, jareta nut, spirituous liquors, and explosives.

American Occupation

June 23, 1898

Creation of the Department of Agriculture and Manufacturing.

Homestead Law

Every Filipino citizen was given the opportunity to acquire at least 24 hectares of land.

Torrens Act

Safeguarded all titles to cultivated lands owned by private persons.

1902

Creation of the Bureau of Agriculture, now known as the Bureau of Plant Industry, to promote agriculture.

Philippine Exports

Major exports included hemp, sugar, tobacco, copra, and embroideries.

President Manuel L. Quezon

Division of Soil Survey

Created to undertake soil and agronomical surveys.

President Jose P. Laurel

Rude Awakening Period

This period highlighted the realization that the Philippines did not raise enough food for its people and was not self-sufficient.

President Manuel A. Roxas

Parity Rights to Americans

Allowed Americans equal rights in the disposition, exploitation, development, and utilization of agricultural, timber, and mineral lands of the public domain.

Rehabilitation Finance Corporation

The Rehabilitation Finance Corporation (RFC), now known as the Development Bank of the Philippines (DBP), was created for the rehabilitation and development of agriculture.

President Elpidio Quirino

Bureau of Agricultural Extension

Made the rice industry the first commodity sector to have an integrated national planning system.

Rural Bank Act

Organized a system of rural banks with substantial capital participation from the government.

Agricultural Credit Cooperative Financing Administration (ACCFA)

Authorized to organize the Farmers Cooperative Marketing Associations (FACOMAS).

President Ramon Magsaysay

UN-FAO Membership

The Philippines became a member of the United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization (UN-FAO).

National Rice and Corn Program

Launched together with the Rice and Corn Coordinating Council.

Rice and Corn Coordinating Council

Became the forerunner of the National Food and Agriculture Council (NFAC), now known as the National Agriculture and Fishery Council (NAFC).

President Carlos P. Garcia

Austerity Program

Encouraged the people to lead simple lives and avoid luxurious lifestyles.

Filipino First Policy

Encouraged patronage of Filipino-made products and promoted Filipino labor.

President Diosdado Macapagal

International Rice Research Institute (IRRI)

Established in UPLB to address problems in the sugar industry and rice production.

President Ferdinand Marcos

RA 6389

Provided automatic conversion of share tenancy to leasehold and lowered land retention rates while supporting the creation of DAR.

Masagana 99

A program involving massive dispersal of modern technology including high-yielding varieties, fertilizers, and pesticides.

Replanting Program for Coconut

Implemented to improve coconut productivity and rehabilitation.

President Corazon Aquino

CARP

Implementation of the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Program.

President Fidel V. Ramos

MDATP

Medium Term Agricultural Development Program.

GPEP

Grains Production Enhancement Program.

KCCDP

Key Commercial Crops Development Program.

MTLDP

Medium Term Livestock Development Program.

MTFMDP

Medium Term Fisheries Management Development Program.

GATT Safety Measure

Adopted to protect agricultural commodities and local industries.

President Joseph E. Estrada

CONCORD

Constitutional Correction for Development.

Agrikulturang Makamasa

Biotechnology was approved in the Philippines during his administration.

President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo

Ginintuang Masaganang Ani (GMA)

A nationwide assistance program for rural employment and agricultural services.

Hybrid Rice Commercialization Program

Promoted hybrid rice production to improve rice productivity.
