🧱 Part 3: Soil Profile & Horizons

Soil Profile

  • A soil profile is a vertical section of the soil from the surface down to the parent material.
  • It shows the different layers or horizons formed through soil-forming processes.
  • Each horizon has distinct physical, chemical, and biological properties.

Soil Horizon

  • A soil horizon is a layer of soil approximately parallel to the land surface.
  • It differs from adjacent layers in color, texture, structure, consistency, and composition.

Master Horizons

O Horizon

  • Organic layer found at the surface.
  • Composed of decomposing plant materials.

A Horizon

  • Topsoil layer with organic matter.
  • Dark in color due to humus.

E Horizon

  • Zone of leaching (eluviation).
  • Light-colored layer.

B Horizon

  • Subsoil where materials accumulate (illuviation).

C Horizon

  • Partially weathered parent material.

R Horizon

  • Unweathered bedrock.

Sequence of Soil Horizons

  • O - A - E - B - C - R
A = topsoil | B = subsoil | C = parent material

Pedogenic Processes

  • Additions
  • Losses

Eluviation & Illuviation

  • Eluviation = removal
  • Illuviation = accumulation
