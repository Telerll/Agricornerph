Some Approaches to Rural Change

The Concentrated Approach

Under this approach, the change agent does not cover the whole area at once but limits operation to a specific area or project.

The extension worker works closely with a limited number of individuals known as cooperators, with the hope that new information or ideas will later spread to other farmers in the community.

Characteristics

  • Works with a limited number of individuals called cooperators.
  • Focuses on a limited area or project.
  • Concentrates on limited priority problems.
  • Suitable for newly covered areas handled by technicians or change agents.

The Team Approach

In this approach, technicians with different fields of specialization work together as a team in developing an area or community.

The team may include:

  • Livestock technician
  • Crop technician
  • Farm management technician
  • Home management technician

Team members reinforce each other’s activities in solving the clientele’s field problems.

This approach usually works well in areas far from agricultural experiment stations or agricultural colleges.

The Cooperative Approach

In this approach, different agencies are involved in rural development activities.

Each agency has its own specialized functions as stated in the program.

Since several agencies participate, written agreements are needed to clearly define the responsibilities of each participating agency.

The Institutional Approach

The main goal of this approach is to organize farmers into associations so they can eventually manage their own programs and activities.

Examples

  • Farmers’ associations
  • Samahang Nayon
  • Cooperatives

Organized groups are used as instruments for rural development activities.

Utilizing Local Leaders

Formal and informal leaders are indispensable in rural development work.

They assist in carrying out development programs in the locality and serve as the link between the people and the extension service.

Proper selection and training should be conducted before local leaders are put into action.
