💪 Muscular System of Farm Animals

Muscular System

Muscular system is the body system responsible for movement of the animal body and internal organs.

It works with the skeletal system to produce locomotion and maintain posture.

Functions of the Muscular System

Movement

Movement is the production of motion of the body, limbs, and internal organs.

Posture

Posture is the maintenance of body position and balance.

Heat Production

Heat production is the generation of heat during muscle contraction.

Support

Support is the role of muscles in stabilizing the body and joints.

Types of Muscle Tissue

Skeletal Muscle

Skeletal muscle is voluntary muscle attached to bones.

It is responsible for body movement and meat production.

Smooth Muscle

Smooth muscle is involuntary muscle found in internal organs.

It controls movement of food, fluids, and air.

Cardiac Muscle

Cardiac muscle is involuntary muscle found only in the heart.

It is responsible for pumping blood.

Structure of Skeletal Muscle

Muscle Fiber

Muscle fiber is a single muscle cell.

Myofibrils

Myofibrils are contractile threads inside muscle fibers.

Sarcolemma

Sarcolemma is the cell membrane of the muscle fiber.

Sarcoplasm

Sarcoplasm is the cytoplasm of the muscle cell.

Muscle Attachment

Tendon

Tendon is a strong fibrous tissue that connects muscle to bone.

Aponeurosis

Aponeurosis is a flat, sheet-like tendon that connects muscle to bone or muscle to muscle.

How Muscles Work

Contraction

Contraction is the shortening of muscle fibers to produce force.

Relaxation

Relaxation is the return of muscle to resting state.

Sliding Filament Theory

Sliding filament theory explains that muscle contraction occurs when actin and myosin filaments slide past each other, causing shortening of the muscle.

Muscles pull, not push.

Antagonistic Muscles

Antagonistic muscles are muscle pairs where one contracts and the other relaxes.

Example in Farm Animals

  • Biceps brachii – flexor muscle that bends the limb
  • Triceps brachii – extensor muscle that straightens the limb

Major Muscle Groups in Farm Animals

Neck Muscles

Support and move the head and neck.

Shoulder Muscles

Help move the forelimbs.

Back Muscles

Maintain posture and support the trunk.

Abdominal Muscles

Support abdominal organs and assist in breathing.

Hindquarter Muscles

Important for locomotion and meat production.

Muscle Growth

Hypertrophy

Hypertrophy is the increase in muscle size due to enlargement of muscle fibers.

This is the main type of muscle growth after birth.

Hyperplasia

Hyperplasia is the increase in the number of muscle cells.

This occurs mainly before birth or during the prenatal stage.

Postnatal muscle growth is mainly due to hypertrophy.

Muscle to Meat Conversion

Rigor Mortis

Rigor mortis is the stiffening of muscles after death due to permanent contraction.

It is important in meat quality, tenderness, and carcass evaluation.

Importance of the Muscular System in Animal Science

  • Important for locomotion and animal performance
  • Important in animal handling and work
  • Major factor in meat production
  • Basis for evaluating carcass quality

Muscles and Animal Movement

Muscles work with bones and joints to allow:

  • Walking
  • Running
  • Grazing
  • Standing

Differences Among Muscle Types

Skeletal Muscle

  • Voluntary
  • Attached to bones
  • Produces body movement

Smooth Muscle

  • Involuntary
  • Found in internal organs
  • Controls internal movement

Cardiac Muscle

  • Involuntary
  • Found in the heart
  • Pumps blood

Importance in Farm Animals

Muscular development affects:

  • Strength
  • Endurance
  • Meat yield
  • Body conformation

Final Summary

Muscular system = movement + posture + heat

Tendon = muscle to bone
Aponeurosis = sheet-like tendon

Muscles pull, not push
Sliding filament = actin + myosin

Hypertrophy = postnatal growth
Hyperplasia = prenatal growth

Rigor mortis = muscle to meat
