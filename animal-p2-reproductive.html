🐄 Reproductive System of Farm Animals

Reproductive System

Reproductive system is the body system responsible for producing offspring and ensuring continuation of the species.

Functions of the Reproductive System

  • Produces sex cells (sperm and ova)
  • Produces reproductive hormones
  • Allows fertilization
  • Ensures continuation of species

Male Reproductive System

Testes

Testes produce sperm and testosterone.

Scrotum

Scrotum holds the testes and regulates temperature.

Sperm production requires lower temperature than body temperature.

Epididymis

Epididymis stores, matures, and transports sperm.

Vas Deferens

Vas deferens transports sperm from epididymis to urethra.

Accessory Glands

Accessory glands produce seminal fluid.

  • Seminal vesicles
  • Prostate gland
  • Bulbourethral gland

Penis

Penis is the organ of copulation that delivers semen.

Sigmoid Flexure

Sigmoid flexure is an S-shaped bend in the penis of bulls, rams, and boars.

It allows retraction and extension of the penis.

Sigmoid flexure is found in bulls, rams, and boars.

Male Reproductive Physiology

Spermatogenesis

Spermatogenesis is the process of sperm production in the testes.

It is controlled by FSH and testosterone.

Testosterone

Testosterone develops male traits, supports libido, and supports sperm production.

Female Reproductive System

Ovaries

Ovaries produce ova, estrogen, and progesterone.

Oviduct (Fallopian Tube)

Oviduct is the site of fertilization.

Uterus

Uterus is the site of fetal development.

Cervix

Cervix is the muscular gateway between the uterus and vagina.

Vagina

Vagina is the organ of copulation and birth canal.

Vulva

Vulva is the external female reproductive organ.

Female Reproductive Physiology

Oogenesis

Oogenesis is the formation of ova in the ovary.

Follicle

Follicle is the structure containing a developing egg.

Ovulation

Ovulation is the release of a mature egg from the ovary.

Corpus Luteum (CL)

Corpus luteum is the structure formed after ovulation.

It produces progesterone.

Estrous Cycle

Proestrus

Follicle develops.

Estrus

Female is in heat and accepts the male.

This is the best breeding time.

Metestrus

Ovulation occurs and corpus luteum forms.

Diestrus

Corpus luteum is functional and progesterone is high.

Prostaglandin (PGF2α)

Prostaglandin F2α (PGF2α) causes luteolysis or destruction of the corpus luteum.

  • Destroys corpus luteum
  • Lowers progesterone
  • Restarts estrous cycle if not pregnant
PGF2α = cycle reset hormone

Hormonal Control of Reproduction

Hypothalamus → GnRH → Pituitary → FSH/LH → Ovary/Testes
  • FSH = follicle development / sperm production
  • LH = ovulation / testosterone release
  • Estrogen = heat signs
  • Progesterone = maintains pregnancy
  • PGF2α = destroys CL

Fertilization

Fertilization is the union of sperm and egg.

It occurs in the oviduct.

Gestation

Gestation is the pregnancy period from conception to birth.

  • Cow ≈ 283 days
  • Sow ≈ 114 days
  • Ewe ≈ 150 days

Parturition

Parturition is the process of giving birth.

Oxytocin in Parturition

Oxytocin stimulates uterine contractions during birth.

Lactation

Lactation is milk production after parturition.

Prolactin

Prolactin stimulates milk synthesis.

Oxytocin

Oxytocin stimulates milk let-down or milk ejection.

Prolactin = makes milk
Oxytocin = releases milk + uterine contraction

Reproduction in Farm Animals

  • Determines fertility
  • Affects productivity
  • Critical in herd management

Reproductive Efficiency

  • Calving interval
  • Conception rate
  • Litter size
  • Milk production
