🌾 Part 9: Soil Fertility & Nutrition

Soil Fertility

  • Soil fertility is the ability of soil to supply essential nutrients to plants in adequate amounts.
  • It is a key factor in crop productivity.

Essential Plant Nutrients

  • Plants require essential elements for growth and development.
  • These nutrients are classified into macronutrients and micronutrients.

Macronutrients

Primary Macronutrients

  • Nitrogen (N) – promotes leaf and vegetative growth
  • Phosphorus (P) – supports root development and flowering
  • Potassium (K) – improves overall plant health and disease resistance

Secondary Macronutrients

  • Calcium (Ca)
  • Magnesium (Mg)
  • Sulfur (S)

Micronutrients

  • Required in small amounts but still essential.
  • Examples:
    • Iron (Fe)
    • Zinc (Zn)
    • Copper (Cu)
    • Boron (B)
    • Manganese (Mn)
    • Molybdenum (Mo)
    • Chlorine (Cl)

Nutrient Movement in Soil

  • Mass Flow – nutrients move with water toward roots
  • Diffusion – movement from high to low concentration
  • Root Interception – roots grow and contact nutrients

Fertilizers

Organic Fertilizers

  • Derived from plant or animal materials
  • Examples: compost, manure
  • Improve soil structure and organic matter

Inorganic Fertilizers

  • Manufactured chemical fertilizers
  • Provide nutrients quickly
  • Examples: urea, ammonium sulfate

Nutrient Deficiency Symptoms

  • Nitrogen deficiency – yellowing of leaves (chlorosis)
  • Phosphorus deficiency – poor root growth, purplish leaves
  • Potassium deficiency – leaf edge burning

Factors Affecting Soil Fertility

  • Soil pH
  • Organic matter content
  • Soil texture and structure
  • Climate and rainfall
  • Crop management practices

Soil Fertility Management

  • Balanced fertilization
  • Use of organic matter
  • Crop rotation
  • Soil testing
  • Proper irrigation and drainage

Good Fertility Condition of Soil

  • Adequate nutrient supply
  • Balanced nutrient availability
  • Healthy plant growth and high yield
