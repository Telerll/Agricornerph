Slaughter and Processing

Final Integration • Ultra Master Summary

1. Slaughter and Processing Overview

Slaughter and processing cover the conversion of live animals into safe, usable, and marketable meat products.

This includes:

  • Humane slaughter
  • Meat inspection
  • Postmortem changes
  • Meat grading
  • Processing and preservation
  • By-product utilization
Slaughter and processing convert live animals into safe meat and useful products.

2. Slaughtering Procedures

Slaughtering is the step-by-step conversion of live animals into carcasses for meat production.

Main steps include:

  • Receiving
  • Lairage (resting and fasting)
  • Antemortem inspection
  • Stunning
  • Sticking
  • Bleeding / Exsanguination
  • Skinning / Scalding
  • Evisceration
  • Washing
  • Chilling
Proper slaughter = humane killing + clean carcass.

3. Meat Inspection

Meat inspection ensures that only safe and wholesome meat reaches consumers.

  • Antemortem inspection = live animal inspection
  • Postmortem inspection = carcass and organ inspection

Inspection protects public health by detecting:

  • Disease
  • Contamination
  • Abnormal carcasses
  • Unfit meat
Meat inspection protects consumers and public health.

4. Postmortem Changes in Meat

After slaughter, muscle undergoes biochemical changes and becomes meat.

Main sequence:

  • Glycogen breakdown
  • Lactic acid formation
  • pH decline
  • Rigor mortis
  • Aging / tenderization
Glycogen → Lactic Acid → pH Decline → Rigor Mortis → Aging

5. Meat Quality and Grading

Meat quality refers to the traits that determine meat value, acceptability, and eating satisfaction.

Main quality traits:

  • Color
  • Tenderness
  • Juiciness
  • Flavor
  • Marbling

Grading evaluates meat by:

  • Quality Grade = eating quality
  • Yield Grade = usable lean meat
Quality = palatability • Yield = cutability.

6. Meat Processing and Preservation

Meat processing converts fresh meat into value-added products with improved safety and shelf life.

Main preservation methods:

  • Chilling
  • Freezing
  • Curing
  • Drying
  • Smoking
  • Canning
  • Fermentation

Important processing concepts:

  • Myosin = main emulsifier
  • Sodium nitrite = prevents botulism
  • Danger Zone = 5°C to 60°C
Processing preserves meat, improves safety, and adds value.

7. By-products and Waste Utilization

By-products are secondary animal materials with economic value beyond the main carcass meat.

  • Edible by-products = liver, heart, kidney, tripe
  • Inedible by-products = hide, bones, horns, hooves, feathers

Waste can be converted into useful products through rendering and waste utilization.

Waste can become profit through proper utilization.
