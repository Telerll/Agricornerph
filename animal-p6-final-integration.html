Part 6 Final Integration: Poultry Production

Poultry Production

Poultry production is the branch of animal science concerned with the breeding, raising, management, and utilization of domesticated birds for food and economic purposes.

  • Chicken
  • Duck
  • Turkey
  • Quail
  • Goose
Poultry production is the efficient raising of birds for egg and meat production.

Importance of Poultry Production

  • Provides affordable animal protein
  • Supports food security
  • Generates income
  • Produces eggs and meat efficiently
  • Has fast turnover and high feed efficiency
Poultry is one of the most efficient animal industries.

Poultry Breeds and Classes

  • American = dual-purpose, yellow skin
  • English = large dual-purpose, white skin
  • Mediterranean = egg-type, white ear lobes
  • Asiatic = heavy birds, feathered shanks
Breeds are classified by origin, body type, and production use.

Poultry Anatomy

Poultry anatomy includes external parts, internal organs, and body systems.

  • Comb = health indicator
  • Beak = feeding and pecking
  • Breast = major meat region
  • Vent = egg laying and excretion
  • Shank = breed and age clue
Poultry anatomy is important for identification and management.

Housing and Management

  • Housing = protection and comfort
  • Brooding = chick care
  • Ventilation = fresh air
  • Lighting = controls activity and laying
  • Litter management = bedding control
  • Biosecurity = disease prevention
Good housing improves poultry comfort, health, and productivity.

Nutrition and Feeding

  • Water = most important nutrient
  • Carbohydrates = energy
  • Protein = growth
  • Amino acids = protein units
  • Vitamins = regulation
  • Minerals = bones and eggshells
Good nutrition supports growth, health, egg production, and meat yield.

Nutrition Board Traps

  • Curled toe = Riboflavin deficiency
  • Crazy chick = Vitamin E deficiency
  • Rickets = Vitamin D / Calcium / Phosphorus deficiency
  • Soft shell = Calcium deficiency
  • Perosis = Manganese deficiency
  • Star-gazing = Thiamine deficiency
Board exams often give symptoms first, then ask the missing nutrient.

Reproduction and Incubation

  • Left ovary = functional ovary in hens
  • Infundibulum = site of fertilization
  • Magnum = albumen formation
  • Isthmus = shell membrane formation
  • Uterus / shell gland = shell formation
  • Utero-vaginal junction = sperm storage site
Reproduction produces fertile eggs; incubation develops chicks.

Incubation Golden Numbers

  • Temperature = 37.5°C / 99.5°F
  • Chicken = 21 days
  • Duck / Peking = 28 days
  • Muscovy duck = 35 days
  • Turkey = 28 days
  • Quail = 16–18 days
Muscovy duck = 35 days is a common board exam outlier.

Egg and Meat Production

  • Layer = egg production
  • Broiler = meat production
  • Point of lay = 18–20 weeks
  • Lighting = 14–16 hours for layers
  • Broiler market age = 5–7 weeks
  • FCR = feed efficiency
Layers produce eggs; broilers produce meat.

Poultry Health and Disease Management

  • Sanitation = cleanliness
  • Disinfection = kills pathogens
  • Biosecurity = blocks disease entry
  • Vaccination = stimulates immunity
  • Isolation = separates sick birds
  • Quarantine = restricts exposed birds
Prevention is the most effective disease control strategy.

Disease Board Traps

  • Newcastle disease = twisting neck
  • Fowl pox = skin lesions
  • IBD / Gumboro = bursa damage
  • Coccidiosis = bloody diarrhea
  • Infectious bronchitis = respiratory disease
  • Marek’s disease = paralysis
Disease recognition is a major poultry board exam topic.

Poultry production follows one complete chain:

Breed Selection → Housing → Feeding → Reproduction → Incubation → Egg / Meat Production → Health Management
