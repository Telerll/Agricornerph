AgLE REVIEWER MODULE

🌾 Major Pests of Important Crops

Entomology and Pest Management Reviewer

Introduction

Major pests of important crops are insects, mollusks, and other organisms that regularly damage economically important crops such as rice, corn, sugarcane, vegetables, legumes, root crops, fruits, coconut, coffee, and cacao.

This topic is highly important in the AgLE Board Examination because many questions ask for the pest, scientific name, crop attacked, order, damage symptom, and natural enemy.

CEREALS

Rice

1. Green Leafhopper

Scientific Name: Nephotettix virescens / Nephotettix nigropictus

Order: Hemiptera / Homoptera in older classification

Importance

Green leafhopper is one of the most important rice pests because it transmits Rice Tungro Virus.

Nature of Damage

  • Piercing-sucking feeder
  • Sucks plant sap from rice plants
  • Weakens plants and causes yellowing
  • Transmits tungro virus

Symptoms

  • Yellow-orange discoloration
  • Stunting
  • Reduced tillering
  • Poor grain production

Board Tip: Green Leafhopper = Tungro vector.

2. Brown Planthopper

Scientific Name: Nilaparvata lugens

Order: Hemiptera

Importance

Brown planthopper is a destructive rice pest known for causing hopperburn and transmitting viral diseases.

Nature of Damage

  • Sucks sap from the base of rice plants
  • Causes drying and wilting
  • Transmits grassy stunt virus
  • Transmits ragged stunt virus

Characteristic Damage

Hopperburn is the drying and browning of rice plants due to heavy planthopper feeding.

Board Tip: Brown Planthopper = Hopperburn.

3. Rice Bug

Scientific Name: Leptocorisa oratorius

Order: Hemiptera

Nature of Damage

Rice bug attacks rice during the milk stage by sucking developing grains.

Symptoms

  • Empty grains
  • Unfilled grains
  • Discolored grains
  • Poor grain quality

Board Tip: Rice Bug = pest of rice at milk stage.

4. Rice Stem Borers

Order: Lepidoptera

Major Species

  • Striped stem borer — Chilo suppressalis
  • Yellow stem borer — Scirpophaga incertulas
  • Pink stem borer — Sesamia inferens
  • White stem borer — Scirpophaga innotata

Nature of Damage

Larvae bore inside rice stems and damage the growing point or panicle.

Classic Symptoms

  • Deadheart — central shoot dries during vegetative stage.
  • Whitehead — empty white panicles during reproductive stage.

Board Tip: Deadheart = vegetative stage; Whitehead = reproductive stage.

5. Golden Apple Snail

Scientific Name: Pomacea canaliculata

Group: Mollusca / Megastropoda

Importance

Golden apple snail is a destructive pest of direct-seeded rice and newly transplanted rice seedlings.

Nature of Damage

  • Feeds on young rice seedlings
  • Causes missing hills
  • Reduces plant stand

Identification

  • Bright pink egg masses
  • Eggs are laid above water surface

Board Tip: Golden Apple Snail = pink eggs above water.

Corn

1. Asian Corn Borer

Scientific Name: Ostrinia furnacalis

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae bore into corn stalks, tassels, and ears, causing tunneling, broken stalks, poor ear development, and yield reduction.

Board Tip: Asian Corn Borer = major corn borer.

2. Corn Semi-looper

Scientific Name: Chrysodeixis chalcites

Larvae feed on corn leaves and may cause defoliation, especially when populations are high.

3. Corn Seedling Maggot

Scientific Name: Atherigona oryzae

Order: Diptera

Maggots attack seedlings and may cause wilting, deadheart-like symptoms, and poor crop establishment.

4. Corn Earworm

Scientific Name: Helicoverpa armigera

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae feed on corn ears, silks, and kernels, reducing grain quality and market value.

5. White Grub

Scientific Name: Leucopholis irrorata

Order: Coleoptera

White grubs feed on roots, causing wilting, poor growth, and plant death.

Stored Grains

1. Rice Weevil

Scientific Name: Sitophilus oryzae

Order: Coleoptera

Rice weevil attacks stored rice and grains. Adults and larvae damage kernels, causing holes, powdery residues, weight loss, and reduced seed viability.

2. Corn Weevil

Scientific Name: Sitophilus zeamais

Order: Coleoptera

Corn weevil damages stored corn grains and causes serious storage losses.

3. Rice Moth

Scientific Name: Corcyra cephalonica

Rice moth larvae feed on stored grains and produce webbing and contamination.

SUGAR CROP

Sugarcane

1. Root Grub

Scientific Name: Leucopholis irrorata

Order: Coleoptera

Root grubs feed on sugarcane roots, causing poor growth, wilting, lodging, and reduced cane yield.

2. Sugarcane Deadheart Borer

Scientific Name: Tetramoera schistaceana

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae bore into young shoots and cause deadheart symptoms in sugarcane.

3. Yellow Tip Borer

Scientific Name: Chilo infuscatellus

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae bore into shoots and stems, causing drying of central leaves and reduced cane growth.

VEGETABLE CROPS

Crucifers

Crucifers include pechay, cabbage, cauliflower, radish, and related crops.

1. Diamondback Moth

Scientific Name: Plutella xylostella

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae feed on leaves and create holes or windowpane damage. It is a major pest of cabbage and other crucifers.

Board Tip: Diamondback moth = key pest of crucifers.

2. Cabbage Worm

Scientific Name: Crocidolomia binotalis

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae feed on leaves and growing points, causing defoliation and head damage in cabbage.

3. Cutworm

Scientific Name: Spodoptera litura

Order: Lepidoptera

Cutworms cut seedlings near the soil surface and may cause serious stand reduction.

Cucurbits

Cucurbits include bottle gourd, ampalaya, squash, patola, and related crops.

1. Yellow Squash Beetle

Scientific Name: Aulacophora similis

Order: Coleoptera

Adults feed on leaves, while larvae may feed on roots and underground parts.

2. Melon Fruit Fly

Scientific Name: Bactrocera cucurbitae

Females lay eggs in fruits. Larvae feed inside fruits, causing rotting, premature fruit drop, and market rejection.

Solanaceous Crops

Solanaceous crops include tomato, eggplant, pepper, and related crops.

1. Tomato Fruitworm

Scientific Name: Helicoverpa armigera

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae bore into tomato fruits and cause holes, rotting, and reduced market quality.

2. Tomato Lady Beetle

Scientific Name: Epilachna vigintisexpunctata philippinensis

Order: Coleoptera

Adults and larvae feed on leaves and may cause skeletonization.

3. Eggplant Fruit and Shoot Borer

Scientific Name: Leucinodes orbonalis

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae bore into shoots and fruits, causing wilting of shoots and damaged fruits.

Board Tip: Eggplant fruit and shoot borer = major eggplant pest.

Malvaceous Crops

Malvaceous crops include cotton, okra, and kenaf.

1. Cotton Stainer

Scientific Name: Dysdercus cingulatus

Order: Hemiptera

Cotton stainer sucks sap from cotton bolls and stains cotton fibers, reducing fiber quality.

2. Cotton Bollworm

Scientific Name: Helicoverpa armigera

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae feed on cotton squares, flowers, and bolls, causing yield loss.

Legumes

Legumes include cowpea, mungbean, soybean, bush sitao, and related crops.

1. Bean Fly

Scientific Name: Ophiomyia phaseoli

Order: Diptera

Larvae tunnel inside stems, causing swelling, cracking, wilting, and seedling death.

2. Aphids

Scientific Name: Aphis craccivora

Order: Homoptera / Hemiptera

Aphids suck plant sap and may transmit plant viruses. Heavy infestation causes curling, stunting, and honeydew deposits.

3. Bean Pod Borers

Scientific Names: Maruca testulalis and Etiella zinckenella

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae bore into pods and damage developing seeds.

4. Leaffolders

Scientific Name: Homona coffearia

Order: Homoptera in source notes

Larvae fold or roll leaves and feed inside, reducing photosynthetic area.

5. Leafhopper

Scientific Name: Empoasca ricini

Order: Homoptera / Hemiptera

Leafhoppers suck sap and cause yellowing, curling, and hopperburn-like symptoms.

6. Bean Lycaenid

Scientific Name: Euchrysops cnejus

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae attack flowers and pods of legumes, reducing seed production.

ROOT CROPS

Sweet Potato Weevil

Scientific Name: Cylas formicarius formicarius

Order: Coleoptera

Larvae bore into sweet potato vines and tubers, causing bitter taste, tunnels, rotting, and reduced marketability.

FRUIT CROPS

Mango

1. Mango Leafhopper

Scientific Names: Idioscopus niveosparsus / Idioscopus clypealis

Order: Hemiptera

Mango leafhoppers suck sap from flowers and shoots. They cause flower drop, poor fruit set, and honeydew that promotes sooty mold.

Board Tip: Mango leafhopper = major pest during flowering.

2. Philippine Fruit Fly

Scientific Name: Bactrocera philippinensis

Order: Diptera

Females lay eggs in fruits. Maggots feed inside fruits, causing fruit rot and premature fruit drop.

3. Mango Twig Borer

Scientific Names: Niphonoclea albata / Niphonoclea capito

Larvae bore into twigs and branches, causing dieback and weakening of mango shoots.

Banana

1. Banana Leafroller

Scientific Name: Erionatha thrax

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae roll banana leaves and feed inside, reducing photosynthetic area.

2. Banana Weevil

Scientific Name: Cosmopolites sordidus

Order: Coleoptera

Larvae bore into the corm, weakening plants and causing poor growth, lodging, and yield reduction.

3. Abaca Aphid

Scientific Name: Pentalonia nigronervosa

Order: Hemiptera

Abaca aphid is important because it transmits bunchy top disease in banana and abaca.

Board Tip: Abaca aphid = vector of bunchy top disease.

Coconut

1. Asiatic Palm Weevil

Scientific Name: Rhynchophorus ferrugineus

Order: Coleoptera

Larvae bore into palm tissues, causing internal damage, wilting, and possible palm death.

2. Coconut Rhinoceros Beetle

Scientific Name: Oryctes rhinoceros

Order: Coleoptera

Adults bore into the crown and unopened fronds, producing V-shaped cuts when leaves open.

Board Tip: Rhinoceros beetle = V-shaped cuts on coconut fronds.

Coffee

1. Coffee Berry Borer

Scientific Name: Hypothenemus hampei

Order: Coleoptera

Female beetles bore into coffee berries and damage seeds, reducing quality and yield.

2. Coffee Leaffolder

Scientific Name: Homona coffearia

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae fold leaves and feed inside, reducing leaf area and plant vigor.

Cacao

1. Pachyrrhynchid Beetle

Scientific Name: Pachyrrhynchus moniliferus

Order: Coleoptera

Adults feed on leaves and may cause defoliation of cacao seedlings and young trees.

2. Pink Mealybug

Scientific Name: Planococcus lilacinus

Order: Hemiptera

Mealybugs suck sap and produce honeydew, which encourages sooty mold.

3. Cacao Pod Borer

Scientific Name: Conopomorpha cramerella

Order: Lepidoptera

Larvae bore into cacao pods and damage beans, causing poor bean development and reduced yield.

4. Mosquito Bugs

Scientific Names: Helopeltis collaris / Helopeltis bakeri

Order: Hemiptera

Mosquito bugs suck sap from shoots and pods, causing lesions, dieback, and pod damage.

NATURAL ENEMIES

A. Parasitoids

Parasitoids are organisms that require only one host to complete their life cycle. A parasitoid is usually smaller than its host and eventually kills the host.

1. Trichogramma evanescens

Order: Hymenoptera

Parasitizes eggs of corn borer.

2. Xanthopimpla stemmator

Order: Hymenoptera

Parasitizes larvae of striped stem borer.

3. Diadegma semiclausum / Diadegma insulare

Important parasitoid of diamondback moth.

4. Cotesia glomerata / Cotesia plutellae

Small dark-colored wasps that attack diamondback moth and Asian corn borer.

B. Predators

Predators feed on several prey individuals to complete their life cycle.

1. Coccinellid Beetles

Cheilomenes sexmaculatus and Micraspis discolor feed on aphids and other soft-bodied insects.

2. Brown Lacewing

Pseudomicromus igorotus feeds on aphids.

3. Wolf Spiders

Lycosa pseudoannulata preys on aphids, leafhoppers, planthoppers, and larvae.

4. Earwigs

Euborellia annulata preys on eggs, larvae, and pupae of lepidopterans, coleopterans, and dipterans.

5. Flower Bug

Orius tantillus preys on eggs and larvae of lepidopterans.

6. Praying Mantids

Tendora sp. preys on other insects.

C. Pathogens

Pathogens are microorganisms that cause disease in pests.

1. Metarhizium anisopliae

A fungal pathogen that infects rhinoceros beetle grubs, especially in coconut pest management.

2. Bacillus thuringiensis

A bacterial pathogen that produces toxins that kill larvae of diamondback moth and other caterpillars.

ALE Quick Facts

  • Green leafhopper transmits tungro virus.
  • Brown planthopper causes hopperburn.
  • Rice bug attacks rice during milk stage.
  • Rice stem borers cause deadheart and whitehead.
  • Golden apple snail lays pink eggs above water.
  • Asian corn borer is a major corn pest.
  • Diamondback moth is a major pest of crucifers.
  • Eggplant fruit and shoot borer attacks eggplant shoots and fruits.
  • Abaca aphid transmits bunchy top disease.
  • Coconut rhinoceros beetle causes V-shaped cuts on fronds.
  • Trichogramma is an egg parasitoid.
  • Ladybird beetles are predators of aphids.
  • Bacillus thuringiensis is a microbial insect pathogen.

ALE High-Yield Exam Tips

Board Trap #1

Green leafhopper is important not only because of sap sucking but mainly because it transmits tungro virus.

Board Trap #2

Brown planthopper is associated with hopperburn, grassy stunt, and ragged stunt.

Board Trap #3

Deadheart occurs during vegetative stage, while whitehead occurs during reproductive stage.

Board Trap #4

Diamondback moth is the classic key pest of crucifers.

Board Trap #5

Parasitoids usually kill one host, while predators kill many prey.

Summary

Major pests of important crops include insects and other organisms that attack cereals, sugarcane, vegetables, legumes, root crops, fruit crops, coconut, coffee, cacao, and stored grains. Proper knowledge of pest names, scientific names, host crops, orders, symptoms, and natural enemies is essential for accurate diagnosis and effective pest management. This topic is highly important in the AgLE Board Examination because many questions are based on crop-pest association and characteristic damage symptoms.
