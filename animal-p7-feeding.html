Swine Feeding Systems and Feeding Management

Swine feeding systems and feeding management refer to the methods, practices, and control measures used in supplying feed to pigs according to their age, physiological stage, and nutrient requirements.

It includes feeding systems, feed allowance, feed ingredients, feed formulation, feeding schedule, feeding stages, feed efficiency, and feeding management.

Swine feeding systems ensure proper feed delivery and efficient production.

Feeding System

A feeding system is the method used to provide feed to pigs.

Feeding system is the way feed is supplied to pigs.

Importance of Feeding Systems

  • Improves feed efficiency
  • Reduces feed waste
  • Improves growth
  • Supports reproduction
  • Lowers production cost
  • Improves profitability
Proper feeding systems improve efficiency and profitability.

Feeding Management

Feeding management is the proper planning, scheduling, and control of feeding practices in swine production.

Feeding management ensures pigs receive the right feed at the right time.

Feed

Feed is any edible material given to pigs for nourishment.

Feed is the food supplied to pigs.

Feed Ingredient

A feed ingredient is any material used in preparing swine feed.

Feed ingredients are the components of feed.

Types of Feed Ingredients

  • Energy feeds
  • Protein feeds
  • Mineral supplements
  • Vitamin supplements
  • Feed additives
Feed ingredients are grouped by nutrient contribution.

Energy Feeds

Energy feeds are feed ingredients rich in carbohydrates and energy.

  • Corn
  • Rice bran
  • Cassava
  • Sorghum
Energy feeds mainly supply calories.

Protein Feeds

Protein feeds are feed ingredients rich in protein.

  • Soybean meal
  • Fish meal
  • Copra meal
Protein feeds mainly supply amino acids.

Mineral Supplements

Mineral supplements are feed ingredients added to supply essential minerals.

  • Limestone
  • Dicalcium phosphate
  • Salt
Mineral supplements supply minerals for bones and metabolism.

Vitamin Supplements

Vitamin supplements are added to supply essential vitamins that may be lacking in natural feed ingredients.

Vitamin supplements prevent vitamin deficiency.

Feed Additives

Feed additives are substances added to improve feed efficiency, health, preservation, or performance.

  • Probiotics
  • Enzymes
  • Antioxidants
  • Acidifiers
  • Mold inhibitors
Feed additives improve feed performance, health, and preservation.

Ration

A ration is the total amount of feed given to a pig in 24 hours.

Ration is the daily feed allowance.

Balanced Ration

A balanced ration contains all nutrients in the correct amount and proportion needed by the pig.

Balanced ration supplies complete nutrition.

Feed Formulation

Feed formulation is the process of combining feed ingredients to meet the nutrient requirements of pigs at practical cost.

Feed formulation balances nutrients in the ration.

Feeding Schedule

A feeding schedule is the planned timing and frequency of feeding.

Feeding schedule controls feeding time and frequency.

Feeding Frequency

Feeding frequency refers to how often pigs are fed each day.

Feeding frequency is the number of feedings per day.

Ad Libitum Feeding

Ad libitum feeding means pigs have free access to feed at all times.

Ad libitum = unlimited feeding.

Restricted Feeding

Restricted feeding means feed is given in controlled or limited amounts.

Restricted feeding = controlled feed intake.

Hand Feeding

Hand feeding is the manual distribution of feed by workers.

Hand feeding is manual feed delivery.

Self-Feeding

Self-feeding is a feeding method where pigs obtain feed from self-feeders.

Self-feeding allows pigs to eat on demand.

Dry Feeding

Dry feeding is the feeding of dry feed without added water.

Dry feeding uses dry feed form.

Wet Feeding

Wet feeding is the feeding of feed mixed with water.

Wet feeding uses moist feed form.

Creep Feeding

Creep feeding is the practice of feeding piglets solid feed while they are still nursing from the sow.

It helps piglets adjust to solid feed before weaning.

Creep feeding prepares piglets for weaning.

Starter Feeding

Starter feeding is the feeding of young pigs after weaning.

Starter feed is usually highly digestible and nutrient-dense to support early post-weaning growth.

Starter feeding supports early post-weaning growth.

Grower Feeding

Grower feeding is the feeding of pigs during the growing stage.

It supports skeletal development, muscle growth, and steady weight gain.

Grower feeding supports body growth.

Finisher Feeding

Finisher feeding is the feeding of pigs during the final stage before market.

It supports final weight gain and efficient market preparation.

Finisher feeding supports finishing weight.

Sow Feeding

Sow feeding is the feeding program for breeding females.

It supports gestation, lactation, body condition, milk production, and future reproductive performance.

Sow feeding supports gestation and lactation.

Boar Feeding

Boar feeding is the feeding program for breeding males.

It supports body condition, libido, semen quality, and breeding performance.

Boar feeding supports breeding performance.

Feed Conversion Ratio

Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) is the amount of feed required to produce one unit of weight gain.

FCR = Feed Consumed ÷ Weight Gain

Lower FCR = better feed efficiency.

Feed Efficiency

Feed efficiency is the ability of pigs to convert feed into body weight efficiently.

Better feed efficiency means lower feed cost.

Feed Waste

Feed waste is feed lost due to poor handling, spoilage, feeder design, overfeeding, or spillage.

Feed waste increases production cost.

Underfeeding

Underfeeding is giving less feed than required by the pig.

It may cause poor growth, poor reproduction, low body condition, and weak performance.

Underfeeding causes poor growth and poor performance.

Overfeeding

Overfeeding is giving more feed than required by the pig.

It may cause feed waste, excess fat, poor reproductive condition, and increased cost.

Overfeeding causes waste and excess fat.

Feeding Errors

Common feeding errors include:

  • Underfeeding
  • Overfeeding
  • Unbalanced ration
  • Poor feed quality
  • Irregular feeding
  • Improper feed storage
  • Using moldy or spoiled feeds
Feeding errors reduce performance and profitability.

Board Exam Trap Terms

Ad Libitum vs. Restricted Feeding

  • Ad libitum = unlimited feed access
  • Restricted = controlled feed intake
Difference = feed access.

Ration vs. Balanced Ration

  • Ration = daily feed amount
  • Balanced ration = complete daily nutrition
Ration = quantity. Balanced ration = quality.

Hand Feeding vs. Self-Feeding

  • Hand feeding = manual feed delivery
  • Self-feeding = pigs eat from self-feeders
Difference = method of feed delivery.

Starter vs. Grower vs. Finisher

  • Starter = young pigs after weaning
  • Grower = growing pigs
  • Finisher = pigs before market
Difference = production stage.

Board Exam Master Concept

Swine feeding questions commonly test feeding methods, feed ingredients, ration terms, feeding stages, and FCR.

Swine feeding focuses on proper feed delivery, efficiency, and cost control.
