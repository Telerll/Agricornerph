Topic 14

Promotion

Expanded discussion on promotion, communication, and marketing strategies in agricultural marketing.

Promotion

Promotion refers to the set of activities used to inform, persuade, and remind consumers about agricultural products and services.

It is a communication tool used by producers and marketers to influence consumer buying behavior.

Importance of Promotion

Helps increase awareness of available agricultural products in the market.

Encourages consumers to purchase and try new products.

Reduces information gaps between producers and consumers.

Supports market expansion and product acceptance.

Promotional Mix

The promotional mix refers to the combination of different tools used to communicate with the market.

Promotional Tools

1. Advertising

Paid, non-personal communication through media such as TV, radio, print, and online platforms.

2. Personal Selling

Direct interaction between seller and buyer to persuade purchase decisions.

3. Sales Promotion

Short-term incentives such as discounts, coupons, and special offers to encourage immediate purchase.

4. Public Relations

Activities aimed at building a positive image and relationship with the public.

Objectives of Promotion

To increase product awareness and visibility.

To stimulate demand and increase sales.

To build customer loyalty and trust.

To support long-term market growth.
