Introduction to Dairy Production

This lesson introduces dairy production, dairy animals, milk, lactation, milking, sanitation, clean milk production, and basic dairy management concepts.

Introduction to Dairy Production

Dairy Production

Dairy production refers to the raising and management of dairy animals primarily for milk production.

The dairy industry focuses on milk production, milk processing, dairy product manufacturing, and herd management.

Dairy production is the production of milk and dairy products.

Dairy Industry

The dairy industry is an important agricultural sector involved in producing, processing, and marketing milk and milk products.

Dairy industry contributes to food supply and economy.

Importance of Dairy Production

Dairy production is important because it provides:

  • Food
  • Nutrition
  • Livelihood
  • Employment
  • Income

Milk is an important source of protein, calcium, vitamins, and minerals.

Milk is a highly nutritious food.

Dairy Animals

Animals commonly used for dairy production include:

  • Dairy cattle
  • Carabao
  • Goat
  • Sheep
Dairy cattle are the major source of commercial milk.

Dairy Cattle

Dairy cattle are cattle breeds specifically developed for high milk production.

  • Large udder
  • Wedge-shaped body
  • High feed intake
  • Efficient milk production
Dairy cattle are selected for milk production traits.

Milk

Milk is the normal secretion of the mammary gland produced after parturition.

Milk is considered a nearly complete food because it contains essential nutrients.

Milk is the primary product of dairy animals.

Mammary Gland

The mammary gland is the milk-producing organ of dairy animals.

Mammary gland produces milk.

Udder

The udder is the external milk-producing structure of dairy animals.

It functions in milk production and milk storage.

Udder stores and secretes milk.

Teat

Teats are projections of the udder through which milk is removed.

Milk exits through the teat.

Lactation

Lactation is the process of milk secretion by the mammary gland.

Lactation = milk production process.

Lactation Period

Lactation period refers to the duration during which milk is produced after calving.

Lactation period follows parturition.

Dry Period

Dry period is the non-lactating resting period before the next calving.

It allows udder recovery and preparation for the next lactation.

Dry period allows udder recovery.

Colostrum

Colostrum is the first milk produced after parturition.

It is rich in antibodies and high in nutrients.

Colostrum provides passive immunity.

Milking

Milking refers to the removal of milk from the udder.

  • Hand milking
  • Machine milking
Milking removes milk from the udder.

Hand Milking

Hand milking involves manual removal of milk from the udder.

Hand milking is done manually.

Machine Milking

Machine milking uses mechanical equipment to remove milk.

  • Faster milking
  • Labor efficiency
  • Improved sanitation
Machine milking improves efficiency.

Dairy Products

Milk can be processed into various dairy products such as:

  • Cheese
  • Butter
  • Yogurt
  • Ice cream
  • Evaporated milk
Milk is processed into dairy products.

Dairy Management

Dairy management refers to proper feeding, breeding, housing, milking, and health care of dairy animals.

Proper dairy management improves milk production.

Dairy Housing

Proper housing provides:

  • Comfort
  • Ventilation
  • Cleanliness
  • Protection
Proper housing improves dairy productivity.

Dairy Nutrition

Proper nutrition is essential for milk production, reproduction, and animal health.

Nutrition affects milk yield.

Water Requirement

Dairy animals require large amounts of clean water because milk contains high water content.

Water is essential in milk production.

Feeding Management

Feeding management ensures dairy animals receive balanced nutrients.

Important nutrients include:

  • Protein
  • Energy
  • Minerals
  • Vitamins
Proper feeding improves milk production.

Reproduction in Dairy Animals

Efficient reproduction is necessary for continuous milk production.

Reproduction supports continuous lactation.

Calving

Calving refers to parturition in cattle.

Calving initiates lactation.

Heat Detection

Heat detection identifies animals ready for breeding.

  • Mounting behavior
  • Mucus discharge
  • Restlessness
Proper heat detection improves conception.

Artificial Insemination

Artificial insemination is the artificial introduction of semen into the reproductive tract.

It supports genetic improvement and disease control.

AI improves dairy genetics.

Health Management

Proper health management includes:

  • Vaccination
  • Sanitation
  • Disease prevention
  • Deworming
Good health management improves milk production.

Sanitation

Sanitation is important in dairy production to prevent milk contamination and disease.

Sanitation improves milk quality.

Clean Milk Production

Clean milk production refers to production of milk free from dirt, bacteria, and contaminants.

Clean milk is safe for consumption.

Pasteurization

Pasteurization is heating milk to destroy harmful microorganisms.

Pasteurization improves milk safety.

Milk Hygiene

Milk hygiene involves sanitary handling of milk during production and processing.

Milk hygiene maintains milk quality.

Dairy Farm Records

Important records include:

  • Milk production records
  • Breeding records
  • Health records
  • Feeding records
Record keeping improves dairy management.

Economic Importance of Dairy Production

Dairy production contributes to nutrition, employment, livelihood, and the national economy.

Dairy industry supports economic development.
