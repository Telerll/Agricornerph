1. Entomology

Entomology is the branch of zoology that deals with the scientific study of insects.

It includes the study of insect structure, classification, physiology, reproduction, behavior, ecology, economic importance, and methods of insect control.

  • Study of insects
  • Study of beneficial and harmful insects
  • Important in agriculture and pest management
  • Helps improve crop production and protection

2. Insects

Insects belong to the Phylum Arthropoda and Class Insecta. They are the largest and most diverse group of animals.

General Characteristics of Insects

  • Body divided into head, thorax, and abdomen
  • Bilateral symmetry
  • Segmented body
  • Possess chitinous exoskeleton
  • Jointed appendages
  • Three pairs of legs
  • Usually possess wings
  • Respiration through tracheal system

Insects are considered highly successful organisms because of their adaptability, rapid reproduction, small size, ability to fly, and metamorphosis.

3. Importance of Entomology

Agricultural Importance

  • Helps control insect pests
  • Improves crop production
  • Supports integrated pest management
  • Protects crops from damage and diseases

Medical Importance

Some insects transmit diseases affecting humans and animals.

  • Mosquitoes – malaria and dengue
  • Flies – mechanical transmission of pathogens
  • Lice and fleas – disease transmission

4. Beneficial Insects

Beneficial insects provide useful services to humans, agriculture, and the environment.

  • Pollination of crops
  • Biological control of pests
  • Production of commercial products
  • Decomposition of organic matter

Examples of Beneficial Insects

  • Honeybees
  • Butterflies
  • Lady beetles
  • Dragonflies
  • Parasitoid wasps

5. Harmful Insects

Harmful insects damage crops, stored products, animals, and human health.

  • Destroy crops and reduce yield
  • Transmit plant diseases
  • Damage stored grains
  • Cause nuisance and irritation

Examples of Harmful Insects

  • Rice stem borers
  • Aphids
  • Armyworms
  • Fruit flies
  • Termites

6. Products From Insects

Apiculture

Apiculture refers to the rearing and management of honeybees for honey and other bee products.

  • Honey
  • Beeswax
  • Royal jelly
  • Propolis

Sericulture

Sericulture is the rearing of silkworms for silk production.

Shellac

Shellac is a resinous substance produced by lac insects used in varnishes and polishes.

Cochineal

Cochineal is a red dye obtained from female cactus insects.

Chitin

Chitin is a hard material found in the insect exoskeleton and used in medicine, agriculture, and biotechnology.

  • Medical dressings
  • Biodegradable materials
  • Water purification
  • Agricultural products
