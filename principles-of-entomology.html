📘 Entomology

Entomology is the branch of zoology that deals with the scientific study of insects. It involves the study of insect structure, classification, behavior, reproduction, ecology, physiology, economic importance, and methods of control.

Entomologists study both beneficial and harmful insects. Some insects are useful because they help in pollination, biological control, and production of commercial products, while others are considered pests because they damage crops, stored products, animals, and even humans.

Importance of Entomology:
  • Helps improve agricultural production
  • Provides knowledge in pest management
  • Helps control insect-borne diseases
  • Supports environmental conservation
  • Contributes to food security and economy

🐞 Insects

Insects are animals belonging to the Phylum Arthropoda and Class Insecta. They are the largest and most diverse group of animals on Earth.

General Characteristics of Insects

  • Segmented Body – The body is divided into three main regions: head, thorax, and abdomen.
  • Bilateral Symmetry – The left and right sides of the body are equal.
  • Chitinous Exoskeleton – A hard outer covering made of chitin that protects the insect body and prevents water loss.
  • Jointed Appendages – Legs, antennae, and mouthparts are movable because they are jointed.
  • Three Pairs of Legs – Adult insects have six legs attached to the thorax.
  • Usually Have Wings – Most adult insects possess one or two pairs of wings.
  • Respiration Through Tracheae – Air enters through spiracles and passes through tracheal tubes.
Why Insects are Successful Organisms:
  • Small body size
  • Ability to fly
  • Rapid reproduction
  • Metamorphosis reduces competition among life stages
  • Ability to adapt to many environments

🍯 Products From Insects

Insects are economically important because they produce useful products that are used in food, medicine, cosmetics, industry, and agriculture.

1. Apiculture

Apiculture refers to the rearing and management of honeybees (Apis mellifera) for the production of honey and other bee products.

Products from Honeybees:
  • Honey – Used as food and medicine
  • Beeswax – Used in candles, cosmetics, and polish
  • Royal Jelly – Nutritious secretion fed to queen bees
  • Propolis – Sticky material with medicinal properties
  • Bee Venom – Used in some therapeutic treatments

2. Sericulture

Sericulture is the rearing of silkworms (Bombyx mori) for the production of silk.

Silkworm larvae produce silk fibers that are processed into silk cloth, which is highly valued in the textile industry.

3. Shellac

Shellac is a sticky resinous substance produced by lac insects. It is used in:

  • Wood polish
  • Varnishes
  • Electrical insulators
  • Sealing wax
  • Hair sprays and cosmetics

4. Cochineal

Cochineal is a natural red dye obtained from dried female cactus mealybugs.

It is commonly used in:

  • Food coloring
  • Cosmetics
  • Textile dyeing
  • Medicinal preparations

5. Chitin

Chitin is a tough material found in the exoskeleton of insects and crustaceans.

Chitin and its derivative chitosan are used in:

  • Medical dressings
  • Tissue repair
  • Water purification
  • Biodegradable materials
  • Agricultural products

🌱 Importance of Insects

Beneficial Insects

  • Pollinate crops and flowering plants
  • Produce commercial products
  • Act as biological control agents
  • Help decompose organic matter
  • Serve as food source for other animals

Harmful Insects

  • Damage crops and reduce yield
  • Transmit plant diseases
  • Destroy stored grains and products
  • Cause irritation and diseases in humans and animals
  • Damage household materials and structures
