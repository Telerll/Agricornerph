🌍 Part 10: Soil Management & Classification

Soil Management

  • Soil management refers to practices used to maintain or improve soil productivity.
  • It aims to sustain soil fertility and prevent degradation.

Importance of Soil Management

  • Maintains soil fertility.
  • Prevents soil erosion and degradation.
  • Improves crop yield and productivity.
  • Ensures sustainable use of land resources.

Soil Erosion

  • Soil erosion is the removal of topsoil by wind or water.
  • It reduces soil fertility and productivity.

Types of Soil Erosion

  • Sheet erosion – uniform removal of thin soil layer
  • Rill erosion – small channels formed by runoff
  • Gully erosion – large channels or gullies

Soil Conservation Practices

  • Contour farming
  • Terracing
  • Strip cropping
  • Cover cropping
  • Mulching
Conservation = prevention of soil loss

Land Capability Classification

  • System used to classify land based on its ability for agricultural use.
  • Helps determine proper land use and management practices.

Soil Survey

  • Systematic study and mapping of soils in a particular area.
  • Provides information about soil types, properties, and distribution.

Soil Classification

  • Arrangement of soils into groups based on their properties and characteristics.
  • Helps in understanding soil behavior and management.

Soil Classification Levels

  • Order
  • Suborder
  • Great Group
  • Subgroup
  • Family
  • Series

Common Soil Orders

  • Entisols – young soils with little profile development
  • Inceptisols – slightly developed soils
  • Alfisols – fertile soils with good productivity
  • Mollisols – rich, dark soils with high organic matter

Importance of Soil Classification

  • Helps in land use planning.
  • Guides agricultural practices.
  • Improves soil management decisions.

Sustainable Soil Management

  • Maintaining soil productivity for future generations.
  • Combining conservation, proper fertilization, and good farming practices.
Sustainable soil = long-term productivity
