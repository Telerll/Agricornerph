Introduction to Animal Nutrition

Animal Nutrition

Animal nutrition is the science of feeding animals to support maintenance, growth, reproduction, and production.

It deals with:

  • What animals eat
  • How nutrients are used
  • How feed affects animal performance
Animal nutrition is one of the most important foundations of livestock production.

Importance of Animal Nutrition

Proper nutrition is essential for:

  • Survival
  • Growth
  • Reproduction
  • Health
  • Milk production
  • Egg production
  • Meat production
  • Work performance
Without proper nutrition, animals cannot perform efficiently.

Nutrition and Production

Animal production depends heavily on nutrition.

Good nutrition improves:

  • Growth rate
  • Feed efficiency
  • Fertility
  • Immunity
  • Product quality
  • Profitability

Poor nutrition causes:

  • Slow growth
  • Low fertility
  • Weak immunity
  • Poor production
  • Higher disease risk
Nutrition directly affects productivity and profitability.

Functions of Nutrition

Maintenance

Maintenance refers to nutrients needed to keep the animal alive.

Growth

Growth refers to nutrients needed to build body tissues.

Reproduction

Reproduction refers to nutrients needed for fertility, pregnancy, and lactation.

Production

Production refers to nutrients needed for milk, meat, eggs, and work.

Maintenance always comes first before production.

Animal Nutrition in Livestock Production

In livestock, nutrition affects:

  • Growth rate
  • Feed conversion
  • Body condition
  • Fertility
  • Milk yield
  • Egg production
  • Meat quality
  • Disease resistance
Nutrition is the foundation of efficient livestock production.

Nutrition and Health

Proper nutrition improves:

  • Immunity
  • Disease resistance
  • Recovery
  • Body condition

Poor nutrition increases:

  • Disease risk
  • Weakness
  • Stress
  • Mortality
Nutrition is directly linked to health and survival.

Nutrition and Reproduction

Good nutrition improves:

  • Puberty
  • Fertility
  • Conception
  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation

Poor nutrition causes:

  • Delayed puberty
  • Poor conception
  • Weak offspring
  • Low milk production
Reproduction depends heavily on nutrition.

Nutrition and Economics

Feed is the largest cost in livestock production.

Feed often accounts for 60–70% of total production cost.

Good nutrition:

  • Reduces waste
  • Improves efficiency
  • Improves profit
Feeding is both a biological and economic decision.

Board Exam Master Concept

  • Feeding is not just giving food.
  • Feeding is supplying nutrients based on animal needs.
Correct feeding = better production.

Bioavailability

Bioavailability is the amount of a nutrient in feed that the animal can actually digest, absorb, and use.

Not all nutrients present in feed are fully available to the animal.

Feed value depends not only on nutrient content, but also on nutrient availability.

Ad Libitum Feeding

Ad libitum means feed or water is given free choice, allowing the animal to consume as much as it wants.

Common in:

  • Broilers
  • Layers
  • Growing pigs
  • Dairy cattle
Ad libitum feeding supports rapid growth and production.

Nutrient Density

Nutrient density is the amount of nutrients contained in a given amount of feed.

High nutrient density means more nutrients per kilogram of feed.

Important for:

  • Broilers
  • Lactating dairy cows
  • Fast-growing animals
Animals with high production demand require more nutrient-dense diets.
