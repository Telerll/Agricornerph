📘 Agricultural Marketing

Complete Reviewer with Defined Marketing Terms and Concepts

Marketing

Marketing is the process of planning, pricing, promoting, and distributing goods and services to satisfy consumer needs and organizational goals.

Marketing Efficiency

Marketing efficiency refers to delivering products to consumers at the lowest possible cost while maintaining quality and customer satisfaction.
  • Pricing or Economic Efficiency – improving buying, selling, and pricing activities to respond to market demand.
  • Marketing Margins – difference between producer price and final selling price.
  • Input-Output Measurement – comparison of resources used and output produced.
  • Product Loss and Waste – spoiled or damaged products during marketing.
  • Load Factor Efficiency – effectiveness of transportation and storage usage.

Integration

  • Vertical Integration – one company controls multiple stages of production or marketing.
  • Horizontal Integration – one company controls several businesses at the same level.
  • Forward Integration – producers gain control over distributors or retailers.
  • Backward Integration – companies gain control over suppliers.

The Marketing Mix – 4P’s

  • Product – goods or services offered to satisfy consumer needs.
  • Price – amount paid by consumers for products or services.
  • Place/Distribution – movement of products from producers to consumers.
  • Promotion – communication activities encouraging consumers to buy.

1. Product

A product is anything offered to consumers for use, acquisition, or consumption to satisfy needs or wants.

Product Categories

  • Raw/Fresh Products – products sold in natural form.
  • Semi-Processed Products – partially processed products.
  • Processed Products – fully manufactured finished products.

Objectives of Product Decisions

  • Profitability – ability of a product to generate profit.
  • Market Share – percentage of total market sales controlled by a company.
  • Growth – increase in sales, customers, or production.
  • Budget Determination – planning expenditures for product activities.

Classifications of Products

Consumer Goods

Products bought for personal or household use.
  • Convenience Goods – frequently purchased with little effort.
  • Staple Goods – products bought routinely.
  • Impulse Goods – products bought without planning.
  • Emergency Goods – products purchased during urgent situations.
  • Shopping Goods – products compared before purchase.
  • Specialty Goods – unique products with strong brand preference.

Industrial Goods

Products used for producing other goods and services.

Tangibility Classification

  • Durable Goods – products used repeatedly over time.
  • Non-Durable Goods – products consumed quickly.
  • Services – intangible activities without ownership transfer.

Three Levels of Product

  • Core Product – main benefit consumers seek.
  • Augmented Product – added services and benefits.
  • Formal Product – physical and visible aspects such as design and packaging.

Product Mix

  • Product Mix – complete set of products offered by a company.
  • Product Line – group of related products.
  • Product Item – specific product version.
  • Wide Product Mix – many product lines.
  • Deep Product Mix – many variations within product lines.
  • Consistent Product Mix – closely related products.

Branding

  • Brand – name, sign, symbol, or design identifying a product.
  • Brand Name – spoken or written portion of the brand.
  • Brand Mark – visual symbol or logo of the brand.
  • Trademark – legally protected brand.
  • Manufacturer Brand – brand owned by producers.
  • Distributor Brand – brand owned by wholesalers or retailers.
  • Unbranded Goods – products sold without brand identity.

Packaging and Labeling

  • Packaging – container or wrapping protecting and presenting products.
  • Label – information attached to product packaging.
  • Grade Labels – indicate product quality.
  • Descriptive Labels – explain product use or maintenance.
  • Brand Labels – identify brand names or symbols.
  • Open Dating – indicates expiration or useful life.

New Products

  • Continuous Innovations – minor product improvements.
  • Dynamically Continuous Innovations – moderate changes affecting habits.
  • Discontinuous Innovations – major innovations creating new behaviors.

New Product Development Stages

  • Idea Generation – creating product ideas.
  • Screening – selecting feasible ideas.
  • Business Analysis – evaluating profitability.
  • Product Concept – detailed product description.
  • Product Development – creating actual products.
  • Market Testing – testing products in limited markets.
  • Commercialization – full market introduction.

Legal Aspects of Products

  • Product Liability – legal responsibility for defective products.
  • Negligence Theory – liability due to carelessness.
  • Breach of Warranty – failure to fulfill product promises.
  • Express Warranty – written or spoken guarantee.
  • Implied Warranty – warranty required by law.
  • Strict Liability – manufacturer liable even without negligence.
  • Misrepresentation or Fraud – misleading claims about products.

Product Quality

  • Product Quality – ability of products to satisfy customer needs.
  • Intrinsic Cues – physical characteristics indicating quality.
  • Extrinsic Cues – external indicators such as brand and packaging.
  • Durability – product lifespan.
  • Reliability – consistency of performance.
  • Precision – accuracy in product performance.

ISO 9000

ISO 9000 is a set of international standards for quality management systems.
  • ISO Certification – recognition that a company complies with ISO standards.

2. Price

Price is the amount consumers pay for products or services.

Mechanisms of Price Discovery

  • Individual Negotiations – prices determined through bargaining.
  • Organized Markets – centralized pricing systems.
  • Commodity Exchanges – markets for commodity trading.
  • Spot Market – immediate buying and selling.
  • Future Contracts – agreements for future delivery.
  • Auction Markets – prices determined by bidding.
  • Administered Prices – prices controlled by government.
  • Formula Pricing – prices based on formulas.

Price Fluctuations

  • Seasonal Variations – price changes due to seasons.
  • Annual Fluctuations – yearly price changes.
  • Trends – long-term price movements.
  • Cyclical Variations – price changes based on economic cycles.

Pricing Strategies

  • Premium Pricing – charging high prices for unique products.
  • Penetration Pricing – setting low introductory prices.
  • Economy Pricing – low-cost pricing strategy.
  • Price Skimming – charging high introductory prices then lowering later.
  • Psychological Pricing – pricing appealing emotionally to buyers.
  • Product Line Pricing – different prices within product ranges.
  • Optional Product Pricing – charging separately for extras.
  • Captive Product Pricing – low main product price but high complementary product price.
  • Product Bundle Pricing – combining products into one package.
  • Promotional Pricing – temporary reduced prices.
  • Geographical Pricing – pricing based on location.
  • Value Pricing – low prices offering good value.
  • Competitive Pricing – pricing similar to competitors.
  • Unit Pricing – pricing per unit quantity.
  • Price Lining – pricing products at several quality levels.

3. Place / Distribution

  • Distribution – movement of products from producers to consumers.
  • Channel Distribution – organizations involved in product flow.
  • Intermediaries or Resellers – middlemen assisting distribution.

4. Promotion

Promotion refers to communication activities encouraging consumers to buy products.

Advertising

  • Advertising – paid non-personal communication.
  • Product Advertising – promotes products.
  • Competitive Advertising – stimulates brand demand.
  • Comparative Advertising – compares brands directly.
  • Institutional Advertising – promotes company image.
  • Trade Advertising – targets resellers or dealers.
  • Cooperative Advertising – shared advertising expenses.
  • Advertising Campaign – coordinated promotional efforts.

Other Promotional Methods

  • Personal Selling – direct oral communication with buyers.
  • Public Relations – creating positive company image.
  • Publicity – unpaid media exposure.
  • Sales Promotion – short-term incentives increasing sales.
  • Consumer Promotion – coupons, samples, rebates.
  • Trade Promotion – dealer incentives.
  • Sales-force Promotion – bonuses and awards for salespeople.

Problems in Agricultural Marketing

  • Perishability – products spoil easily.
  • Seasonality – production changes with seasons.
  • Bulkiness – products are costly to transport.
  • Heterogeneity – products differ in quality.
  • Inadequate Market Information – lack of accurate data.
  • Marketing Support Services – transportation, storage, financing, and communication support.

Demand for Agricultural Products

  • Demand – quantity consumers are willing and able to buy.
  • Effective Demand – desire supported by purchasing power.
  • Law of Demand – as price increases, quantity demanded decreases.
  • Giffen’s Paradox – quantity demanded increases even when price rises.
  • Market Demand – total consumer demand in a market.
