Breeding and Reproduction in Dairy Cattle

This lesson covers breeding, reproduction, estrus, artificial insemination, gestation, calving, lactation, dry period, reproductive efficiency, and board exam recall terms in dairy cattle.

Breeding and Reproduction in Dairy Cattle

Breeding and Reproduction

Breeding and reproduction are essential components of dairy production because continuous milk production depends on successful reproduction.

Proper reproductive management improves milk production, herd replacement, genetic improvement, and profitability.

Efficient reproduction supports dairy productivity.

Breeding

Breeding refers to the mating of animals for reproduction and genetic improvement.

Breeding improves herd quality.

Reproduction

Reproduction is the biological process of producing offspring.

Reproduction maintains herd population.

Objectives of Breeding

  • Improve milk yield
  • Improve adaptability
  • Improve fertility
  • Improve disease resistance
Breeding improves desirable traits.

Selection

Selection is the process of choosing superior animals for breeding.

Selection improves genetics.

Culling

Culling refers to the removal of inferior or unproductive animals.

Culling improves herd quality.

Dairy Bull

A dairy bull is a mature male used for breeding purposes.

Bulls contribute genetics to offspring.

Dairy Cow

A dairy cow is a mature female producing milk.

Dairy cows are the primary milk producers.

Heifer

A heifer is a young female cattle that has not yet calved.

Heifers are future dairy cows.

Calf

A calf is a young cattle from birth to about one year old.

Calves are replacement stock.

Puberty

Puberty is the stage when animals become sexually mature.

Puberty marks reproductive capability.

Estrous Cycle

The estrous cycle is the recurring reproductive cycle in female cattle.

The average estrous cycle is about 21 days.

Dairy cattle have approximately 21-day estrous cycle.

Estrus

Estrus refers to the heat period when the cow is receptive to mating.

  • Mounting behavior
  • Restlessness
  • Mucus discharge
  • Bellowing
Estrus = heat period.

Ovulation

Ovulation is the release of egg cells from the ovary.

Ovulation occurs after estrus.

Heat Detection

Heat detection is the identification of cows in estrus.

Accurate heat detection improves conception.

Signs of Heat

  • Standing to be mounted
  • Nervousness
  • Swollen vulva
  • Mucus discharge
  • Reduced feed intake
Standing heat is the best sign of estrus.

Artificial Insemination

Artificial insemination is the introduction of semen into the reproductive tract using artificial methods.

  • Genetic improvement
  • Disease control
  • Reduced bull maintenance
AI improves genetic progress.

Natural Mating

Natural mating involves direct mating between bull and cow.

Natural mating uses live bulls.

Semen

Semen contains sperm cells used for fertilization.

Semen carries male reproductive cells.

Fertilization

Fertilization is the union of sperm and egg cells.

Fertilization forms the embryo.

Conception

Conception occurs when fertilization successfully results in pregnancy.

Successful conception leads to gestation.

Pregnancy

Pregnancy refers to the period during which the fetus develops inside the uterus.

Pregnancy supports fetal development.

Gestation Period

Gestation period in cattle is approximately 280 days.

Dairy cattle gestation = about 280 days.

Parturition

Parturition refers to the process of giving birth.

Parturition = calving.

Calving

Calving is the birth process in cattle.

Calving produces new offspring.

Dry Period

The dry period is the non-lactating period before calving. It allows udder recovery and preparation for the next lactation.

Dry period improves future milk production.

Lactation

Lactation is the secretion of milk after calving.

Lactation begins after parturition.

Lactation Period

The standard lactation period commonly lasts about 305 days.

Standard lactation period = 305 days.

Colostrum Production

Colostrum is produced immediately after calving.

Colostrum provides immunity to calves.

Breeding Age

Heifers should be bred at proper age and body weight.

Proper breeding age improves reproductive efficiency.

Breeding Weight

Adequate body weight is important before breeding.

Proper body weight supports conception.

Breeding Methods

  • Natural mating
  • Artificial insemination
Breeding methods affect genetic improvement.

Inbreeding

Inbreeding is mating between closely related animals. Excessive inbreeding may cause reduced vigor and genetic defects.

Excessive inbreeding is undesirable.

Crossbreeding

Crossbreeding combines desirable traits from different breeds.

  • Hybrid vigor
  • Adaptability
  • Improved production
Crossbreeding improves performance.

Hybrid Vigor

Hybrid vigor refers to improved performance of crossbred offspring.

Crossbreeds may perform better than parent breeds.

Pregnancy Diagnosis

Pregnancy diagnosis determines whether the cow is pregnant.

  • Rectal palpation
  • Ultrasound
Pregnancy diagnosis improves herd management.

Reproductive Efficiency

Reproductive efficiency refers to successful breeding and calving performance.

Efficient reproduction improves profitability.

Calving Interval

Calving interval refers to the period between successive calving. The ideal calving interval is about 12 months.

Short calving interval improves productivity.

Service Period

Service period is the interval from calving to successful conception.

Shorter service period improves efficiency.

Days Open

Days open refers to days from calving until conception.

Excessive days open reduce profitability.

Infertility

Infertility is reduced ability to reproduce.

  • Poor nutrition
  • Disease
  • Poor management
Infertility reduces herd productivity.

Abortion

Abortion is the premature expulsion of fetus.

Abortion causes reproductive losses.

Dystocia

Dystocia refers to difficult calving. Causes include large fetus and abnormal presentation.

Dystocia requires assistance.

Retained Placenta

Retained placenta occurs when fetal membranes fail to expel after calving.

Retained placenta may cause infection.

Nutrition and Reproduction

Proper nutrition improves fertility, conception, and pregnancy maintenance.

Nutrition affects reproduction.

Record Keeping

Reproductive records include breeding dates, calving dates, and pregnancy status.

Records improve reproductive management.

Importance of Reproductive Management

Proper reproductive management improves milk production, herd replacement, and profitability.

Efficient reproduction improves dairy productivity.

Board Exam Trap Terms

  • Estrus = heat period
  • Ovulation = release of egg
  • AI = artificial insemination
  • Gestation period = about 280 days
  • Parturition = calving
  • Lactation period = about 305 days
  • Dry period = non-lactating period
  • Calving interval = time between calving
  • Days open = calving to conception interval
  • Hybrid vigor = improved crossbred performance
These are common board recall terms in dairy reproduction.
