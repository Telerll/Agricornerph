Breeds of Dairy Cattle

This lesson covers major dairy cattle breeds, breed characteristics, milk yield, butterfat content, adaptability, tropical breeds, temperate breeds, and board exam recall points.

Breeds of Dairy Cattle

Breeds of Dairy Cattle

Dairy cattle breeds are cattle specifically developed and selected for efficient milk production.

Different breeds vary in milk yield, milk fat content, adaptability, body size, and climate tolerance.

Dairy breeds differ in milk production and adaptability.

Importance of Dairy Breeds

Knowledge of dairy breeds is important for breed selection, milk production efficiency, climate adaptation, and herd improvement.

Proper breed selection improves dairy productivity.

Holstein Friesian

Holstein Friesian is the most popular dairy breed worldwide.

  • Black and white color
  • Large body size
  • Highest milk yield among dairy breeds
  • Excellent milk production
  • Rapid growth

One disadvantage is lower heat tolerance in tropical climates.

Holstein Friesian = highest milk-producing dairy breed.

Jersey

Jersey cattle are small dairy cattle known for high butterfat content.

  • Light brown or fawn color
  • Small body size
  • Prominent eyes
  • High butterfat milk
  • Efficient feed utilization
  • Early maturity
Jersey = high butterfat milk breed.

Brown Swiss

Brown Swiss is one of the oldest dairy breeds.

  • Brown to gray color
  • Large body
  • Strong feet and legs
  • Good milk production
  • Heat tolerance
  • Calm temperament
Brown Swiss = hardy dairy breed.

Ayrshire

Ayrshire cattle are known for strong udder attachment and good milk production.

  • Red and white color
  • Medium body size
  • Strong udder
  • Good grazing ability
  • Hardy breed
Ayrshire = strong udder dairy breed.

Guernsey

Guernsey cattle are known for producing golden-colored milk.

  • Reddish fawn and white color
  • Medium body size
  • High carotene content in milk
  • Rich flavored milk
Guernsey = golden milk breed.

Milking Shorthorn

Milking Shorthorn is a dual-purpose breed used for milk and meat production.

  • Red, white, or roan color
  • Medium to large body
  • Good milk production
  • Adaptable breed
Milking Shorthorn = dual-purpose dairy breed.

Red Dane

Red Dane cattle are red-colored dairy cattle known for milk production and adaptability.

Red Dane = red dairy breed.

Sahiwal

Sahiwal is a tropical dairy breed originating from South Asia.

  • Reddish brown color
  • Heat tolerant
  • Disease resistant
  • Suitable for tropical climates
  • Good milk production under hot conditions
Sahiwal = heat-tolerant tropical dairy breed.

Gir

Gir cattle are dairy cattle originating from India.

  • Curved forehead
  • Long pendulous ears
  • Heat tolerance
  • Adaptability to tropical climate
  • Disease resistance
Gir = tropical dairy breed with pendulous ears.

Native Dairy Cattle

Native cattle are locally adapted breeds with lower milk production but better resistance to environmental stress.

  • Heat tolerance
  • Disease resistance
  • Adaptability
Native cattle are adaptable to local conditions.

Breed Selection and Improvement

Crossbreeding

Crossbreeding combines desirable traits from two or more breeds.

  • Improves milk yield
  • Improves adaptability
  • Improves disease resistance
Crossbreeding improves herd performance.

Dairy Breed Selection

Selection of dairy breeds depends on climate, feed availability, management system, and production goals.

Proper breed selection improves productivity.

Temperate Dairy Breeds

Temperate breeds perform best in cooler climates.

  • Holstein Friesian
  • Jersey
  • Brown Swiss
  • Ayrshire
  • Guernsey
Temperate breeds prefer cooler climates.

Tropical Dairy Breeds

Tropical breeds tolerate high temperature and humidity.

  • Sahiwal
  • Gir
Tropical breeds are heat tolerant.

Milk Yield

Milk yield refers to the amount of milk produced by dairy cattle.

It is affected by breed, nutrition, management, and health.

Breed strongly affects milk production.

Butterfat Content

Butterfat refers to the fat content of milk.

Higher butterfat is important for butter and cheese production.

Jersey cattle produce high butterfat milk.

Adaptability

Adaptability refers to the ability of cattle to survive and perform under different environments.

Adaptability improves dairy performance.

Heat Tolerance

Heat tolerance is essential in tropical dairy production.

Heat-tolerant breeds perform better in hot climates.

Disease Resistance

Disease-resistant breeds experience lower production losses.

Disease resistance improves herd health.

Feed Efficiency

Feed efficiency refers to efficient conversion of feed into milk.

Efficient breeds reduce production cost.

Udder Quality

Good udder quality is important for milk production, milking efficiency, and udder health.

Good udders improve milk production.

Reproductive Efficiency

Efficient reproduction supports continuous milk production.

Reproductive efficiency improves profitability.

Longevity

Longevity refers to long productive life.

Long productive life increases profitability.

Economic Importance of Dairy Breeds

Improved dairy breeds contribute to increased milk production, higher income, and improved dairy industry.

Dairy breeds contribute to economic development.

Importance of Proper Breed Management

Proper management allows dairy breeds to achieve maximum productivity.

Good management improves breed performance.

  • Guernsey = golden milk breed
