Integrated Physiology

Integrated physiology means all body systems work together to keep the animal alive, healthy, and productive.

No body system works alone. Every system depends on the others.

Body Systems Work Together

Digestive System

Breaks down feed into nutrients.

Respiratory System

Provides oxygen for metabolism.

Circulatory System

Transports nutrients, gases, hormones, and wastes.

Muscular System

Enables movement and production.

Skeletal System

Supports the body and protects organs.

Nervous System

Controls rapid responses and coordination.

Endocrine System

Regulates long-term body processes through hormones.

Immune System

Protects the animal against disease.

Reproductive System

Ensures continuation of species.

Excretory System

Removes wastes and maintains fluid balance.

Thermoregulation

Maintains body temperature.

All systems are interconnected.

Homeostasis

Homeostasis is the maintenance of a stable internal environment.

This is the central goal of all body systems.

All systems work together to maintain:

  • Body temperature
  • Water balance
  • pH balance
  • Blood sugar
  • Electrolyte balance
Homeostasis = balance. If homeostasis fails, production declines.

System Integration: From Feed to Growth

This is the biological flow of production:

  1. Animal eats feed
  2. Digestive system breaks down nutrients
  3. Nutrients are absorbed
  4. Respiratory system supplies oxygen
  5. Circulatory system transports nutrients and oxygen
  6. Cells produce energy
  7. Muscles and tissues grow
  8. Excretory system removes wastes
This is the biological basis of growth and production.

Stress and Production

Stress affects many body systems at the same time.

Stress causes:

  • Nervous system activation
  • Cortisol release
  • Lower immunity
  • Reduced digestion
  • Poor growth
  • Lower reproduction
  • Lower production
Stress = lower productivity + lower profitability

Negative Feedback Loop

Negative feedback loop is the body’s main control mechanism for maintaining homeostasis.

It works like a thermostat: when body conditions move away from normal, the body responds to bring them back to normal.

Parts of the Negative Feedback Loop

Sensor

Sensor detects change in the internal environment.

Control Center

Control center receives information and decides what response is needed.

Common control centers include the brain, hypothalamus, and endocrine glands.

Effector

Effector carries out the response to restore balance.

Examples include muscles, glands, kidneys, and lungs.

Sensor → Control Center → Effector → Balance Restored

Example: Body Temperature Control

  1. Body temperature rises
  2. Sensor detects the increase
  3. Hypothalamus acts as the control center
  4. Sweating or panting begins
  5. Heat is lost
  6. Body temperature returns to normal
This is negative feedback.

Why Negative Feedback Matters

Most physiological regulation depends on negative feedback.

Examples:

  • Body temperature
  • Blood sugar
  • Water balance
  • Hormone levels
  • Blood pressure
Negative feedback is the foundation of homeostasis.
