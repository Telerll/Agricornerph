Concepts of Plant Disease

Plant disease is an abnormal condition of a plant caused by a pathogen or an unfavorable environmental factor. It interferes with the normal growth, structure, and function of the plant.

  • A disease develops over a period of time.
  • An injury usually happens instantly, such as breaking of a twig.
  • Disease may be caused by living or non-living agents.

Classification of Plant Diseases

According to Affected Plant Organ

Diseases may be classified based on the plant part affected.

  • Root diseases
  • Foliage diseases
  • Fruit diseases
  • Stem diseases

According to Symptom

Diseases may also be grouped according to the visible symptoms they produce.

  • Leaf spots
  • Rusts
  • Smuts
  • Anthracnose
  • Mosaic
  • Wilts
  • Fruit rots

According to Type of Affected Plant

  • Vegetable diseases
  • Forest tree diseases
  • Field crop diseases
  • Ornamental plant diseases

According to Type of Pathogen

This classification is commonly preferred because knowing the causal agent helps determine how the disease develops, spreads, and how it can be controlled.

Infectious Diseases

Infectious diseases are caused by living organisms called pathogens. These diseases can spread from one plant to another.

  • Diseases caused by fungi
  • Diseases caused by mycoplasmas
  • Diseases caused by bacteria
  • Diseases caused by viruses
  • Diseases caused by viroids
  • Diseases caused by protozoa
  • Diseases caused by parasitic flowering plants
  • Diseases caused by nematodes

Non-Infectious Diseases

Non-infectious diseases are caused by non-parasitic or abiotic agents. These are not contagious and do not spread from plant to plant.

  • Extremely high or excessively low temperatures
  • Unfavorable oxygen relations
  • Unfavorable moisture conditions
  • Nutrient deficiencies
  • Mineral toxicities
  • Air pollution
  • Toxicity of pesticides

Common Terms in Plant Pathology

Pathogen

Any agent that causes disease. It is usually a living organism such as a fungus, bacterium, virus, or nematode.

Parasite

An organism that depends wholly or partly on another living organism for food.

Obligate Parasite

A parasite that can live only on living tissues.

Facultative Parasite

An organism that normally lives as a saprophyte but can become parasitic.

Saprophyte

An organism that lives on dead organic or inorganic matter.

Host

A plant being attacked by a parasite or pathogen.

Pathogenicity

The capacity of a pathogen to cause disease.

Pathogenesis

The development of disease in the plant.

Virulence

The degree or amount of disease a pathogen can cause.

Aggressiveness

The rate at which disease develops.

Disease Resistance

The inherited ability of a plant to overcome or reduce the effects of a pathogen.

Susceptibility

The inability of a plant to resist disease.

Tolerance

The ability of a plant to withstand disease without severe reduction in yield.

Masked Symptoms

Symptoms that are not expressed because of unfavorable conditions.

Symptomless Carrier

A host that does not show symptoms even though it carries the pathogen.

Disease Triangle

The disease triangle shows the three factors needed for disease development.

  • Susceptible plant
  • The pathogen
  • Favorable environment

If one or more of these factors is absent, disease will not develop.
