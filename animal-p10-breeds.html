Breeds of Beef Cattle

This lesson covers major beef cattle breeds, breed classification, breed characteristics, adaptability, carcass traits, and board exam breed identifiers.

Breeds of Beef Cattle

Breed

A breed refers to a group of animals with common ancestry and similar physical and productive characteristics that are passed from one generation to another.

Beef cattle breeds are developed and selected primarily for meat production, growth rate, feed efficiency, carcass quality, and adaptability.

Beef breeds are selected mainly for meat production traits.

Classification of Beef Cattle Breeds

Beef cattle breeds are generally classified into Bos taurus and Bos indicus.

  • Bos taurus = European origin, temperate adaptation, heavier muscling, better carcass quality
  • Bos indicus = tropical origin, heat tolerant, disease resistant, presence of hump
Bos taurus = European cattle
Bos indicus = Zebu cattle

Bos taurus Breeds

Bos taurus breeds originated from Europe and are commonly used for beef production because of rapid growth, superior carcass quality, and heavy muscling.

However, they are generally less tolerant to heat and parasites compared to Bos indicus breeds.

Bos taurus breeds are known for meat quality.

Bos indicus Breeds

Bos indicus breeds are commonly called zebu cattle.

They usually possess a hump over the shoulder, loose skin, large dewlap, heat tolerance, and parasite resistance.

These breeds are highly adapted to tropical climates.

Bos indicus breeds are adapted to tropical conditions.

Brahman

The Brahman is one of the most important Bos indicus beef breeds. It was developed in the United States from Indian zebu cattle.

  • Hump
  • Loose skin
  • Large ears
  • Heat tolerance
  • Parasite resistance
  • Useful in tropical adaptation and crossbreeding
Brahman = heat-tolerant zebu breed.

Hereford

Hereford is a popular Bos taurus beef breed known for its red body and white face.

  • Red body
  • White face
  • Good beef quality
  • Docile temperament
  • Efficient grazing ability
  • Good carcass quality
Hereford = red body with white face.

Angus

Angus is a highly popular beef breed known for superior meat quality and excellent marbling.

  • Black color
  • Naturally polled
  • Excellent marbling
  • High carcass quality
  • Important in premium beef production
Angus = black polled beef breed.

Red Angus

Red Angus is similar to Angus except for its red coat color.

  • Red body color
  • Polled
  • Good carcass quality
  • Efficient growth
Red Angus = red-colored Angus breed.

Charolais

Charolais is a large-framed beef breed originating from France.

  • White or cream color
  • Rapid growth
  • Heavy muscling
  • Large body size
  • Useful in meat production and crossbreeding
Charolais = large white beef breed.

Limousin

Limousin originated from France and is known for lean meat production.

  • Golden-red color
  • Heavy muscling
  • High dressing percentage
  • Lean carcass
Limousin = muscular lean-meat breed.

Simmental

Simmental is a dual-purpose breed used for both beef and milk production.

  • Large body
  • Rapid growth
  • Good milk production
  • Good mothering ability
Simmental = dual-purpose breed.

Shorthorn

Shorthorn is a dual-purpose cattle breed developed for beef and milk production.

  • Red, white, or roan color
  • Good adaptability
  • Moderate size
  • Dual-purpose use
Shorthorn = dual-purpose cattle breed.

Santa Gertrudis

Santa Gertrudis is a synthetic beef breed developed from Brahman and Shorthorn.

  • Heat tolerance
  • Adaptability
  • Beef production
Santa Gertrudis = Brahman × Shorthorn cross.

Brangus

Brangus is a hybrid breed developed from Brahman and Angus.

  • Heat tolerance
  • Black color
  • Polled
  • Good carcass quality
Brangus = Brahman × Angus cross.

Beefmaster

Beefmaster is a beef breed developed for adaptability, fertility, growth, and beef production.

  • Tropical adaptation
  • Good mothering ability
  • Heat tolerance
  • Good beef traits
Beefmaster = adaptable beef breed.

Nelore

Nelore is a major zebu breed originating from India and widely used in tropical regions.

  • White coat
  • Hump
  • Heat tolerance
  • Parasite resistance
Nelore = tropical zebu breed.

Belgian Blue

Belgian Blue is known for extreme muscling.

  • Double muscling
  • Heavy carcass yield
  • Lean meat production
Belgian Blue = double-muscled breed.

Gelbvieh

Gelbvieh is a German breed known for fertility, maternal ability, and growth performance.

Gelbvieh = maternal beef breed.

Breed Improvement Concepts

Crossbreeding

Crossbreeding involves mating animals from different breeds to combine desirable traits.

  • Hybrid vigor
  • Improved growth
  • Better adaptability
  • Improved fertility
Crossbreeding combines desirable traits.

Hybrid Vigor

Hybrid vigor or heterosis refers to improved performance of crossbred offspring.

It may improve growth rate, survivability, fertility, and adaptability.

Hybrid vigor improves performance.

Adaptability of Breeds

Breed adaptability is important in selecting cattle suitable for specific environments.

Important adaptation traits include heat tolerance, parasite resistance, feed efficiency, and grazing ability.

Adaptability affects production success.

Temperament of Breeds

Temperament differs among breeds. Good temperament improves handling, safety, and management efficiency.

Temperament affects handling.

Growth Performance

Growth performance refers to weight gain, feed efficiency, and growth rate.

Fast-growing breeds improve meat production efficiency.

Growth performance affects profitability.

Carcass Quality

Carcass quality includes marbling, tenderness, dressing percentage, and lean meat yield.

Some breeds are known for premium carcass quality.

Carcass quality affects market value.

Dressing Percentage

Dressing percentage refers to carcass weight relative to live weight.

Breeds with higher dressing percentage produce more meat.

Dressing percentage measures carcass yield.

Maternal Ability

Maternal ability includes milk production, calf care, and mothering instinct.

Good maternal ability improves calf survival.

Maternal ability affects calf growth.

Feed Efficiency

Feed efficiency refers to the ability to convert feed into body weight gain.

Efficient breeds reduce production cost.

Feed efficiency affects profitability.

Selection of Breeds

Breed selection depends on climate, management system, production goal, market demand, and feed availability.

Proper breed selection improves productivity.

Importance of Beef Breeds

Beef breeds contribute to meat production, food security, farm income, and livestock improvement.

Beef breeds support livestock production.

  • Bos indicus = Zebu cattle
  • Brahman = heat-tolerant breed
  • Hereford = white-faced breed
  • Angus = black polled breed
  • Charolais = white large breed
  • Limousin = lean muscular breed
  • Simmental = dual-purpose breed
  • Brangus = Brahman × Angus
  • Santa Gertrudis = Brahman × Shorthorn
  • Belgian Blue = double-muscled breed
  • Hybrid vigor = heterosis
  • Dressing percentage = carcass yield
These are common board recall terms in beef cattle breeds.
