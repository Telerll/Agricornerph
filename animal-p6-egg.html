Egg and Meat Production

Egg and meat production is the branch of poultry production concerned with the efficient production of eggs and poultry meat for human consumption.

It focuses on egg production, meat production, layer management, broiler management, productivity, efficiency, and profitability.

Egg and meat production are the two main economic outputs of poultry production.

Importance of Egg and Meat Production

  • Provides affordable animal protein
  • Supports food security
  • Generates income
  • Supplies eggs and meat for human nutrition
  • Supports poultry industry profitability
Eggs and meat are the major commercial products of poultry.

Egg Production

Egg production is the process of producing eggs for human consumption or hatching.

It is the primary function of layers and breeder hens.

Egg production is the main purpose of layer birds.

Layer

A layer is a female chicken raised primarily for egg production.

Layers are birds raised for eggs.

Layer Production

Layer production is the management of hens for efficient and continuous egg production.

Layer production focuses on efficient egg output.

Point of Lay

Point of lay is the age when pullets begin egg production.

This usually occurs at about 18–20 weeks of age.

Point of lay is the beginning of egg production.

Pullet and Hen

Pullet is a young female chicken before the onset of laying.

Hen is a mature female chicken, especially one in egg production.

Pullet = young female before laying. Hen = mature laying female.

Egg Production Cycle

The egg production cycle is the laying period during which hens produce eggs.

Egg production follows a laying cycle.

Peak Production

Peak production is the period when hens produce the highest number of eggs.

Peak production is the highest egg output period.

Persistency of Lay

Persistency of lay is the ability of hens to maintain egg production over time.

Persistency means sustained egg production.

Broodiness

Broodiness is the tendency of hens to stop laying and sit on eggs for incubation.

Broodiness reduces egg production.

Egg

An egg is the reproductive product of the hen consisting of yolk, albumen, membranes, and shell.

Egg is the reproductive product of the hen.

Major Parts of the Egg

  • Shell = hard outer covering that protects the egg contents
  • Shell membranes = protective membranes beneath the shell
  • Albumen = egg white that surrounds and protects the yolk
  • Yolk = yellow nutrient-rich part of the egg
  • Chalaza = twisted cord-like structure that holds the yolk in position
  • Air cell = air pocket found at the large end of the egg
Each egg part has a specific protective or nutritional function.

Factors Affecting Egg Production

  • Breed
  • Nutrition
  • Lighting
  • Health
  • Age
  • Management
Egg production depends on genetics and management.

Lighting in Egg Production

Light stimulates reproductive hormones and supports egg production.

Layers usually require about 14–16 hours of light per day.

Lighting stimulates laying.

Egg Quality

Egg quality refers to the external and internal quality of eggs.

  • External quality = shell cleanliness, shell strength, shell texture, shell shape
  • Internal quality = yolk quality, albumen quality, air cell size
Egg quality affects market value, freshness, and hatchability.

Candling of Eggs

Candling is the use of light to examine internal egg quality.

Candling checks egg quality and fertility.

Meat Production

Meat production is the raising of poultry for meat.

It is the primary function of broilers.

Meat production is the main purpose of broilers.

Broiler

A broiler is a chicken raised primarily for meat production.

Broilers are birds raised for meat.

Broiler Production

Broiler production is the management of chickens for rapid growth and meat yield.

Broiler production focuses on fast growth and efficient meat output.

Market Age

Market age is the age at which broilers are ready for sale.

Broilers are usually marketed at about 5–7 weeks.

Broilers are marketed young.

Market Weight

Market weight is the body weight at which broilers are sold.

Market weight is the target selling weight.

Feed Conversion Ratio

Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) is the amount of feed needed to produce one unit of gain.

Lower FCR means better broiler efficiency.

Dressing Percentage

Dressing percentage is the percentage of carcass weight from live weight after slaughter.

Dressing % = (Carcass Weight ÷ Live Weight) × 100

Carcass

A carcass is the dressed body of the bird after slaughter and removal of feathers, blood, and viscera.

Carcass is the dressed body for meat.

Factors Affecting Meat Production

  • Breed
  • Nutrition
  • Health
  • Growth rate
  • Management
Meat production depends on genetics and management.

Meat Quality

Meat quality refers to the acceptability of poultry meat based on tenderness, juiciness, color, and flavor.

Meat quality affects consumer acceptance.

Board Exam Master Concept

Poultry production has two main outputs:

  • Eggs = layers
  • Meat = broilers
Layers produce eggs, broilers produce meat.
