Agricultural Knowledge System (AKS)

Definition

The Agricultural Knowledge System (AKS) is a system of beliefs, cognitions, models, theories, concepts, and other products of the human mind related to agriculture.

It includes the people, organizations, technologies, and institutions involved in generating, transferring, and using agricultural knowledge for development.

Pluralistic Extension Policy

A pluralistic extension policy is the practice of allowing several organizations to provide extension work to different farmers in the country.

Extension work may be done by DA industry bureaus, DA commodity agencies, SUCs, NGOs, and private companies.

AKS as Technology / Knowledge System

Four Components of AKS

  1. Technology Generation
  2. Technology Transfer
  3. Technology Utilization
  4. Agricultural Policy

1. Technology Generation

Technology generation consists of planning, administration, and implementation of research activities that develop, assess, adapt, and test improved agricultural technology for farmers and other users.

It focuses on creating new knowledge and innovations through research.

2. Technology Transfer

Technology transfer further evaluates and adapts research outputs for users and widely disseminates knowledge and inputs to different target adopters.

It connects research results to farmers through extension workers, training, demonstrations, and information campaigns.

3. Technology Utilization

Technology utilization encompasses the users of agricultural technology, including user awareness, adaptation, and adoption of technology.

This stage focuses on the actual use of agricultural innovations by farmers and other users.

4. Agricultural Policy

Agricultural policy relates to government development goals and strategies, market and price policies, and levels of resource investment in the agricultural system.

Policies guide agricultural programs and influence how technologies, services, and resources are delivered to farmers.

Importance of AKS

  • Links research, extension, education, and farmers.
  • Improves dissemination of agricultural information.
  • Promotes adoption of innovations.
  • Strengthens agricultural productivity.
  • Supports sustainable agricultural development.
  • Improves farmers’ quality of life.
