Nutritional Deficiency Disorders

Nutritional Deficiency Disorders

Nutritional deficiency disorders are diseases or abnormal conditions caused by inadequate intake, poor absorption, or improper utilization of nutrients.

They occur when animals do not receive enough:

  • Energy
  • Protein
  • Vitamins
  • Minerals
  • Water
Deficiency disorders reduce health, growth, and productivity.

Causes of Nutritional Deficiency Disorders

Deficiencies may result from:

  • Poor quality feed
  • Unbalanced ration
  • Low feed intake
  • Poor digestion
  • Poor absorption
  • Disease or parasites
  • Nutrient antagonism
Deficiency may result from poor feeding or poor nutrient use.

Effects of Nutritional Deficiency

Deficiency causes:

  • Poor growth
  • Weight loss
  • Low fertility
  • Weak immunity
  • Poor production
  • Higher disease risk
Deficiency reduces performance and profitability.

Energy Deficiency

Energy deficiency occurs when animals do not receive enough energy.

Causes:

  • Low feed intake
  • Poor quality roughage
  • Inadequate concentrates

Effects:

  • Weight loss
  • Weakness
  • Poor growth
  • Low milk yield
  • Poor reproduction
Energy deficiency = poor body condition and low production.

Protein Deficiency

Protein deficiency occurs when animals do not receive enough protein.

Effects:

  • Poor growth
  • Muscle wasting
  • Poor hair coat
  • Low milk production
  • Low fertility
Protein deficiency reduces growth and tissue repair.

Vitamin Deficiency

Vitamin deficiency occurs when animals lack essential vitamins.

Common vitamin deficiencies include:

  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin D
  • Vitamin E
  • Vitamin K
  • B-complex vitamins
Vitamin deficiency affects growth, vision, immunity, and metabolism.

Vitamin A Deficiency

Vitamin A deficiency affects vision, epithelial health, immunity, and reproduction.

Effects:

  • Night blindness
  • Poor vision
  • Weak immunity
  • Poor growth
  • Reproductive failure
Vitamin A = vision and epithelial health.

Vitamin D Deficiency

Vitamin D deficiency reduces calcium absorption and affects bone health.

Effects:

  • Rickets in young animals
  • Osteomalacia in adult animals
  • Weak bones
  • Poor growth
Vitamin D = calcium absorption and bone health.

Vitamin E Deficiency

Vitamin E deficiency affects antioxidant protection, muscle health, and fertility.

Effects:

  • Muscle weakness
  • White muscle disease
  • Poor fertility
Vitamin E = antioxidant and muscle protection.

Mineral Deficiency

Mineral deficiencies are common and highly important in livestock.

Common mineral deficiencies include:

  • Calcium
  • Phosphorus
  • Iron
  • Iodine
  • Selenium
  • Zinc
Mineral deficiency often causes major production loss.

Calcium Deficiency

Calcium deficiency affects bones, muscle function, milk production, and eggshell quality.

Effects:

  • Weak bones
  • Rickets
  • Poor growth
  • Milk fever
  • Poor eggshell quality
Calcium = bones, milk, muscle.

Phosphorus Deficiency

Phosphorus deficiency affects bone development, energy metabolism, and reproduction.

Effects:

  • Poor growth
  • Weak bones
  • Low fertility
  • Depraved appetite or pica
Phosphorus = bones, energy, fertility.

Iron Deficiency

Iron deficiency reduces hemoglobin formation and oxygen transport.

Effects:

  • Anemia
  • Weakness
  • Pale mucous membranes

Iron deficiency is common in piglets.

Iron deficiency = anemia.

Iodine Deficiency

Iodine deficiency affects thyroid hormone production.

Effects:

  • Goiter
  • Weak newborns
  • Poor growth
Iodine = thyroid function.

Selenium Deficiency

Selenium deficiency affects antioxidant function, immunity, and reproduction.

Effects:

  • White muscle disease
  • Weak immunity
  • Retained placenta
Selenium = antioxidant and reproduction.

Zinc Deficiency

Zinc deficiency affects skin condition, growth, and enzyme function.

Effects:

  • Poor skin condition
  • Poor growth
  • Parakeratosis, especially in swine
Zinc = skin, growth, enzyme function.

Water Deficiency

Water deficiency occurs when animals do not receive enough water.

Effects:

  • Dehydration
  • Poor appetite
  • Low growth
  • Poor milk production
  • Death in severe cases
Water is the most critical nutrient.

Deficiency and Immunity

Nutrient deficiency weakens:

  • Immune response
  • Disease resistance
  • Vaccine response
Poor nutrition weakens immunity.

Prevention of Nutritional Deficiency

Prevent nutritional deficiency by:

  • Feeding balanced ration
  • Using quality feed
  • Supplementing minerals and vitamins
  • Ensuring clean water
  • Proper management
Prevention is cheaper than treatment.

Board Exam Master Concept

Deficiency disorders are often not caused by lack of feed alone.

They may result from:

  • Poor quality feed
  • Poor digestion
  • Poor absorption
  • Poor balance
Animals may eat enough and still be deficient.

Board Exam Clinical Shortcuts

Rickets

Rickets is the softening and poor mineralization of bones in young animals caused by calcium, phosphorus, or vitamin D deficiency.

Rickets = weak bones in growing animals.

Osteomalacia

Osteomalacia is the softening of mature bones in adult animals caused by calcium, phosphorus, or vitamin D deficiency.

Osteomalacia = weak bones in adults.

Goiter

Goiter is the enlargement of the thyroid gland caused by iodine deficiency.

Goiter = enlarged thyroid.

Anemia

Anemia is a condition in which the blood has reduced oxygen-carrying capacity due to low red blood cells or hemoglobin.

Commonly linked to deficiency of:

  • Iron
  • Copper
  • Vitamin B12
Anemia = low oxygen-carrying blood.

Parakeratosis

Parakeratosis is a skin disorder in swine caused by zinc deficiency.

Signs:

  • Thickened skin
  • Rough skin
  • Crusty lesions
Parakeratosis = zinc deficiency skin disease in pigs.

Curled Toe Paralysis

Curled toe paralysis is a condition in poultry caused by riboflavin or vitamin B2 deficiency.

Signs:

  • Curled toes
  • Weakness
  • Difficulty walking
Curled toe paralysis = B2 deficiency in poultry.
