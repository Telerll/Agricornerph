Sound Cooperative Management

Sound cooperative management refers to the efficient and effective utilization of cooperative resources as a business organization. Its goal is to serve the needs of members while following universally accepted cooperative principles.

Potential Advantages of Cooperative Organizations

Farmers and rural households may either produce inputs themselves or purchase them. Cooperatives provide several advantages compared to individual buyers and sellers.
Advantages:

a. Economies of scale such as bulk purchasing that reduces cost
b. Reduction of transaction costs including information, implementation, control, and exchange expenses
c. Reduction of uncertainty in prices and availability of inputs
d. Avoidance of linked markets where buying inputs is tied to loan conditions
e. Provision of additional services and access to resources not available individually
