Agriculture and Related Laws

Agricultural laws are rules and regulations created to improve agricultural production, protect farmers and consumers, conserve natural resources, promote food security, and regulate agricultural activities in the Philippines.

These laws guide the development of Philippine agriculture and help ensure the welfare of farmers, fisherfolk, consumers, agricultural professionals, and the environment.

Agrarian Reform Laws

PD 27 — Tenants Emancipation Decree of 1972

PD 27 provided for the transfer of rice and corn lands under tenancy to the actual tillers of the land.

  • Known as the Tenants Emancipation Decree of 1972
  • Provided land ownership opportunities to qualified tenant-farmers
  • Focused mainly on rice and corn lands under tenancy

Proclamation No. 131

Proclamation No. 131 was issued in 1987 and instituted the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Program covering public and private agricultural lands.

Executive Order No. 229

Executive Order No. 229 provided the mechanisms necessary for the implementation of the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Program.

RA 6657 — Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Law of 1988

RA 6657 is the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Law of 1988. It governs the implementation of agrarian reform in the Philippines.

  • Covers public and private agricultural lands
  • Includes other lands of public domain suitable for agriculture
  • Aims to promote equitable land distribution and social justice
  • Seeks to improve agricultural productivity and farmers’ welfare

RA 3844 as Amended by RA 6389 — Code of Agrarian Reform

RA 3844, as amended by RA 6389, is known as the Code of Agrarian Reform. It governs the implementation of agrarian reform programs in the Philippines.

It was promulgated to institute land reforms, including the abolition of share tenancy, transfer of capital to industry, and establishment of implementing agencies.

Land Reform

Land reform refers to government programs aimed at redistributing agricultural lands to farmers and improving land tenure systems.

  • Promotes social justice
  • Improves productivity
  • Reduces rural poverty
  • Strengthens farmer ownership

Land Tenure

Land tenure refers to the relationship between people and agricultural land, including ownership, tenancy, leasehold, and stewardship arrangements.

Rights of Farmers and Farmworkers

Farmers and farmworkers have rights related to land ownership, fair wages, safe working conditions, organization, government support, training, and agricultural assistance.

RA 8435 — Agriculture and Fisheries Modernization Act of 1997 (AFMA)

RA 8435 or AFMA prescribes urgent measures to modernize the agriculture and fisheries sectors in the Philippines. It aims to improve profitability and prepare the sector for the challenges of globalization through adequate and focused support services.

It became effective beginning February 9, 1998.

Major Concerns of AFMA

  • Food security
  • Poverty alleviation
  • Income enhancement and profitability, especially for farmers and fisherfolk
  • Global competitiveness
  • Sustainability

Poverty Alleviation Law

RA 8425 — Social Reform and Poverty Alleviation Act

RA 8425 strengthened anti-poverty programs in the Philippines. It created the National Anti-Poverty Commission or NAPC.

This law supports poverty reduction, food security, and assistance for marginalized sectors, including farmers and fisherfolk.

Pest Control Laws

Pest control laws regulate pesticides, fertilizers, plant quarantine, and pest management systems to protect crops, consumers, and the environment.

PD 1152 — Philippine Environment Code

PD 1152 regulates the use of fertilizers and pesticides in agriculture and prescribes tolerance levels for their safe use.

PD 1433 — Plant Quarantine Law of 1978

PD 1433 revised and consolidated existing quarantine laws to strengthen plant quarantine services under the Bureau of Plant Industry.

This law helps prevent the entry and spread of dangerous plant pests and diseases.

Memorandum Order 126 Series of 1993

This order implemented the KASAKALIKASAN Program, a National Integrated Pest Management Program focused on rice, corn, and vegetable production.

Integrated Pest Management encourages environmentally sound pest control and reduces excessive pesticide use.

Fertilizer and Pesticide Authority (FPA)

The Fertilizer and Pesticide Authority regulates fertilizers and pesticides in the Philippines.

  • Registers pesticides and fertilizers
  • Regulates agricultural chemical use
  • Monitors safe usage
  • Promotes farmer education on pesticide safety

Safe Use of Pesticides

  • Follow label instructions
  • Use protective equipment
  • Avoid overapplication
  • Store chemicals properly
  • Dispose pesticide containers safely
  • Prevent contamination of soil and water

Consumer Protection

RA 7394 — Consumer Act of the Philippines of 1991

RA 7394 protects the interests of consumers, promotes general welfare, and establishes standards of conduct for business and industry.

Objectives

  • Protection against hazards to health and safety
  • Protection against deceptive, unfair, and unconscionable sales acts and practices
  • Provision of information and education to help consumers make sound choices
  • Provision of adequate rights and means of redress
  • Involvement of consumer representatives in formulating social and economic policies

Food Safety

Food safety ensures that food products are clean, safe, and suitable for consumption. It protects public health, prevents contamination, and improves market confidence.

Product Labeling

Product labeling provides important information to consumers, such as ingredients, expiration dates, nutritional information, manufacturer details, and safety warnings.

The PRC and the Board of Agriculture

RA 8981 — PRC Modernization Act of 2000

RA 8981 modernized the Professional Regulation Commission by prescribing its powers, functions, and regulatory responsibilities.

RA 8435 Section 75 — Agriculture and Fisheries Board

RA 8435 provided for the creation of the Agriculture and Fisheries Board under the Professional Regulation Commission to upgrade the agriculture and fisheries profession.

Licensure Examination for Agriculturists

The Licensure Examination for Agriculturists evaluates the competence of agriculture graduates before they become licensed professionals.

Code of Ethics for Agriculturists

  • Uphold honesty and integrity
  • Protect the environment
  • Serve farmers and communities responsibly
  • Promote sustainable agriculture
  • Practice professionalism

Crop Insurance

PD 1467 — Philippine Crop Insurance Corporation (PCIC)

PD 1467 institutionalized the Philippine Crop Insurance Corporation. Under this law, PCIC provides insurance protection to farmers against losses caused by natural disasters, plant diseases, and pest infestation.

RA 8175

RA 8175 amended the charter of PCIC to make it more stable and beneficial to farmers and the national economy.

Importance of Crop Insurance

  • Protects farmer income
  • Reduces production risk
  • Encourages investment in farming
  • Helps farmers recover after disasters

Risks in Agriculture

  • Typhoons
  • Floods
  • Drought
  • Pests and diseases
  • Unstable prices
  • Climate change

RA 7900 — High Value Crops Law

RA 7900 promotes the production, processing, marketing, and distribution of high-value crops.

Examples of high-value crops include mango, coffee, cacao, vegetables, flowers, spices, and other crops with strong market potential.

Other Important Agricultural and Environmental Laws

RA 3601 — National Irrigation Administration (NIA)

RA 3601 established the National Irrigation Administration as a government-owned and controlled corporation. It aims to achieve optimal and diversified use of water through integrated irrigation projects.

RA 2152 — Irrigation Laws of the Philippines

RA 2152 covers laws related to irrigation development and management in the Philippines.

PD 1144 — Fertilizer and Pesticide Authority Law

PD 1144 created the Fertilizer and Pesticide Authority and abolished the Fertilizer Industry Authority.

PD 1151 — Philippine Environmental Policy

PD 1151 introduced environmental impact assessment in development activities and provided measures for preventing or mitigating adverse environmental effects.

PD 175 — Cooperative Movement Law

PD 175 strengthened the cooperative movement in the Philippines.

RA 2508 — Rural Credit Law

RA 2508 promoted rural credit and agricultural financing.

PD 775 — Sugar Marketing Cooperative Law

PD 775 strengthened sugar marketing cooperatives.

PD 269 — Electric Cooperative Law

PD 269 supported the development of electric cooperatives.

EO 898 — Transport Cooperative Law

EO 898 supported transport cooperatives and organized transport services.

RA 7308 — Seed Industry Development Act

RA 7308 promoted and developed the seed industry in the Philippines and created the National Seed Industry Council.

RA 7606 — Magna Carta for Small Farmers

RA 7606 provides protection and support for small farmers.

RA 9003 — Ecological Solid Waste Management Act

RA 9003 governs proper solid waste management and defines the roles of national agencies, local governments, and stakeholders.

RA 7586 — National Integrated Protected Areas System (NIPAS) Act

RA 7586 established and managed the National Integrated Protected Areas System to protect environmentally sensitive areas.

RA 8749 — Philippine Clean Air Act of 1999

RA 8749 promotes clean air and the balancing of development with environmental protection.

RA 8550 — Philippine Fisheries Code

RA 8550 provides for the development, management, and conservation of fisheries and aquatic resources.

RA 3571

RA 3571 prohibits the cutting, destroying, or injuring of planted or growing trees, flowering plants, shrubs, or scenic plants along public roads, plazas, parks, school premises, or public pleasure grounds.

RA 9147 — Wildlife Resources Conservation and Protection Act

RA 9147 protects the country’s fauna from illegal trade, abuse, and destruction.

EO 430 Series of 1990

EO 430 created the National Committee on Biosafety of the Philippines.

PD 1067 — Water Code of the Philippines

PD 1067 governs the ownership, appropriation, utilization, exploitation, development, conservation, and protection of water resources.

RA 8371 — Indigenous Peoples’ Rights Act of 1997

RA 8371 protects the rights of indigenous peoples and ancestral domains.

RA 7942 — Philippine Mining Act of 1992

RA 7942 governs mining development and regulation in the Philippines.

RA 7638 — Department of Energy Act of 1992

RA 7638 created the Department of Energy.

RA 7916 — Special Economic Zone Act of 1995

RA 7916 governs special economic zones in the Philippines.

RA 9136 — Electric Power Industry Reform Act of 2001

RA 9136 restructured the electric power industry and provided for the privatization of certain assets of the National Power Corporation.

Gintong Masaganang Ani Program (GMA Program)

The Gintong Masaganang Ani Program was a banner program for agricultural development and served as a blueprint for putting AFMA at work.

Other Relevant Notes

ASEAN Free Trade Agreement (AFTA)

AFTA is ASEAN’s trade agreement which required ASEAN countries to reduce tariffs between zero to five percent depending on the commodities traded.

Free Trade Agreement (FTA)

Free Trade Agreements allow tariff-free or reduced-tariff trade among member countries.

Comparative Advantage

Comparative advantage is the ability of one country or region to produce a commodity at a lower cost compared to another country or region.

International Trade

International trade refers to the exchange of goods and services across international boundaries.

General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT)

GATT was the forerunner of the World Trade Organization. It encouraged signing countries to negotiate multilateral and mutually advantageous tariff reductions.

Uruguay Round

The Uruguay Round was GATT’s eighth round of negotiation. It was first held in Punta del Este, Uruguay in 1986 and finally signed in Marrakesh, Morocco on April 15, 1994.
