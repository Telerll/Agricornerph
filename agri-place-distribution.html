Place / Distribution

Expanded discussion on distribution systems and how products reach consumers.

Place / Distribution

Place or distribution refers to the process of making products available to consumers at the right location, time, and condition.

It involves the movement of goods from producers through intermediaries until they reach the final consumers.

Importance of Distribution

Ensures products are delivered from production areas to consumers efficiently.

Reduces the gap between producers and final consumers.

Helps maintain product availability in different markets.

Distribution Channels

Distribution channels refer to the pathways through which products move from producers to consumers.

These channels involve intermediaries such as wholesalers, retailers, and agents.

Intermediaries

Intermediaries are individuals or organizations that help in the movement of products without necessarily producing them.

They assist in storage, transportation, and selling of goods.

Functions of Distribution

1. Physical Distribution

Movement and handling of products from producers to consumers.

2. Storage Function

Keeping products until they are needed in the market.

3. Transportation Function

Moving products from production areas to consumption centers.
